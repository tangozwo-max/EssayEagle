Point to add

Potential overshadowing of neurodevelopmental needs

Suggested statement: In complex cases like Tommy’s, behaviour may be interpreted primarily through ACEs or oppositionality, but this can risk overshadowing co-occurring neurodevelopmental needs. A good formulation should stay open to interacting pathways rather than assuming one single cause.

Why this matters: Maltreatment and neurodevelopmental difficulties often co-occur, yet co-twin evidence suggests the increased neurodevelopmental symptom load in maltreated children is not simply proof that maltreatment directly caused all of the neurodevelopmental burden.

Best wording
  • Short slide version: ACE-related explanations are important, but they should not obscure possible co-occurring neurodevelopmental needs.
  • Speaker note version: Because maltreatment and neurodevelopmental symptoms often co-occur, an ACE- or ODD-only reading of behaviour may miss overlapping autism, ADHD, or other neurodevelopmental needs; formulation should stay open to multiple pathways.
  • Cautious line: Adversity may also delay recognition and access to support for some neurodevelopmental conditions, particularly autism.
