Slide 1 — Tommy presenting issues and formulation

Working slide logic: Tommy’s school presentation can be framed through an internalizing–externalizing lens, then understood more clinically through formulation rather than simple misconduct language.

Current slide argument

Tommy’s aggression, impulsivity and relational strain may coexist with fear, uncertainty and emotional distress; formulation helps explain this pattern in context.

  • Visible school behaviour: aggression, impulsivity, struggles with interaction.
  • Possible internalizing states: fear, uncertainty, dysregulation, distress.
  • Clinical bridge: move from “what is wrong with this child?” to “what happened to this child?”
  • Frameworks used: internalizing/externalizing + 5Ps formulation.
Speaker direction
  • Open with what school may see first: behaviour and peer difficulty.
  • Then show that outward behaviour can coexist with hidden inward distress.
  • State that co-occurring internalizing and externalizing symptoms usually indicate a more complex and impairing presentation.
  • End by introducing the 5Ps as the structure for understanding Tommy’s whole story.
Use “greater psychiatric vulnerability” rather than a direct claim that this slide alone proves later depression.

Suggested final slide wording

Tommy’s presenting issues can be understood through an internalizing–externalizing lens.

  • Outward difficulties such as aggression, impulsivity and peer strain may coexist with inward distress such as fear, uncertainty and dysregulation.
  • When both patterns co-occur, this suggests a more impaired and clinically complex presentation.
  • A formulation approach helps move beyond judging behaviour as simple misconduct by asking how Tommy’s developmental history, caregiving experiences and wider context may have shaped his presentation.
  • The 5Ps structure this understanding into presenting, predisposing, precipitating, perpetuating and protective factors.
