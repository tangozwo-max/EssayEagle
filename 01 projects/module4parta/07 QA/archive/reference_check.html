💬 Original characterisation — ecological systems
Bronfenbrenner defines the developmental ecology as four nested levels: the microsystem (direct settings — family, school), the mesosystem (interrelations between microsystems), the exosystem (settings the child does not enter but that affect them — parents' workplace, healthcare system), and the macrosystem (overarching cultural, economic, and political patterns). In a later revision he added the chronosystem — the dimension of time across the life course and across historical changes. Summary of the canonical definition from Bronfenbrenner, U. (1979) The Ecology of Human Development, Harvard University Press. Cross-verified via multiple academic sources (Simply Psychology, LibreTexts, EBSCO Research Starters).
