## Key Claims
- Children exposed to threat (abuse, violence) show reduced amygdala, mPFC, and hippocampal volume and heightened amygdala threat reactivity across the majority of studies.
- Children exposed to deprivation show reduced volume and altered function in frontoparietal regions (not seen in threat-exposed children) — this dissociation supports the dimensional model.
- The dimensional model of adversity (threat vs. deprivation) better explains neural outcomes than a unitary ACE score.
- Evidence for stress acceleration (early maturation) is limited but emergent in some neurodevelopmental metrics.
- MRI-based evidence across 109 studies; findings are consistent enough to inform intervention targets.

## Numerical Findings
- Systematic review of 109 MRI-based studies; children and adolescents
- Reduced amygdala/hippocampal volume: consistent finding across majority of threat-exposure studies
- Frontoparietal alterations: consistent in deprivation-exposed, not threat-exposed

## Study Design
Pre-registered systematic review; searched major databases; included MRI structural and functional studies of childhood adversity; evaluated two competing theoretical models (dimensional model vs. stress acceleration).

## Limitation
Most included studies cross-sectional; limited longitudinal designs; precise adversity assessment varies widely across studies; larger diverse samples needed.
