## Key Claims
- Externalising behaviour trajectories (aggression, opposition, property/status violations) follow distinct developmental patterns from age 4 to 18.
- Aggression, opposition, and property violations decrease on average across development; status violations increase over time.
- Males show higher levels of externalising behaviour than females, but trajectory shapes are similar across sexes.
- Group-based trajectories cluster at different levels of the average curve; deviations from these norms can be identified as clinically meaningful.
- The high intercorrelation between proactive and reactive aggression (measurement artifact) obscures differential developmental correlates.

## Numerical Findings
- n = 2,076 children; longitudinal multiple birth cohort study; age 4–18 years
- Parent-reported externalising behaviours
- Multilevel growth curve analyses + semiparametric mixture models

## Study Design
Prospective longitudinal multiple birth cohort; parent-report across 14 years; multilevel growth curves and semiparametric group-based trajectory modelling.

## Limitation
Parent-report only (no multi-informant); Dutch cohort (generalisability may vary); birth cohort design means participants share historical context.
