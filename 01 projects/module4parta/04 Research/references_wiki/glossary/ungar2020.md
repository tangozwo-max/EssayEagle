## Key Claims
- Resilience is not a fixed individual trait but a multisystemic process that depends on individual, family, community, and cultural resources working together.
- Positive mental health outcomes following adversity are produced by navigating and negotiating access to resources across multiple ecological levels.
- Cultural context shapes what counts as a resource and what constitutes a resilient outcome — universal definitions of resilience are limited.
- Interventions targeting only the individual are insufficient; community-level and structural supports are essential for resilience-promoting outcomes.
- Mental health professionals should assess resilience-enabling environments, not only individual vulnerabilities.
