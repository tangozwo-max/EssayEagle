## Key Claims
- Maternal prenatal stress negatively affects fetal neurodevelopment through HPA axis dysregulation and glucocorticoid exposure.
- The placenta plays a key role in regulating fetal exposure to maternal cortisol via 11β-HSD2 enzyme; placental insufficiency increases fetal cortisol exposure.
- Prenatal stress is associated with child outcomes including increased anxiety, ADHD, cognitive difficulties, and altered stress reactivity.
- There are sensitive periods during gestation where the fetus is particularly vulnerable to stress effects.
- Possible mechanisms include: epigenetic modification, altered HPA axis programming, altered serotonin system development.

## Numerical Findings
- Book chapter/review; no primary data
- Synthesises human observational and animal experimental literature on prenatal stress

## Study Design
Book chapter in edited volume; narrative review of biological mechanisms linking maternal stress to fetal neurodevelopmental outcomes.

## Limitation
Narrative chapter format; causal inference limited (human studies largely observational); confounding with postnatal environment is difficult to separate; animal model extrapolation is speculative for human outcomes.
