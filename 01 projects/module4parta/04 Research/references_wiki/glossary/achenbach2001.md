## Key Claims
- The ASEBA (Achenbach System of Empirically Based Assessment) provides standardised, normed, multi-informant assessment of child behavioural and emotional problems.
- Core instruments: Child Behaviour Checklist (CBCL/6-18) for parents, Teacher Report Form (TRF), and Youth Self-Report (YSR).
- Generates empirically derived syndrome scales: internalising (anxious/depressed, withdrawn, somatic complaints) and externalising (rule-breaking, aggressive) domains.
- DSM-oriented scales allow alignment with diagnostic categories without imposing a categorical diagnosis.
- Wide normative data (multi-national); highly cited; gold standard for multi-informant assessment in child psychopathology research.

## Numerical Findings
- Assessment manual; normative data from large representative samples across multiple countries
- T-scores ≥70 (>98th percentile) indicate clinically significant range; 65–69 borderline clinical

## Study Design
Assessment system manual; documents development, norming, and psychometric properties of the ASEBA school-age instruments.

## Limitation
No PDF available; parent/teacher/self-report subject to reporter bias; cross-informant disagreement is common and clinically meaningful; does not replace clinical assessment; cultural adaptation required for non-Western populations.
