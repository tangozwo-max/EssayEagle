## Key Claims
- Maternal prenatal stress negatively impacts fetal and infant development, though the downstream mechanisms remain unclear and debated.
- Evidence suggests maternal stress disrupts functional connectivity of the amygdala in offspring, but sub-regional specificity of brain impact is unknown.
- Timing of fetal stress exposure influences which developmental domains are affected.
- Environmental disasters and war settings provide evidence of adverse fetal outcomes from extreme maternal stress.
- Georgia state (USA) fetal mortality data show poor maternal healthcare correlates with increased fetal mortality, warranting further structural investigation.

## Numerical Findings
- Georgia OASIS platform data: fetal mortality trends 2013–2023 analysed
- Observational/correlational; causation cannot be established from this data
- Review synthesis (not meta-analytic); exact effect sizes not aggregated

## Study Design
Narrative comprehensive review; includes literature review plus an original analysis of Georgia OASIS public health data on fetal mortality and maternal care rates.

## Limitation
Narrative (not systematic) review; Georgia data analysis is correlational only; debate remains over whether maternal stress is a mediator or causal factor; under-representation in research from diverse populations noted by authors.
