## Key Claims
- Safe, Stable, and Nurturing relationships (SSNRs) act as protective factors that buffer the negative health effects of ACE exposure.
- Children with higher ACE scores who also have SSNRs show better physical and mental health outcomes than those without SSNRs.
- Protective factors can moderate the ACE–outcome relationship — adversity is not deterministic.
- SSNRs include consistent, responsive caregiving, household stability, and safe community environments.
- Identifying and strengthening SSNRs in assessment and intervention is as important as identifying ACEs.

## Numerical Findings
- Cross-sectional survey data; specific sample size and effect sizes not available from metadata alone
- Outcomes: physical health, mental health; moderation analyses for ACE × SSNR interaction

## Study Design
Cross-sectional survey study; South Carolina data; examines moderating role of SSNRs in ACE–health outcome relationships.

## Limitation
Cross-sectional design precludes causal inference; self-report/parent-report measures; South Carolina sample may limit generalisability; SSNRs measured broadly without distinguishing component effects.
