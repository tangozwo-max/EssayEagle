## Key Claims
- Trauma is stored in the body — physiological dysregulation, not just memories, drives trauma symptoms.
- The Window of Tolerance (co-originating with Siegel) describes the functional arousal zone; trauma pushes survivors out of this window into hyperarousal or hypoarousal.
- Trauma disrupts brain regions including the thalamus, prefrontal cortex, amygdala, and Broca's area (disrupting verbal expression of trauma).
- Healing requires addressing the body and nervous system, not only cognitive processing — supports somatic, EMDR, movement-based interventions.
- Developmental trauma (complex PTSD in childhood) produces a different profile to single-incident adult trauma.

## Numerical Findings
- Book — not a primary research study; narrative clinical account with reference to van der Kolk's own research program
- No systematic data reported; anecdotal case material integrated with research summaries

## Study Design
Practitioner/popular science book; synthesises trauma neuroscience, clinical experience, and qualitative case material; readable account for clinicians and the public.

## Limitation
Not peer-reviewed as primary research; author's own interpretations are prominently featured; some claims extend beyond the evidence base; should be cited as clinical context/practitioner perspective, not empirical support.
