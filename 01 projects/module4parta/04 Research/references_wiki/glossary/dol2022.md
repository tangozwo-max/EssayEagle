## Key Claims
- Children with internalising–externalising comorbidity show significantly worse functioning than children with strictly internalising conditions.
- Parent psychological distress independently predicts worse child functioning (β = 0.01, p = 0.004).
- Distance to paediatric care also predicts worse functioning, implicating access barriers.
- Comorbid presentations are common in child and adolescent psychiatry and worsen health outcomes relative to single diagnoses.
- Health professionals should factor comorbidity into treatment planning and functional assessment.

## Numerical Findings
- Internalising–externalising comorbidity associated with worse functioning: β = 0.32 (p = 0.041)
- Parent psychological distress: β = 0.01 (p = 0.004)
- Sample: n = 100 children aged 4–17, clinical sample, single academic paediatric hospital

## Study Design
Cross-sectional clinical sample; comorbidity assessed via MINI-KID; functioning via WHO-DAS 2.0; linear regression adjusting for demographics.

## Limitation
Small single-centre clinical sample (n = 100); cross-sectional design precludes causal inference; limited generalisability beyond academic hospital populations.
