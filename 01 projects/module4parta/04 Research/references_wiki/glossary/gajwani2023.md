## Key Claims
- Children with neurodevelopmental conditions (e.g. ADHD, ASD) are disproportionately exposed to ACEs, creating a 'double jeopardy' of biological vulnerability plus adversity.
- Neurodevelopmental conditions increase susceptibility to the negative effects of ACEs; the combination produces worse outcomes than either alone.
- Current clinical systems often assess neurodevelopmental needs or trauma history separately, missing the interaction between the two.
- Integrated assessment models are needed that capture both neurodevelopmental profile and trauma history simultaneously.
- Tommy-relevant: underscores why a formulation must consider both biological vulnerabilities and environmental adversities together.
