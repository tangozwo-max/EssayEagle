## Key Claims
- Adolescent brain plasticity is a resource for learning and positive development, not only a vulnerability period.
- Connectivity changes during adolescence (particularly in prefrontal–limbic and social brain networks) support the acquisition of social and academic competencies.
- Contextual factors (family, school, community) shape how adolescent neural plasticity is channelled — toward adaptive or maladaptive outcomes.
- The connecting brain framework highlights adolescence as a sensitive period during which environmental supports can have particularly strong developmental effects.
- Supports intervention rationale: adolescence is a window of heightened plasticity where targeted support can redirect developmental trajectories.

## Numerical Findings
- Review article; synthesises neuroimaging and developmental literature
- Recent (2025); incorporates contemporary adolescent neuroscience

## Study Design
Narrative review; integrates neuroscience of adolescent plasticity with ecological/contextual developmental theory; focuses on connectivity changes and their functional implications.

## Limitation
Review article (not systematic); 'connecting brain' framework is new — empirical validation of the full model is ongoing; translation from neural findings to intervention design is speculative.
