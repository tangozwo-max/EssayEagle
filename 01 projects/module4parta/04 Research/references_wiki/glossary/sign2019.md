## Key Claims
- FASD (Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorders) results from prenatal alcohol exposure; even small amounts carry risk with no established 'safe' threshold.
- FASD presents with a broad profile of neurodevelopmental impairments: attention, memory, executive function, adaptive behaviour, and social communication.
- Children with FASD are frequently misdiagnosed (commonly as ADHD or attachment disorder) because FASD is not widely recognised by practitioners.
- Early identification and appropriate support significantly improves outcomes; specialist multidisciplinary assessment is required.
- Recommendations for education, health, and social care practitioners on recognition, referral, and management.

## Limitation
