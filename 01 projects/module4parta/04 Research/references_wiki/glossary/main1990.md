## Key Claims
- The foundational chapter establishing that parental unresolved trauma (particularly loss or abuse) is specifically associated with infant disorganised attachment.
- Disorganised/disoriented attachment (D attachment) arises when the caregiver is simultaneously the source of fear and the infant's only source of comfort — an irresolvable paradox.
- Frightened or frightening parental behaviour (not necessarily deliberate) is the proximal mechanism linking parental unresolved trauma to infant disorganisation.
- This chapter introduced the 'Main and Hesse hypothesis' that became the foundation of subsequent disorganised attachment research.
- Intergenerational transmission pathway: parent's own unresolved trauma → frightening/frightened caregiving → infant disorganisation.

- Empirical chapter; specific statistics require PDF access (not available)

## Study Design
Empirical study using Adult Attachment Interview (AAI) to assess parental unresolved status; Strange Situation Procedure to assess infant attachment; linked parent and infant classifications.
