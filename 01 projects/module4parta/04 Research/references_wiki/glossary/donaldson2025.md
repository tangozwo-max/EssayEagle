## Key Claims
- Four distinct mental health trajectory classes across the primary–secondary school transition: 'persistently elevated', 'emotional and peer problems', 'hyperactivity and conduct problems', and 'persistently low'.
- Low SES, special educational needs (SEN), and experiencing two or more negative life events (NLEs) increase likelihood of elevated mental health difficulty trajectories.
- Children worried about school transition are more likely to show trajectories characterised by elevated emotional problems.
- Transition is a vulnerable developmental period during which pre-existing mental health patterns consolidate or worsen.

## Numerical Findings
- n = 1,861 children, South East England; mean age at baseline 11.2 years (SD = 0.29); 46.8% female
- Three assessment points spanning primary–secondary transition (6-month intervals)
- Latent class growth analysis (LCGA) used to model trajectory classes

## Study Design
Prospective longitudinal cohort; three time-points; LCGA modelling; multinomial logistic regression for predictors of trajectory class membership.

## Limitation
Single geographic region (South East England) limits generalisability; attrition across three time-points not fully reported; no intervention component.
