## Key Claims
- Attachment is a fundamental biological system: infants are biologically predisposed to form a selective, preferential bond with one primary caregiver (the attachment figure).
- The attachment system is activated under threat, illness, or novelty; the infant seeks proximity to the attachment figure as a safe haven.
- The attachment figure also serves as a 'secure base' from which exploration occurs — exploration and attachment are complementary, not competing systems.
- Internal Working Models (IWMs) develop from attachment experiences; these cognitive-emotional schemas shape expectations about self, others, and relationships throughout life.
- Secure attachment supports healthy developmental outcomes; disruptions (separation, loss, frightening caregiving) create developmental risk.
