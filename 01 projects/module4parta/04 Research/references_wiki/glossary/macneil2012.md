## Key Claims
- Psychiatric diagnosis alone is insufficient to guide treatment planning; case formulation provides the individualised contextual understanding that diagnosis cannot.
- Formulation integrates biological, psychological, and social factors to explain why this person developed these problems at this time.
- Case formulation improves therapeutic alliance and helps clinicians select and tailor interventions beyond what diagnosis suggests.
- Diagnosis and formulation are complementary: diagnosis provides a shared language; formulation provides the individual narrative.
- In complex presentations (comorbidity, adversity history), formulation is essential to avoid treatment mismatch.

## Numerical Findings
- Commentary article; no primary data
- Draws on clinical and theoretical literature on formulation and diagnosis

## Study Design
Commentary/discussion article in BMC Medicine; conceptual argument for formulation-based practice supplementing diagnostic classification.

## Limitation
Opinion/commentary — not an empirical study; no systematic review of evidence for formulation-based vs. diagnosis-only interventions; limited operationalisation of when formulation adds measurable value.
