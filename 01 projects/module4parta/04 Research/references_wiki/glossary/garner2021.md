## Key Claims
- Safe, Stable, and Nurturing Relationships (SSNRs) are the primary mechanism for buffering childhood adversity and building resilience — biological necessities, not optional.
- An ecobiodevelopmental (EBD) model explains how early adverse and nurturing experiences are biologically embedded and influence health across the life span.
- Paediatric care requires a paradigm shift from identifying and managing adversity to proactively building relational health.
- Serve-and-return interaction (responsive caregiving) is a core mechanism through which SSNRs promote brain development and stress regulation.
- Public health approach needed: integrate primary, secondary, and tertiary prevention vertically AND across public service sectors horizontally.
