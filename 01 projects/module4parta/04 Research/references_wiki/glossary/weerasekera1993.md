## Key Claims
- Presents the multiperspective formulation model (the '4 Ps': Predisposing, Precipitating, Perpetuating, Protective factors) as a comprehensive clinical framework.
- A good formulation must accommodate multiple theoretical perspectives (biological, psychological, social) rather than privileging one orientation.
- The 4 Ps structure provides a comprehensive, memorable, and practically usable framework for case conceptualisation.
- Formulation should suggest treatment direction by identifying which factors are modifiable and which require management.
- Designed for use across professional disciplines and theoretical orientations in psychiatry and mental health.

## Numerical Findings
- Conceptual paper; no primary data
- Framework validated through clinical application rather than empirical testing at time of publication

## Study Design
Theoretical/conceptual paper; reviews existing formulation approaches and proposes an integrative multiperspective grid; aimed at practising clinicians.

## Limitation
Conceptual paper — no empirical validation of the model's reliability or predictive validity; original publication is now over 30 years old; more recent formulation frameworks (e.g. biopsychosocial-ecological) have extended this model.
