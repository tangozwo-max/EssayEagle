## Key Claims
- Parental ACEs negatively affect children's outcomes either directly or indirectly; nearly all 68 studies in the review confirmed this effect — only one study (Beveridge et al., 2022) found no significant direct or indirect association.
- The predominant mediating pathway is parental mental health (depression, anxiety, distress, PTSD): 27 of 35 mediation studies (77.1%) tested this pathway; parenting behaviours (sensitivity, discipline, attachment) are the second most-studied mediator class.
- Six outcome domains identified: infant/toddler development (motor, language, cognitive, social-emotional); physical/physiological problems (obesity, asthma, cortisol, telomere length); mental health; internalising/externalising problems; temperament; academic performance.
- Research has expanded rapidly — 88.2% of the 68 qualifying studies were published in the 5 years 2018–2022; the evidence base is growing but still lacks methodological maturity.
- Paternal ACEs are severely understudied: 80.9% of studies exclusively examined maternal ACEs; only 1 study assessed paternal ACEs alone.
- Moderators found to buffer transmission: secure child attachment, high family social support, emotional availability, and home visiting programme involvement.

## Numerical Findings
- Scoping review of 68 qualifying studies; 10 databases searched (PubMed, APA PsycArticles, CINAHL, Social Work Abstracts, Google Scholar, others)
- 60/68 studies (88.2%) published 2018–2022; 34 (50%) from USA, 17 (25%) from Canada
- 55/68 studies (80.9%) exclusively examined maternal ACEs
- 37/68 (54.4%) focused on children aged 0–5; fewer studies on adolescent and adult offspring
- 35 studies (51.5%) examined mediating mechanisms; 16 (23.5%) tested moderation
- 27/35 mediation studies (77.1%) tested parental mental health as mediator
- ACE measurement: 40/68 (58.8%) used original or adapted ACE checklist; 7 (10.3%) used Childhood Trauma Questionnaire

## Study Design
Scoping review following Arksey and O'Malley (2005) five-stage framework; 10 databases searched (May 2022); inclusion required: cumulative ACE measure (≥4 categories), intergenerational design, quantitative assessment of children's post-birth outcomes; 1,973 articles screened after deduplication → 68 included; narrative synthesis approach.

## Limitation
Scoping (not systematic) review — no formal quality appraisal of included studies; 27/68 studies (39.7%) had n < 200 (underpowered); most evidence retrospective (ACEs recalled by parents); cross-sectional mediation analyses (14/35) cannot establish causal direction; paternal ACEs neglected; outcomes in adolescence and adulthood underrepresented.
