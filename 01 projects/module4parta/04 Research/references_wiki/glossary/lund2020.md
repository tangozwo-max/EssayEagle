## Key Claims
- ACEs are consistently associated with executive function (EF) difficulties in children across multiple EF domains.
- Affected EF domains include working memory, inhibitory control, cognitive flexibility, and planning/problem-solving.
- The association between ACEs and EF is robust across different types of adversity (abuse, neglect, household dysfunction) and different EF assessment methods.
- EF difficulties mediate the pathway from ACEs to academic and behavioural problems in children.
- Interventions targeting EF development may buffer the impact of ACEs on school functioning.

## Numerical Findings
- Systematic review; specific effect sizes and number of included studies not available from metadata alone
- Covers multiple EF domains across varied ACE types

## Study Design
Systematic review; searched multiple databases; included studies examining relationships between ACE exposure and EF outcomes in children.

## Limitation
Heterogeneity in ACE measurement and EF assessment tools; most included studies cross-sectional; causal direction (ACEs → EF or shared underlying vulnerability) not established; publication bias likely.
