## Key Claims
- Human development occurs within nested ecological systems: microsystem (family, school), mesosystem (interactions between microsystems), exosystem (indirect influences), macrosystem (cultural/societal values), and chronosystem (time/change).
- Development is the product of ongoing, bi-directional interactions between the individual and their environments ('proximal processes').
- Ecological validity matters: laboratory studies of child behaviour strip away the contextual factors that shape real development.
- No developmental outcome can be understood without reference to the multiple nested systems in which it is embedded.
- Classic text for the systems-thinking underpinning modern developmental formulation.

## Study Design
Theoretical book; proposes the ecological systems model; critiques laboratory developmental psychology; argues for naturalistic, contextually embedded research designs.
