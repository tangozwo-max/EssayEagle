## Key Claims
- Adequate nutrition in the first 1,000 days (conception to age 2) is essential for brain development; deficiencies in key micronutrients have lasting neurodevelopmental effects.
- Iron, zinc, iodine, and long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acids (LC-PUFAs) are particularly critical for early neural development.
- Malnutrition during sensitive developmental periods can impair myelination, synaptic development, and neurotransmitter systems.
- The effects of early nutritional deficits on cognitive and motor development may persist even after nutritional correction.
- Relevant to Tommy: prenatal and early postnatal nutritional status is part of the biological risk layer in the formulation.

## Numerical Findings
- Review article; synthesises RCT and observational data on nutrition interventions and brain outcomes

## Study Design
Narrative review; covers nutritional influences on brain structure/function from conception through early childhood; addresses intervention evidence.

## Limitation
Narrative (not systematic) review; most intervention evidence from low-income country settings — generalisability to higher-income contexts varies; isolating single nutrient effects is methodologically difficult.
