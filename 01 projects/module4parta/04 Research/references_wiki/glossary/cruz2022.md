## Key Claims
- Developmental Trauma (DT) arises from ACEs and pervasive interpersonal trauma, causing a significant shift in developmental trajectory beyond standard PTSD criteria.
- DT disrupts social cognition, physiological and behavioural regulation, and parent–child attachment — all simultaneously, not as isolated symptoms.
- Critical developmental periods mean that stress-mediated adaptations (e.g. chronic HPA activation, altered attachment) are multi-level and trans-theoretical in nature.
- Diagnostic formulation for traumatised children must integrate psychological, medical, neurological, and attachment perspectives — a single diagnosis is insufficient.
- A comprehensive assessment framework for DT addresses comorbidities (ADHD, mood disorders, dissociation) and informs multi-modal treatment planning.

## Numerical Findings
- Narrative review; no primary data collected
- Reviews contemporary literature on Developmental Trauma Disorder (DTD) conceptualisation and treatment

## Study Design
Narrative conceptual review examining the construct of Developmental Trauma, its associated risks, comorbidities, and multilevel treatment implications in children, adolescents, and adults.

## Limitation
Narrative (not systematic) review; no systematic search protocol reported; Frontiers in Psychiatry is peer-reviewed but has a broad submission model. Paper synthesises existing frameworks without new empirical data. Does not provide outcome data for treatments described.
