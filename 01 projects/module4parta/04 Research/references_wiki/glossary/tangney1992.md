## Key Claims
- Shame-proneness is positively associated with anger and aggression; guilt-proneness is negatively associated with anger.
- The shame → anger pathway operates via externalisaton: shame involves self-condemnation which is deflected outward as anger toward others.
- Guilt, by contrast, focuses on the behaviour rather than the self, and is associated with empathy and constructive response.
- The shame–aggression link provides a mechanism for understanding reactive and externalising behaviour in shame-prone individuals.
- Relevant to formulation: Tommy's shame about his background/deficits may fuel reactive aggression as an anger-deflection mechanism.

## Numerical Findings
- Correlational study; sample of adults
- Shame-proneness × anger/aggression: positive correlation; guilt-proneness × anger: negative correlation
- Specific effect sizes not available from metadata

## Study Design
Correlational study; self-report measures of shame-proneness, guilt-proneness, anger, and aggression; university sample (likely adult, not child — generalisability to children requires caution).

## Limitation
Adult sample — direct applicability to child populations requires validation; self-report aggression; correlational design; 1992 publication — construct of shame has since been refined in developmental research.
