Which Adversities Matter Most?

6+ ACE categories present (Felitti et al., 1998) — what matters is clustering, interaction, and timing in development.
Prenatal adversity
  • DV → maternal cortisol crosses placenta
  • Substance use → FASD risk
  • Fragmented antenatal care
Stress-calibration before birth
Disrupted & frightening caregiving
  • Warmth + fear from the same person
  • NICU → no co-regulation at sensitive period
No coherent relational pattern available
Abuse and neglect
  • Verbal + physical aggression
  • Emotional abandonment · unmet needs
  • During window of self-regulation formation
Home as source of threat
Poverty & community stress
  • 6+ house moves · chronic precarity
  • Social isolation
  • Reduces Mary's buffering capacity
Structural conditions that made consistent caregiving difficult (Bronfenbrenner)
They interact and compound — each adversity further eroded the relational conditions Tommy needed at precisely the moments they mattered most.

How Might These Adversities Affect Development?

Developmental hypothesis, not diagnosis. Key phrase throughout: consistent with — not caused by.
Biological / Neurodevelopmental
Stress-system shaped by threat
  • HPA axis calibrated to hypervigilance
  • Latent vulnerability (Shonkoff & Garner, 2012)
  • FASD may further compound neurodevelopment
Cognitive-Regulatory
Attention, impulse control, learning
  • Executive function difficulties
  • Sustained attention · impulse regulation
  • Same profile as ADHD / FASD → see Slide 4
Social-Emotional
Attachment, trust, reading others
  • Disorganised attachment (Main & Hesse, 1990)
  • IWM: adults = unpredictable; safety = fragile (Bowlby)
  • Neutral interactions → read as hostile
Systemic, not modular — stress biology disrupts cognitive regulation; impaired regulation disrupts social-emotional development; disrupted attachment increases stress reactivity.

What Must Not Be Overlooked?

A formulation based solely on ACEs may be incomplete — and an incomplete formulation shapes the wrong response.

Trauma dysregulation / ADHD / FASD (prenatal alcohol) — clinically overlapping profiles.
Cannot be distinguished without comprehensive assessment.
No such assessment appears to have occurred.

No developmental screening Mary disengaged from healthcare — felt judged and withdrew. Itself a consequence of adversity, not a character failure.
Diagnostic overshadowing M4 curriculum explicit: ACEs can mask ADHD / autism; maltreated children need full neurodevelopmental assessment.
Risk of mislabelling as conduct / ODD Without multidisciplinary assessment, dysregulation may be labelled ODD — M4 curriculum explicit: requires full contextual assessment (W4 P53).
Neuroaffirmative: neurodevelopmental support + trauma-informed care may both be needed — simultaneously, not as alternatives.

Why Is Outcome Not Fixed?

ACE–outcome relationship is dose-response and probabilistic — not deterministic. Tommy's trajectory carries elevated risk. It is not sealed.
Protective factors — already in the vignette
  • Mary's warm moments (Peek-A-Boo, babbling, pride) → serve-and-return → partial relational revision
  • Predictable teachers → felt safety (Siegel, 2012) → window of tolerance
  • LSA emotion-naming → co-regulation → hitting → verbal communication
  • School meals → physiological baseline (precondition for relational repair)
Resilience = environmental, not individual
  • Ordinary magic (Masten, 2001) — from consistent adults, not exceptional traits
  • Not: "Is Tommy resilient enough?"
  • But: "Can these conditions be sustained and extended?"
Cumulative adversity shaped this trajectory — consistent, attuned relationships can still change it.
This formulation points toward understanding, and toward comprehensive assessment and relational support.

Evidence & Sources
Central argument
Formulation-based lens. ACEs plausibly shaped trajectory but are not the complete explanation. Co-occurring neurodevelopmental needs may be overlooked. Elevated risk, not fixed destiny.
Felitti et al. (1998)
ACE study foundation: 10 categories, dose-response relationship with health and mental health outcomes across the lifespan.
Formulation approach
Clinical formulation: hypothesis about how difficulties developed and are maintained. Preferable to premature diagnosis in complex developmental presentations. Provisional and revisable.
Achenbach (1966)
Internalising/externalising taxonomy: externalising (aggression, impulsivity) primary; internalising (shame, withdrawal) emerging. Both carry distinct long-term risk profiles.
Felitti et al. (1998)
Dose-response: 4+ ACEs significantly elevates risk. Tommy's profile: 6+ categories. But the count is not the analysis — clustering and interaction is.
Felitti et al. (1998) — ACE clusters
Original 10 categories grouped analytically: prenatal adversity, disrupted caregiving, abuse/neglect, community/poverty. Each cluster selected for developmental impact at sensitive periods.
Bronfenbrenner (1979)
Ecological systems theory: required by the assignment. Macro-level adversity reduces parental buffering capacity — the mechanism by which community stress becomes relational harm.
Interaction, not addition
Key analytical point: adversities compound. Poverty makes caregiving harder; DV increases maternal cortisol; substance use reduces emotional availability. The clusters are not independent.
Shonkoff & Garner (2012)
Toxic stress: sustained, unbuffered HPA activation. Latent vulnerability as the biological trace. Evidence-based — use "consistent with," not deterministic language.
Main & Hesse (1990)
Disorganised attachment: produced by frightening/frightened caregiving. No coherent strategy available. Most strongly associated with later social-emotional and regulatory difficulties.
Bowlby (1969)
Internal Working Model: cognitive-emotional template from primary caregiver, generalised to all relationships. Tommy's template — adults as unpredictable — is the social-emotional consequence.
Domain interaction
Stress biology → disrupts cognitive regulation → disrupts social-emotional development. Three domains feed into each other — systemic, not parallel.
Diagnostic overshadowing
Prominent presenting explanation draws attention away from co-occurring conditions. In trauma contexts: ADHD and FASD most commonly overlooked.
FASD overlap
SIGN guidance (M4 W2 P29): FASD profile overlaps significantly with ADHD and trauma responses — attention, impulsivity, social difficulties. Prenatal alcohol exposure established in vignette.
Neuroaffirmative framing
Acknowledges neurodevelopmental differences as valid presentations requiring support independent of (or alongside) trauma-informed approaches. Not pathologising.
Felitti et al. — probabilistic
Dose-response is not determinism. The mediating variable between adversity and disorder is the protective environment. Same research that documents risk also documents variability.
Shonkoff — serve-and-return
Mary's warm moments as partial relational repair. Even inconsistent warmth has documented protective value when it produces responsive serve-and-return exchanges.
Siegel (2012) · Masten (2001)
Felt safety: regulated adult keeps child in window of tolerance. Ordinary magic: resilience as environmental output — the adults' consistency is the protective factor, not Tommy's character.
