# Brainstorming — Module 4 Part A Presentation: Tommy & ACEs

## Narrative angle options

| Option | Central thread | Strength | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Cumulative adversity — ACEs compound; dose matters | Directly maps to Felitti ACE score concept | Can feel like a list rather than analysis |
| B | Developmental timing — prenatal/early period most consequential; critical windows disrupted | Strong neuroscience content | Heavy on biology, harder to link protective factors |
| C | Relational disruption → relational repair — all ACEs act through disrupted attachment; protective factors work by restoring relational safety | Elegant arc, maps to taught content, strongest for Analysis/Evaluation criterion | Needs tight discipline to not sprawl |

## Decision: Option C — Relational disruption → relational repair

**Through-line:** Every ACE in Tommy's life disrupted the relational environment he needed for safe development. Every protective factor worked by partially restoring it.

Rationale: Rubric gives 50% to Analysis/Evaluation. This thread lets every ACE link to a mechanism AND an outcome AND a reversal — exactly what A+ demands. Prevents the presentation being a catalogue of facts.

## Slide-by-slide content map

### Slide 1 — Tommy's ACEs: Three Levels
- Frame with Bronfenbrenner's ecological model (individual/family/community)
- Community: deprivation, unemployment, substance use, crime
- Family: domestic violence, Mary's substance misuse, mental health, intergenerational trauma (Mary's 15 foster placements), poverty, housing instability
- Individual/prenatal: prenatal stress, substance exposure, prematurity, low birth weight
- Tommy has 6+ ACE categories from Felitti et al. (1998) + community-level ACEs from extended models
- Anchor stat: ACE score ≥4 → 4–12x increased risk of mental health, substance use, chronic disease (Felitti et al., 1998)

### Slide 2 — Biological and Neurological Impact
- Prenatal: glucocorticoid programming, HPA axis sensitisation; FASD risk spectrum; preterm brain (white matter vulnerability)
- Toxic stress model (Shonkoff/Harvard CoDC): absence of buffering adult is the key variable
- Brain: amygdala hyperactivation (hypervigilance), hippocampal volume reduction (memory/learning), PFC underdevelopment (executive function, impulse control)
- Epigenetics: glucocorticoid receptor methylation (Meaney & Szyf) — trauma alters gene expression
- Tommy's evidence: premature birth + NICU + later attention/focus/impulsivity all consistent with this pathway

### Slide 3 — Cognitive, Social, Emotional & Mental Health
- Attachment: Mary's inconsistency → disorganised attachment (Main & Hesse, 1990) — no coherent strategy; hyperactivated threat response
- Emotional dysregulation: amygdala hijack, shame responses, "over the top" reactions
- Cognitive: impulsivity, focus difficulties = PFC underdevelopment; hostile attribution bias (Crick & Dodge)
- Mental health risk: elevated cortisol → depression/anxiety vulnerability; shame accumulation
- Tommy's neuroplasticity already visible: responds to stable teacher relationships

### Slide 4 — Protective Factors & Resilience
- Mary's attuned moments: Peek-A-Boo = serve-and-return (Harvard CoDC) — builds neural connections even within chaos
- Tight-knit community: social capital as partial buffer
- School: LSA = emotion coaching (Gottman) + safe attachment figure
- Stable predictable teachers = corrective relational experience; felt safety (Siegel)
- Free school meals/vitamins: physical stability
- Masten (2001): resilience = "ordinary magic" — access to ordinary developmental supports, not extraordinary strength
- Key point: protective factors don't erase ACEs; they build capacity to manage effects

### Slide 5 — ACE–Outcome Relationships: Synthesis
- ACE pyramid (Felitti/Anda): adversity → disrupted neurodevelopment → social/emotional/cognitive impairment → risk behaviours → disease
- Tommy sits mid-pyramid: disrupted neurodevelopment evident, but not at the bottom yet
- Dose-response is real but not deterministic: neuroplasticity + sensitive adults + early intervention can alter trajectory
- Individual/family/community all contributed to ACEs AND protective factors — policy needs all three levels
- Close: Tommy's story is not finished. What happens next in the relational environment determines outcome
