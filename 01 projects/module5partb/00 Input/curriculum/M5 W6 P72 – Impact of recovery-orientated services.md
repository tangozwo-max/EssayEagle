## Impact of recovery-orientated services

The impact of recovery-oriented services for addiction and dependency is that they focus on supporting individuals in achieving long-term recovery through a holistic and person-centered approach.

Services should allow people access to the medical model when needed within a broader recovery-oriented service for longer-term support and goals.

### Holistic approach

Holistic care is about taking care of the whole person. This includes thinking about not just the person themselves, but the systems and environments in which they live and work.

Recovery-orientated treatment should address not only the substance use disorder or the behavioral disorder, but also the overall well-being of the individual in their physical, mental, and social health. In a small study, Utomo et al. found that with holistic care, people's overall quality of life significantly improved.

\[Source: Utomo et al.\]

### Personalized care

Treatment plans that are tailored to an individual's unique needs, preferences, and circumstances can have an impact that enhances engagement with the plan and the effectiveness of the plan.

### Empowerment and self-management

This approach encourages individuals to take an active role in their recovery, fostering a sense of empowerment and self-efficacy. The focus should be on individual strengths and abilities, not on deficits.

The challenge is to provide support that empowers people but is also directive enough during challenging times. Finding the right balance is difficult but can have a positive impact through helping people to feel more in control of their lives and their disorder.

### Supportive community

Services that aim to educate family members and communities about addiction and dependence can have a real impact by reducing stigma and discrimination and by increasing the support that is available for service users.

Social support networks comprised of family, friends, and peer support groups can provide ongoing encouragement and accountability to the individual and thus help them sustain long-term recovery.

### Focus on long-term recovery

As you learned earlier, recovery is a long process that can take a lifetime to travel. Services that aim to sustain recovery over the long term can have a greater impact than those that just address immediate addiction issues.

### Resources and training

Implementing a comprehensive, personalized treatment plan can be resource-intensive, requiring significant time, effort, and financial investment. People working for the recovery team will also require training and funding.

Access will therefore depend on location and the availability of these resources. In lower-resourced areas, it may not be possible to gather the resources required.
### Personal commitment

Success in recovery-oriented treatment often requires a high level of commitment from the individual, which can be challenging for those in the early stages of recovery.

The other challenge for people in recovery is that despite the comprehensive approach, relapse is still a possibility, and individuals may need multiple attempts to achieve sustained recovery.

The impact of these challenges is the long-term commitment and energy required of people and their supporters if they are to succeed in their recovery journey.
### Coordination

The provision of holistic services is complex and requires effective coordination across multiple providers and services. This will require people who are able to facilitate and support effective communication and collaboration across all teams. Again, this is resource intensive and may be difficult to provide in areas where communication is difficult and resources are lower.
### Consistency
Recovery is defined and experienced differently by both individuals and cultures. These definitions will change the way services are provided and the end goals for any interventions.

Because recovery programs are not based on one consistent and systematic program, there is a challenge in terms of measuring effectiveness and in building a good evidence base for different interventions.
