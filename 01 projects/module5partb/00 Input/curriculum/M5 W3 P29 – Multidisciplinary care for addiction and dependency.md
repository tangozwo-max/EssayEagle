## Multidisciplinary care for addiction and dependency

People experiencing substance misuse disorder or disordered behavior such as that which occurs in gambling disorder, need holistic, well-coordinated, multidisciplinary care because it is a multifactorial health condition. Person- or patient-centered care (PCC) is widely recognized as an evidence-based practice to improve care. PCC has four key principles:

- a holistic, biopsychosocial approach to care
- an individualized focus to a person’s unique goals, and preferences
- shared power and responsibility between the person and the healthcare provider
- an enhanced therapeutic alliance between disciplines

Although most areas are making efforts to increase the availability of pharmacological, psychosocial, and community-based treatments, substance misuse and behavioral disorders remain a huge problem. Globally, an estimated one in six people with substance misuse disorder are unable to receive treatment.

The ideal approach to care for people experiencing these disorders is through a multidisciplinary team such as you encountered earlier in this course.

### Psychologists
**Assessment and diagnosis:** Psychologists conduct comprehensive assessments to understand the individual’s mental health status, substance use patterns, and any comorbid disorders. This helps in creating a tailored care plan.

**Therapeutic interventions:** They provide evidence-based therapies such as cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), motivational interviewing (MI), and other psychotherapeutic approaches to help individuals understand and change their substance use behaviors.

**Education and training:** Psychologists educate service users and their families about substance use disorders, treatment options, and coping strategies. They also provide training to other healthcare professionals on psychological aspects of substance use and effective intervention techniques.

**Research and evaluation:** They contribute to research on substance use disorders and evaluate the effectiveness of treatment programs. This helps in improving treatment approaches and outcomes.

### Psychiatrists

**Medical assessment and diagnosis:** Psychiatrists conduct thorough medical evaluations to diagnose substance use disorders and any co-occurring mental health conditions. They assess the individual’s overall mental and physical health to inform treatment planning.

**Medication management:** They prescribe and manage medications that can help with withdrawal symptoms, reduce cravings, and treat co-occurring mental health disorders. Medications such as methadone, buprenorphine, and naltrexone are commonly used in treating substance use disorders.

**Therapeutic interventions:** While psychologists often provide psychotherapeutic interventions, psychiatrists may also provide therapy sessions, particularly when integrated with medication management.

**Crisis intervention:** Psychiatrists are trained to handle acute psychiatric crises, which can be common in individuals with substance use disorders. They provide immediate care and stabilization during episodes of severe distress or psychiatric emergencies.

Psychiatrists are also likely to be engaged in research and evaluation, and education and support alongside the psychologists.

### Mental healthcare nurses

**Assessment and monitoring:** Initial and ongoing assessments of physical and mental health. Monitoring of vital signs, withdrawal symptoms, and overall well-being, ensuring any changes are promptly addressed.

**Medication administration and management:** Administration of medications prescribed by psychiatrists or other medical professionals, management of medication schedules, and monitoring for side effects or adverse reactions.

**Patient education and support:** Education of service users and their families about substance use disorders, treatment plans, and healthy lifestyle choices. Provision of emotional support, helping service users develop coping strategies.

**Coordination of care:** Acting as a liaison between members of the multidisciplinary team, ensuring that all aspects of the person's care are coordinated and that information is shared effectively.

**Therapeutic interventions:** Facilitate group therapy sessions, support groups, or individual counseling sessions, helping people work through their challenges and build resilience.

### Counselors and other therapists

Addiction counselors assess how substance use and addiction impact mental health, considering factors such as substance-use patterns, comorbid mental health disorders, cravings, triggers, relapse-prevention strategies, and recovery-support networks. They help individuals to address underlying issues contributing to addiction, and to develop healthy coping skills.

Art and music therapists recognize the therapeutic value of creative expression in promoting emotional expression, stress reduction, relaxation, self-awareness, and interpersonal connection. They use artistic and musical activities to enhance mental health and well-being.

### Peer support workers

**Lived experience and empathy:** Peer support workers bring their personal experiences of recovery from substance use disorders. This lived experience allows them to connect with service users on a deeper level, offering empathy and understanding that can be incredibly motivating and reassuring.

**Building trust and rapport:** They help build trust and rapport with service users, which can be crucial for engagement and retention in treatment. Their shared experiences can make people feel understood and less isolated.

**Support and encouragement:** Peer support workers provide ongoing support and encouragement, helping people navigate the challenges of recovery. They offer hope by demonstrating that recovery is possible.

Along with the rest of the team, peer support workers will help with:

- education and advocacy
- facilitating access to services
- crisis support
- promoting self-determination

> [!-info] -info
> **Further info:** The team should advocate for the service user's needs and preferences, ensuring that the care provided is patient-centered and respects the individual's dignity and autonomy.
> 
> They should also provide holistic care that addresses not only the physical aspects of substance use disorder but also the emotional, social, and spiritual needs of the individual. This holistic approach helps in promoting overall well-being and recovery.
