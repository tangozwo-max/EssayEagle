Addiction and dependency, whether substance use or behavior such as gambling, has a wide-ranging impact on all aspects of society. This can include:

- increasing crime
- impacts of incarceration on families
- economic impacts on an area when people are unable to work
- creating a need for high levels of services
- making people feel unsafe
- breaking down of community and social structures

\[Source: Lievens et al.\]

Certain communities are disproportionately affected by addictions and associated behaviors. This is especially true in areas of high social deprivation, where different social determinants of health come into play.

- A higher prevalence of addictions exists among prisoners, military veterans, and people who are homeless than in the general population.
- The incidence of substance use among migrants is low but there are barriers to them accessing addiction services if they do need it.
- Addiction is often an unseen problem for older people. Their health is placed at greater risk from many substances than the health of younger people. They may need increased access to support.
- Men are typically more likely to have a substance use disorder than women, but women may experience different types of risk that require good access to services.

\[Source: European Drugs Agency; Lievens et al.\]

Indigenous communities experience all the negative impacts of addiction and dependency but also face the risk of losing aspects of their core culture, such as cultural practices.

These communities may experience systemic inequalities that place their members at more risk of developing addiction and dependency.

Examples include gambling addictions among Native Americans and alcohol addictions among the aboriginal communities of Australia.

\[Source: Neurolaunch; Conigrave et al.\]

Saiyed is the Imam for his community—a religious leader. He encourages healthy behavior through religious teachings and he supports his community through advocation. Saiyed sometimes has to counsel members of his community about addiction and dependency.

**What kind of issues do you think Saiyed has to deal with?**

Some of the issues associated with addiction and dependency that Saiyed has to deal with are:

- traffic accidents
- intimidation from drug dealers
- violent crime
- feeling unsafe
- people avoiding mosques as drug use is haram
- families experiencing stigma and shame because drug use is against religion
- community breakdown as a result of the above
