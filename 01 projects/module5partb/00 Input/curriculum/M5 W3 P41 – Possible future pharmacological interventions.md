## Possible future pharmacological interventions

Research is ongoing to find new interventions and new uses for established interventions for substance use and some behavioral disorders.

Select each heading to learn more new research.
### opiod antagonist and atypical antipsychotics for gambling disorder
The evidence for these is limited and of low quality. Dowling et al. suggest that opioid antagonists and atypical antipsychotics (but not antidepressants) might help reduce gambling symptom severity. However, there is not enough information to determine if these medications can improve other gambling-related or psychological symptoms. The effectiveness of mood stabilizers remains uncertain. Additionally, the long-term effectiveness of these medications is unknown. More research is necessary to draw definitive conclusions about the effectiveness of medications for gambling problems.

\[Source: Dowling et al.\]
### pharmacotherapies for alcohol use disorders
Swift and Aston reviewed possible new pharmacological interventions for alcohol use disorder. They investigated the possible use of kudzu, metadoxine, ghrelin antagonists, and pharmacotherapies that could reduce stress response.

\[Source: Swift, Aston\]
### Ncotone cessation
Lindson et al. found that there is strong evidence that bupropion can help people quit smoking long-term. However, it may increase serious adverse events (SAEs) compared to placebo or no treatment. People taking bupropion are more likely to stop treatment than those on placebo or no treatment. Nortriptyline also helps with quitting smoking, but bupropion might be more effective.

Bupropion is about as effective as single-form nicotine replacement therapy (NRT) but less effective than combination NRT and varenicline. Due to limited data, it is hard to draw firm conclusions about the harms and tolerability of these treatments. More research is needed to compare bupropion with placebo, but current evidence suggests no clear advantage of bupropion over other smoking cessation treatments like NRT and varenicline. Future studies should focus on the harms and tolerability of antidepressants for smoking cessation.

\[Source: Lindson et al.\]
### antidepressant for shopping disorder
Vasiliu reviewed evidence for treatment of shopping disorder, which can have a negative impact on general functioning and quality of life. He found that while serotoninergic antidepressants may be used to treat this disorder, it was better to combine these with psychotherapy, particularly cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT).

\[Source: Vasiliu\]
### Psychedelics for substance use disorders
Urban et al. found that the exact contributions of top-down (brain to body) versus bottom-up (body to brain) psychedelic effects are still unclear. More research is needed to understand these mechanisms, especially long-term effects. Current evidence suggests psychedelics significantly change cognition and emotional processing, potentially improving substance use disorders (SUDs) and challenging existing addiction treatments.

\[Source: Urban et al.\]
### pharmacotherapies for stimulant use disorders
Chan et al. studied pharmacotherapies for the treatment of cocaine use disorder. They found that most were not effective, although bupropion, psychostimulants, and topiramate may improve abstinence, and antipsychotics may improve retention. They recommended further research on the topic.

Bentzley et al. also studied treatments for cocaine use disorder. They found that opioid agonist therapies were significantly associated in a reduction of cocaine use.

\[Source: Chan et al.; Bentzley et al.\]
