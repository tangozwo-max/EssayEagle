## M5 W1 P12 – Summary for addiction and dependency - risk factors

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Addiction can affect anyone: some groups of people are more impacted by addiction because of societal marginalization and discrimination.
- People with addiction often experience anxiety, depression, and other mental health conditions.
- Socioeconomic risk factors for the development of a substance use disorder include adverse childhood experiences, community poverty, dysfunctional family relations, and drug experimentation at an early age.
- The researchers involved in the Rat Park experiment concluded that substance misuse could also be significantly influenced by social and environmental factors like poverty, loneliness, and lack of engaging activity.
- Studies of families with addiction suggest that a person’s genetics (with multiple risk genes involved) account for 40% to 60% of their risk.
- Further studies focused on specific genes may provide pathways to the prevention and treatment of psychotic symptoms and build on existing evidence around medications such as naltrexone.
- Psychological risk factors for substance misuse include personality factors, emotion regulation capacity, coping skills, and the potential for developing comorbid mental health conditions like depression and anxiety.
- Interventions that have proved helpful in supporting people with substance use disorders include detoxification services, residential treatment, behavioral therapies, counseling, and harm reduction services.
