## M5 W2 P27 – Summary for substance mechanisms of action

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Alcohol use disorder (AUD) is a brain disorder characterized by an inability to control alcohol use despite negative consequences.
- Misuse of alcohol during adolescence can alter brain development, resulting in long-lasting changes to brain structure and function.
- Heavy use of cannabis alters the brain’s network for self-awareness reducing anxiety, impairing memory, and affecting motivation and personal experience.
- Benzodiazepines are a risk for drug-related death when combined with opioid use, as they act by depressing the central nervous system.
- Abrupt cessation of benzodiazepines can cause potentially fatal, uncontrollable convulsions.
- Cocaine has psychoactive and addictive effects caused by excess dopamine production and dopamine dysregulation.
- Cocaine also causes overproduction of stress hormones such as cortisol.
- Methamphetamines are highly addictive and neurotoxic, causing neuronal inflammation and degeneration.
