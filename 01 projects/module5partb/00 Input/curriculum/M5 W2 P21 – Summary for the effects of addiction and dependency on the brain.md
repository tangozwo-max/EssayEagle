## M5 W2 P21 – Summary for the effects of addiction and dependency on the brain

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Some regions of the brain are affected more by addiction and dependence than others.
- Dopamine helps the brain to remember pleasurable experiences.
- If addictive substances cause pleasure, the dopamine burst will make the brain remember this and want to repeat the experience.
- The addiction cycle has three phases that each feed into the other—the binge/intoxication phase, the withdrawal/negative effect phase, and the preoccupation/anticipation phase.
- Tolerance develops when receptors in the brain diminish, which diminishes the reward for taking the substance.
- Tolerance is an adaptive mechanism attempting to restore normal reward function and involves changes in receptor function.
- Withdrawal occurs when a person stops taking a substance. An imbalance in the brain’s chemistry causes the symptoms of withdrawal.
- Gambling disorder causes similar changes to the brain as substance use disorders.
