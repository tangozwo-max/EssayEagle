## Attachment Theory
**Definition:** Bowlby's foundational theory proposing that the bond between infant and primary caregiver shapes internal working models and all future relationships
**Kontext:** Developmental Psychology, Clinical Mental Health
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[attachment-styles]], [[internal-working-model]], [[john-bowlby]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Brain Architecture
**Definition:** NSPCC metaphor comparing brain development to house construction; emphasises strong foundations in early years
**Kontext:** Neuroscience, Child Development, Parenting
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[serve-and-return]], [[toxic-stress]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Developmental Coordination Disorder
**Definition:** Persistent neurodevelopmental difficulty with sequencing and executing coordinated movements; associated with parietal lobe dysfunction
**Kontext:** Neurodevelopmental Disorders, Clinical Assessment
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[executive-function]], [[developmental-milestones]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Executive Function
**Definition:** Mental skills for planning, prioritisation, impulse control, and sustained focus; develops from infancy through mid-twenties
**Kontext:** Cognitive Development, Neurodevelopmental Disorders
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[working-memory]], [[brain-architecture-nspcc]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Internal Working Model
**Definition:** Mental representations of self, others, and relationships formed through early attachment experiences; templates for future relationships
**Kontext:** Attachment Theory, Cognitive Psychology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[attachment-theory]], [[mentalizing]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Joint Attention
**Definition:** Shared focus between infant and caregiver on an object or event (4-6 months); foundational for language and social cognition
**Kontext:** Social-Emotional Development, Language Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[social-referencing]], [[developmental-milestones]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Mentalizing
**Definition:** Ability to understand mental states (thoughts, feelings, intentions) underlying own and others' behaviour
**Kontext:** Psychotherapy, Attachment Theory, Social Cognition
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[attachment-theory]], [[peter-fonagy]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Object Permanence
**Definition:** Understanding that objects continue to exist when not visible; cognitive milestone emerging around 12 months
**Kontext:** Cognitive Development, Infancy
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[theory-of-mind]], [[developmental-milestones]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Separation Anxiety
**Definition:** Normal infant distress upon separation from primary caregiver; peaks around 12 months as indicator of secure attachment
**Kontext:** Developmental Psychology, Attachment
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[attachment-theory]], [[attachment-styles]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Serve-and-Return
**Definition:** Reciprocal caregiver-child interaction analogous to tennis rally that builds and strengthens neural circuits
**Kontext:** Brain Development, Early Childhood Parenting
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[brain-architecture-nspcc]], [[toxic-stress]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Social Referencing
**Definition:** Infant behaviour (~12 months) wherein child looks to caregiver's emotional expression to determine appropriate emotional response
**Kontext:** Emotional Development, Social Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[attachment-theory]], [[emotional-regulation]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Still Face Experiment
**Definition:** Tronick's 1978 paradigm demonstrating infant distress when caregiver becomes emotionally unresponsive
**Kontext:** Developmental Research, Attachment Theory
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[edward-tronick]], [[attachment-theory]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Storm and Stress
**Definition:** G. Stanley Hall's 1904 term describing adolescence as period of emotional upheaval, identity formation, and risk-taking
**Kontext:** Adolescent Development, Emotional Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[emotional-development]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Theory of Mind
**Definition:** Ability to understand that others have thoughts, beliefs, and perspectives different from one's own; develops ~10-11 years
**Kontext:** Cognitive Development, Social Cognition
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[mentalizing]], [[developmental-milestones]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Toxic Stress
**Definition:** Excessive or prolonged stress activation without protective relationships; disrupts brain development and neural connection formation
**Kontext:** Neuroscience, Child Development, Mental Health
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[brain-architecture-nspcc]], [[executive-function]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

## Working Memory
**Definition:** Foundational executive function; mental workspace for temporarily storing and processing information
**Kontext:** Cognitive Development, Neurodevelopmental Disorders
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[executive-function]], [[developmental-milestones]]
**Quellen:** M4 W1

*Letztes Update: 2026-04-17 | Eintraege gesamt: 16 neue M4W1*

## Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE)
**Definition:** Potentially traumatic or stressful events before age 18, including abuse, neglect, household dysfunction, loss, and violence exposure. High ACE scores associated with poorer physical and mental health outcomes.
**Kontext:** Trauma, Risk Factors, Child Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[trauma]], [[child-maltreatment]], [[toxic-stress]], [[intergenerational-trauma]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
**Definition:** Lifelong neurodevelopmental condition characterized by differences in social communication and presence of restricted, repetitive behavioural patterns. Mental health affected by acceptance, accommodation, and freedom from masking.
**Kontext:** Neurodevelopmental Disorders, Mental Health, Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]], [[masking-autism]], [[stimming]], [[neurodiversity]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Child Maltreatment
**Definition:** Abuse and neglect of children including physical, sexual, emotional abuse and neglect. Major ACE causing trauma, associated with depression, anxiety, behavioral problems, and multiple neurodevelopmental conditions.
**Kontext:** Trauma, Child Welfare, Risk Factors
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[trauma]], [[adverse-childhood-experiences]], [[double-jeopardy]], [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Continuum of Need
**Definition:** Framework that categorises varying levels of support or intervention (universal, selective, indicated, specialist) required by individuals or families based on their needs and circumstances
**Kontext:** Service Delivery, Public Health, Prevention
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[universal-prevention]], [[selective-prevention]], [[indicated-prevention]], [[specialist-services]], [[public-mental-health]]
**Quellen:** M4 W3
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Distal Factors
**Definition:** Non-specific factors increasing likelihood of subsequent risks, operating indirectly. Example: poverty increases exposure to multiple risks. Distinguished from proximal factors which directly affect individuals.
**Kontext:** Epidemiology, Risk Framework
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[proximal-factors]], [[risk-factors]], [[socioeconomic-status]], [[poverty]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Double Jeopardy
**Definition:** Compounded mental health risk when child has both neurodevelopmental condition and adverse childhood experience. Maltreated children are 10x more likely to have multiple neurodevelopmental conditions.
**Kontext:** Risk, Neurodevelopmental, Trauma
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]], [[adverse-childhood-experiences]], [[child-maltreatment]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD)
**Definition:** Range of neurodevelopmental conditions from prenatal alcohol exposure, causing variable cognitive, motor, behavioural, and adaptive difficulties depending on timing and amount of exposure.
**Kontext:** Neurodevelopmental Disorders, Prenatal Risk, Toxicology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[teratogen]], [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]], [[prenatal-risk]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Intergenerational Trauma
**Definition:** Trauma effects passing across generations without direct exposure to original traumatic event, through biological, psychological, and social mechanisms including epigenetics and parenting impacts.
**Kontext:** Trauma, Family Systems, Neurobiology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[trauma]], [[ptsd]], [[epigenetics-stress]], [[hpa-axis]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Intimate Partner Violence (IPV)
**Definition:** Abuse within intimate relationships affecting both survivor and exposed children. Creates toxic stress, disrupts attachment, increases mental health risk across developmental stages.
**Kontext:** Family, Trauma, Risk Factors
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[toxic-stress]], [[attachment]], [[perinatal-depression]], [[child-risk]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Latent Vulnerability
**Definition:** Increased future mental health disorder risk from early trauma. Brain adaptations helping children survive abuse create difficulties in safe settings through altered threat, memory, reward systems.
**Kontext:** Trauma, Neurobiology, Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[trauma]], [[ptsd]], [[hypervigilance]], [[amygdala]], [[hippocampus]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Masking (Autism)
**Definition:** Suppressing autistic traits to appear non-autistic in social interactions. Requires considerable effort and energy; chronic masking associated with anxiety and depression.
**Kontext:** Neurodevelopmental, Mental Health, Wellbeing
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[autism]], [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]], [[anxiety]], [[depression]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Neurodevelopmental Conditions (NDC)
**Definition:** Group of disorders with onset in developmental period impacting functioning, with known or potential causes and developmental onsets. Includes ADHD, autism, language disorders, learning disorders.
**Kontext:** Neurodevelopmental Disorders, Diagnosis, Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[neurodiversity]], [[neurodivergence]], [[autism]], [[adhd]], [[double-jeopardy]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Neurodiversity
**Definition:** Natural variation in human brain functioning and development encompassing all people. Paradigm viewing neurological differences as variations rather than deficits.
**Kontext:** Neurodevelopment, Philosophy, Diversity
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[neurodivergence]], [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]], [[neurotypical]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Neurodivergence
**Definition:** Established differences in cognitive functioning without always-specified cause or onset. Nonmedical term describing people whose brains develop or work differently from neurotypical patterns.
**Kontext:** Neurodevelopment, Diagnosis, Diversity
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[neurodiversity]], [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]], [[autism]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Perinatal Depression
**Definition:** Depression during pregnancy (antenatal) or after birth (postnatal). Common in all income countries, linked to poor infant neurocognitive outcomes, growth, development. Risk factors include IPV, low SES, lack of support.
**Kontext:** Mental Health, Maternal Health, Perinatal Period
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[depression]], [[intimate-partner-violence]], [[socioeconomic-status]], [[infant-development]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Poverty
**Definition:** Economic deprivation with insufficient income and resources for basic needs. Major social determinant affecting child mental health through food insecurity, housing instability, and parental stress.
**Kontext:** Social Determinants, Risk Factors, Inequality
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[socioeconomic-status]], [[food-insecurity]], [[housing-instability]], [[social-determinants]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Protective Factors
**Definition:** Aspects of child's environment and personal qualities that buffer against risk, including secure attachment, social support, resources, and environmental safety and stability.
**Kontext:** Risk & Resilience, Child Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[resilience]], [[risk-factors]], [[secure-attachment]], [[social-support]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Proximal Factors
**Definition:** Factors directly affecting the individual. Examples: abuse, neglect, loss, bullying. Distinguished from distal factors which increase probability of exposure to proximal risks.
**Kontext:** Epidemiology, Risk Framework
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[distal-factors]], [[risk-factors]], [[adverse-childhood-experiences]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Public Mental Health
**Definition:** Organised efforts and informed choices of society, organisations, communities, and individuals to promote mental health and prevent mental health disorders at the population level
**Kontext:** Public Health, Prevention, Population Health
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[continuum-of-need]], [[prevention]], [[evidence-based-practice]], [[protective-factors]], [[risk-factors]]
**Quellen:** M4 W3

## Resilience
**Definition:** Capacity to adapt to and recover from adversity. In children, emerges from interaction of personal qualities, relationship quality, and environmental systems; focus should be on improving environment.
**Kontext:** Risk & Resilience, Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[protective-factors]], [[risk-factors]], [[adverse-childhood-experiences]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Risk Factors
**Definition:** Characteristics, conditions, or experiences increasing likelihood of negative mental health outcomes. Can be individual, family, community, or societal and operate directly or indirectly.
**Kontext:** Risk & Resilience, Child Development
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[protective-factors]], [[resilience]], [[distal-factors]], [[proximal-factors]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Socioeconomic Status (SES)
**Definition:** Social and economic position measured by income, education, and occupation. Major determinant of child mental health; low SES is strongest predictor of childhood mental health disorders.
**Kontext:** Social Determinants, Risk Factors, Inequality
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[poverty]], [[food-insecurity]], [[housing-instability]], [[social-determinants]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Specialist Services
**Definition:** Intensive mental health interventions for individuals with severe, enduring, and complex needs requiring formal diagnosis; includes acute, identification, and recovery phases
**Kontext:** Clinical Care, Service Delivery, Mental Health
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[continuum-of-need]], [[universal-prevention]], [[selective-prevention]], [[indicated-prevention]]
**Quellen:** M4 W3

## Spectrum of Interventions
**Definition:** Full range of mental health intervention options across the continuum of need, from universal services to specialist clinical interventions
**Kontext:** Framework, Prevention, Service Delivery
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[continuum-of-need]], [[universal-prevention]], [[selective-prevention]], [[indicated-prevention]], [[specialist-services]]
**Quellen:** M4 W3

## Targeted / Early Help Services
**Definition:** Services for individuals with additional needs and at risk of developing complex mental health needs; non-diagnostic, focused on timely preventative interventions
**Kontext:** Prevention, Service Delivery, Early Intervention
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[continuum-of-need]], [[selective-prevention]], [[indicated-prevention]], [[public-mental-health]]
**Quellen:** M4 W3

## Teratogen
**Definition:** Substance or agent causing birth defects when exposure occurs during pregnancy. Alcohol (FASD), certain medications, recreational drugs, tobacco are examples with varying evidence strength.
**Kontext:** Prenatal, Toxicology, Risk
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[fetal-alcohol-spectrum-disorder]], [[prenatal-risk]], [[maternal-health]]
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## Toxic Stress
**Definition:** Chronic activation of stress response in adversity without adequate support, particularly damaging in childhood during brain development. Alters threat, reward, and memory systems.
**Kontext:** Neurobiology, Development, Trauma
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[hpa-axis]], [[cortisol]], [[fight-or-flight]], [[intimate-partner-violence]], [[adverse-childhood-experiences]]

## 5Ps Model of Formulation
**Definition:** CBT-based case formulation framework organising factors into five categories: Presenting difficulties, Predisposing factors, Precipitating factors, Perpetuating factors, and Protective/positive factors.
**Kontext:** Therapy / Formulation, CAMH
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[5ps-formulation]], [[clinical-approach-camh]], [[CBT]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## Adultification
**Definition:** Bias where children from Black, Asian, and minoritised ethnic communities are perceived as more mature and less vulnerable, leading to under-protection and overrepresentation in the youth justice system.
**Kontext:** Safeguarding, Anti-racism, Child Protection
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[adultification]], [[safeguarding]], [[implicit-bias]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

## Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)
**Definition:** Psychiatric diagnosis characterised by emotional instability, altered thinking/perception, impulsive behaviour, and unstable relationships; diagnosis in under-18s is ethically contested.
**Kontext:** Disorders / Psychiatry, CAMH Ethics
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[bpd]], [[dbt-adolescents]], [[emotion-dysregulation]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

## CAMH (Child and Adolescent Mental Health)
**Definition:** Specialist mental health services and approaches for children and young people up to age 18, requiring systemic, trauma-informed, and developmentally appropriate care.
**Kontext:** Clinical / Services
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[clinical-approach-camh]], [[multidisciplinary-team]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## DBT-A (Dialectical Behavior Therapy for Adolescents)
**Definition:** Adapted form of DBT for young people experiencing emotion dysregulation, self-harm, suicidality, and impulsive behaviours; four skill modules: mindfulness, emotion regulation, distress tolerance, interpersonal effectiveness.
**Kontext:** Therapy / Treatment, CAMH
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[dbt-adolescents]], [[nssi]], [[emotion-dysregulation]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)
**Definition:** Senior leadership team member in a school responsible for overseeing all safeguarding and child protection policies, training, and inter-agency liaison.
**Kontext:** Education / Safeguarding
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[safeguarding]], [[school-mental-health]]
**Quellen:** M4 W5

## Emotionally Based School Avoidance (EBSA)
**Definition:** Anxiety-driven difficulty attending school; not a psychiatric diagnosis but frequently co-occurs with anxiety/mood disorders; rates doubled post-COVID; autistic young people are disproportionately affected.
**Kontext:** CAMH / Education, Anxiety
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[ebsa]], [[anxiety-in-children]], [[autism]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## Fraser Guidelines
**Definition:** Five criteria determining whether under-16s can receive contraception/sexual health advice without parental consent; sexual activity under 13 always triggers child protection referral.
**Kontext:** Legal / Ethics, CAMH
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[fraser-guidelines]], [[gillick-competence]], [[safeguarding]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

## Gender Dysphoria
**Definition:** Distress from incongruence between expressed and assigned gender; not a mental health condition; transgender youth have disproportionately high rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidality.
**Kontext:** Identity / CAMH Ethics
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[gender-dysphoria]], [[gender-affirming-care]], [[puberty-blockers]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

## Gillick Competence
**Definition:** UK legal framework for determining whether a child under 16 has sufficient maturity and understanding to consent to medical treatment without parental permission.
**Kontext:** Legal / Ethics, CAMH, Consent
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[gillick-competence]], [[fraser-guidelines]], [[informed-consent]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

## MHST (Mental Health Support Team)
**Definition:** UK NHS teams embedded in schools to provide early intervention and support whole school approach implementation; introduced under the 2017 Transforming CAMH Green Paper.
**Kontext:** Services / Education
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[whole-school-approach]], [[school-mental-health]]
**Quellen:** M4 W5

## NSSI (Non-Suicidal Self-Injury)
**Definition:** Deliberate, self-inflicted harm without suicidal intent; affects approximately 17.7% of adolescents; strong predictor of later suicidality (50x higher risk).
**Kontext:** CAMH / Risk, Safeguarding
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[nssi]], [[dbt-adolescents]], [[suicide-risk-factors-youth]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## ODD (Oppositional Defiant Disorder)
**Definition:** Persistent pattern of angry/irritable mood and argumentative/defiant behaviour; typically first appears in preschool; parenting interventions (8–16 sessions) are the evidence-based first-line treatment.
**Kontext:** CAMH / Disorders, Behavioral
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[oppositional-defiant-disorder]], [[conduct-disorder]], [[parenting-interventions]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## PCIT (Parent-Child Interaction Therapy)
**Definition:** Joint parent-child sessions with live coaching to strengthen attachment and manage behaviour; 14–16 sessions; significantly reduces physical abuse and parenting stress.
**Kontext:** Therapy / Treatment, Attachment
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[parenting-interventions]], [[systemic-family-therapy]], [[attachment-theory]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## Safeguarding
**Definition:** Measures protecting children from abuse, harm, and neglect while upholding their rights; a shared responsibility across education, health, and social care; most failures stem from poor information sharing.
**Kontext:** Child Protection, CAMH, Legal
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[safeguarding]], [[adultification]], [[gillick-competence]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

## SENDCO (Special Educational Needs and Disability Coordinator)
**Definition:** School role responsible for coordinating provision for pupils with social, emotional, mental health (SEMH) and SEND needs; often part of the senior leadership team.
**Kontext:** Education
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[school-mental-health]], [[team-in-school]]
**Quellen:** M4 W5

## Social GRACES Model
**Definition:** Burnham's framework for reflecting on dimensions of social identity (Gender, Race, Religion, Age, Culture/Class, Ethnicity, Education, Sexuality, Spirituality) in clinical practice; distinguishes visible/invisible and spoken/unspoken aspects.
**Kontext:** Systemic Therapy, Cultural Competence
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[social-graces-model]], [[systemic-family-therapy]], [[intersectionality]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## STPP (Short-Term Psychodynamic Psychotherapy)
**Definition:** Brief form of psychodynamic therapy; meta-analyses suggest effectiveness for children with a sleeper effect — benefits continue and strengthen after therapy ends.
**Kontext:** Therapy / Treatment, CAMH
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[psychodynamic-therapy-children]], [[therapeutic-alliance]]
**Quellen:** M4 W4

## TAS (Team Around the School)
**Definition:** Multidisciplinary support team including CAMHS, social services, charities, mental health practitioners, and healthcare providers coordinating around a specific school's mental health needs.
**Kontext:** Education / CAMH, Multi-agency
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[school-mental-health]], [[whole-school-approach]]
**Quellen:** M4 W5

## Whole School Approach (WSA)
**Definition:** PHE/DfE eight-principle framework for school-wide mental health promotion covering ethos, curriculum, student voice, staff development, needs identification, parental engagement, targeted support, and leadership.
**Kontext:** Education / Policy, School Mental Health
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[whole-school-approach]], [[mhst]], [[school-mental-health]]
**Quellen:** M4 W5
**Quellen:** M4 W2

## CBPR
**Definition:** Community-Based Participatory Research — ein kollaborativer Forschungsansatz, bei dem Gemeindeangehörige gleichberechtigte Partner im gesamten Forschungsprozess sind.
**Kontext:** Research Methods / Community Psychology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[community-psychology]]
**Quellen:** M4 W5

## CDI
**Definition:** Collaborating Districts Initiative — ein WHO-Programm zur Stärkung gemeindebasierter psychischer Gesundheitsversorgung in Ländern mit niedrigem und mittlerem Einkommen.
**Kontext:** Global Mental Health / Policy
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[community-psychology]]
**Quellen:** M4 W5

## GIDS
**Definition:** Gender Identity Development Service — spezialisierter NHS-Dienst für Kinder und Jugendliche mit Genderdysphorie (in England durch NHSE 2023 geschlossen und durch regionale Hubs ersetzt).
**Kontext:** Child & Adolescent MH / Ethics
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[gender-dysphoria]], [[gillick-competence]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

## GnRH
**Definition:** Gonadotrophin-Releasing Hormone (Analog) — Hormon, das in der Pubertätsblockade-Behandlung bei Genderdysphorie eingesetzt wird; hemmt die Hormonproduktion der Gonaden.
**Kontext:** Neurobiology / Gender-affirming Care
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[gender-dysphoria]], [[gids]]
**Quellen:** M4 W6

*M5 W1 Einträge — 2026-06-15*

## Addiction
**Definition:** Compulsive engagement with a substance or behavior that persists despite harmful consequences; characterized by loss of control and cravings. DSM-5 uses "Substance Use Disorder" instead.
**Kontext:** Substance Use, Clinical Diagnosis
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[dependency]], [[substance-use-disorder]], [[behavioral-addiction]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD)
**Definition:** Most harmful substance to individual and society (Nutt et al. harm score 72/100). WHO 2024: 400 million adults affected globally. Linked to socioeconomic deprivation, especially low education. Naltrexone effective when OPRM1 gene variant present.
**Kontext:** Substance Use Disorder, Epidemiology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[substance-use-disorder]], [[naltrexone]], [[benzodiazepines]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Behavioral Addiction
**Definition:** Addiction to a behavior (rather than a substance) that becomes compulsive and persists despite negative consequences. DSM-5 recognizes only gambling disorder; ICD-11 also includes gaming disorder and compulsive sexual behavior.
**Kontext:** DSM-5, ICD-11, Behavioral Psychology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[gambling-disorder]], [[griffiths-brown-criteria]], [[compulsive-behavior]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Benzodiazepines
**Definition:** Class of sedatives/anxiolytics (e.g., Valium, Xanax, Klonopin). Effective short-term; 35% of users develop physical dependence after 4+ weeks. Frequently overprescribed to older adults; only 1.5% develop SUD.
**Kontext:** Pharmacology, Dependency
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[dependency]], [[tolerance-withdrawal]], [[substance-use-disorder]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Compulsive Behavior
**Definition:** Behavior performed to reduce anxiety or distress rather than for pleasure; neutralizes difficult thoughts or feelings. Distinguished from addiction by absence of reward-seeking as primary driver.
**Kontext:** Psychology, OCD, Behavioral Addiction
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[behavioral-addiction]], [[gambling-disorder]], [[internet-addiction]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Dependence Syndrome
**Definition:** WHO/ICD-11 diagnostic concept: cluster of behavioral, cognitive, and physiological phenomena after repeated substance use. Binary diagnosis (present/absent); requires at least 3 of 6 criteria.
**Kontext:** ICD-11, WHO, Substance Use
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[dependency]], [[substance-use-disorder]], [[icd]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Dependency (Substance)
**Definition:** Physiological adaptation to prolonged substance use resulting in tolerance and withdrawal symptoms when use stops. More common than addiction; does not require compulsive behavior. Normal with long-term medication use.
**Kontext:** Pharmacology, Substance Use
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[addiction]], [[tolerance-withdrawal]], [[substance-use-disorder]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Emotion Regulation
**Definition:** Processes by which a person influences their emotions or expression. Adults with SUD show significantly greater difficulties in emotion regulation and impulse control. Deficits linked to internet addiction and compulsive behaviors.
**Kontext:** Psychology, SUD Risk Factor
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[impulse-control]], [[addiction]], [[internet-addiction]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Gambling Disorder
**Definition:** The only behavioral addiction recognized in DSM-5. Reclassified from impulse-control disorder in 2013 based on neuroimaging evidence showing dopamine system changes identical to substance addictions.
**Kontext:** Behavioral Addiction, DSM-5
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[behavioral-addiction]], [[griffiths-brown-criteria]], [[dsm]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Genetic Risk for Addiction
**Definition:** Genetics account for 40–60% of addiction risk. 19 gene variants commonly inherited across addiction disorders, all linked to dopamine signaling. Key genes: AKT1 (cannabis-psychosis), CHRNA5 (nicotine dependence), OPRM1 (naltrexone response).
**Kontext:** Genetics, Biological Risk
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[addiction]], [[naltrexone]], [[dopamine-signaling]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Griffiths/Brown Criteria
**Definition:** Six-criterion framework for diagnosing behavioral addiction (salience, euphoria, tolerance, withdrawal, conflict, relapse/reinstatement). Developed by Mark Griffiths, building on R.I. Brown's framework. Functional impairment required.
**Kontext:** Behavioral Addiction, Diagnostic Framework
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[behavioral-addiction]], [[gambling-disorder]], [[mark-griffiths]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Harm Reduction
**Definition:** Public health approach reducing negative consequences of drug use without requiring abstinence. Examples: needle exchange programs, naloxone distribution, safe consumption sites.
**Kontext:** Public Health, Treatment, Substance Use
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[substance-misuse]], [[substance-use-disorder]], [[rehabilitation]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Internet Addiction
**Definition:** Compulsive internet use producing addiction-like consequences. Not in DSM-5 but emerging concern. European prevalence 4–14% pathological, 14–55% problematic. Risk factors: poor emotion regulation, introversion, loneliness, depression.
**Kontext:** Behavioral Addiction, Technology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[behavioral-addiction]], [[emotion-regulation]], [[compulsive-behavior]]
**Quellen:** M5 W1

## Naltrexone
**Definition:** FDA-approved medication for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Blocks opioid receptors to prevent euphoric effects; no misuse potential. OPRM1 gene variant predicts treatment response, enabling personalized prescribing.

## Psychoactive Substance
**Definition:** Any substance that when consumed alters consciousness, mood, or thinking (WHO). Includes alcohol, cannabis, opioids, stimulants, sedatives, hallucinogens, caffeine, and nicotine. Legal status varies greatly by culture.

## Rat Park Experiment
**Definition:** Late-1970s study by Bruce K. Alexander. Rats in enriched social environments consumed far fewer drugs than isolated rats, demonstrating that social and environmental factors are central to addiction risk — not just drug pharmacology.

## Substance Misuse
**Definition:** Use of a substance in a way that causes harm to the user or others, deviating from medical or social norms. Can potentially lead to SUD but does not indicate one is present. "Abuse" is considered stigmatizing.

## Substance Use Disorder (SUD)
**Definition:** DSM-5 unified diagnostic term replacing "substance abuse" and "substance dependence." Severity continuum: mild (2–3 criteria), moderate (4–5), severe (6+). Severe SUD = colloquial "addiction."

## Tolerance and Withdrawal

## ACE–Addiction Pathway
**Definition:** The integrated neurobiological pathway linking adverse childhood experiences to addiction vulnerability via three signalling systems: oxytocin (social bonding), dopamine (reward/stress-sensitisation), and glucocorticoids/cortisol (HPA axis dysregulation). Structural brain changes (hippocampal/amygdala shrinkage, locus coeruleus hyperactivity) persist into adulthood.
**Kontext:** Addiction Neuroscience, ACE, Developmental Trauma
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[adverse-childhood-experiences]], [[oxytocin]], [[cortisol]], [[hpa-axis]], [[dopamine]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Alcohol Blackout
**Definition:** Alcohol-induced amnesia in which the person remains conscious but does not form long-term memories of events. Mechanism: alcohol impairs NMDA glutamate receptors, disrupting hippocampal memory consolidation. Types: fragmentary (partial amnesia) and en bloc (complete amnesia for an episode).
**Kontext:** Alcohol Use Disorder, Neuropharmacology, Memory
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[alcohol-use-disorder]], [[glutamate]], [[nmda-receptor]], [[wernicke-korsakoff]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Allostasis
**Definition:** A regulatory strategy in which the brain maintains stability through continuous change in set-points (feed-forward mechanism), as opposed to homeostasis (reactive error-correction). In addiction, allostasis shifts the reward baseline downward — the person uses substances just to feel normal. Drives tolerance, withdrawal severity, and cycle escalation.
**Kontext:** Neuroscience, Addiction, Pharmacology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[homeostasis]], [[tolerance-withdrawal]], [[addiction-cycle]], [[camp-pathway]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Anti-Reward System
**Definition:** Neurochemical systems that counteract the positive reward pathway: CRF (corticotropin-releasing factor), norepinephrine, and dynorphin. Develop during chronic addiction; activated during withdrawal, causing aversive states that drive negative reinforcement-based drug use.
**Kontext:** Addiction Neuroscience, Withdrawal
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[extended-amygdala]], [[corticotropin-releasing-factor]], [[dynorphin]], [[addiction-cycle]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Basal Ganglia
**Definition:** Subcortical nuclei (caudate, putamen, nucleus accumbens) that process reward, form habits, and constitute the core of the brain's reward circuit. Over-activated by addictive substances in the binge/intoxication stage; reduced activation causes dysphoria in withdrawal.
**Kontext:** Neuroanatomy, Addiction Neuroscience
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[nucleus-accumbens]], [[dopamine]], [[reward-system]], [[addiction-cycle]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## BrainAGE (Brain Age Gap Estimation)
**Definition:** A machine learning measure comparing predicted brain age (from sMRI cortical thickness analysis) to chronological age. A positive BrainAGE gap indicates premature brain aging. Found in cocaine use disorder, suggesting neurotoxic acceleration of cortical aging.
**Kontext:** Neuroimaging, Addiction, Neurotoxicity
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[neuroimaging-addiction]], [[smri]], [[cocaine]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## cAMP Pathway (Cyclic AMP Signalling)
**Definition:** Intracellular second messenger system in neurons. cAMP is synthesised from ATP by adenylate cyclase. D1 receptors activate it (excitatory); D2 receptors inhibit it. Opioids suppress cAMP; chronic use triggers allostatic upregulation; abrupt cessation causes cAMP overproduction and neuronal hyperactivity (withdrawal symptoms).
**Kontext:** Pharmacology, Neuroscience, Tolerance
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[d1-d2-receptors]], [[allostasis]], [[tolerance-withdrawal]], [[locus-coeruleus]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Cannabis Use Disorder (CUD)
**Definition:** A substance use disorder arising from compulsive cannabis use. Approximately 30% of cannabis users develop CUD; risk is significantly higher with onset before age 18. Characterised by tolerance, withdrawal (slow-onset due to lipid binding), cravings, and impaired executive function.
**Kontext:** Substance Use Disorder, Public Health
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[cannabis]], [[thc-cbd]], [[cannabinoid-receptors]], [[substance-use-disorder]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Cannabinoid Receptors (CB1/CB2)
**Definition:** G-protein-coupled receptors for endocannabinoids and THC. CB1 receptors are found throughout the CNS and mediate psychoactive and addictive effects of cannabis; CB2 receptors are primarily peripheral/immune. THC binds CB1, inhibiting cAMP via Gi coupling.
**Kontext:** Pharmacology, Neuroscience, Cannabis
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[cannabis]], [[thc-cbd]], [[camp-pathway]], [[gaba]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Cocaine
**Definition:** A stimulant drug derived from the coca plant that blocks the dopamine transporter (DAT), preventing reuptake and causing massive dopamine accumulation in the nucleus accumbens, producing intense euphoria. Also stimulates cortisol/HPA axis. Causes structural brain changes (dendrite growth, gene expression) accumulating with use.
**Kontext:** Substance Neuroscience, Addiction
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[dopamine-transporter]], [[nucleus-accumbens]], [[cortisol]], [[neuroimaging-addiction]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Corticotropin-Releasing Factor (CRF)
**Definition:** A neuropeptide (also known as CRH) produced in the hypothalamus and extended amygdala. Initiates the HPA stress response. In addiction, elevated CRF in the extended amygdala drives anxiety and compulsive drug-taking during withdrawal. CRF antagonists can block excessive drug-taking — a therapeutic target.
**Kontext:** Stress Neuroscience, Addiction, Anti-Reward System
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[anti-reward-system]], [[extended-amygdala]], [[hpa-axis]], [[dynorphin]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## D1 and D2 Dopamine Receptors
**Definition:** Two major dopamine receptor families with opposing intracellular effects. D1 (Gs-coupled): activates adenylate cyclase → cAMP rises → excitatory. D2 (Gi-coupled): inhibits adenylate cyclase → cAMP falls → inhibitory; also auto-receptor. D2 density is reduced in chronic cocaine use (detectable on PET).
**Kontext:** Pharmacology, Neuroreceptors, Addiction
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[camp-pathway]], [[mesolimbic-pathway]], [[dopamine]], [[raclopride]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Dopamine Transporter (DAT)
**Definition:** A membrane protein that reabsorbs dopamine from the synaptic cleft back into presynaptic neurons, terminating the dopamine signal. Cocaine blocks DAT (reuptake inhibition); methamphetamine reverses it (active pumping out). Downregulated in chronic stimulant use, detectable by PET.
**Kontext:** Pharmacology, Stimulant Addiction
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[cocaine]], [[methamphetamine]], [[dopamine]], [[d1-d2-receptors]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Dynorphin
**Definition:** An endogenous opioid peptide acting on kappa-opioid receptors (KOR), producing dysphoria. Part of the anti-reward system: released in response to dopamine surges to counteract reward, contributing to tolerance and anhedonia. KOR antagonists are being researched as addiction and depression treatments.
**Kontext:** Neuropeptides, Anti-Reward System, Addiction
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[anti-reward-system]], [[corticotropin-releasing-factor]], [[opioids]], [[addiction-cycle]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Extended Amygdala
**Definition:** A group of interconnected brain structures (central amygdala nucleus, bed nucleus of stria terminalis, part of nucleus accumbens) that processes stress and negative emotions. In addiction, drives the withdrawal/negative affect stage. Sensitised by chronic substance use and ACE; site of anti-reward system plasticity.
**Kontext:** Neuroanatomy, Addiction, Stress Neuroscience
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[addiction-cycle]], [[anti-reward-system]], [[basal-ganglia]], [[locus-coeruleus]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Gaming Disorder
**Definition:** A behavioural addiction to digital gaming recognised by the ICD-11 (but not DSM-5 standalone). Shares neurobiological features with substance use disorders: dopamine reward circuit changes, tolerance, withdrawal-like states, impulsivity. Causes sleep deprivation, stress hormone elevation, and neurocognitive impairment.
**Kontext:** Behavioural Addiction, ICD-11
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[behavioral-addiction]], [[gambling-disorder]], [[dopamine]], [[impulse-control]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Locus Coeruleus (LC)
**Definition:** A brainstem nucleus and the brain's primary source of norepinephrine. Normally involved in arousal, attention, and stress. In opioid withdrawal, hyperactive LC neurons (due to cAMP upregulation) produce jitteriness, insomnia, panic, sweating, and tachycardia — exaggerated versions of its normal functions.
**Kontext:** Neuroanatomy, Withdrawal, Opioid Neuroscience
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[camp-pathway]], [[tolerance-withdrawal]], [[anti-reward-system]], [[noradrenaline]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Mesolimbic Pathway
**Definition:** The primary dopamine reward pathway running from the ventral tegmental area (VTA) to the nucleus accumbens. Central to reward processing, motivation, and addiction. All addictive substances increase dopamine activity in this pathway; drugs produce surges far exceeding natural rewards.
**Kontext:** Neuroanatomy, Addiction Neuroscience
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[dopamine]], [[nucleus-accumbens]], [[basal-ganglia]], [[reward-system]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Methamphetamine
**Definition:** A highly addictive psychostimulant that causes active release of dopamine/serotonin/norepinephrine from neurons AND blocks the DAT. Glutamate overstimulation causes neuronal inflammation and necrosis (primary neurotoxicity mechanism). Produces permanent white matter damage (temporal, frontal/parietal, occipital lobes) and Parkinson's-like symptoms.
**Kontext:** Substance Neuroscience, Stimulant Addiction
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[dopamine-transporter]], [[glutamate]], [[neurodegeneration]], [[cocaine]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Neuroimaging in Addiction
**Definition:** Use of PET, fMRI, and sMRI to demonstrate addiction-related brain changes. Key findings: PET with [C11]Raclopride shows cue-induced dopamine release correlating with craving; fMRI shows persistent D2 receptor loss and frontal hypometabolism after cocaine cessation; sMRI shows accelerated cortical thinning (BrainAGE) in cocaine use disorder.
**Kontext:** Neuroimaging, Addiction Research
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[pet-scan]], [[fmri]], [[smri]], [[raclopride]], [[cocaine]], [[nora-volkow]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)
**Definition:** The most anterior frontal lobe region responsible for executive functions: planning, decision-making, self-control, and impulse inhibition. Last brain region to fully mature (mid-20s). In addiction, the preoccupation/anticipation stage hijacks PFC, driving compulsive drug-seeking over daily tasks.
**Kontext:** Neuroanatomy, Executive Function, Addiction
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[basal-ganglia]], [[extended-amygdala]], [[addiction-cycle]], [[executive-function]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Raclopride ([C11]Raclopride)
**Definition:** A competitive D2 dopamine receptor antagonist used as a PET radioligand. Measures dopamine release indirectly: low raclopride binding = high dopamine; high binding = low dopamine. Used by Volkow et al. to show cue-induced dorsal striatum dopamine release correlating with cocaine craving severity.
**Kontext:** Neuroimaging, PET, Pharmacology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[pet-scan]], [[d1-d2-receptors]], [[neuroimaging-addiction]], [[cocaine]], [[nora-volkow]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Reward System (Brain)
**Definition:** Interconnected circuits (VTA → nucleus accumbens → basal ganglia + prefrontal cortex + hippocampus) that identify and reinforce behaviours promoting survival. Generates dopamine-mediated pleasure signals encoding "this was beneficial — repeat this." Hijacked by all addictive substances through disproportionate dopamine surges.
**Kontext:** Neuroscience, Addiction, Behaviour
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[mesolimbic-pathway]], [[dopamine]], [[basal-ganglia]], [[nucleus-accumbens]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## THC and CBD
**Definition:** The two primary pharmacologically active compounds in cannabis. THC (delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol): psychoactive; binds CB1 receptors; drives cannabis's reinforcing/addictive properties. CBD (cannabidiol): non-psychoactive; anti-inflammatory and anxiolytic; may moderate adverse THC effects.
**Kontext:** Pharmacology, Cannabis, Substance Neuroscience
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[cannabis]], [[cannabinoid-receptors]], [[camp-pathway]]
**Quellen:** M5 W2

## Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome
**Definition:** A neurological disorder caused by thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency, most commonly from chronic heavy alcohol use. Wernicke encephalopathy (acute): ophthalmoplegia, ataxia, confusion. Korsakoff syndrome (chronic): severe anterograde amnesia, confabulation. Part of Alcohol-Related Brain Damage (ARBD).
**Kontext:** Alcohol Neuroscience, Neurology
**Verwandte Begriffe:** [[alcohol-use-disorder]], [[alcohol-blackout]], [[fetal-alcohol-spectrum-disorder]]
