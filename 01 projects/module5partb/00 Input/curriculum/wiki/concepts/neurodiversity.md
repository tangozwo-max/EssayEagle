# Neurodiversity

The natural variation in how human brains work and develop. Neurodiversity encompasses all people, including neurotypical (typically developing) individuals as well as those with neurodivergence or neurodevelopmental conditions.

## Key concept

Neurodiversity is a paradigm that views differences in brain functioning as natural variations rather than disorders or deficits. This perspective values neurological diversity alongside other forms of human diversity (cultural, ethnic, etc.).

## Related terms

- **Neurotypical** — individuals whose brains develop and function in typical/expected ways
- **Neurodivergence** — established differences in cognitive functioning without always-specified cause
- **Neurodevelopmental conditions** — diagnosed medical/psychiatric disorders with known/potential causes
