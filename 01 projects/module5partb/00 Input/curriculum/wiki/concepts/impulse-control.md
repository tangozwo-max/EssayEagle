# Impulse Control (as SUD Risk Factor)

Impulsivity — the tendency to act on urges without adequate consideration of consequences — is a longstanding and cross-cutting psychological vulnerability for substance use disorders. Research suggests that impulsivity predates substance use in many cases, making it both a risk marker and a potential intervention target.

## Link to SUD
- Adults with SUDs display greater difficulty controlling impulsive behaviors compared to those without SUDs (Stellern et al.)
- Impulsivity is associated with problem gambling as well as multiple substance use disorders
- Genetic studies suggest impulsivity may itself have heritable components that overlap with addiction genetics

## Developmental perspective
Adolescents are particularly vulnerable to impulsivity-driven substance use, as prefrontal cortex development (which governs impulse regulation) is not complete until the mid-20s.

## Protective factors (counterbalancing impulsivity)
- Positive self-esteem
- Optimistic outlook
- Effective coping and resilience skills
- Stable family environment
