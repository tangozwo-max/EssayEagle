# Bronfenbrenner's Ecological Systems Theory

Urie Bronfenbrenner's developmental framework. Places the **individual at the centre**, surrounded by concentric layers of influence that shape development and behaviour.

```
[ Macrosystem — societal/cultural context (norms, values, ideology) ]
[ Exosystem   — indirect environments (gov policy, community resources) ]
[ Mesosystem  — interactions between microsystems (family↔school) ]
[ Microsystem — immediate contacts (family, peers, teachers) ]
              [ Individual (age, sex, health) ]
[ Chronosystem — passage of time and life events across ]
```

- **Microsystem** — most direct impact; relationship is bidirectional — children influence AND are influenced by their close contacts.
- **Mesosystem** — interactions among microsystem elements (e.g., family↔school).
- **Exosystem** — environments the child doesn't directly interact with but that affect them (government policies, community resources).
- **Macrosystem** — broader societal and cultural context.
- **Chronosystem** — life events, transitions, historical period.

## Influence on mental health
Used widely in community psychology, public health, and Sippel et al.'s mapping of Gabon clinical-ethics issues onto micro/meso/macro levels.
