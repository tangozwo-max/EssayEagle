# Fight-or-Flight Response

Physiological reaction to acute threat. Originally described by **Walter Bradford Cannon (1915)** — animals under threat release adrenaline → rapid increases in HR, respiration, oxygen/energy flow to muscles.

## Modern-day mismatch
Prehistoric survival mechanism now misapplied to daily stressors (traffic, email, deadlines). Misinterpretation may present as panic attack — person may feel like they are "having a heart attack" or "going mad."

## Psychological component
Quickening thoughts, focused attention on threat, potential escape routes.

## Support for misinterpretation
- Education about physiology
- Relaxation and grounding strategies
