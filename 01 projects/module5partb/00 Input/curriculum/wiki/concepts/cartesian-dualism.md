# Cartesian Dualism (Mind-Body Dualism)

**René Descartes** (17th century): the mind and body are **distinct substances** that exist independently.

- **Body**: extended (has measurable dimensions), divisible
- **Mind**: unextended, immaterial, indivisible — where thoughts, consciousness, and mental experience occur
- Descartes believed they interacted, but acknowledged the mechanism remained mysterious

## Impact
- Enabled scientific inquiry into the body (anatomy, physiology) — previously restricted by religious concerns about the soul
- Established **positivism** and the **biomedical model** of medicine
- Isolated mental health from medical inquiry for 200+ years

## Critique
- The concept of the **lived body** (phenomenology) challenges this by asserting mind and body are inseparable
- [[biopsychosocial-model]] developed as a response to the limitations of dualism
