# ACE–Addiction Neurobiological Pathway

The integrated neurobiological model describing how adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) increase vulnerability to addiction and substance use disorder. Three key signalling systems mediate this link: oxytocin, dopamine, and glucocorticoids.

## Three signalling pathways (Strathearn et al.; Kim et al.)

### 1. Oxytocin system
- Oxytocin: hypothalamic hormone, neuroregulator of social bonding, attachment, and stress regulation
- Early adverse experiences dysregulate long-term oxytocin system functioning
- Dysregulated oxytocin → social impairment, aggressive behaviour, novelty/reward-seeking
- These behaviours increase susceptibility to substance use

### 2. Dopamine system
- Early adverse experiences shape dopamine-related systems:
  - Reduced dopamine transporter binding
  - Increased baseline dopamine levels
  - Exaggerated dopamine release in response to stress
- These changes produce susceptibility to sensation-seeking, impulsive risk-taking, and active pursuit of rewards
- People may seek stimulant drugs that activate dopamine pathways

### 3. Glucocorticoid / cortisol system
- Deficits in early caregiving → prolonged/exaggerated cortisol response to stress
- Chronic cortisol elevation contributes to anxiety and trauma-related disorders
- HPA axis dysfunction links stress exposure to:
  - Onset of substance misuse
  - Diminished motivation to abstain
  - Heightened relapse risk

## Structural brain changes from ACE

ACEs cause structural alterations persisting into adulthood:
- Hippocampal/amygdala volume reduction → memory deficits
- Locus coeruleus hyperactivity + increased norepinephrine → unpleasant arousal states (mitigated by alcohol/opioids)
- Amygdala defects → disrupted oxytocin systems → impaired pair bonding
- Glucocorticoid pathway disruption → increased abdominal fat deposits

## Intergenerational cycle

Parental addiction impairs caregiving capacity, creating conditions that may constitute ACEs for children — perpetuating the ACE-addiction pattern across generations.

## Approaches

Two complementary lenses:
1. **Neurobiological** — receptor, pathway, and structural changes as described above
2. **Psychodynamic/attachment** — insecure attachment from ACEs → destructive behaviour and substance misuse
