# UK Mental Health Act (1983, amended 2007)

Sets out rights of people experiencing mental health problems in England and Wales.

## Coverage
- Assessment and treatment in hospital
- Treatment in the community
- Pathways into hospital (civil or criminal)
- Rights: information, appeal, advocacy, meetings, complaints, communication, after-care

## Two forms of inpatient treatment
- **Informal patients** — voluntary admissions
- **Formal patients** — involuntary admissions ("sectioned"), comprise >50% of UK inpatients

## Criteria for sectioning
- Urgent assessment/treatment needed
- Health at risk of worsening without treatment
- Person's safety (or others') at risk
- Doctor believes hospital assessment/treatment needed
- Three professionals must agree (UK)

## Section 2
Detention for up to **28 days** for assessment.
