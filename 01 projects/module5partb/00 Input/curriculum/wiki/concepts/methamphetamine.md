# Methamphetamine

A highly addictive psychostimulant drug from the amphetamine family. Also known as crystal meth (in its pure smoked form). One of the world's most abused substances, and uniquely neurotoxic.

## Mechanism of action

Methamphetamine differs from cocaine in its mechanism:
- **Active release:** meth causes neurons to actively **release** dopamine, serotonin, and norepinephrine (rather than just blocking reuptake)
- **Reuptake inhibition:** meth also blocks the **dopamine transporter (DAT)**, preventing dopamine reabsorption
- The combined effect produces a much larger and longer-lasting dopamine surge than cocaine

**Neurotoxicity pathway:**
- Meth causes elevated **glutamate** levels
- Excess glutamate **overstimulates neurons** → excitotoxicity → **neuronal inflammation and necrosis** in the mesolimbic region
- This is the primary mechanism of methamphetamine's neurotoxicity

## Effects and duration

- High lasts 6–12 hours (much longer than cocaine)
- Can cause extended wakefulness (up to 10 days reported)
- Psychological effects: exhilaration, arousal, alertness; also paranoia, confusion, aggression, disinhibition
- Long-term: hallucinations, paranoia, skin-picking behaviour (due to hyperthermia + dehydration)

## Neurodegeneration

Long-term meth use causes:
- **Degeneration of midbrain dopamine neurons** → Parkinson's-like symptoms
- Decreased dopamine and dopamine transporter levels
- **Neuronal inflammation**
- **White matter damage** in temporal, frontal/parietal, and occipital lobes (cortex, basal ganglia, brainstem, and cerebellum are spared per Moeller et al.)

## Addiction cycle

| Stage | Characteristic |
|---|---|
| Binge/intoxication | Intense, long-lasting euphoria; racing thoughts; hypersexuality; insomnia |
| Withdrawal/negative affect | Hallucinations, near-psychotic state; then crash (sleep for days), depression, anxiety |
| Preoccupation/anticipation | Negative physical/emotional sensations drive compulsive drug-seeking |
