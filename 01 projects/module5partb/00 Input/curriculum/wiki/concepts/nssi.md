# Non-Suicidal Self-Injury (NSSI)

NSSI is defined as deliberate, self-inflicted harm without suicidal intent. Pooled prevalence among adolescents: 17.7% (21.4% female, 13.7% male). NSSI is a strong predictor of later suicidality — having self-harmed (suicidal or not) confers ~50x higher risk of future suicide. Prior suicide attempt is the most significant single risk factor. Key risk factor categories per McEvoy et al. umbrella review: psychiatric/psychological (conduct disorder, personality disorders, depression/anxiety — the top three), adverse life events (childhood abuse/neglect #1), behavioural (substance use), social (poor family relationships; being in foster care pOR=3.89). DBT-A shows promise as treatment.
