# Perinatal Depression

Depression occurring during pregnancy (antenatal) or after childbirth (postnatal/postpartum). Both are common in high-income and middle/low-income countries and represent significant risk factors for infant and child development.

## Risk factors for perinatal depression

- Somatic symptoms and physical health complications
- Intimate partner violence
- Unintended pregnancy
- Low maternal educational attainment
- Low socioeconomic status
- Lack of social support
- History of mental health disorders

## Effects on infant and child outcomes

### During pregnancy (antenatal depression)
- Increased risk of medical complications (preterm delivery, low birth weight)
- Untreated depression linked to poor neonatal outcomes

### After birth (postnatal depression)
- Impaired infant physical growth
- Early cessation of breastfeeding
- Delayed language development
- Sleep disturbances
- Developmental behaviours not expected for age/stage
- Lower infant social and motor development scores

## Clinical significance

Untreated perinatal depression is associated with ongoing postnatal depression and poor neurocognitive developmental outcomes in the child. Early identification and treatment are critical protective interventions.
