# Neuroaffirmative Approach

A neuroaffirmative approach is a framework for supporting neurodivergent children and young people that affirms neurodiversity as natural variation rather than pathology. Rather than aiming to reduce or eliminate neurodivergent traits, neuroaffirmative interventions focus on improving quality of life, supporting relationships, and modifying environments.

## Key principles

- **Affirms neurodiversity**: recognises neurodivergence (autism, ADHD, dyslexia, etc.) as natural variation
- **Quality of life focus**: centres on what matters most for the child's wellbeing and functioning
- **Relationship-based**: supports key relationships (parents, teachers, peers)
- **Environmental modification**: changes environments and systems to support the child
- **Avoids pathologisation**: does not treat non-harmful neurodivergent traits as symptoms to eliminate

## Ethical concerns with traditional approaches

Traditional early intervention approaches (e.g., applied behaviour analysis) have been criticised for:
- Aiming to extinguish autistic traits
- Pathologising non-harmful behaviours
- Teaching children to "mask" or hide neurodivergent characteristics
- Operating against the wishes and values of the neurodivergent community

## Neuroaffirmative alternatives

Rather than symptom reduction, neuroaffirmative approaches include:
- Supporting self-understanding and self-acceptance
- Building on strengths and interests
- Improving communication and relationships
- Modifying environments to reduce sensory or cognitive demands
- Addressing genuine support needs rather than "normalisation"
