# Four Principles of Bioethics (Beauchamp & Childress)

Foundational framework of Western bioethics, formalised in the **Belmont Report (1978)** and elaborated by Beauchamp & Childress.

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Respect for Persons / Autonomy** | Uphold people's capacity to make informed, independent decisions about their care (central to [[informed-consent]]). |
| **Beneficence** | Act from compassion and empathy — maximise anticipated benefits, minimise harm. |
| **Nonmaleficence** | *Primum non nocere* — "first do no harm." Avoid causing harm; minimise risk. |
| **Justice** | Be fair, equitable, and compassionate. Forbids exploitation of vulnerable people. Includes [[distributive-justice]]. |

## Notes from the literature
- A 2012 study (Page) with 94 first-year psych students found participants ranked **nonmaleficence** highest — weight twice as large as any other — but in practice did not explicitly use them during decisions.
- Principles "provide a start but not a conclusion" (Silva et al.). Disagreements often arise in interpretation, shaped by culture, class, gender.
