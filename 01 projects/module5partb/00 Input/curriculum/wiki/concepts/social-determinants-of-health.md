# Social Determinants of Health (SDH)

Defined by the **WHO** as *"the non-medical factors that influence health outcomes."* The conditions in which people are born, grow, work, live, and age — plus the wider set of forces and systems shaping daily life.

## Core domains
Interplay between:
- education
- employment
- income / wealth
- working conditions
- job security
- housing
- poverty
- adverse childhood experiences ([[adverse-childhood-experiences]])

## Key models and frameworks
- [[dahlgren-whitehead-model]] (1991) — concentric circles of influence
- [[csdh-framework]] (WHO, 2008) — structural vs intermediary determinants
- [[lund-five-domains]] (2018) — demographic, economic, neighbourhood, environmental, social

## Why it matters
- Mental-disorder burden is unlikely to be relieved by improved treatment access alone.
- Drives argument that *"many of the things we can do for mental health are not mental health interventions"* (Richard Bentall).
- Basis for WHO CSDH action principles and the [[sustainable-development-goals]].
