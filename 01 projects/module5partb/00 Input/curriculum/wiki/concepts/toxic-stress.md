# Toxic Stress

Chronic, prolonged activation of the stress response system in the context of adversity without adequate support. In children, toxic stress is particularly damaging because it occurs during critical periods of brain development, altering how the stress response system develops.

## Effects on children

### Immediate impact
- Chronic activation of fight-or-flight response
- Impairs time available for learning and development
- Disrupts attachment relationships with caregivers
- Affects emotional regulation capabilities

### Neurobiological consequences
- Altered development of threat detection systems (amygdala hypervigilance)
- Changes to stress hormone production (cortisol dysregulation)
- Impacts on brain regions involved in learning, memory, and emotional regulation

## Sources of toxic stress in childhood

- Domestic abuse exposure
- Child abuse or neglect
- Severe poverty and instability
- Violence exposure
- Loss and grief
- Parental mental illness or substance use

## Contrast with manageable stress

Manageable stress (challenge with support) supports development. Toxic stress without adequate buffering or support causes harm.
