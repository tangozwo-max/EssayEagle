# Patient-Centred Care (PCC)

An evidence-based framework for delivering health care that is respectful of and responsive to individual preferences, needs, and values. In addiction and dependency care, PCC is widely recognised as the foundation for effective [[multidisciplinary-team]] delivery.

## Four core principles (Marchand et al.)

1. **Holistic, biopsychosocial approach** — addressing physical, psychological, and social dimensions together
2. **Individualized focus** — care plans built around the person's unique goals, preferences, and life circumstances
3. **Shared power and responsibility** — active partnership between the service user and the care team; the person is not a passive recipient
4. **Enhanced therapeutic alliance** — collaborative, trusting relationships across all disciplines

## Key features in practice

- **Respect for individuals** — dignity, involvement in decision-making
- **Coordination and integration** — avoids duplication and gaps across services
- **Information and education** — person has access to information about their condition and options
- **Emotional and social support** — family and community included as appropriate
- **Continuity and transition** — smooth handoffs between care settings
- **Physical comfort** — pain management, environmental wellbeing

## In addiction care
PCC is the recommended framework for structuring multidisciplinary addiction services. It moves away from a "one size fits all" model and toward care plans co-created with the person, responsive to trauma history, cultural background, and personal goals.
