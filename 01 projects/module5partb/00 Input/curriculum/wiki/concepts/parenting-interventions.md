# Parenting Interventions

Parenting interventions are skills-based programs, typically 8–16 sessions, endorsed by NICE and WHO as the primary intervention for ODD and the strongest evidence base for disruptive behaviour disorders in children. They are effective in both group and individual formats. Key phases: (1) enhancing parent-child relationship through warmth, child-centred play, and positive reinforcement; (2) positive discipline: clear rules, effective instructions, consistent nonviolent consequences. Parent-child interaction therapy (PCIT) specifically involves joint parent-child sessions with coaching to strengthen attachment and reduce physical abuse. Oregon Parent Management Training is among the most evidence-supported models.
