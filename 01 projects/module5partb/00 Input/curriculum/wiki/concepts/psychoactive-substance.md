# Psychoactive Substance

Any substance that, when consumed or administered, alters consciousness, mood, or thinking processes (WHO definition). Psychoactive substances can produce toxic effects on human organs, interfere with regulatory functions, and increase risk of physical and mental health conditions.

## WHO sociolegal categories of use
1. **Medical use** — prescribed psychoactive substances used as medications (e.g., sedatives, anxiolytics)
2. **Illicit/illegal use** — prohibited under international drug conventions or national legislation
3. **Legal non-medical use** — using a psychoactive substance outside its intended medical purpose (e.g., using sedatives for euphoria)

## Common substance categories (NIDA)
- Alcohol, caffeine, cannabis, synthetic cannabinoids
- Hallucinogens (psilocybin, LSD), inhalants, khat, mescaline
- Opioids (heroin, morphine, prescription painkillers), synthetic opioids
- Sedatives, hypnotics, and anxiolytics (e.g., [[benzodiazepines]])
- Stimulants (cocaine, methamphetamine)
- Tobacco and nicotine (including vaping)
- Other/unknown (anabolic steroids, etc.)

## Sociolegal complexity
The legal status of psychoactive substances varies greatly by culture and context:
- Mescaline (peyote) is legally protected for specific Native American religious ceremonies in the US
- Khat is widely used in East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula but illegal in most Western countries
- Nitrous oxide is common medically and in aerosols but increasingly regulated recreationally
