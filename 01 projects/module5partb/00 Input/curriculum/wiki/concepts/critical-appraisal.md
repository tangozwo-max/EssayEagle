# Critical Appraisal

Systematic process of evaluating a research paper for **trustworthiness, results, and relevance**. Uses checklist-based tools.

## Key tool providers
- **CASP** — Critical Appraisal Skills Programme; checklists for RCTs, cohort, case-control, qualitative studies. Three core questions: (1) results relevant? (2) results valid? (3) will results help locally?
- **CEBM** — Centre for Evidence-Based Medicine (Oxford). Multi-language worksheets for diverse study types.

## Core appraisal dimensions
Evaluate a study's [[reliability-validity-relevance-applicability]].

## Typical checklist format
Yes / No / Can't tell — with hints per question. Covers research-question clarity, method appropriateness, sampling, variables, ethics, bias mitigation.
