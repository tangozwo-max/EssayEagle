# HIC / LMIC — Country Income Classifications

World Bank / WHO classifications used throughout global mental health.

- **HIC** — High-Income Country
- **MIC** — Middle-Income Country
- **LIC** — Low-Income Country
- **LMIC** — Low- and Middle-Income Countries (often grouped)

## Key mental-health statistics
- **76–85 %** of people with severe mental disorders in LMICs receive **no treatment**.
- **35–50 %** of people with severe mental disorders in HICs receive no treatment.
- Less than **$2/person/year** spent on mental health globally.
- **< $0.25/person/year** spent in low-income countries.
- In low-income countries, **~67 %** of mental-health spending goes to stand-alone mental hospitals — despite evidence this is associated with poor outcomes and, in some places, human-rights violations.

## Barriers faced in LMICs
- Gaps in treatment
- Variability in quality of care
- Absent or unenforced ethics frameworks
- Poorly administered policies and plans
- Weak civil-society and peer-support movements
- Medication and laboratory-test unavailability
- Undertrained personnel
- Lack of multisectoral integration

## Scaling strategies
- Non-specialist, lay workers (e.g., StrongMinds in Uganda & Zambia — interpersonal therapy for depression).
- Task-shifting to community workers.
- Transdiagnostic interventions ([[pm-plus]], [[sh-plus]]).
