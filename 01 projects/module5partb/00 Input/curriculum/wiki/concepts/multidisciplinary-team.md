# Multidisciplinary Mental Health Team (MDT)

A team of diverse mental-health professionals who **collaborate to deliver holistic care**, each approaching determinants from a different disciplinary lens.

## Typical roles

| Role | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Psychiatrist** | Medical assessment, diagnosis, pharmacotherapy |
| **Psychologist** | Cognitive patterns, emotional regulation, coping, trauma history, therapy |
| **Mental health nurse** | Day-to-day clinical care, medication management, care coordination |
| **Social worker** | Social determinants, housing, benefits, family, community linkage |
| **Therapist / counsellor** | Psychosocial interventions, CBT, MI, group work |
| **Occupational therapist** | Functional living, return-to-work, activity scheduling |
| **Peer-support worker** | Lived-experience guidance, destigmatisation, engagement |
| **MDT lead** | Coordinates communication, treatment planning, supervision, external liaison, training, quality monitoring |

## Role of MDT lead
- Facilitate communication across professions.
- Oversee assessment → planning → implementation → evaluation of treatment plans.
- Supervise and mentor staff; run team meetings.
- Monitor quality, identify improvement opportunities.
- Manage resources (staffing, budget, equipment).
- Liaise externally (primary care, community services, local authorities).
- Coordinate CPD / training.

Roles and titles vary across countries, cultures, and settings.

## MDT in addiction and dependency (M5 W3)
In addiction services, the MDT typically includes:
- **Psychologists** — CBT, MI, assessment, research
- **Psychiatrists** — medications (methadone, buprenorphine, naltrexone), crisis management, medically assisted withdrawal supervision
- **Mental healthcare nurses** — monitoring withdrawal/vital signs, medication administration, patient education, care coordination
- **Counselors and therapists** — relapse prevention, coping skills, art/music therapy
- **Peer support workers** — lived experience, trust-building, role modeling, community reintegration

Cases are discussed as a whole team even when service users are seen individually, ensuring holistic care coordination. The team advocates for person-centred care and service user autonomy.
