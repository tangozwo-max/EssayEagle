# Comorbidity

The **simultaneous presence of two or more diseases or conditions** in the same person. Also called: multimorbidity, multiple chronic conditions, co-existing conditions.

## Key statistics
- Depression is **2-3 times more common** in people with a chronic physical health problem
- Occurs in ~20% of people with a chronic health condition
- People with severe mental disorders face a **10-20 year reduced lifespan** from preventable physical health conditions

## Common co-existing conditions
- Depression + ischaemic heart disease (20% of cases)
- Depression + diabetes, COPD, obesity, cancer

## Barriers to recognition
- Overlapping symptoms (e.g., fatigue in both depression and COPD)
- People may not connect physical symptoms to psychological causes
- Concurrent conditions like anxiety or substance use

## Management
- [[person-centered-care]]
- [[collaborative-care]]
- Address iatrogenic risks from [[iatrogenic-effects|polypharmacy]]

## In M5 context — SUD and mental health comorbidity
M5 W1 focuses specifically on comorbidity between substance use disorders (SUDs) and mental health conditions:
- ~50% of people with a mental health condition will also develop a SUD, and vice versa
- High rates of comorbidity with: anxiety disorders, depression, bipolar disorder, psychosis, BPD, ASPD
- Comorbidity between schizophrenia and tobacco use particularly well-documented (70-80% of people with schizophrenia smoke)
- EMCDDA guidelines for treating specific comorbidities: CBT and integrated care are key approaches; SSRIs not efficacious when comorbid SUD is present; [[dialectical-behavioral-therapy]] effective for eating disorders + SUD
