# Systemic Family Therapy

Systemic (family) therapy views individuals as embedded in interconnected systems, focusing on improving family dynamics rather than treating individuals in isolation. Evidence shows medium-to-large effect sizes (two-thirds of cases improve), comparable to individual therapy. Key modalities include: child-parent psychotherapy (intensive; resolves ambivalent attachment), parent-child interaction therapy (PCIT; strengthens attachment + manages behaviour), multisystemic therapy (MST; community-based for conduct disorder), and trauma-focused CBT (TF-CBT; best-supported for child maltreatment). For adolescent anorexia nervosa, 50–90% achieve full weight recovery; relapse rates much lower than after inpatient treatment. Therapy for disruptive behaviour disorders shows small but significant effect sizes (0.26–0.36).
