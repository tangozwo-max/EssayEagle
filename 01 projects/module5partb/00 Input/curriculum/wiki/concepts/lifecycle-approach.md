# Lifecycle Approach to Risk

A framework for understanding risk factors chronologically, mapping relevant risks from conception through development and across generations. This approach recognizes that risk factors change across developmental stages and that experiences at one stage affect vulnerability in subsequent stages.

## Key developmental periods

### Preconception
- Genetic risk factors (inherited conditions like Huntington's disease)
- Circumstances around conception
- Short interpregnancy intervals
- Consanguinity (reproduction between relatives)

### Prenatal and perinatal
- Maternal physical and mental health
- Adequate prenatal care access
- Maternal adaptation to pregnancy
- Birth complications and early health concerns

### Infancy and early childhood
- Secure attachment relationships
- Nurturing, stimulating caregiving
- Access to early intervention services for developmental delays or neurodevelopmental conditions

### School age
- Peer relationships and social acceptance
- Academic experiences and learning support
- Family stability and support
- Exposure to trauma or adversity

### Adolescence
- Peer relationships and social belonging
- Identity development
- Risk-taking behaviour, substance use
- Ongoing family and educational support

### Lifelong risks
- Genetic predisposition to mental health conditions
- Physical and mental health comorbidities
- Environmental hazards and toxin exposure
- Violence, abuse, or neglect exposure
