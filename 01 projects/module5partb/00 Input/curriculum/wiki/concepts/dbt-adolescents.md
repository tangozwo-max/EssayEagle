# Dialectical Behavior Therapy for Adolescents (DBT-A)

DBT-A is an adaptation of adult DBT (originally for BPD) for use with adolescents experiencing emotion dysregulation, self-harm, suicidality, and impulsive behaviours. Four core skill modules: mindfulness (present-moment awareness), interpersonal effectiveness (improving relationships), emotion regulation (understanding and managing emotions), and distress tolerance (managing crises without harmful behaviour). Typically includes weekly individual therapy, family sessions, and telephone support. Treatment is more intensive and longer than most other youth psychological therapies. Evidence is promising for self-harming adolescents but studies involved resource-intensive interventions — simpler DBT skills programs are not equivalent. Debate remains about applying a BPD-derived model to adolescents whose personalities are still developing.
