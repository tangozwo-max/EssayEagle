# Monoamine Deficiency Hypothesis

Historical theory: depression caused by deficiency in monoamine neurotransmitters (**serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine**). Basis for SSRI/SNRI antidepressant development.

## Current status
- ~90 % of public still believes it, but increasingly challenged.
- Joanna Moncrieff (UCL): "not grounded in science."
- New directions: dysfunction in **glutamate** and **GABA** as potential causes.
- Depression likely reflects **loss of connections between nerve cells** under chronic stress.
- Neuroimaging: depressed and treated brains differ; effective treatment returns brain to "healthy" patterns.

> "We are in a new era of psychiatry. This is a paradigm shift away from monoaminergic deficiency..." — Rachel Katz, Yale
