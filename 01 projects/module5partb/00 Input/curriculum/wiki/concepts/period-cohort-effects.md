# Period and Cohort Effects

Two epidemiological concepts that explain how **time-bound influences** shape mental health at population level.

## Period effect
Influence of **events, trends, or conditions common to a particular time period** — affects people *regardless of age or generation*.

Examples:
- COVID-19 pandemic
- Economic recessions
- Cultural shifts
- Armed conflicts

## Cohort effect
Influence of **factors specific to the generation or birth cohort** to which individuals belong.

Examples:
- Millennials / Gen Z exposure to cyberbullying and social-media comparison
- Post-war baby boomers' economic trajectory
- Children of the opioid crisis

## COVID-19 as a case study (period effect)
Typical psychological impacts during the pandemic:
- Loneliness, isolation (lockdowns, distancing)
- Fear of contracting virus / health of loved ones
- Depression due to job loss
- Grief exacerbated by inability to gather
- Stress of home-work + home-schooling
- Substance use
- ~20 % increase in under-18 access to UK public mental health services (March 2020 – July 2022)
- ~10 % of population reported *improved* mental health (reduced commute, quality family time, high resilience)
