# Mental Health Literacy

Mental health literacy refers to the knowledge, understanding, and awareness of mental health conditions, mental health promotion, and mental health services. It is a key component of mental health promotion and prevention efforts at the population level.

## Components

Mental health literacy includes:
- **Knowledge of mental health conditions**: understanding common mental health disorders, their symptoms, and course
- **Awareness of risk and protective factors**: recognising factors that influence mental health
- **Understanding help-seeking**: knowledge of available services and how to access them
- **Mental health skills**: ability to promote own mental health and support others
- **Understanding of mental health rights**: knowledge of rights and responsibilities in mental health contexts

## Promotion methods

Mental health literacy can be promoted through:
- School-based curricula addressing mental health awareness
- Media campaigns increasing mental health awareness
- Community education programs
- Professional training for healthcare and education workers
- Public health communication

## Impact

Increased mental health literacy is associated with:
- Earlier recognition of mental health difficulties
- Improved help-seeking behaviour
- Reduced stigma and discrimination
- Better mental health outcomes
