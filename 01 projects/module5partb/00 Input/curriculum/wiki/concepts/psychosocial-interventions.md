# Psychosocial Interventions

Psychosocial interventions are approaches combining psychological, social, and community-based strategies to address mental health needs and promote well-being. They are particularly effective in humanitarian emergencies, trauma recovery, and prevention contexts.

## Key characteristics

- **Multi-component**: address psychological, social, and environmental factors
- **Evidence-based**: interventions selected based on research evidence
- **Context-responsive**: adapted to specific populations and emergencies
- **Community-focused**: include family and community involvement

## Common psychosocial interventions

- Stress management techniques
- Relaxation strategies
- Care for well-being activities
- Trauma-focused cognitive-behavioural therapy
- Group-based interventions
- Community support programs

## Application contexts

Psychosocial interventions are used for:
- Adolescents who have experienced humanitarian emergencies
- Trauma survivors
- Adolescents experiencing pregnancy
- Adolescents with emotional difficulties
- Communities affected by disasters
