# Specialist Services

Specialist mental health services provide intensive interventions for individuals with severe, enduring, and complex mental health needs. These services require formal diagnosis for access and often involve multi-agency involvement and statutory interventions.

## Three phases of specialist intervention

### Acute Phase
Providing specialist interventions to alleviate symptoms before or alongside formal diagnosis. Focus is on crisis management and symptom reduction.

### Identification Phase
Identifying and assessing those in need of more specialised mental health services and formalising diagnosis where necessary.

### Recovery Phase
Supporting people through recovery over the long term, including rehabilitation, relapse prevention, and social reintegration.

## Key characteristics

- **Diagnostic requirement**: individuals typically require a formal diagnosis to access services
- **Intensive**: services are more resource-intensive than universal or targeted services
- **Multi-disciplinary**: often involve collaboration between different healthcare professions and agencies
- **Statutory involvement**: may include legal or statutory interventions where necessary

## Examples

Specialist services might include intensive outpatient treatment, inpatient psychiatric care, specialist community mental health teams, and crisis intervention services for young people with severe conditions such as early-onset psychosis, severe eating disorders, or complex trauma.
