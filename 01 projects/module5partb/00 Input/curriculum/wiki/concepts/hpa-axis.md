# HPA Axis — Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis

The core **neuroendocrine stress-response system**. Dysregulation is linked to depression, anxiety, PTSD, and long-term physical illness.

## Cascade (acute stress response)

```
Stressor
   │
   ▼
Hypothalamus ── releases ──▶ CRH (corticotropin-releasing hormone)
                                   │
                                   ▼
                          Pituitary gland ── releases ──▶ ACTH (adrenocorticotropic hormone)
                                                               │
                                                               ▼ (via bloodstream)
                                                       Adrenal glands ── release ──▶ Cortisol
```

## Chronic dysregulation
When activation is **prolonged and repeated** (e.g. ACEs, chronic stress), three brain systems are affected:
- **Reward** — blunted responsiveness
- **Memory** — hippocampal changes (see [[hippocampus]])
- **Threat** — amygdala sensitisation (see [[amygdala]])

Developing brains are especially vulnerable. Children with chronic HPA activation may:
- Misread social cues
- Struggle to trust or bond
- Develop mental-health conditions in later life
