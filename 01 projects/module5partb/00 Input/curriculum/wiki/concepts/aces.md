# Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)

Potentially traumatic events experienced in childhood (before age 18). Originally identified in the landmark CDC-Kaiser ACE Study.

## Types
- Physical, emotional, or sexual abuse
- Physical or emotional neglect
- Household dysfunction: domestic violence, parental substance use, parental MH conditions, parental incarceration, divorce

## Impact
High ACE scores predict significantly elevated adult risk of:
- Depression, anxiety, PTSD
- Obesity, type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cancer
- Substance use disorders
- Reduced life expectancy

## Clinical case (M2 W4)
Sally's ACEs (childhood sexual abuse) are understood as **common antecedents** for her weight gain, diabetes, hypertension, and CPTSD — illustrating how ACEs shape lifelong health.
