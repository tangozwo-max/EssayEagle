# Neurodevelopmental Periods

| Period | Key processes |
|---|---|
| **Prenatal** | Neural tube forms (1st trimester); neurogenesis begins; rapid brain growth (2nd-3rd trimester); neurons differentiate; synapses form |
| **Postnatal / infancy** | Rapid increase in synaptic connections (peak ~age 2); synaptic pruning begins; associative learning |
| **Childhood (2–12)** | High plasticity; continued pruning; Hebbian strengthening |
| **Adolescence** | Pruning focuses on prefrontal cortex; peak Hebbian learning; myelination extends into late 20s (humans) |
| **Early adulthood (18–25)** | PFC fully matures → emotional regulation, impulse control |

Disruption → neurodevelopmental disorders or later mental-health conditions. Risks include ACEs, drug exposure, environmental toxins, disrupted social relationships.
