# Biopsychosocial Model

A holistic framework proposed by **George Engel (1977)** that understands health as the interplay of **biological, psychological, and social** factors. It rejects mind-body dualism and reductive biomedical models.

## Core claim
To understand a person's mental health condition, clinicians must examine **all three domains**, not only biology:
- **Biological** — genetics, neurobiology, hormones, nutrition, brain structure
- **Psychological** — cognitions, emotions, coping, personality, trauma history
- **Social** — environment, socioeconomic status, relationships, culture, discrimination

## Why it matters
- Frames mental health professionals to see the **whole person**, not just presenting symptoms.
- Provides a shared language for [[multidisciplinary-team]] work.
- Underpins most modern assessment and treatment approaches.

## Related / extends
- Extends into [[social-determinants-of-health]] frameworks ([[dahlgren-whitehead-model]], [[csdh-framework]]).
- Companion to the phenomenological concept of the [[lived-body]].
