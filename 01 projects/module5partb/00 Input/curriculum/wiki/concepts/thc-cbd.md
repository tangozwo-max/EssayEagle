# THC and CBD (Cannabis Compounds)

The two most pharmacologically significant active compounds in the cannabis plant (*Cannabaceae* family). Cannabis contains hundreds of biologically active compounds, but THC and CBD are the most studied.

## THC (Delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol)

- **Primary psychoactive compound** in cannabis
- Responsible for the "high" — euphoria, altered perception, relaxation
- Also responsible for the **reinforcing properties** of cannabis (drives addiction)
- Mechanism: binds to **CB1 receptors** throughout the CNS; modulates cAMP via G-protein inhibition
- Influences pain, mood, appetite, nausea, and sexual activity
- Remains detectable in the body long after use due to lipid binding
- Directly affects memory, learning, attention, decision-making, coordination, emotion, and reaction time

## CBD (Cannabidiol)

- **Non-psychoactive compound** — does not produce a "high"
- Has anti-anxiety, anti-inflammatory, and possibly antipsychotic properties
- Primary receptor: CB2 (peripheral/immune); complex and indirect modulation of other receptors
- Being investigated as a treatment for epilepsy, anxiety, and pain
- May moderate some of THC's adverse effects

## Cannabis forms and detection

- Cannabis binds to lipids → remains detectable for weeks (longer in people with higher body fat)
- Slow lipid binding explains why cannabis withdrawal has a slow onset compared to opioids or alcohol
