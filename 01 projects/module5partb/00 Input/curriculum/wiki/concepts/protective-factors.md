# Protective Factors

Aspects of a child's internal qualities, relationships, and environment that buffer against risk and support healthy development. Protective factors reduce the impact of adversity and promote positive outcomes even in the presence of stress.

## Key protective factors in child development

### Individual factors
- Secure attachment relationships with caregivers
- Effective emotional regulation and coping skills
- Positive self-esteem and sense of agency
- Cognitive abilities and learning capacity
- Good physical health and development

### Family factors
- Warm, nurturing parenting
- Consistent, responsive caregiving
- Family stability and cohesion
- Parental mental health and wellbeing
- Access to family support networks

### Community and social factors
- Positive peer relationships and social belonging
- Supportive school environment
- Access to quality education
- Community networks and social capital
- Safe, stable housing and adequate resources

### Systemic factors
- Access to healthcare and social services
- Economic stability and reduced poverty
- Supportive policies and institutions
- Cultural acceptance and inclusion

## Philosophy

Rather than focusing on helping children "build resilience" to cope with adversity, systems should aim to strengthen the environment and reduce the need for resilience.
