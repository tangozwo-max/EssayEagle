# Phenomenology and the Lived Body

**Phenomenology** (20th century philosophical movement): focuses on human consciousness and subjective experience. Challenges [[cartesian-dualism]].

**The Lived Body**: the body is not an instrument transmitting signals to the mind — it *is* the center of consciousness and intentionality. The way humans experience the world **through senses, movements, and interactions**.

> The mind and body comprise a **living system**, part of larger systems (communities, cultures) that permanently interact with the environment.

## Clinical implications
- Healthcare professionals must consider not only physical symptoms but psychological, social, and cultural aspects
- Closely aligned with the [[biopsychosocial-model]]
- Enables **person-centered care**
- Holistic treatment of depression beyond "mind-body" dichotomy
