# Gaming Disorder

A behavioural addiction characterised by persistent or recurrent gaming behaviour (digital gaming or video gaming) that leads to significant impairment in personal, family, social, educational, or occupational functioning. Recognised as a diagnosis by the **ICD-11** (2019); not yet a standalone DSM-5 diagnosis (listed under "conditions for further study" as internet gaming disorder).

## ICD-11 recognition

The ICD-11 recognises gaming disorder alongside gambling disorder as a behavioural addiction, in contrast to DSM-5, which only gives gambling disorder standalone diagnostic criteria.

## Neuroscience parallels with SUD

Gaming disorder shares neurobiological overlap with substance use disorders:
- Activates the dopamine reward system (basal ganglia circuits)
- Affects multiple brain systems: memory/learning, motivation, inhibitory control, reward/salience
- Can produce tolerance (needing more gaming time) and withdrawal-like states (irritability, preoccupation when unable to game)
- Risk traits include impulsivity (also a risk factor for gambling disorder and SUD)

## Neurological and psychological effects

Chronic gaming can lead to:
- Neurocognitive impairment
- Sleep deprivation
- Increased stress hormones
- Reduced emotional flexibility and increased rigidity
- Increased emotional pain/distress
