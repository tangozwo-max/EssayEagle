# Mental Health Prevention

Mental health prevention aims to minimise mental health conditions by addressing determinants of those conditions before a specific issue has been identified in individuals, groups, or populations. The ultimate objective is to reduce the occurrence of future mental health conditions.

## Key approach

- **Risk and protective factor focus**: targets risk factors while promoting protective factors
- **Early identification**: addresses early signs of mental health conditions to prevent escalation
- **Multiple levels**: operates across universal, selective, indicated, and treatment levels

## Prevention levels

### Universal prevention
Accessible to all without assessment of risk. Examples: substance use prevention curriculum in schools, mental health literacy programs.

### Selective prevention
Targeted at individuals or groups exhibiting risk factors for developing poor mental health. Examples: support groups for young carers, resilience programs for children in challenging circumstances.

### Indicated prevention
For high-risk individuals already showing signs of emotional or behavioural difficulties but without formal diagnosis. Examples: school attendance support programs for students with unauthorised absences.

### Treatment
When preventative efforts have not succeeded and formal diagnosis has been made. Examples: psychotherapy for post-traumatic stress disorder following abuse.

## Intensity variation

Prevention efforts vary in intensity and approach depending on their position on the continuum of need and the developmental stage of the child or young person.
