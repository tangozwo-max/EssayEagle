# Dopamine (DA)

A monoamine neurotransmitter with both excitatory and inhibitory effects depending on receptor type. Involved in **reward, motivation, pleasure**, and motor control. Dysregulation is implicated in Parkinson's disease (deficit), schizophrenia (excess), and — centrally — addiction.

## The four dopamine pathways

| Pathway | Route | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Mesolimbic | VTA → Nucleus accumbens | **Reward, addiction** (most relevant to addiction) |
| Mesocortical | VTA → Prefrontal cortex | Cognition, emotional regulation |
| Nigrostriatal | Substantia nigra → Striatum | Motor control; lost in Parkinson's |
| Tuberoinfundibular | Hypothalamus → Pituitary | Prolactin regulation; blocked by antipsychotics |

## How dopamine works at the receptor level

Dopamine neurons from the VTA terminate at GABAergic neurons in the nucleus accumbens, activating **D1 or D2 receptors**:
- **D1 (Gs-coupled):** Activates adenylate cyclase → **cAMP rises** → protein kinase A activation → excitatory effect
- **D2 (Gi-coupled):** Inhibits adenylate cyclase → **cAMP falls** → inhibitory effect; presynaptically acts as autoreceptor

The [[camp-pathway]] is central to both tolerance (chronic opioid/drug use adapts this pathway) and withdrawal (cAMP overproduction when drug stops).

## Dopamine and addiction — the four-step sequence

1. **Reward:** Pleasurable experience → dopamine burst → brain signals "this is beneficial, remember it"
2. **Habit:** Dopamine-driven synaptic changes make the behaviour easier to repeat automatically
3. **Surges:** Addictive substances produce dopamine surges far exceeding natural rewards, reinforcing drug-seeking at the expense of other goals
4. **Cravings:** External cues associated with use trigger conditioned anticipatory dopamine release — cravings can persist long after cessation

## Why drugs are so addictive

Drugs cause much larger dopamine surges than natural rewards. As a result, the brain:
- Loses dopamine receptors (downregulation) → reduced sensitivity to all rewards (tolerance)
- No longer finds natural rewards pleasurable (anhedonia)
- Requires progressively higher doses to achieve the same effect

## ACE effects on dopamine systems

Adverse childhood experiences alter dopamine-related systems:
- Reduced dopamine transporter binding
- Increased baseline dopamine levels
- Exaggerated dopamine release in response to stress
These changes increase susceptibility to novelty-seeking and stimulant drug use.
