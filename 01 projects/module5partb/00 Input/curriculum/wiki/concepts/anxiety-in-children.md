# Anxiety in Children and Adolescents

Anxiety disorders are the most common mental health disorders in children and adolescents — affecting ~6.5% globally. They affect educational and social functioning and create lifelong risk. Youth-specific presentations include separation anxiety disorder (age-inappropriate fear of separation) and selective mutism (consistent inability to speak in specific social situations). Emotionally based school avoidance (EBSA) captures anxiety-driven school non-attendance, which doubled post-COVID-19. Autistic young people are at particularly high EBSA risk (~40% have an anxiety disorder). CBT has the strongest evidence; SSRIs effective short-to-medium term only. The majority of affected children do not access evidence-based treatment.
