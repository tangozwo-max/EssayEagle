# UN Principles for the Protection of Persons with Mental Illness (1991)

Adopted by the UN Human Rights Council to guide nations in safeguarding rights and improving mental health care.

## Key principles
- **Non-discrimination** — no discrimination on basis of mental-health status.
- **Dignity & individual autonomy** — treated with dignity; right to decide when possible.
- **Informed consent** — required except where capacity is absent and care is necessary.
- **Confidentiality** — disclosure only with consent or as legally required.
- **Protection of liberty** — no unlawful or arbitrary deprivation; involuntary admission subject to legal review.
- **Least restrictive alternative** — community-based care preferred over institutionalisation.
- **Quality of care** — competent personnel, standards-compliant facilities.
- **Special protection for vulnerable groups** — children, older persons, persons with disabilities.

## Implementation variance
Countries differ widely. Factors: legal/regulatory frameworks, resources, cultural/social attitudes, training capacity, political priorities, monitoring mechanisms.
