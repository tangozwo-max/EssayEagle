# Griffiths/Brown Criteria for Behavioral Addiction

A six-criterion framework for diagnosing whether a behavior qualifies as an addiction. Developed by Dr. Mark Griffiths ([[mark-griffiths]]), building on an earlier conceptual framework by R.I. Brown. The criteria parallel those used for substance addictions and require that **functional impairment** is present at work, in social relationships, or in other social situations.

## The six criteria

| Criterion | Description |
|---|---|
| **Salience** | The activity dominates the person's life — thoughts, feelings, and behavior |
| **Euphoria** | The activity produces a subjective "buzz" or "high" |
| **Tolerance** | The activity must be engaged in to a progressively greater extent to achieve the same high |
| **Withdrawal** | Stopping the activity produces unpleasant emotions or physical effects |
| **Conflict** | The activity leads to conflict with others or to internal self-conflict |
| **Relapse and reinstatement** | Resumption of the activity at the same level after attempts to abstain, even following negative life consequences |

## Application
Applied to gambling, gaming, internet use, exercise, shopping, sexual behavior, and other potentially compulsive activities. The criteria help distinguish behaviors that are merely habitual or excessive from those meeting the threshold for a genuine addiction.
