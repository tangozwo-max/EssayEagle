# Neurodevelopmental Conditions (NDCs)

A group of disorders with onset in the developmental period that impact an individual's functioning. Unlike neurodiversity (natural brain variation) or neurodivergence (cognitive differences without specified cause), NDCs are diagnosed medical/psychiatric conditions with known or potential causes and developmental onsets.

## Key distinction from related terms

- **Neurodiversity** — refers to differences in how all people's brains work; encompasses everyone
- **Neurodivergence** — established cognitive differences without always-specified cause or onset; nonmedical term
- **Neurodevelopmental conditions** — diagnosed disorders with potentially known cause and developmental onset; medical conditions

## Common NDCs

- Autism spectrum disorder (ASD)
- Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)
- Developmental coordination disorder (DCD)
- Intellectual disability
- Language and speech disorders
- Learning disorders

## Mental health risk

Up to 80% of children with NDCs experience comorbid mental health disorders, affecting quality of life and functional outcomes. Mental health risks are influenced by the condition itself and by family, community, and economic contexts.

## Complex interactions

NDCs affect and are affected by:
- Family support and understanding
- School and workplace accommodations
- Community resources and services
- Economic stability
- Social inclusion and acceptance
