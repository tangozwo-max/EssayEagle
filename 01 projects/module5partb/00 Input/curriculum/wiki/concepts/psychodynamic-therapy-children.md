# Psychodynamic Therapy for Children and Adolescents

Psychodynamic therapy for children uses play, games, and creative activities to access the unconscious and early experiences that influence current behaviour. Core principles: exploring early attachments and significant relationships, identifying defense mechanisms, building a therapeutic alliance, and supporting the child's autonomy. Parents participate in parallel sessions. Foundational contributions from Melanie Klein (object relations; play analysis) and Anna Freud (developmental psychology; ego psychology). Short-term psychodynamic psychotherapy (STPP) shows a "sleeper effect" — benefits continue post-therapy. Evidence base is limited (few RCTs); a large NIHR multicenter trial is ongoing comparing CBT, STPP, and specialist care for adolescent depression.
