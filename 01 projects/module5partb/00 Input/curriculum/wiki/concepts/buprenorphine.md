# Buprenorphine

A partial mu opioid receptor agonist used as a first-line pharmacological treatment for opioid use disorder (OUD). Available as Subutex (buprenorphine alone) or Suboxone (buprenorphine + naloxone, added to deter injection misuse).

## Mechanism of action
- **Partial mu opioid receptor agonist** — activates mu receptors to a lesser degree than full agonists (e.g., morphine, heroin); this creates a ceiling effect on euphoria and respiratory depression, making it safer than methadone at higher doses
- **Kappa opioid receptor partial agonist** — helps reduce the dysphoric and hallucinogenic effects associated with opioid use
- **High receptor binding affinity** — tightly binds to opioid receptors, blocking effects of illicit opioids taken on top of a maintenance dose
- **Long duration of action** — reduces dosing frequency; enables once-daily or alternate-day regimens

## Clinical advantages over methadone
- **Better safety profile** — ceiling effect limits respiratory depression risk; safer in overdose
- **Can be prescribed for home use** more readily than methadone
- **Diversion deterrence** — Suboxone formulation deters injection (naloxone component precipitates withdrawal if injected)
- **Effective for chronic pain** — provides analgesia without the same accumulation risk as methadone

## Clinical uses
1. Opioid detoxification (short-course)
2. Opioid maintenance / substitution therapy (long-term)

## Regulatory position
Co-first-line with [[methadone]] for opioid detoxification under NICE guidelines.
