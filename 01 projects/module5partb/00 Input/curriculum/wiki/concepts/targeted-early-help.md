# Targeted Early Help

Targeted early help services (also known as selective and indicated services) consist of support for individuals who have additional needs and are at risk of developing more complex mental health needs. These services do not require a formal diagnosis and focus on timely interventions to prevent conditions from escalating.

## Key characteristics

- **Responsive**: aimed at individuals identified as having specific risk factors or emerging difficulties
- **Non-diagnostic**: supports individuals before a formal diagnosis is necessary
- **Preventative**: aims to stop escalation into more serious mental health conditions
- **Timely**: delivered at the point where early intervention is most effective

## Examples

Targeted early help might include support groups for young people identified as anxious about school, family support for those experiencing parental conflict, or peer-led interventions in communities identified as having elevated mental health risk.

## Position in continuum

Targeted early help sits between universal services (available to all) and specialist services (for severe, enduring needs) on the continuum of need.
