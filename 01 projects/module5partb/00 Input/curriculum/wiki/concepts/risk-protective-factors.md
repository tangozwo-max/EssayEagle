# Risk and Protective Factors

A public-health framework (SAMHSA) for understanding what **raises** (risk) or **buffers** (protective) the likelihood of mental-illness onset and adverse outcomes.

## Definitions
- **Risk factors** — biological, psychological, family, community, or cultural characteristics that precede or are associated with higher likelihood of mental illness.
- **Protective factors** — characteristics that reduce the impact of risk factors and are associated with lower likelihood of negative outcomes.

## Four principles (SAMHSA)

1. **Factors exist in multiple contexts.** Individual, family, community, society — targeting only one context fails.
2. **Combined effect.** Risk factors correlate positively with each other (cluster), and negatively with protective factors. Cumulative burden raises risk.
3. **Factors are influential across time.** Childhood exposures (e.g., deprivation) shape lifelong trajectories. Effective parenting protects across the lifespan.
4. **Factors are associated with multiple outcomes.** One intervention can benefit many domains (e.g., bereavement support reduces depression incidence + builds general coping skills).

## Fixed vs modifiable
- **Fixed** — genetics, age, sex at birth.
- **Modifiable** — income, employment, peer group, housing, nutrition, social capital.

> "The social determinants of mental health represent **modifiable** factors that, if addressed, could lead to improvements in the mental health of our society." — Compton & Shim
