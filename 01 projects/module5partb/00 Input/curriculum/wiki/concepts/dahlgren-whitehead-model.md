# Dahlgren-Whitehead Model (1991)

A visual model ("rainbow model") of the [[social-determinants-of-health]] developed by Göran Dahlgren and Margaret Whitehead in 1991. Represents health as the outcome of a web of social influences.

## Structure — concentric layers

```
                 ┌─ Individual ────┐
                 │ (age, sex, genes)│  ← fixed
                 └──────────────────┘
            ┌─ Individual lifestyle ─┐
            │ diet, exercise, smoking │
            └─────────────────────────┘
         ┌─ Social & community networks ─┐
         │ family, friends, social support │
         └─────────────────────────────────┘
      ┌─ Living & working conditions ─┐
      │ education, employment, housing, │
      │ healthcare, food, water, services│
      └─────────────────────────────────┘
     [ General socioeconomic, cultural & environmental conditions ]
```

## Interpretation
- Central circle = fixed factors (genetics, age, sex).
- Three surrounding layers = layers of modifiable influence.
- Arrows should NOT be read hierarchically — factors are interconnected.

## Criticisms
- May oversimplify complex interactions.
- Concentric circles easily misread as a hierarchy.
- Cultural factors (e.g., racism) not adequately addressed in the original.
- Downplays biological factors.
- Difficult to quantify determinants for measurement.

## 30th anniversary reflection
Dahlgren & Whitehead (2021) reflect on what the model *does not* do and was never intended to do — often cited in public health training.
