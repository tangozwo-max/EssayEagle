# Substance Use Disorder (SUD)

DSM-5 (2013) diagnostic term: use of one or more substances leading to clinically significant impairment or distress. Replaced the previous terms *substance abuse* and *substance dependence*, measuring severity on a continuum from mild to severe. What people commonly call "addiction" corresponds to a severe SUD.

## Severity levels
- **Mild:** 2–3 criteria met
- **Moderate:** 4–5 criteria met
- **Severe:** 6+ criteria met (= colloquial "addiction")

## The 11 DSM-5 criteria (grouped by domain)

### Impaired control
1. Taking the substance in larger amounts or for longer than intended
2. Persistent desire or unsuccessful attempts to cut down or stop
3. Spending large amounts of time obtaining, using, or recovering
4. Craving or strong urges to use

### Social impairment
5. Failure to fulfill major obligations at work, school, or home
6. Continued use despite persistent social or interpersonal problems
7. Giving up or reducing important activities

### Risky use
8. Recurrent use in physically hazardous situations
9. Continued use despite knowing a physical or psychological problem is caused or worsened by use

### Pharmacological indicators
10. **Tolerance** — needing more to achieve the desired effect
11. **Withdrawal** — characteristic symptoms when use is reduced (not applicable to hallucinogens, PCP, inhalants)

*Note: Tolerance and withdrawal under medically supervised treatment do NOT count as criteria.*

## Why terminology changed (DSM-5)
The APA replaced "abuse" and "dependence" with SUD to reduce stigma, provide a graduated framework, and more accurately identify people who need help even before reaching a "debilitating addiction."

## ICD-11 comparison
ICD-11 uses "dependence syndrome" (≥3 of 6 criteria; binary — present or absent, no severity grading) and "harmful use" as separate categories.
