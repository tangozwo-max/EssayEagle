# Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD)

ODD is characterised by a persistent pattern of angry/irritable mood, argumentative/defiant behaviour, or vindictiveness. Individuals often do not see themselves as angry but as responding to unreasonable demands. First symptoms typically appear in preschool years, rarely after early adolescence. Frequently co-occurs with ADHD and conduct disorder. Increases risk of anxiety, MDD, and suicide attempts independently of conduct disorder. Defiant/argumentative symptoms lead to conduct disorder; angry/irritable symptoms lead to emotional disorders. Evidence: parenting interventions (8–16 sessions; NICE and WHO endorsed) have the strongest evidence base; psychopharmacological treatments are not recommended.
