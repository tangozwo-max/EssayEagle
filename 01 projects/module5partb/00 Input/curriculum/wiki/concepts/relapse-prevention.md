# Relapse Prevention (RP)

A cognitive-behavioural approach to addiction treatment that focuses on identifying and managing triggers, high-risk situations, and coping deficits that may lead to a return to substance use or addictive behaviour after a period of abstinence or reduced use.

## Core components
- **Trigger identification** — recognising internal (emotional states, cravings) and external (people, places, situations) triggers
- **Coping skills development** — alternative behaviours and thought strategies when faced with triggers
- **Relapse rehearsal** — mentally practising responses to high-risk situations before they occur (e.g., guided imagery in Tim's CBT)
- **Crisis planning** — pre-agreed steps to take if relapse occurs, minimising escalation
- **Lifestyle balance** — building positive activities, relationships, and routines that do not revolve around the addictive behaviour

## Normalising relapse
Relapses are expected in the [[transtheoretical-model]] of change — they are part of the process, not failures. The MDT response should be non-judgmental: re-examine triggers, adjust the care plan, and reinforce that recovery remains possible.

## Integration with other interventions
- [[cognitive-behavioural-therapy]] provides the skills training component
- [[motivational-interviewing]] sustains motivation through ambivalence
- [[12-step-programs]] offer peer accountability and community support
- [[contingency-management]] may reinforce abstinence in the maintenance stage
