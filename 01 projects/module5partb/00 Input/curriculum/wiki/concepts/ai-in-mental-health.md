# AI in Mental Health

Artificial intelligence and machine-learning applied to neuroimaging and behavioural data for mental-health diagnosis, risk prediction (e.g., suicide ideation), and treatment decisions.

## Promise
- Pattern recognition across complex neuroimaging data
- Early identification of emerging conditions
- Scalable diagnostic support for under-resourced areas

## Risks
- Algorithmic **bias** — training-data skew perpetuates inequity
- **Data confidentiality** — large-scale behavioural data raises privacy concerns
- Lack of long-term safety data
- No consensus on ethical use
