# Shared Decision-Making (SDM)

Collaborative process between the healthcare professional and person under care for making health-related decisions. Part of the **third leg** of [[ebp-three-legged-stool]].

## Practitioner role
Outline therapy/treatment options with benefits and harms.

## Person's role
State values, preferences, concerns, personal circumstances.

## Five key questions (Hoffmann et al.)
1. What happens if the person waits and watches (no action)?
2. What are the test / treatment options?
3. What are the benefits and harms of each?
4. How do benefits and harms weigh up for *this* person?
5. Does the person have enough information to make an informed choice?

## Notes
- Victor Montori (Mayo Clinic) frames SDM as a **moral obligation** and vehicle for a "patient revolution."
- Some commentators (Desai) caution that paternalistic attitudes in mental-health practice hinder genuine SDM.
