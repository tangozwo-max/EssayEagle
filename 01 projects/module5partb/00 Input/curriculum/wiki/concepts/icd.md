# ICD — International Classification of Diseases

WHO's global standard for morbidity and mortality coding. Latest: **ICD-11** (effective 2022). Often used alongside or instead of DSM for mental-health diagnosis, especially outside North America. Designed to incorporate more culturally diverse perspectives than DSM, which is designed primarily for US clinicians.

## In M5 context — Substance Use
ICD-11 uses different terminology and structure to DSM-5 for substance-related disorders:

- **[[dependence-syndrome]]** — 6 criteria; ≥3 required; binary (present/absent — no severity grading)
- **Harmful use** — clear evidence of physical or psychological harm without meeting full dependence criteria
- **Behavioral addictions** category: gambling disorder AND gaming disorder (ICD-11 recognizes both, vs. DSM-5 which only recognizes gambling)
- "Compulsive sexual behavior disorder" also classified in ICD-11

ICD-11 advises that knowledge of cultural terms for substances improves communication with people seeking support (e.g., khat, mescaline).
