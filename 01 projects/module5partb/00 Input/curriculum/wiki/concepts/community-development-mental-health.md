# Community Development for Mental Health

Community development for mental health involves creating and strengthening programs and structures that support community connections, social support, and collective well-being. It is a key method for promoting positive mental health at the population level.

## Key elements

Community development for mental health includes:
- **Community engagement**: involving community members in identifying needs and developing solutions
- **Social networks**: strengthening connections between individuals and groups
- **Community resources**: identifying and building on existing community strengths and assets
- **Social support**: creating structures supporting mutual aid and reciprocal helping
- **Collective efficacy**: building community capacity to address challenges

## Benefits for mental health

Community development contributes to mental health promotion through:
- Reducing isolation and loneliness
- Building social support networks
- Creating a sense of belonging and community identity
- Strengthening protective factors at community level
- Supporting collective resilience

## Examples

Community development for mental health may include:
- Community centres and gathering spaces
- Peer support and self-help groups
- Community volunteer programs
- Cultural and social programs
- Neighbourhood improvement initiatives
- Community education programs
