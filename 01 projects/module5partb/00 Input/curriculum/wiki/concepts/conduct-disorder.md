# Conduct Disorder

Conduct disorder (DSM-5) is characterised by a repetitive and persistent pattern of behaviour violating others' rights or major societal norms. Four behaviour categories: aggressive conduct causing or threatening harm, nonaggressive property damage, deceitfulness or theft, and serious rule violations. Symptoms rarely begin after age 16; early onset predicts worse outcomes including criminal behaviour and substance use. The diagnosis should not be applied in high-crime or threatening environments where such behaviour is near-normative. Co-occurs with ADHD, ODD, and trauma histories. Treatments include multisystemic therapy (MST), functional family therapy, and brief strategic family therapy.
