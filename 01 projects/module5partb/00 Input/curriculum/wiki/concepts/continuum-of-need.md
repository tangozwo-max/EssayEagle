# Continuum of Need

The continuum of need is a framework that categorises the varying levels of support or intervention required by individuals and families in different circumstances. It recognises that people may move up and down the levels as their circumstances change and is widely used in the United Kingdom by health, social care, and education services.

## Four levels

### Universal
The base level containing services available to all without any specific assessment of need. Focus is on promoting well-being and preventing escalation of needs. Example: health and wellbeing lessons in schools.

### Selective and Indicated (Targeted)
Support for individuals who have additional needs (related to social determinants of health) and are at risk of developing more complex needs. A diagnosis is not required. Focus is on timely interventions to prevent conditions from worsening. Example: targeted support for young people identified as anxious about school.

### Specialist
Specialist interventions cover three phases:
1. **Acute phase**: providing specialist interventions to alleviate symptoms before formal diagnosis
2. **Identification phase**: identifying those in need of more specialised mental health services
3. **Recovery phase**: supporting people through recovery over the long term

This level serves people with severe, enduring, and complex needs who require diagnosis to access services. Multi-agency involvement and statutory interventions may be involved. Example: specialist services for young people who are self-harming.

## Flexibility and context

The services available at each point on the continuum vary significantly across countries and depend on the available healthcare infrastructure.
