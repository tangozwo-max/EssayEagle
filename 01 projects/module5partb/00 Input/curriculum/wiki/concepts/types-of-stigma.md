# Types of Stigma

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Public stigma** (enacted / experienced) | Society-level stereotypes, prejudice, discrimination directed at people with MH conditions. |
| **Perceived stigma** | Person's perception of stigma in their culture. |
| **Anticipated stigma** | Expectation of being stigmatised if the condition becomes known. |
| **Label avoidance** | Avoiding activities that may disclose MH status. |
| **Self-stigma (internalised)** | Person applies negative stereotypes to themselves. |
| **Associative stigma (courtesy / by association)** | Directed at friends, family, caregivers of a person with MH condition. |
| **Structural stigma** | Unfair treatment by governments, institutions, organisations. |
| **Health practitioner stigma** | Healthcare worker allows stereotypes to degrade patient care. |

## Link & Phelan's five conditions for public stigma
1. People perceive differences between groups.
2. Society holds negative stereotypes about the group.
3. "Us vs them" mentality emerges.
4. Labeled group experiences discrimination / loss of status.
5. Disparities in economic, social, or political power exist.
