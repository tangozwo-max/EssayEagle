# WHO CSDH Framework (2008)

Framework produced by the **WHO Commission on Social Determinants of Health** (CSDH, established 2005) to enhance understanding of health determinants and guide policy-making to address health inequities.

## Two-tier distinction
- **Structural determinants** — overarching social and economic factors that shape the distribution of power, income, goods, and services within societies.
  - Examples: socioeconomic status, education, employment, social class.
- **Intermediary determinants** — mechanisms through which structural determinants exert influence on health outcomes.
  - Examples: housing quality, access to healthcare, nutrition, psychosocial factors (e.g., [[social-capital]]), behavioural factors (smoking, diet, activity).

## Three principles of action (CSDH 2008)
1. **Improve the conditions of daily life.**
2. **Tackle the inequitable distribution of power, money, and resources.**
3. **Measure the problem and assess the impact of action.**

## Progress since 2005
Despite global commitments, progress is slow. Exacerbating factors include:
- COVID-19 pandemic
- Climate change
- Global conflict
- Increased migration
- Widening HIC–LMIC gap in technology and digital access ([[hic-lmic]])

The WHO published a renewed **operational framework (2024)** for measurement, assessment, and addressing the social determinants of health and health inequities.
