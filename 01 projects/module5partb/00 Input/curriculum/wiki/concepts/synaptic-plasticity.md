# Synaptic Plasticity

Change and adaptation occurring *at synapses*. Two types:

- **Short-term** — temporary changes in synaptic strength (seconds-minutes); quick neural processing, short-term memory, immediate stimulus adaptation.
- **Long-term** — lasting changes (minutes-years); underpins memory creation and storage. Includes [[ltp]] and [[ltd]].

Proposed by **Donald Hebb (1949)** — [[hebbian-theory]]: "cells that fire together wire together."

## Clinical relevance
Dysfunction in synaptic plasticity at the root of depression, schizophrenia, anxiety, addiction, PTSD. MDD, anxiety, PTSD share synaptic loss in the prefrontal cortex.
