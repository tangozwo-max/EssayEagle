# Coercive Measures

Interventions or actions taken **without the full and informed consent** of the person under care, often to manage behaviour or ensure safety. Ethically controversial; may violate [[article-3-echr]].

## Main forms
- **Physical restraint** — holding down or using straps / handcuffs
- **Seclusion** — placed alone in a room to prevent harm
- **Forced medication** — administered against will, court-ordered or emergency
- **Chemical restraint** — sedation to control behaviour (vs therapeutic intent)

## Lived-experience themes (Brophy et al. 2016)
- Human-rights abuses, trauma, control, isolation, dehumanisation, antirecovery
- Poor practices: excessive force, paternalistic attitudes, lack of communication, lack of alternatives
- Contributing factors: organisational culture, physical environment, under-resourcing, fear, stigma

## Non-European origin populations at particular risk
People of non-European origin are disproportionately subject to coercive interventions in European settings (Sashidharan et al.). [[implicit-bias]] is a major driver.

## Ethical justification test
Coercion may be justified **only** if the [[proportionality-criterion]] is met — harm prevented > harm caused by the measure.
