# Mesolimbic Pathway (Reward Pathway)

One of four major dopamine pathways in the brain. It runs from the **ventral tegmental area (VTA)** in the midbrain to the **nucleus accumbens** in the basal ganglia, and is the primary pathway for reward processing, motivation, and addiction. It is the most studied pathway in addiction neuroscience.

## The four dopamine pathways

| Pathway | From → To | Primary function |
|---|---|---|
| **Mesolimbic** | VTA → Nucleus accumbens | Reward, motivation, addiction |
| **Mesocortical** | VTA → Prefrontal cortex | Cognitive function, emotional regulation |
| **Nigrostriatal** | Substantia nigra → Striatum | Motor control; deficient in Parkinson's |
| **Tuberoinfundibular** | Hypothalamus → Pituitary | Prolactin regulation; blocked by antipsychotics |

## Why it matters in addiction

All addictive substances — and addictive behaviours — increase dopamine activity in the mesolimbic pathway:
- Natural rewards produce a moderate dopamine release
- Drugs produce surges far exceeding natural reward levels
- This disproportionate signal causes the brain to prioritise drug-seeking above other goals
- Over time, the mesolimbic pathway becomes the dominant driver of behaviour

## GABAergic neurons in the nucleus accumbens

Dopamine from the VTA terminates at GABAergic neurons in the nucleus accumbens, which carry D1 and D2 receptors. D1 activation (via cAMP rise) increases neuronal excitability; D2 activation (via cAMP suppression) inhibits it.
