# Lofexidine

A non-opioid medication used to manage opioid withdrawal symptoms. Unlike [[methadone]] and [[buprenorphine]], lofexidine does not substitute for opioids — instead it reduces the autonomic (sympathetic) symptoms of withdrawal by blocking norepinephrine release.

## Mechanism of action
- **Alpha-2 adrenergic receptor agonist** — activates presynaptic alpha-2 receptors, reducing norepinephrine release from the [[locus-coeruleus]]
- Decreased norepinephrine lowers **sympathetic tone**, alleviating withdrawal symptoms driven by autonomic hyperactivity:
  - Anxiety and agitation
  - Sweating
  - Muscle aches
  - Tachycardia and palpitations
  - Insomnia

## Clinical role
- **Second-line/alternative** to methadone or buprenorphine for opioid detoxification
- Enables **shorter duration** withdrawal than opioid-based substitution
- NICE guidelines indicate use when a person:
  - Makes an informed decision to avoid methadone/buprenorphine
  - Chooses a shorter detox timeline
  - Has mild or uncertain opioid dependence

## Limitations
- Does not reduce drug cravings (unlike methadone/buprenorphine)
- Does not provide pain relief
- Risk of hypotension and sedation
