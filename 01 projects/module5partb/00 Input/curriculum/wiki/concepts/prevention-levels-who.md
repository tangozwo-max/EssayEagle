# Prevention Levels (WHO three-level framework)

Three levels of prevention applied to risk factors and determinants of poor mental health.

| Level | Aim |
|---|---|
| **Primary prevention** | Prevent the new onset (*incidence*) of one or more mental disorders, or of suicidal ideation. |
| **Secondary prevention** | Lower the *prevalence* of established cases in the population. |
| **Tertiary prevention** | Reduce disability, enhance rehabilitation, prevent relapses or recurrences. |

## Relationship to M2W5 five-level framework
M2W5 expands this model to 5 levels:
- **Primordial** — structural (addressing root determinants)
- **Primary** — limit exposure
- **Secondary** — early detection
- **Tertiary** — reduce severity / disability
- **Quaternary** — avoid over-medicalisation
