# Ryff's Six Dimensions of Psychological Well-Being

Multidimensional framework developed by psychologist **Carol Ryff**. Captures eudaimonic well-being across six interrelated dimensions.

1. **Autonomy** — self-determination, independence, inner locus of evaluation.
2. **Environmental Mastery** — ability to manage life and surroundings.
3. **Personal Growth** — ongoing development and realisation of potential.
4. **Positive Relations with Others** — warm, satisfying, trusting relationships.
5. **Purpose in Life** — having goals and direction.
6. **Self-Acceptance** — positive attitude toward self and past.

## Evidence base
Longitudinal studies (Ryff, Weiss et al.) link high psychological well-being to:
- Protection against mental illness and psychopathology
- Better biological markers of physical health
- Reduced risk of Alzheimer's disease
- Longer life expectancy
