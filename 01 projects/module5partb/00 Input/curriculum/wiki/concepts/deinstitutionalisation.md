# Deinstitutionalisation

Late-20th-century global policy movement to close large psychiatric institutions and move care to community settings. Intended to end human-rights abuses of long-stay asylums.

## Unintended consequence (US focus)
Many US states closed psychiatric facilities **without funding community replacements**, leading to:
- Increased homelessness among people with severe mental illness
- Increased incarceration — people with mental illness **11× more likely to be victims of violent crime** than the general population; yet >2 million arrested annually; those with severe conditions likely arrested at least once in their lifetime.

> "We de-institutionalize the mentally ill just to turn around and criminalize them." — Reagan Kremer, social worker
