# Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire (SDQ)

The SDQ is a brief screening instrument for behavioural and emotional disorders in children and young people aged 3–17 years. It assesses five subscales: emotional symptoms, conduct problems, hyperactivity/inattention, peer problems, and prosocial behaviour.

## Key characteristics

- **Age range**: 3–17 years
- **Brief**: 25 items, quick to administer
- **Multiple versions**: youth self-report and parent report available
- **Subscales**: emotional symptoms, conduct problems, hyperactivity, peer problems, prosocial behaviour
- **Widely translated**: available in many languages

## Administration

The SDQ can be completed by:
- Parents or teachers
- Mental health workers
- Children and young people themselves (self-report)

## Usage in population screening

When used in population screening, young people are referred for further assessment if scores exceed established clinical cut-off thresholds.

## Strengths

- Widely validated and used internationally
- Brief and efficient
- Covers both difficulties and strengths (prosocial behaviour)
- Low cost
