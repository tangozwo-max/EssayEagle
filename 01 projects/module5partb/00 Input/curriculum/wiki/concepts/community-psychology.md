# Community Psychology

Branch of psychology focused on the **relationships between individuals and communities**. Grew out of the recognition that mental health is shaped by wider environments (Bronfenbrenner) and that community-level interventions can address problems that individual-focused approaches cannot.

## Emphasis
- Prevention over treatment
- Community resources, empowerment, collaboration
- Social justice and equity
- Systems thinking

## M4 W5 Context
Community psychology in CAMH emphasises three practices: relational (trust, peer support), participatory (CBPR — community-as-equal-partner research), and strength-based (leveraging community assets). Co-production is central: equal partnership in design, delivery, and evaluation. Key distinction: CMHTs are statutory/clinical; community-based approaches are more flexible and grassroots. Global examples: MAC-UK (INTEGRATE model, UK), Jigsaw (Ireland), NJR/Headspace-inspired (Netherlands), CHAT (Singapore), Headspace (Australia).
