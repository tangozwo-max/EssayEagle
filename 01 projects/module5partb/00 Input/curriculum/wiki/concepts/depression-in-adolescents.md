# Depression in Adolescents

Depression has low prevalence in childhood but rises sharply in adolescence, particularly in females and those with SEN, chronic illness, or low SES. The first depressive episode typically occurs during adolescence. Compared to adults, adolescents more commonly show irritable mood, appetite/weight changes, insomnia/hypersomnia, and fatigue as core symptoms. About 50% experience recurrence. Risks include later anxiety, bipolar disorder conversion, substance use, self-harm, and suicidality. NICE stepped-care for management: mild = lifestyle changes + psychoeducation + watchful waiting (up to 4 weeks); moderate = specialist care + CBT or IPT; severe = combined medication and psychotherapy, with escalation if no response in 4–6 weeks.
