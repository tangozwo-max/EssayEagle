# Trauma

Distressing events that are so extreme or intense that they overwhelm a person's ability to cope, resulting in lasting negative psychological and neurobiological impacts.

## Definition and exposure pathways

According to DSM-5, trauma relating to PTSD involves exposure to actual or threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence through:
- Direct experience of the traumatic event
- Witnessing the event occurring to others
- Learning the event happened to a close family member or friend
- Repeated or extreme exposure to distressing details of the event

## Trauma as a risk factor for mental health

### Effects on child mental health
- Alters behavior and social interactions
- May cause overreaction or excessive passivity
- Can lead to social isolation and educational difficulties
- Without supportive relationships, children miss crucial developmental experiences

### Neurobiological impacts
Childhood trauma triggers adaptive brain changes affecting:
- **Threat detection** — hypervigilance to danger
- **Memory processing** — easier triggering of traumatic memories
- **Reward processing** — diminished ability to experience pleasure

These changes help children cope in dangerous environments but create difficulties in safer settings.

## Related concepts

- **PTSD** — diagnostic condition with re-experiencing, avoidance, hyperarousal
- **Complex trauma** — multiple interconnected traumatic experiences, often in childhood
- **Intergenerational trauma** — trauma effects passing across generations
- **Latent vulnerability** — increased future mental health risk from early trauma
- **Trauma-informed care** — recognition of trauma impact in service provision
