# Intimate Partner Violence (IPV)

Abuse occurring within intimate relationships, affecting not only the adult survivor but also children and infants exposed to the violence. IPV serves as both a risk factor for maternal depression and an independent risk factor for child mental health.

## Impact across developmental stages

### Prenatal
- Increased miscarriage risk
- Lower birth weight
- Premature birth
- May first occur or intensify during pregnancy

### Infancy and toddlerhood
- Chronic stress exposure (fight-or-flight activation)
- Impaired learning and development (stress limiting cognitive engagement)
- Disrupted attachment relationships with caregivers
- Difficulty with emotional regulation beyond typical age expectations

### Childhood and adolescence
- Emotional dysregulation and difficulty managing emotions
- Social withdrawal and difficulty forming relationships
- Mimicking observed aggressive behaviours or becoming overly passive/compliant
- Distraction from education due to worry and preoccupation
- Difficulty making and maintaining friendships

### Adolescence and beyond
- Increased risk of substance use
- Criminal activity and risk-taking behaviours
- Self-harm and suicidal ideation
- Mental health disorder development

## Relationship to toxic stress

IPV exposure creates chronic toxic stress conditions that impair neurodevelopment, particularly the systems involved in stress response, emotional regulation, and learning.
