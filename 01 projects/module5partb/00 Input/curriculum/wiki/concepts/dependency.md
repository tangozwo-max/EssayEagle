# Dependency (Substance)

A physiological state resulting from prolonged use of a substance in which the body adapts to the substance's presence. Dependency is characterized by **tolerance** (needing more of a substance to achieve the same effect) and **withdrawal symptoms** (unpleasant physical and psychological effects when the substance is reduced or stopped). Physical dependence is considerably more common than addiction.

## Key points
- Dependence can occur even when substances are taken exactly as prescribed (e.g., opioids, benzodiazepines for pain or anxiety)
- DSM-5 notes that tolerance and withdrawal in the context of medically prescribed treatment do **not** count as criteria for a [[substance-use-disorder]]
- Only 1.5% of people taking benzodiazepines are classified as having a SUD, despite many developing physical dependence
- Dependence is a **normal physiological response** and does not inherently indicate addiction

## West et al. (2024) definition
"A *bodily disposition* realized as impaired functioning following reduction or termination of use of a psychoactive substance as a result of long-term physiological adaptation to that substance."

## Dependence vs. addiction
The critical distinction: dependence involves physiological adaptation only. [[Addiction]] additionally involves **compulsive behavior and loss of control** in seeking and using the substance. Dependence can exist without addiction.
