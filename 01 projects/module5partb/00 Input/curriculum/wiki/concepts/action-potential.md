# Action Potential

A rapid, temporary **electrical impulse** travelling along an axon, enabling signal transmission to the next neuron/cell.

## Sequence
1. **Resting potential** — axon membrane polarised; inside ~-70 mV relative to outside.
2. **Stimulus** → voltage-gated Na⁺ channels open; Na⁺ rushes in.
3. Membrane becomes less negative → **depolarisation**.
4. If threshold reached (~-50 to -55 mV) → action potential fires; membrane potential rises sharply.
5. Na⁺ channels close, K⁺ channels open, K⁺ exits → **repolarisation**.
6. Temporary **hyperpolarisation** (overshoot past resting state).
7. **Na⁺/K⁺ pump** restores ion distribution → back to resting polarised state.

Formalised by the [[hodgkin-huxley-model]].
