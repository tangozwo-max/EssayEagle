# Extended Amygdala

A collection of interconnected brain structures including the central nucleus of the amygdala, the bed nucleus of the stria terminalis, and a part of the nucleus accumbens. It is primarily involved in processing stressful feelings — anxiety, irritability, and unease — and in emotional responses to threat and withdrawal.

## Role in addiction

In the [[addiction-cycle]], the extended amygdala drives the **withdrawal/negative affect** stage. With continued substance use, the extended amygdala becomes increasingly sensitised to stress and negative emotions:
- Its heightened sensitivity during withdrawal motivates substance-seeking to relieve discomfort (negative reinforcement)
- It is the primary source of dysphoria, anxiety, and irritability experienced during withdrawal from alcohol, opioids, cannabis, and other substances

## Anti-reward system plasticity

As addiction deepens, the extended amygdala undergoes lasting structural and functional changes — the primary site of **plasticity** in the anti-reward system. Key neurochemical players here include:
- [[corticotropin-releasing-factor]] (CRF) — controls stress responses; CRF antagonists can block compulsive drug-taking
- [[dynorphin]] — kappa-opioid receptor modulator; contributes to dysphoria
- [[noradrenaline]] — norepinephrine; heightened activity contributes to anxiety and unease

## In ACE context

Adverse childhood experiences cause structural defects in the amygdala (reduced volume) and functional hyperactivity, compounding the sensitivity of the extended amygdala stress system and increasing addiction vulnerability.
