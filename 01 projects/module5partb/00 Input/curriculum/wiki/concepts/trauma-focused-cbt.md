# Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Trauma-Focused CBT (TF-CBT) is a specialised form of cognitive-behavioral therapy specifically adapted for individuals, including adolescents, who have experienced trauma. It combines cognitive-behavioral techniques with trauma-sensitive approaches.

## Key elements

- **Trauma processing**: structured exposure and processing of traumatic memories
- **Cognitive restructuring**: identifying and changing unhelpful thoughts related to trauma
- **Behavioural activation**: gradually engaging in avoided activities
- **Coping skills**: building skills for managing trauma-related symptoms
- **Parent involvement**: including parents/caregivers in treatment

## Effectiveness

Research shows TF-CBT has positive effects on:
- Reducing depression in trauma survivors
- Reducing anxiety symptoms
- Reducing stress-related symptoms
- Improving overall functioning

## Application

TF-CBT is particularly effective for:
- Adolescents who have experienced high levels of trauma
- Individuals with post-traumatic stress disorder
- Children and youth affected by abuse, violence, or disasters
