# Evidence-Based Practice (EBP)

Integration of the **best available evidence + professional expertise + the person's unique needs / preferences** into clinical decision-making. Originated in North American clinical epidemiology in the 1960s (Yale, McGill).

## Three integrated components ([[ebp-three-legged-stool]])
1. **Best available current, valid, relevant evidence** (peer-reviewed literature, systematic reviews)
2. **Professional experience and clinical expertise**
3. **Perspective, values, and experience of the person seeking care**

## Sources of evidence
- Peer-reviewed papers
- Best-practice guidelines, consensus statements
- Systematic reviews
- Professional clinical experience
- Narratives of [[pwle]] (People With Lived Experience)
- Carer evidence
- Contextual / anecdotal clinical evidence

## Limitations
- Disagreement on what counts as "best evidence" when data is thin
- Safety / contraindication appraisal is time-intensive
- Easily-measurable interventions dominate the literature
- **English-language bias** in systematic reviews excludes non-English research
- [[research-practice-gap]] — disconnect between trial effects and real-world outcomes
