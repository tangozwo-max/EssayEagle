# Youth Development Programs

Youth development programs are strength-based interventions designed to promote positive mental health and well-being in young people by building life skills, fostering positive relationships, and recognising existing strengths.

## Core characteristics

Effective youth development programs:
- **Provide skill-building opportunities**: help young people develop social, emotional, coping, and life skills
- **Foster positive relationships**: create environments supporting meaningful connections with peers and mentors
- **Strengths-based**: recognise and build on existing strengths demonstrated by young people rather than focusing solely on deficits
- **Community-engaged**: work positively and constructively within young people's communities and educational settings
- **Culturally responsive**: acknowledge diverse contexts and needs of different groups

## Types of programs

Youth development programs may address:
- Social and emotional skill development
- Academic achievement and career readiness
- Community service and civic engagement
- Healthy relationship and life skills
- Substance use prevention
- Mental health promotion and resilience

## Mental health promotion role

Youth development programs contribute to mental health promotion by building protective factors, developing coping capacities, and creating supportive environments for healthy development.
