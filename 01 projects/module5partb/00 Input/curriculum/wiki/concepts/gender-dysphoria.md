# Gender Dysphoria

Gender dysphoria refers to the distress arising from incongruence between a person's expressed gender and the gender assigned at birth. It is not classified as a mental health condition; gender incongruence (marked and persistent difference between experienced and assigned gender) is a diagnostic term in ICD-11. ~1% of the population may experience some degree of gender incongruence. In the UK, referrals to GIDS rose from 94/year (2009–10) to ~50/week. Transgender youth experience disproportionately high rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidal ideation. Medical interventions for youth include puberty blockers (GnRH analogues), hormone therapies, and surgery — all remain contentious for young people due to potentially irreversible lifelong consequences.
