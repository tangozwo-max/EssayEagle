# Socioeconomic Status (SES)

The social and economic position of an individual or family, typically measured by household income, parental education, and occupational status. SES is a major determinant of child mental health outcomes.

## Impact on child mental health

### Epidemiology
- Children from socioeconomically disadvantaged backgrounds are 2-3 times more likely to develop mental health disorders
- Low household income is the strongest predictor of mental health disorders in children and adolescents
- Persistently low SES increases lifetime risk of mental health disorders
- Improvement in SES reduces risk

## Mechanisms of impact

### Direct material factors
- Food insecurity and malnutrition
- Housing instability or homelessness
- Limited access to healthcare
- Reduced educational resources and opportunities
- Environmental hazards and pollution

### Social and psychological factors
- Parental stress and mental health strain
- Limited social capital and support networks
- Exposure to violence and crime
- Reduced sense of control and agency
- Discrimination and social stigma

### Systemic factors
- Limited access to mental health services
- Poor-quality educational opportunities
- Inadequate social safety nets
- Policy and resource allocation patterns

## Protective interventions

Cash transfer programs and other economic support have shown potential benefits in reducing child mental health risk by addressing material deprivation and reducing parental stress.
