# Behavioral Disorders in CAMH

Under DSM-5, behavioral disorders fall within "disruptive, impulse-control, and conduct disorders." All involve difficulties controlling emotions and behaviours, but differ in which type of self-control problem predominates. Category includes: ODD (emotional + behavioural self-control), intermittent explosive disorder (emotional outbursts), conduct disorder (behavioural violations), antisocial personality disorder, pyromania, and kleptomania. Diagnosis is complex due to overlapping symptoms, developmental variability, subjective assessment, and environmental context. International variation in diagnostic rates is significant — some countries diagnose readily in young children, others reserve for severe cases.
