# Gillick Competence

Gillick competence is a UK legal framework determining whether a child under 16 can consent to their own medical treatment without parental permission. Assessment considers: age, maturity, mental capacity, understanding of the treatment and its implications, and ability to make and communicate a reasoned decision. Children aged 16+ are generally presumed to have capacity (assessed under the Mental Capacity Act). If a child is deemed Gillick competent, parents are not informed unless there are serious welfare concerns. Used in medical settings, therapeutic support, and living arrangement decisions. Applied in eating disorder contexts where malnourishment may impair cognitive capacity to appraise severity.
