# Structural MRI (sMRI)

Static anatomical images of the brain. Used to detect structural abnormalities, tumours, lesions.

- **Strengths**: high spatial resolution, superior soft-tissue contrast, non-invasive (no ionising radiation).
- **Limitations**: time-consuming, static (no function), low sensitivity to subtle changes.

Average scan produces ~4–6 million **voxels** (3D pixels).
