# Moral Distress

Sense of unease, fear, or stress experienced by healthcare professionals when they feel ethically conflicted about the quality of care they can give. Can progress to severe mental health conditions including depression and PTSD.

## Causes
- Lack of agency in making best decisions
- Insufficient / non-existent resources
- Practice conflicts with ethical standards
- Being involved in wrongdoing
- End-of-life care decisions

## UK BMA survey findings
- 43.8 % had never heard of moral distress
- 78.4 % reported experiencing it at work once defined
