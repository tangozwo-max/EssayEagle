# Attribution Theory (Stigma)

The *perceived cause* of a stigmatised identity shapes stigmatiser responses.

## Three features
- **Locus of control** — internal (within the person) vs external (outside causes)
- **Stability** — perceived as stable or changeable
- **Controllability** — can the individual manage / control the condition?

## Two types of responsibility
- **Onset responsibility** — agency in developing the condition
- **Offset responsibility** — perceived ability to manage recovery

More perceived responsibility → stronger stigma. **Biogenetic explanations** challenge onset responsibility — but can raise perceived dangerousness (see [[neuroscience-stigma-complication]] implications discussed under [[stigma-reduction-interventions]]).
