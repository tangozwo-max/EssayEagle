# Emotionally Based School Avoidance (EBSA)

EBSA is a developing concept referring to difficulties attending school due to emotional distress, particularly anxiety. It is not a psychiatric diagnosis but frequently co-occurs with anxiety and mood disorders. It involves a cyclical process — avoidance reinforces anxiety. Reasons for avoidance include: escaping uncomfortable feelings (anxiety, low mood), avoiding stressors (academic demands, social pressures), pursuing outside interests, or reducing separation anxiety. School absence rates doubled after the COVID-19 pandemic. Autistic young people are at disproportionately elevated risk due to anxiety and sensory/social demands of school environments.
