# Cannabis

A plant of the *Cannabaceae* family containing hundreds of biologically active compounds. The most pharmacologically potent are **delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC)** and **cannabidiol (CBD)**. Cannabis is one of the most widely used substances globally; adolescents are the most common users.

## Epidemiology (US data, CDC)
- ~19% of Americans used cannabis in 2021
- ~3 in 10 users develop cannabis use disorder (CUD)
- Risk of CUD is greater for people who start before age 18
- Long-term or frequent use is linked to psychosis and schizophrenia
- Cannabis use during pregnancy increases risk of complications

## Mechanism of action

THC primarily binds to **CB1 receptors** throughout the CNS (also lungs, liver, kidneys). CBD binds more to **CB2 receptors** (involved in immune responses, less in brain reward).

THC modulates the **G-protein-coupled inhibition of cAMP** (similar mechanism to opioids). It also modulates transmission of:
- Endocannabinoids
- GABA
- Glutamate
- Serotonin

This alters the brain's network for self-awareness: reducing anxiety, impairing memory, affecting motivation and personal experience.

Cannabis binds to **lipids**, remaining detectable in the body long after use (especially in high body fat individuals). This makes withdrawal a **slow-onset** phenomenon.

Heavy use likely produces a lower dopamine response compared to other substances.

## Addiction cycle for cannabis

| Stage | Brain region | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Binge/intoxication | Nucleus accumbens (basal ganglia) | Dopamine release → euphoria, relaxation |
| Withdrawal/negative affect | Extended amygdala | Irritability, anxiety, mood swings |
| Preoccupation/anticipation | Prefrontal cortex | Altered activity/connectivity; impaired executive function, cravings |
