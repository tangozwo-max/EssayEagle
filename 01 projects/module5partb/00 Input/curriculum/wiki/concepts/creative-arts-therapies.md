# Creative Arts Therapies (for Addiction)

A cluster of therapeutic approaches that use creative and physical modalities — music, art, exercise, yoga, dance, and acupuncture — as adjuncts to standard addiction treatment. They address aspects of substance use disorder that verbal therapies may not reach, including nonverbal emotional expression, body awareness, and stress regulation.

## Music therapy
Delivered by registered music therapists; uses music instead of language as the primary therapeutic medium. A 2022 Cochrane report found music therapy added to as-normal care **reduced substance craving** in people with SUD. Useful for those who are nonverbal or struggle with language-based communication.

## Art therapy
Used with SUD populations since the 1950s. Benefits identified include:
- Decreasing denial
- Supporting emotional expression and communication
- Facilitating group discussions
- Motivating behaviour change
- Moving people from contemplation to action on the [[transtheoretical-model]]

## Exercise
Inpatient and outpatient studies (Patterson et al.) show significant improvements in abstinence days and reduced cravings when exercise is included alongside standard addiction treatment.

## Yoga
Small studies support Hatha yoga as an adjunct to SUD treatment (Brooks et al.), particularly for women; larger studies are needed to confirm efficacy.

## Acupuncture
Emerging evidence suggests acupuncture modulates the [[mesolimbic-pathway]] dopamine system and reduces drug-seeking behaviours (Lee et al.). Results for alcohol use disorder also promising (Shin et al.) but require larger, more rigorous trials.
