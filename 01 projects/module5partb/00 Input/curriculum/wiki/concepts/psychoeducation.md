# Psychoeducation

Teaching service users with MH conditions and their families about the nature of the condition. First used in 1980 (Anderson et al., schizophrenia family treatment).

## 4 Elements
1. **Briefing** — etiology, symptoms, course, prognosis, early relapse signs, dispel myths
2. **Problem-solving training** — coping with the situation
3. **Communication training** — coordinating with treatment team; dos/don'ts for family
4. **Self-assertiveness training** — informed choices between treatment options

## Types
- Individual (tailored, flexible, private; more time-intensive)
- Group (~8 participants, 45–60 min weekly; peer support, cost-effective)
- Family (6–12 sessions; especially important in cultures where MH patients stay with family)
- Community (collaborative effort; reduces stigma)

## Example: Five Ways to Wellbeing
Evidence-based psychoeducation for mild anxiety/depression: Connect, Be Active, Take Notice, Keep Learning, Give.
