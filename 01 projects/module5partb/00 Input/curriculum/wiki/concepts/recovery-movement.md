# Recovery Movement

Originated in the **USA in the 1970s–80s** from people with lived experience. Core claim: mental illness need not permanently impede life goals. Contrasts with medical-model assumptions of poor prognosis.

## Key insight from PWLE
Kindness, hope-inspiring relationships, and support for everyday solutions matter more than technical skills or qualifications.

## Adoption
Common in UK, Ireland, New Zealand, Canada, USA. Gaining in Northern Europe. Developing in India and some African nations. In 2007 the Royal College of Psychiatrists invited members to adopt recovery as a common purpose.

## Framework
Commonly [[chime-framework]] (Connectedness, Hope & optimism, Identity, Meaning, Empowerment).
