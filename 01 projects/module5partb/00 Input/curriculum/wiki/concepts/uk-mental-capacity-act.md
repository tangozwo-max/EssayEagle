# UK Mental Capacity Act (2005)

Protects decision-making rights of vulnerable people. **Capacity is decision- and time-specific** — no-one has permanent lack of capacity for all decisions.

## Four rights
1. People aged 16+ with capacity have the right to make their own decisions (exception: detained under MHA).
2. Capacity is **assumed** unless assessed otherwise.
3. Person must be supported to decide before assumed incapable. Unpopular decisions ≠ lack of capacity.
4. Decisions made for someone must be in their best interests and **least restrictive**.

## Two-stage capacity test
- Stage 1: Is there an impairment of / disturbance in the functioning of the mind or brain?
- Stage 2: Is the impairment sufficient that the person cannot make *this particular* decision?

A person lacks capacity for a decision if unable to: **understand · remember · weigh up · communicate**.

## Three decision categories
- Health and welfare
- Financial
- Advance

## Decisions that CAN'T be made for someone else
Marriage, civil partnership, divorce, sexual relationships, adoption, voting, consent to fertility treatment.
