# Historical Mental-Health Treatments

Techniques used in the asylum era and mid-20th century — mostly obsolete or highly regulated today.

| Treatment | Mechanism | Status today |
|---|---|---|
| **Insulin shock therapy** | Insulin-induced coma | Abandoned |
| **Hydrotherapy** | Water immersion, spraying | Rarely used |
| **Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)** | Induced seizure under anaesthesia | Regulated use — severe, treatment-resistant depression |
| **Lobotomy** | Surgical severing of PFC connections | Abandoned |
| **Restraint and seclusion** | Physical confinement | Tightly regulated, last resort |

## Lesson
Illustrates the risk of widespread use without adequate evidence — central to modern insistence on [[evidence-based-practice]].
