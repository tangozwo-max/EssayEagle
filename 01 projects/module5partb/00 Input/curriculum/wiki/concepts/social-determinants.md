# Social Determinants of Health

Social determinants of health are the non-medical factors that influence health outcomes, including the conditions in which people are born, grow, live, work, and age. These factors shape access to health services, resilience, and risk of developing mental health conditions.

## Key domains

Social determinants include:
- **Economic stability**: income, employment, financial security, housing stability
- **Education**: access to quality education and literacy
- **Social and community context**: social connections, community participation, exposure to discrimination
- **Healthcare access**: availability and affordability of mental and physical health services
- **Neighbourhood and built environment**: safety, environmental quality, access to healthy resources

## Mental health relevance

In the continuum of need framework, individuals who have additional needs related to social determinants of health are identified as candidates for selective and indicated (targeted) early help interventions. Social determinants often create inequalities in mental health outcomes and access to care.

## Health equity perspective

Understanding social determinants is essential for addressing health disparities and ensuring that public mental health interventions reach those most in need.
