# ACE Score

A **0–10 numeric score** summarising how many categories of [[adverse-childhood-experiences]] a person experienced during childhood and adolescence. Each "yes" = 1 point.

## Dose-response evidence (per Stop Abuse Campaign table)

| Outcome | ACE = 0 | ACE = 4 | ACE > 4 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alcoholism | 2.5 % | 11 % | ~4× increase |
| Severe depression | 15 % ♀ / 10 % ♂ | 42 % ♀ / 30 % ♂ | ~3× increase |
| Problems with working | 5 % | 15 % | ~3× increase |
| Financial problems | 10 % | 20 % | ~2× increase |
| Heart disease | 3.7 % | 5.6 % | ~2× increase |
| Stroke | 2.6 % | 4.1 % | ~2× increase |

**Takeaway:** Clear dose-response relationship — the higher the ACE score, the higher the probability of chronic depression, SUD, financial problems, CVD, stroke, and functional impairment.

## Why it matters
- Simple, rapid screening tool used in primary care and trauma-informed practice.
- Does NOT predict individual outcomes — many people with high ACE scores show strong [[resilience]].
