# Clinical Approach in CAMH

The standard clinical process in child and adolescent mental health follows four stages: **Assessment** (clinical interviews, questionnaires, reports from significant others, observations, neuropsychological tests); **Formulation** (holistic sense-making integrating biological, psychological, and social factors into a narrative); **Intervention** (tailored plan including therapy, medication, school accommodations); and **Evaluation** (ongoing monitoring using questionnaires, with adjustment of the plan as needed). The approach must be developmentally appropriate and is fundamentally different from adult mental health care — children are embedded in family, school, and community systems.
