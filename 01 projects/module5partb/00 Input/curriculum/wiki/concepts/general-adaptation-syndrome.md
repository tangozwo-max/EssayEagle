# General Adaptation Syndrome (Hans Selye, 1936)

Three-stage model of the body's response to stress. Austrian endocrinologist Hans Selye — "father of stress research" — introduced GAS at McGill University based on animal studies.

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| **1. Alarm** | Fight-or-flight initiation; adrenal glands release stress hormones; ↑ HR, BP, energy; sweating, alertness. |
| **2. Resistance** | Defences engage; body adapts to prolonged stressor. Hormones, HR still elevated. Symptoms: irritability, frustration, poor concentration. |
| **3. Exhaustion** | Resources deplete; body's resistance declines. Fatigue, burnout, depression, anxiety, decreased stress tolerance. |
