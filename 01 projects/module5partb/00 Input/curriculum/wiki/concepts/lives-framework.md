# LIVES Framework

WHO framework for creating psychologically safe spaces and delivering trauma-informed care. Prevents retraumatization.

## Components
| Letter | Meaning | Key actions |
|---|---|---|
| **L** | Listen | Empathically, nonjudgmentally; watch for non-verbal stress; use professional interpreter |
| **I** | Inquire | Gentle open questions about injuries, relationships, unspoken trauma; strength-based acknowledgment |
| **V** | Validate | Counteract invalidation; reflect that what happened was not their fault |
| **E** | Enhance safety | Psychological and physical safety before history-taking; respect personal space, give control |
| **S** | Support | Ongoing connection; regular appointments; connect to community support |

## Key principles
- Safety FIRST — before detailed history
- Avoid probing deeply into trauma details (risk of retraumatization)
- Use validated, self-administered checklists where possible (e.g., THQ, PCL-5)
