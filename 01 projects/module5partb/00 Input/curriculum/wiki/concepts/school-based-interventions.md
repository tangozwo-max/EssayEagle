# School-Based Interventions

School-based interventions are mental health promotion and prevention programs delivered within school settings, reaching large populations of children and young people. Schools are ideal settings for promoting mental health and preventing mental health difficulties because they are universal institutions reaching most young people.

## Types of school-based interventions

### Universal interventions
- Social-emotional learning curricula
- Mental health literacy and awareness programs
- Substance use prevention programs
- Health and wellbeing lessons

### Selective interventions
- Support groups for specific risk groups
- Peer support programs
- Mentoring programs

### Indicated interventions
- Early intervention programs for students showing signs of difficulty
- School attendance support programs
- Counselling and mental health services

## Key features

Effective school-based interventions:
- Are embedded within whole-school approaches
- Engage teachers, students, parents, and wider community
- Build social and emotional skills
- Promote mental health literacy
- Create supportive school environments
- Address bullying, violence, and peer difficulties

## Mental health promotion role

School-based interventions contribute to mental health promotion by:
- Building protective factors (social skills, resilience, coping)
- Creating supportive environments
- Increasing mental health awareness and literacy
- Supporting early identification of difficulties
