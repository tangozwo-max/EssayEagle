# Werther and Papageno Effects

Two contrasting effects of media coverage on suicide:

## Werther Effect
**Media contagion / copycat suicide**. Named after Goethe's *The Sorrows of Young Werther* (1774) — wave of European suicides followed its publication. Sensationalized/glorified suicide depictions in media increase suicide rates.

## Papageno Effect
**Protective media effect**. Stories showing people overcoming suicidal crises (as Papageno does in Mozart's *The Magic Flute*) may reduce suicide rates. Promotes hope and alternative coping.

## Clinical implication
Media reporting guidelines on suicide exist specifically to mitigate Werther Effect while leveraging Papageno Effect.
