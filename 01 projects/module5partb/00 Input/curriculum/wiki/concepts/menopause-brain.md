# Menopause and the Brain (Mosconi)

Neurological transition reshaping the female brain during midlife. Key findings (Mosconi et al. 2021):

- Perimenopause: gray matter volume dips.
- Estrogen helps regulate serotonin, norepinephrine, dopamine.
- Neurogenesis significantly reduced during perimenopause.
- Post-menopause: observed recovery of gray matter volume — brain has adaptive mechanisms.
- Parallel to pregnancy: neurogenesis in precuneus decreases then recovers by weaning.

## Clinical implications
- Antidepressants should NOT be first-line for menopausal low mood (UK Menopause Charity).
- Estrogen-receptor-based therapies (HRT) often more appropriate.
