# Parenting Training

Parenting training programs are evidence-based interventions that teach parents and caregivers skills for promoting healthy child development and managing behavioural difficulties. They are based on social learning approaches and have been shown to be effective for reducing disruptive, aggressive, and oppositional behaviours.

## Key components

- **Behavioural management skills**: strategies for responding to child behaviour
- **Communication strategies**: promoting positive parent-child interactions
- **Problem-solving**: teaching parents structured approaches to challenges
- **Emotional support**: providing guidance and validation
- **Skills practice**: opportunities to practice and refine new skills

## Theoretical basis

Parenting training programs are based on social learning theory, which proposes that child behaviour is shaped by parent-child interactions and environmental contingencies.

## Effectiveness

Research shows parenting training can:
- Reduce aggressive, disruptive, and oppositional behaviour in children
- Prevent mental health disorders such as depression and anxiety
- Promote positive mental health
- Improve parent-child relationships
- Support school attendance and academic engagement

## Applications

Parenting training is used for:
- Children with behavioural difficulties
- Prevention of conduct problems
- Parenting support programs in universal settings
- Family-based multimodal interventions
