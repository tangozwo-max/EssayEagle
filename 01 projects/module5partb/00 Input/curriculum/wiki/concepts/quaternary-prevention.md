# Quaternary Prevention

Based on the Hippocratic principle: **primum non nocere** (first, do no harm). Involves:

1. Identifying those at risk of **excessive medical treatment**
2. Protecting them from unnecessary new medical procedures
3. Recommending ethically acceptable interventions

## Mental health application
Avoiding **medicalization of normal human experiences** (e.g., grief):
- Labeling grief as MDD overrides individual and cultural responses to loss
- Undermines the person's sense of agency
- Leads to unnecessary medication with side effects
- Risk of negative drug interactions

## Case: Shirley (M2 W5)
Shirley lost her mother and is experiencing depressive symptoms. Quaternary prevention: validate grief, support natural coping (time with family), avoid medicating natural bereavement. "No set duration for grief."
