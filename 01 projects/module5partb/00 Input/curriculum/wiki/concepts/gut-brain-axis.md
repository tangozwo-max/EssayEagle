# Gut-Brain Axis

**Two-way communication system** between the gastrointestinal (GI) tract and the central nervous system (CNS).

## Key components
- **Serotonin (5HT)**: mediates GI and CNS functions. ~95% of the body's serotonin is in the gut.
- **Enteric Nervous System (ENS)**: "second brain" — network of neurons in GI lining, operates autonomously
- **Vagus Nerve (CNX, Cranial Nerve X)**: longest nerve; carries signals bidirectionally between gut and brain
- **Gut microbiota**: trillions of bacteria, viruses, fungi; produce metabolites that cross the gut barrier and influence neurological function

## Clinical relevance
- IBS, CFS/ME may involve gut-brain dysregulation
- Serotonergic pathways shared between gut and mood regulation
- Target for novel therapeutic approaches (psychobiotics, dietary interventions)
