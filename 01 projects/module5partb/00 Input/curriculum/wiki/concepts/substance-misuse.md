# Substance Misuse

Substance misuse occurs when a person uses a substance in a way that causes harm to themselves and/or others around them, deviating from accepted medical or social norms. Examples include taking more opioids than prescribed or frequently drinking alcohol to excess. Misuse can potentially lead to the development of a [[substance-use-disorder]], but it does not automatically indicate one is present.

## Key distinctions
- Misuse refers to *behavior* (how a substance is used) rather than a diagnostic state
- Physical [[dependency]] can exist without misuse (e.g., when medications are taken as prescribed)
- Misuse is distinct from — but can precede — [[substance-use-disorder]]
- Term is preferred in public health contexts; "abuse" is considered stigmatizing
