# Revised Child Anxiety and Depression Scale (RCADS)

The RCADS is a screening instrument designed to evaluate a range of anxiety and depression disorders in children and young people aged 8–18 years.

## Key characteristics

- **Age range**: 8–18 years
- **Scope**: evaluates anxiety and depression symptoms
- **Two versions**: youth self-report and parent report available
- **Standardised**: validated with established cut-off scores
- **Condition-specific**: particularly useful for screening anxiety and mood disorders

## Administration

The RCADS can be completed by:
- Children and young people (self-report)
- Parents (parent report)

## Subscales

The RCADS measures several anxiety and depression domains including:
- Separation anxiety
- Social phobia
- Generalised anxiety
- Panic disorder
- Major depression

## Clinical use

RCADS scores above clinical cut-offs indicate possible anxiety or depressive disorders requiring further assessment and potential intervention.

## Advantages

- Condition-specific (anxiety and depression)
- Both youth and parent perspectives available
- Established validity and reliability
