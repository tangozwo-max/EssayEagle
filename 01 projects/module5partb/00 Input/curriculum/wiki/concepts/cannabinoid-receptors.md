# Cannabinoid Receptors (CB1 / CB2)

G-protein-coupled receptors that form the primary molecular targets for both endogenous cannabinoids (endocannabinoids) and exogenous cannabinoids such as THC. Part of the **endocannabinoid system**, which regulates pain, mood, appetite, memory, nausea, and sexual activity.

## CB1 Receptors

- **Distribution:** Throughout the CNS; also found in lungs, liver, and kidneys
- **Primary ligand (exogenous):** THC (delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol)
- **Signalling:** Gi-coupled → inhibits adenylyl cyclase → **reduces cAMP** (similar to D2 dopamine receptors and opioid receptors)
- **Effects when activated:** Pain reduction, memory impairment, altered mood and appetite, motor effects, euphoria
- **Addiction relevance:** Primary mediator of THC's reinforcing and addictive properties
- **Cell types:** Affects microglial cells more than neurons; astrocytes may regulate endocannabinoid turnover

## CB2 Receptors

- **Distribution:** Primarily peripheral immune system; expressed to a lesser extent in the brain
- **Primary ligand (exogenous):** CBD (cannabidiol) — though CBD has low direct affinity for CB2; mainly modulates the receptor indirectly
- **Effects when activated:** Immune modulation, anti-inflammatory effects; less involved in psychoactive effects

## Endocannabinoid system overview

The endocannabinoid system includes:
- **Endocannabinoids:** Anandamide (AEA), 2-arachidonoylglycerol (2-AG) — produced on-demand from lipid precursors
- **CB1 and CB2 receptors**
- **Degrading enzymes:** FAAH (fatty acid amide hydrolase), MAGL

Cannabis binds to lipids and therefore remains detectable long after use — making cannabis withdrawal a slow-onset phenomenon.
