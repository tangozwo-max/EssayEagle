# Contingency Management (CM)

A behavioural intervention for substance use disorder that provides tangible incentives — typically vouchers or cash prizes — to reward abstinence or adherence to treatment. CM is grounded in operant conditioning: desired behaviours (abstinence, attendance) are positively reinforced with immediate, tangible rewards.

## Evidence base
- Significant evidence base across a range of populations and substance types
- In the short term, CM tends to confer more benefits than talking therapies or mindfulness for reducing substance use
- Long-term efficacy after reward cessation is less certain — a key limitation

## Mechanism
CM exploits the same reward pathways that addictive substances hijack, but with non-drug reinforcers. The immediacy of rewards counters the impulsivity and delay discounting common in SUD.

## Implementation
Typically used alongside other interventions (not standalone). Requires:
- Clear, measurable target behaviour (verified abstinence via urine drug screen)
- Immediate delivery of reward on target met
- Escalating reward schedules for sustained compliance

## Limitations
- Long-term effects after programme ends are uncertain (Rains et al.)
- Cost of providing incentives can limit widespread implementation
- Not universally accepted — ethical debates around "paying people to not use drugs"
