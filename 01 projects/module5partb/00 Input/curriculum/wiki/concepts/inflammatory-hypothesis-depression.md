# Inflammatory (Cytokine) Hypothesis of Depression

Chronic, low-grade inflammation plays a role in establishment, progression, and maintenance of depression. People with depression show elevated CRP, IL-6, TNF-α.

## Associated signs
- Autoimmune disorders, chronic infections, inflammatory bowel disease
- Lack of response to antidepressants
- Fatigue, joint pain

## Mechanistic links
Inflammation influences NT activity, oxidative stress, mitochondrial activity, and HPA-axis activity.

## Risk factors
Childhood abuse/trauma, chronic psychosocial stress, poor diet, low activity, obesity, CVD, digestive disorders.
