# Neurasthenia

A 19th-century diagnostic category describing chronic fatigue, weakness, and nervous exhaustion — coined by American neurologist George Beard (1880). Attributed to the demands of modern industrialised life depleting "nervous energy."

Largely replaced in Western psychiatry by specific diagnoses (MDD, anxiety disorders, CFS). Still used in ICD-11 (F48.0) and in some Asian clinical traditions (notably China, Japan) to describe medically unexplained fatigue with somatic features.

Historically significant as it illustrates how cultural context shapes psychiatric classification.
