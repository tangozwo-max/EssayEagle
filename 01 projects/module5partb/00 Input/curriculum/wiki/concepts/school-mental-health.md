# School Mental Health

Schools are the primary environment for children after home, and school staff are well-placed to detect early mental health signs. Mental health and learning have a bidirectional relationship. In a typical UK classroom of 30, ~5 have a mental health disorder, ~7 have self-harmed, ~11 have been bullied. School mental health work operates at multiple system levels (Bronfenbrenner's ecological model) and includes a team in the school (educational psychologists, SLT, SENDCO, school nurses) and a team around the school (CAMHS, social services, charities). Interventions can be indirect (consultation, reflective practice, staff training) or direct (SEL, individual therapy, group approaches).
