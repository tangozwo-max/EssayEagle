# Spectrum of Interventions

The spectrum of interventions refers to the full range of mental health intervention options available across the continuum of need, from universal services available to all populations through to specialist clinical interventions for those with complex needs.

## Components

The spectrum includes:
- **Universal interventions**: services and health promotion available to all without assessment of need
- **Selective interventions**: targeted services for those identified as at risk
- **Indicated interventions**: early interventions for those showing early signs of difficulty
- **Specialist interventions**: intensive clinical services for those with diagnosed, severe mental health conditions

## Purposes

Different parts of the spectrum serve different purposes:
- Promoting mental health and well-being across the population
- Preventing escalation of mental health difficulties
- Treating diagnosed mental health conditions
- Supporting recovery and long-term management

## Clinical reasoning

Matching individuals to the appropriate level of the spectrum requires assessment of their current needs, risk status, protective factors, and available resources. People may move between levels as their circumstances change.
