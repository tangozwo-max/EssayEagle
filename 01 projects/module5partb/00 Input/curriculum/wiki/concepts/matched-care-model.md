# Matched Care Model

A service delivery framework developed in Scotland, grounded in the Psychological Therapies Matrix, that allocates the right level and intensity of intervention from the outset of treatment rather than starting at the lowest level and escalating only after failure.

## Contrast with Stepped Care

| Feature | Stepped Care | Matched Care |
|---|---|---|
| Starting point | Least intensive intervention | Appropriate level from start |
| Escalation | Move up after failure | Allocated to correct level immediately |
| Efficiency | May delay effective treatment | Better initial fit of resource to need |

## Foundation
All service delivery should be grounded in **psychologically informed care** delivered by all clinical and care staff — reflecting the recommendation that addiction services be both psychologically informed and [[trauma-informed-care]].

## Three elements of care planning
When building an individual care plan, three elements must be explicitly addressed:
1. **Intervention** — what therapeutic or pharmacological work is being delivered
2. **Practitioner** — which professional or peer worker delivers it
3. **Situation** — the setting and context (inpatient, outpatient, community, peer support, third sector)
