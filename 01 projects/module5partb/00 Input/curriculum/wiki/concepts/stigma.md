# Stigma

An attribute that is **deeply discrediting**, resulting in the stigmatized person being seen as tainted and disregarded by society (Goffman; Sheehan et al.). A socially constructed, multi-level phenomenon with individual, interpersonal, structural, and cultural dimensions.

## Core triad (social-cognitive)
- **Stereotypes** — cognitive generalisations about a group
- **Prejudice** — emotional reactions arising from agreement with stereotypes
- **Discrimination** — behaviour arising from stereotypes and prejudice

## In M5 context — Addiction Stigma
M5 W1 highlights the historical and ongoing stigma around addiction and substance use:
- Terms like "addict" and "drug abuser" are considered unacceptable among healthcare agencies
- "Dependency" and "substance use" cause confusion and sometimes inadvertent stigma
- For ~100 years, addiction was treated as a moral failing requiring punishment, not healthcare
- Neurobiological research (Volkow, NIDA) has reframed addiction as a medical condition, but social stigma persists
- Stigma causes people who misuse alcohol to minimize the issue; building non-judgmental, empathetic relationships is essential for treatment engagement
