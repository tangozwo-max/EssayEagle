# Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD)

A range of lifelong neurodevelopmental conditions resulting from prenatal exposure to alcohol. FASD affects brain development and structure, leading to variable cognitive, motor, behavioral, and adaptive difficulties depending on timing and amount of exposure.

## Causation and variability

FASD occurs when a mother consumes alcohol during pregnancy. The specific effects depend on:

- **Timing** — which brain structures were developing during alcohol exposure
- **Quantity and frequency** — amount of alcohol consumed
- **Maternal factors** — nutrition, metabolism, genetics
- **Fetal factors** — genetics, alcohol metabolism

## Diagnostic features

### Facial characteristics
- Short palpebral fissure (eye opening)
- Thin upper lip
- Smooth philtrum (groove between nose and mouth)

### Neurodevelopmental impacts
- Deficits in general intelligence
- Motor skill difficulties
- Language delays
- Academic achievement problems
- Memory and attention difficulties
- Emotional regulation challenges
- Adaptive behavior deficits

### Associated difficulties
- Hyperactivity and impulsivity
- Social cognition problems
- Communication difficulties

## Important notes

The developing brain is extremely vulnerable to teratogens (toxic substances like alcohol, certain medications, drugs, tobacco). While the evidence for alcohol is strong, other teratogens may lack consistent evidence bases.

## Mental health risk

FASD represents a direct risk factor for mental health difficulties due to the neurodevelopmental impairments it causes, as well as through social and environmental factors that may accompany the condition.
