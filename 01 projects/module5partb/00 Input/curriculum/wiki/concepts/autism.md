# Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

A neurodevelopmental condition characterized by differences in social communication and the presence of restricted, repetitive patterns of behaviour, interests, or activities. Autism is a lifelong neurodevelopmental difference affecting social interaction, communication, and behavioural regulation.

## Social and psychological factors affecting autistic mental health

### Acceptance and identity
- Autistic people need to feel accepted for their authentic selves
- Judgment and social pressure can affect self-worth
- Intense interests bring joy and expertise — pursuing them is protective for mental health

### Social connection
- Access to positive relationships within family and community is essential
- Autistic people may face stigma, discrimination, and social adversity
- Educational and employment settings must be supportive to prevent mental health consequences

### Masking
- Suppressing autistic traits to appear non-autistic requires considerable effort and energy
- Chronic masking is associated with anxiety and depression
- Recognition of masking as a barrier to mental health (rather than a positive coping strategy) is important

## Biological factors

### Sensory differences
- Atypical sensory processing requiring acceptance and accommodation
- Stimming (repetitive movements/actions) provides self-regulation and emotional expression
- Sensory needs should be respected rather than suppressed

### Physical health comorbidities
- Co-occurring physical health conditions complicate mental health risks
- Healthcare integration is important

## Mental health support

Supportive environments in schools and workplaces, acceptance of sensory differences, and encouragement to pursue autistic interests are essential for mental wellbeing.
