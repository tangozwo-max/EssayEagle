# Whole School Approach (WSA) to Mental Health

The whole school approach (WSA) means developing and implementing policies and practices to promote mental health for pupils, parents/caregivers, and staff alike. Public Health England and the Department for Education identified eight principles: ethos and environment, curriculum/teaching/learning, student voice, staff development, identifying need and monitoring impact, working with parents and carers, targeted support, and leadership/management. WHO's Health Promoting Schools model covers formal curriculum, school ethos, and community engagement. NICE endorses trauma-informed and neuro-affirming practices. Mental Health Support Teams (MHSTs) were introduced in the UK to provide early intervention and support WSA implementation.
