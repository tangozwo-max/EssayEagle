# Harm Reduction

A public health approach to substance use that aims to reduce the negative health, social, and economic consequences of drug use without necessarily requiring abstinence. Harm reduction acknowledges that some people will continue to use substances and focuses on minimizing the associated risks.

## Examples of harm reduction services
- **Needle exchange programs** — provision of sterile injecting equipment to prevent transmission of blood-borne infections (HIV, hepatitis C)
- Safe consumption sites / supervised injection facilities
- Naloxone distribution to reverse opioid overdoses
- Drug checking services

## In the continuum of SUD services (M5 context)
Harm reduction sits alongside — not in opposition to — other treatment approaches. The full spectrum of substance use care includes:
- Detoxification services (supervised withdrawal)
- Intensive inpatient / residential treatment
- Behavioral therapies and counseling (e.g., CBT)
- Group therapy (Alcoholics Anonymous, Narcotics Anonymous)
- Family therapy
- Harm reduction services
- Holistic and complementary therapies
