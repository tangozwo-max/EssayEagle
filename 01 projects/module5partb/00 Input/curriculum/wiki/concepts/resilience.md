# Resilience

Resilience refers to the capacity to maintain or regain well-being and positive mental health despite facing adversity, stress, or risk. It is a dynamic process involving the interaction of individual characteristics, relationships, and environmental supports.

## Key understanding

Rather than an individual trait to be "built" in response to adversity, resilience is best understood as emerging from a supportive environment that minimises the need for resilience in the first place. Public mental health goals include promoting resilience through strengthening protective factors at individual, family, community, and societal levels.

## Components

Resilience involves:
- **Emotional regulation**: ability to manage emotional responses to stress
- **Problem-solving**: capacity to identify and implement solutions
- **Social support**: access to meaningful relationships and community
- **Sense of purpose**: connection to values and meaningful goals
- **Adaptability**: flexibility in response to changing circumstances
