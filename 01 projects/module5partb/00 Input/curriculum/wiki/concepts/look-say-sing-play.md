# Look, Say, Sing, Play

Look, Say, Sing, Play is an NSPCC campaign and resource suite designed to help parents and carers engage in brain-building activities with babies and toddlers. The framework elegantly captures four dimensions of everyday interactions that, through their simplicity and accessibility, empower parents to understand themselves as crucial contributors to their child's neural development. Each element addresses a distinct domain of development whilst emphasising that sophisticated brain-building requires neither specialist equipment nor extensive time—only attentive, responsive engagement.

## Look: Building Attention and Attachment

Looking—making sustained eye contact and observing the child—establishes the foundation of attachment. Eye contact triggers the release of oxytocin and strengthens the parent-child bond. Observing the child's cues and responses enables attunement and responsive caregiving. When parents "look" at their children—noticing subtle signals of need, distress, interest—they communicate worth and importance. This attentional focus builds neural circuits supporting attention, social engagement, and emotional security.

## Say: Supporting Language and Communication

Talking to infants and young children, even before they understand words, provides rich linguistic input essential for language development. Speaking to children about their experiences, naming emotions, explaining cause-and-effect, and engaging in turn-taking conversation builds vocabulary, grammar, and communication skills. Parent narration of daily activities ("Now we're changing your nappy. You're kicking your legs!") provides labelled experience that facilitates learning and creates shared meaning.

## Sing: Enhancing Memory and Word Learning

Singing to children combines melody, rhythm, and language in ways that enhance memory consolidation and word learning. Songs provide repeated, patterned exposure to language in emotionally engaging contexts. The rhythm and melody of songs are particularly memorable, and singing together creates moments of joy and connection. Research demonstrates that children learn vocabulary more readily through songs than through non-musical speech.

## Play: Stimulating Imagination and Problem-Solving

Play is the medium through which children learn. Through play, children explore cause-and-effect, develop problem-solving skills, engage imagination, and practise social interactions. Parent-child play, particularly responsive play wherein parents follow the child's lead and interests, builds executive function, creative thinking, and a secure sense that exploration is safe and valued.

## Integration and Accessibility

Look, Say, Sing, Play is effective precisely because it is accessible. These activities require no special materials, no classes, no expense. They are woven naturally into daily routines—during nappy changing, meal preparation, bath time, and outings. By framing ordinary parental interactions as brain-building, the campaign affirms parents' crucial role and removes barriers to engagement.
