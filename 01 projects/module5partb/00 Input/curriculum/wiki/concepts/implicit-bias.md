# Implicit Bias

**Unintentional, automatic associations** between members of social groups (sex, ethnicity, etc.) and certain traits (e.g., intelligence, dangerousness). Healthcare providers show the same level of implicit bias as the general population.

## Documented effects in mental health
- Affects patient–provider interactions, diagnostic processes, health outcomes (Sashidharan et al.).
- Men perceived as more aggressive than women → longer duration of coercive measures.
- Black men disproportionately associated with "threatening" → higher coercion rates.
- People with psychosis falsely associated with dangerousness.
