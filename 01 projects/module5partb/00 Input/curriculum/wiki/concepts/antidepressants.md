# Antidepressants

Drug treatments that work by **increasing the activity of neurotransmitters** (mainly serotonin and noradrenaline) in the brain.

## Types
| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **SSRIs** | Block serotonin reabsorption | Fluoxetine, sertraline, escitalopram, paroxetine, citalopram, fluvoxamine |
| **SNRIs** | Block serotonin + noradrenaline reuptake | Venlafaxine, duloxetine |
| **TCAs** | Block serotonin + noradrenaline; older | Amitriptyline |

## Prescribing guidance (WHO mhGAP)
- Start at **lowest therapeutic dose**
- Weekly/fortnightly reviews; titrate dose upward if needed
- Full response expected: **4–6 weeks**
- Once symptoms resolve: continue for **6–9 months**
- Stopping: gradual reduction, shared decision

## Contraindications
- NOT if symptoms < 2 weeks or not impairing
- NOT in recent bereavement/major loss
- NOT as first-line in pregnancy/breastfeeding
- NOT in children <12; caution in adolescents 12-18
