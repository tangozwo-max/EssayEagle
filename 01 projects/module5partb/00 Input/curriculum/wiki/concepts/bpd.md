# Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)

BPD is a psychiatric diagnosis characterised by four symptom domains: emotional instability (affective dysregulation), altered patterns of thinking or perception, impulsive behaviour, and intense/unstable interpersonal relationships. It is controversial due to: associated stigma, high prevalence of trauma histories in those diagnosed, insufficient attention to social context, and historical critiques of misogynistic pathologising of women's distress. DBT was originally developed for adults with BPD. Diagnosing BPD in under-18s is debated: advocates argue early diagnosis enables support and understanding; critics note difficulty confirming pervasiveness, overlap with normal adolescent development, and risk of self-stigma.
