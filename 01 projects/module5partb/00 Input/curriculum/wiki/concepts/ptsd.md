# PTSD — Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder

The **only DSM-5 diagnosis with a known single cause** — exposure to trauma. ~70 % of people worldwide experience trauma; only ~5.6 % develop PTSD.

## Prevalence
- Veterans: ~7 % lifetime
- General population: ~6 %
- Female veterans: ~13 %; male veterans: ~6 %
- Females > males globally (linked to interpersonal violence exposure)

## Leading cause
**Motor vehicle accidents** (general population).

## Neurobiology
- ↓ Medial PFC activity → failure to inhibit amygdala
- ↑ Amygdala activity → hyperactive fear, hypervigilance
- ↓ Hippocampal volume (may be pre-exposure risk factor, not just consequence)
- ↑ Autonomic reactivity to trauma cues
- Chronic HPA dysregulation
- Genetic vulnerability: 30–72 % heritability
