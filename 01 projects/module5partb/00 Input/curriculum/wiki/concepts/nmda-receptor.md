# NMDA Receptor

N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor. Glutamate receptor acting as a **controlled-access gate** requiring *two* simultaneous signals to open:
1. **Glutamate binding**
2. **Voltage change** — postsynaptic cell must be depolarised

When open, allows Na⁺ and Ca²⁺ to enter. Important for **long-term changes: learning and memory**.

Ketamine is a **non-competitive antagonist** of NMDA (channel blocker acting at an allosteric site).
