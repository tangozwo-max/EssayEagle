# Mental Health Promotion

Mental health promotion involves intervening to optimise positive mental health by addressing determinants of positive mental health before a specific mental health condition has been identified. The ultimate goal is to improve overall positive mental health of the population.

## Key characteristics

- **Proactive approach**: enhances well-being, resilience, and protective factors before mental health conditions arise
- **Population-focused**: aims to benefit entire populations or communities, not just at-risk individuals
- **Strengths-based**: builds on strengths and positive capacities rather than focusing solely on risk reduction
- **Holistic**: addresses multiple determinants including social support, coping skills, lifestyle choices, and healthy environments

## Methods for promoting mental health

- Providing early childhood interventions supporting families through pregnancy and early life
- Supporting children to develop needed skills (social, emotional, coping)
- Supporting vulnerable groups (minorities, Indigenous peoples, refugees) with targeted programs
- Creating school-based programs promoting mental health
- Creating programs preventing antisocial behaviour and violence
- Supporting community development and social connections

## Youth development programs

Effective youth development programs:
- Provide opportunities to build life skills while fostering positive relationships
- Recognise and build on strengths demonstrated by young people
- Engage positively and constructively within communities and educational facilities
