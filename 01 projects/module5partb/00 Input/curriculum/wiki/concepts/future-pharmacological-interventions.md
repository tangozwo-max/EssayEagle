# Future Pharmacological Interventions (for SUD)

An overview of pharmacological approaches currently under research for substance use disorders and behavioral addictions beyond the established treatment guidelines.

## Gambling disorder
- **Opioid antagonists** and **atypical antipsychotics** show limited, low-quality evidence for reducing symptom severity (Dowling et al.)
- **Antidepressants** show no clear benefit for gambling disorder specifically
- Long-term effectiveness data is lacking; no medications are currently approved

## Alcohol use disorder
Investigational compounds include:
- **Kudzu extract** (plant-based; may reduce alcohol craving)
- **Metadoxine** (accelerates alcohol metabolism)
- **Ghrelin antagonists** (ghrelin is a hunger hormone linked to alcohol reward)
- **Pharmacotherapies targeting the stress response** (Swift and Aston)

## Nicotine cessation
- **Bupropion** — strong evidence for long-term quit success; may increase serious adverse events vs placebo; approximately equivalent to single NRT, less effective than varenicline or combination NRT
- **Nortriptyline** — some evidence, likely less effective than bupropion

## Psychedelics for SUD
- Classical psychedelics (psilocybin, LSD, ketamine) show significant effects on **cognition and emotional processing**, potentially improving SUD outcomes
- Exact contributions of top-down (brain-to-body) vs bottom-up (body-to-brain) mechanisms remain unclear
- Ongoing research may reshape addiction treatment — potential to break entrenched patterns of drug-seeking via neuroplasticity effects (Urban et al.)

## Cocaine/stimulant use disorders
- Most pharmacotherapies have not proven effective
- **Bupropion**, **psychostimulants**, and **topiramate** may improve abstinence rates
- **Antipsychotics** may improve treatment retention
- **Opioid agonist therapies** show association with reduced cocaine use (Bentzley et al.)
