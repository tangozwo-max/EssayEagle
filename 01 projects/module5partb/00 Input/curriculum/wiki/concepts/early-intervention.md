# Early Intervention

Early interventions are targeted programmes addressing mental health difficulties in children and young people before they become more serious or entrenched. These interventions are often delivered at the indicated or selective level of the continuum of need, targeting children with identified risk factors or early symptoms.

## Developmental approaches

### Infants
Early interventions for infants focus on:
- Improving parenting skills and responsiveness
- Nurturing secure attachment
- Reducing toxic stress (exposure to conflict, displacement, family discord, substance use)
- Supporting healthy relationship formation

### Young children (school-aged)
Interventions include:
- Behavioural-based parenting support
- Play therapy
- Psychosocial and social-emotional interventions
- Art and music therapy
- Positive psychology interventions
- Mindfulness-based interventions
- Self-regulation support (ADHD)
- Sensory-based interventions (ASD)

### Adolescents
Three categories of intervention:
- **Family/individual-based**: CBT, psychotherapy, multisystemic therapy
- **School-based**: solution-focused brief therapy, interpersonal psychotherapy
- **Community-based**: creative activities, computerized CBT, environmental interventions

## Effectiveness

Early intervention has been shown to:
- Prevent escalation of difficulties
- Produce sustained improvements
- Support healthy development
- Prevent need for more intensive treatment
