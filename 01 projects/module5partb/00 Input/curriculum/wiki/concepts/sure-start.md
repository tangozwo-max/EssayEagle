# Sure Start

Sure Start is a UK government early intervention programme providing holistic, multidisciplinary support to young families in areas with high indicators of social deprivation. The programme aims to improve families' health and well-being through a "one-stop shop" approach.

## Services provided

Sure Start centers typically provide:
- Health advice and services
- Parenting advice and support
- Education support
- Employment support
- Mental health and behavioral support

## Staffing and coordination

Sure Start centers are staffed by:
- Children's support workers
- Health workers
- Social workers
- Parenting support workers
- Other specialists

Centers liaise with education, health, local government, and charitable services to provide coordinated support.

## Focus

Sure Start was not directly aimed at improving mental health; rather, it aimed to improve social determinants of health and reduce risk factors. This holistic approach has been shown to lead to improved mental health outcomes.

## Evidence of effectiveness

Research by the Institute for Fiscal Studies found:
- Reduced hospitalisation among children of primary school age
- Reductions in infections (younger children) and injuries (older children)
- Improved educational grades at age 16
- Earlier recognition of special educational needs
- Reduced hospitalisation for mental health-related reasons in adolescence
