# Biomedical Model of Medicine

Emerged in the **19th century**, underpinned by [[cartesian-dualism]] and [[positivism]]. Views disease as a deviation from biological norms, caused by an identifiable physical or chemical event.

**Characteristics:**
- Reductionist — reduces disease to biological causes within the body
- Emphasizes empirical evidence, laboratory tests, pathophysiological explanations
- Humans as biological organisms: anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, physics

## Distinction from Medical Model of MH
- **Medical model** (M1): treats MH conditions *like* physical illness — organic or psychological cause
- **Biomedical model**: MH disorders arise *primarily from biological abnormalities* (neurotransmitters, genetics, brain structure)
