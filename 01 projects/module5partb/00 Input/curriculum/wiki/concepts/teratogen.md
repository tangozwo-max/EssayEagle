# Teratogen

A substance or agent that can cause birth defects when exposure occurs during pregnancy, particularly during critical periods of fetal development.

## Examples of teratogens

- **Alcohol** — strong evidence for causing FASD
- **Certain medications** — anti-epileptic drugs and others
- **Recreational drugs** — cocaine, methamphetamine, opioids
- **Tobacco** — nicotine and other components
- **Chemicals** — pesticides, heavy metals, others
- **Infections** — rubella, toxoplasma, others

## Mechanism of harm

Teratogens are most harmful during periods when the affected organ system is developing. For the brain, vulnerability extends throughout pregnancy but is particularly critical during neural development periods.

## Evidence and challenges

- **Strong evidence** — alcohol (FASD) has clear causal relationship to birth defects
- **Inconsistent evidence** — many teratogens lack consistent evidence base because:
  - Often trigger spontaneous miscarriage before pregnancy is known
  - Effects may depend on timing, dose, individual factors
  - Ethical limitations on human research

## Prevention

Universal prenatal guidance recommends avoiding potentially teratogenic substances during pregnancy, particularly alcohol and recreational drugs.
