# Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)

Social-emotional learning programs teach children and young people the knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary for healthy social and emotional functioning. These programs are a core component of universal mental health interventions.

## Key components

WHO-recommended social-emotional learning areas include:
- **Emotional regulation**: understanding and managing emotional responses
- **Problem-solving**: developing solutions to challenges
- **Interpersonal skills**: building positive relationships and communication
- **Mindfulness**: present-moment awareness and acceptance
- **Assertiveness**: expressing needs and boundaries respectfully
- **Stress management**: coping with stress and pressure

## Effects

Social-emotional learning programs can help:
- Promote positive mental health
- Prevent suicidal behaviour
- Reduce mental health disorders (depression, anxiety)
- Reduce aggressive, disruptive, and oppositional behaviour
- Reduce substance use
- Improve academic engagement and school attendance

## Delivery

SEL programs are typically delivered through:
- School-based curricula
- Classroom lessons and activities
- Youth development programs
- Community programs
