# Psychodynamic Theory (Freud)

**Sigmund Freud** — Austrian neurologist; founded psychoanalysis. Proposed unconscious drives, the Oedipus complex, defence mechanisms, and the structural model (id, ego, superego).

Influential on 20th-century psychotherapy. Later research (behaviourism, cognitive therapies, neuroscience) challenged specific claims. Modern view: Freud's ideas and the psychoanalytic approach **generally continue** to influence even where the original formulations are disputed.

## Clinical relevance
- Concept of trauma as formative
- Talk therapy tradition
- Transference / counter-transference
