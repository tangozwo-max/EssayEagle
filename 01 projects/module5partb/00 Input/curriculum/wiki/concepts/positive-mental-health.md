# Positive Mental Health

Positive mental health refers to a state of well-being, resilience, and capacity for meaningful living, not simply the absence of mental health conditions. Promoting positive mental health is a key goal of public health approaches to child and adolescent mental health.

## Key components

Positive mental health involves:
- **Well-being**: subjective sense of happiness, life satisfaction, and quality of life
- **Resilience**: capacity to adapt to and recover from adversity
- **Protective factors**: social support, coping skills, healthy lifestyles, secure relationships
- **Environmental conditions**: security, freedom to live a healthy life, access to community and social connections
- **Life meaning**: engagement in meaningful activities and relationships

## Promotion focus

Rather than focusing solely on reducing mental illness, promoting positive mental health emphasises:
- Actively building strengths and capacities
- Creating environments that support well-being
- Encouraging healthy behaviours and lifestyle choices
- Strengthening social connections and community support
