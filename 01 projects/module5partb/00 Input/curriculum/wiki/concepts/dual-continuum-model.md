# Dual-Continuum Model (Corey Keyes)

Framework considering **two interconnected dimensions** that together determine mental well-being and psychological functioning.

## The two axes
- **Mental-illness continuum** — from clinical illness to absence of illness
- **Mental-health continuum** — from *languishing* (dissatisfaction, low engagement) to *flourishing* (high well-being)

Four quadrants emerge — e.g., a person with a diagnosed disorder can still flourish; a person without any diagnosis can still languish.

## Key implications (Keyes 2013)
> "The absence of mental illness does not imply the presence of mental health."
> "The presence of mental illness does not imply the absence of mental health."

- In 2013, just over 75% of US adults aged 25–74 were free of three common mental-health conditions in the previous year — but many were languishing.
