# Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) Programs

SEL programs promote well-being and emotional development in school settings through games, activities, and group tasks. CASEL (Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning) established the Collaborating Districts Initiative (CDI) in 2011 for systematic SEL in large US urban districts. High-quality SEL leads to: better academic performance, improved classroom behaviour, less emotional distress, and more positive self-view. Sustained implementation (10-year CDI data) attributed to: strong leadership, core district policies, customisable implementation pathways, positive staff culture, co-creation with students/families/communities, and communities of practice. SEL is delivered universally (classrooms, assemblies) and can be embedded in school policies and hiring.
