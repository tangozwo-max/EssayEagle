# Structural Competency

A clinical training framework (Jonathan Metzl & Helena Hansen, 2014) that teaches practitioners to recognise how **social structures** — racism, poverty, housing insecurity, immigration status — shape patients' health and mental health.

Moves beyond cultural competency (individual cultural awareness) to address upstream structural determinants. Practitioners trained in structural competency can:
- Identify structural contributors to presenting problems
- Advocate for policy change
- Collaborate across sectors (health, social services, legal)
- Avoid pathologising structurally produced distress
