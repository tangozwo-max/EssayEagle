# Opioid Substitution Therapy (OST)

A pharmacological approach to opioid use disorder in which a prescribed opioid medication replaces illicit opioid use, reducing harms associated with drug-seeking behaviour, unsafe injecting, and criminal activity while stabilising the person for psychosocial recovery work.

## First-line agents
- **[[methadone]]** — full mu opioid receptor agonist; long half-life stabilises blood levels; first-line option
- **[[buprenorphine]]** — partial mu agonist; safer ceiling effect on respiratory depression; first-line option; available as Subutex (mono) or Suboxone (with naloxone to deter injection)

## Second-line / alternative
- **[[lofexidine]]** — alpha-2 adrenergic agonist; not an opioid but reduces withdrawal symptoms; considered under NICE for shorter detox, milder dependence, or patient preference against opioids

## Goals of OST
- Reduce or eliminate illicit opioid use
- Manage withdrawal symptoms comfortably
- Reduce harm (infection risk, overdose, criminal activity)
- Enable engagement with psychosocial interventions and recovery
- Improve quality of life, employment, family functioning

## Selection criteria
Practitioner and service user consider:
- Current use pattern and dependence severity
- Whether the person is already on one of these medications
- Personal preferences and goals
- Comorbidities (chronic pain, mental health)
