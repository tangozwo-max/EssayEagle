# Stigma Reduction Interventions

Strategies identified as a key priority in the **WHO Mental Health Action Plan 2013–2020**.

## Approach types
- **Education** — general education (e.g., [[mental-health-first-aid]])
- **Contact-based education** — PWLE interacting with learners
- **Protest** — challenging harmful portrayals (media campaigns, letters, boycotts)
- **Advocacy** — awareness campaigns, policy influence (Mind UK, NAMI, Beyond Blue)
- **Legislative reform** — anti-discrimination laws, human-rights protections
- **Stigma self-management** — through recovery frameworks like [[chime-framework]]

## Operating levels
[[macro-meso-micro]]

## Neuroscience nuance
Biological explanations can *reduce* perceived blame but *increase* perceived dangerousness and desire for social distance (meta-analysis of 26 studies). Communication should emphasise **complexity and plasticity** over reduction and fixity.
