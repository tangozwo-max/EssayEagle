# Resilience — Neurobiology and Correlates

**Not a fixed trait.** APA defines resilience as the ability to adapt well in the face of adversity, trauma, tragedy, threats, or significant sources of stress.

## Neurobiological correlates
- More **robust prefrontal cortexes** — stronger top-down emotion regulation.
- More balanced **HPA-axis output** (less chronic cortisol elevation).
- Stronger dopamine and serotonin systems.
- Gene variants associated with resilience; significant overlap with genes regulating depression.
- Responds to **epigenetic influences** — resilience can be built through experience.

## Protective factors
- Active coping strategies (problem- or emotion-focused)
- Resilience-enhancing activities: mindfulness, physical exercise, meditation, yoga
- Supportive relationships
- Meaning-making

## Children
Early childhood displays considerable resilience — early mastery promotes future resilience. ACEs undermine but do not deterministically predict outcomes.
