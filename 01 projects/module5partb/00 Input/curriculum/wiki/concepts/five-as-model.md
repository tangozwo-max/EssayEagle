# 5As Model of Evidence-Based Practice

Five-step clinical process first described in 1992.

1. **Ask** — formulate a searchable, answerable question.
2. **Acquire** — retrieve the best available evidence via literature review.
3. **Appraise** — critically evaluate validity, relevance, bias, applicability.
4. **Apply** — implement results with the person seeking care.
5. **Analyze / Assess** — evaluate the outcome of the new or improved practice.

Person-centric — every step begins and ends with the individual.
