# Lund et al. — Five Domains of Social Determinants of Mental Health

A conceptual framework derived from a systematic review of **289 peer-reviewed articles** (Lund et al., 2018) mapping the social determinants of mental disorders across five interacting domains.

## The five domains

| Domain | Key factors | Example mental-health associations |
|---|---|---|
| **Demographic** | sex, age, gender, ethnicity | Female sex ↔ MDD, anxiety, eating disorders. Male sex ↔ suicide, SUD. Minority ethnicity ↔ psychosis, depression. |
| **Economic** | poverty, unemployment, housing, macroeconomics | Poverty ↔ common mental disorders; homelessness ↔ depression, anxiety; debt ↔ suicidal ideation. |
| **Neighbourhood** | socioeconomic deprivation, urbanicity, social amenities | Deprivation ↔ psychosis, depression. Urban ↔ schizophrenia, mood and anxiety disorders. Community violence ↔ PTSD. |
| **Environmental** | natural disasters, armed conflict, toxins, climate | Disaster survivors ↔ 3–4× higher rates of PTSD, anxiety, depression. Lead ↔ behavioural problems in children. |
| **Social** | education, relationships, culture, participation | Low education ↔ suicide, dementia, late-life depression. Intimate-partner abuse ↔ postnatal depression/anxiety/PTSD. Loss of cultural identity ↔ negative outcomes. |

## Key claim
> "The association between poverty and mental disorders is complex and bidirectional … acting across the life course, from childhood to old age." — Lund et al.

## Potential interventions (by domain)
- **Demographic** — reduction of gender-based violence, child maltreatment, racial discrimination, xenophobia
- **Economic** — cash transfers, basic income, reduction of inequality, improved employment
- **Neighbourhood** — improved housing, safe neighbourhoods
- **Environmental** — reductions in violence, early disaster response, ecosystem protection
- **Social** — improved education, social support, strengthened [[social-capital]]
