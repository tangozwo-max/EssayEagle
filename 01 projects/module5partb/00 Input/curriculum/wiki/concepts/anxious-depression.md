# Anxious Depression

A mixed presentation where clinically significant anxiety and depressive symptoms co-occur. Often referred to informally as **anxious depression** or formally as **mixed anxiety–depressive disorder** (MADD).

The DSM-5 introduced a specifier: *MDD with anxious distress*, recognising that 40–50% of MDD patients have clinically significant anxiety. This comorbidity:
- Increases treatment resistance
- Prolongs episode duration
- Elevates suicide risk
- Complicates medication choice (SSRIs are first-line for both)
