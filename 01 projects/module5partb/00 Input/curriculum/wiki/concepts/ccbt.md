# Computerised Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (cCBT)

Delivery of CBT principles via **software, apps, or web-based platforms** with minimal or no therapist involvement. Examples: **Beating the Blues** (depression/anxiety), **FearFighter** (panic/phobias), **Silvercloud** (mood/anxiety).

## Evidence
Meta-analyses show effect sizes comparable to face-to-face CBT for mild-to-moderate depression and anxiety (around d = 0.5–0.8). NICE recommends cCBT as a low-intensity intervention in stepped care.

## Advantages
- Scalable, cost-effective
- 24/7 accessibility
- Reduces stigma barrier
- Can be guided (therapist reviews progress) or unguided

## Limitations
- High dropout rates (unguided programmes)
- Less effective for complex presentations
- Digital divide for older/less-connected populations
