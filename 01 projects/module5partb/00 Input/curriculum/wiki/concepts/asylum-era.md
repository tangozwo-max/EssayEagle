# Asylum Era

19th-to-mid-20th-century institutional care for people with mental illness. Large asylums housed people indefinitely; conditions often poor. Treatments of the era included:

- **Insulin shock therapy** (induced coma)
- **Hydrotherapy** (immersion, water therapy)
- **Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)**
- **Lobotomy**
- Restraints and confinement

## Transition
See [[deinstitutionalisation]] — late-20th-century policy shift to community care. Critics (e.g., paradigm-shift advocates) argue the "asylum mindset" was carried into community settings.
