# Suicide Risk Assessment

Essential when someone presents with depression. Four dimensions:

1. **Intent** — general wish to no longer be alive vs. specific desire to die
2. **Plans** — specific methods, access to means (gun, medication)
3. **Actions** — past attempts (recent or distant)
4. **Prevention** — protective factors: religious faith, family, children at home, sense of responsibility, problem-solving skills

## Key points
- Asking about suicide does NOT increase risk — evidence-based
- Use nonjudgmental approach; "normalize" that many people experience such thoughts
- Cultural variation: standard questions may need adaptation
- Werther Effect vs. Papageno Effect: media can either spread or prevent suicide
