# Dependence Syndrome

A diagnostic concept defined by the WHO and used in ICD-10 and ICD-11 to describe a cluster of behavioral, cognitive, and physiological phenomena that develop after repeated substance use. It is diagnosed when three or more of six criteria are met. Unlike DSM-5's [[substance-use-disorder]], dependence syndrome is a binary diagnosis — it is either present or absent, with no severity grading.

## ICD-11 criteria (≥3 of 6 required for diagnosis)
1. Strong desire or compulsion to use the substance
2. Difficulties controlling substance use behavior (onset, termination, or level of use)
3. Physiological withdrawal state when substance use is reduced or ceased
4. Tolerance (needing more substance to achieve the same effect)
5. Neglect of alternative pleasures or interests in favor of substance use
6. Persistent use despite harmful consequences

## Comparison with DSM-5
| Feature | ICD-11 Dependence Syndrome | DSM-5 SUD |
|---|---|---|
| Severity grading | Binary (present/absent) | Mild/Moderate/Severe |
| Cultural scope | Designed for global use | Primarily US clinical context |
| Diagnostic term | Dependence syndrome / Harmful use | Substance use disorder |

## ICD-11 also defines "harmful use"
A separate category diagnosed when there is clear evidence that substance use causes physical or psychological harm, without meeting full criteria for dependence syndrome.
