# Adult Neurogenesis

Formation of **new neurons in the adult brain** from neural stem cells. Occurs in at least two regions in most mammals:
- **Hippocampus** (especially dentate gyrus)
- **Olfactory bulb** (in humans: negligible; rodents: substantial)

Rate: ~**700 new neurons per day** in the adult human hippocampus → ~1.75 % annual turnover of the renewing fraction. Modest decline with aging; detected into the ninth decade of life.

## Discovery
- Proposed based on Eriksson et al. (1998) and confirmed via [[c-14-dating]] of DNA (Spalding et al., 2013).
- Rocks prior assumption that adult nervous system was fixed.

## Stages
1. **Proliferation** — neural stem cells → [[neural-stem-cells]] and neural progenitor cells (NPCs)
2. **Differentiation** — neuroblasts migrate, form initial synaptic framework
3. **Maturation** — 3–4 weeks; integration into existing neural networks

## Mental health relevance
- Decreased by stress, sleep deprivation, inflammation, early-life adversity
- Increased by exercise, socialisation, enriched environment, antidepressants, ketamine, ECT/TMS
- Linked to depression, schizophrenia, addiction
