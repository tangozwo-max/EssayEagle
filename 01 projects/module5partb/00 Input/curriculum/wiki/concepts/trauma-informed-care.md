# Trauma-Informed Care

Framework for service delivery that assumes trauma is widespread, recognises its impact on presentation and behaviour, and actively avoids re-traumatisation. Informs trauma assessments, disclosure practices, safety planning, and integration with the [[lives-framework]] for first-line support.

## Six key features in addiction services (M5 W3)
1. **Safety** — ensuring physical and emotional safety for both service users and staff
2. **Trustworthiness and transparency** — consistent, clear communication builds trust
3. **Peer support** — incorporating voices and experiences of people who have lived through trauma
4. **Collaboration and mutuality** — partnership between service user and provider; shared decision-making
5. **Empowerment, voice, and choice** — prioritising service user autonomy and choices in treatment
6. **Cultural, historical, and gender sensitivity** — responding to unique needs of diverse populations

Trauma-informed care is important in addiction services even when the person is not seeking care for trauma or PTSD specifically — trauma history profoundly shapes addiction risk and treatment engagement.
