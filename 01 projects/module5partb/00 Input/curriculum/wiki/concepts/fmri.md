# Functional MRI (fMRI)

Indirectly measures brain activity via the **BOLD response** — oxygenated blood flow changes tracked in real-time. Increased oxygenated blood in a region = increased neuronal activity there.

- **Strengths**: non-invasive, high spatial resolution, captures activity during specific tasks.
- **Limitations**: temporal delay of several seconds, complex data interpretation, expensive.

Often **superimposed** on sMRI images or combined with EEG/MEG for complementary views.
