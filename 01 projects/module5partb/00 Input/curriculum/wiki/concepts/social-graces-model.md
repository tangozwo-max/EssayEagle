# Social GRACES Model

The Social GRACES model, developed by John Burnham and colleagues, is a framework for understanding and reflecting on the diverse aspects of personal and social identity in a clinical context. GRACES is an acronym encompassing Gender, Race, Religion, Age, Culture/Class, Ethnicity, Education, Employment, Sexuality, and Spirituality (among other dimensions). It distinguishes between visible/invisible and spoken/unspoken aspects of identity — drawing attention to what practitioners and service users may or may not acknowledge about their social positioning. Originally developed within systemic therapy, it is used across CAMH to promote cultural reflection and reduce bias.
