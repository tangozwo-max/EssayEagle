# DSM — Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders

Standardised diagnostic classification system first published in **1952** by the American Psychiatric Association. Each revision incorporates empirical research — moving diagnoses toward more evidence-based criteria.

## Current editions
- DSM-5 (2013), DSM-5-TR (2022)

## In M5 context — Substance Use
DSM-5 introduced major terminological changes for addiction-related diagnoses:
- Replaced "substance abuse" and "substance dependence" with the unified term **[[substance-use-disorder]]** (SUD)
- SUD severity: mild (2-3 criteria), moderate (4-5), severe (6+)
- The term "addiction" is NOT used in DSM-5; severe SUD is its equivalent
- Only [[gambling-disorder]] is recognized as a behavioral addiction
- Recognized that tolerance and withdrawal in medically supervised treatment do NOT indicate SUD

The change was intended to reduce stigma and provide a more inclusive framework for identifying people who need help at all severity levels.
