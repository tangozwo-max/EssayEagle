# Fraser Guidelines

Fraser guidelines are five criteria used by UK healthcare practitioners to determine if a young person can receive contraception or sexual health advice without parental consent: (1) cannot be persuaded to inform parents or allow practitioner to inform them; (2) understands the advice given; (3) physical or mental health will suffer unless they receive it; (4) it is in their best interest to receive it without parental consent; (5) they are very likely to continue having sex regardless. Sexual activity under 13 always triggers a child protection referral regardless of Fraser guidelines. Repeated STIs or pregnancy terminations may indicate CSE. Practitioners should always encourage parental involvement and explore reluctance sensitively.
