# Psychosomatic Conditions

Conditions involving both **mind (psyche) and body (soma)**. Physical symptoms usually lack adequate medical explanation; excessive concern about symptoms affects functioning.

**Alternative terms:** somatoform disorders, functional disorders, bodily stress syndrome, medically unexplained physical symptoms (MUPS)

## Common symptoms
Fatigue, insomnia, pain, indigestion, shortness of breath

## Examples
- **Chronic Fatigue Syndrome / ME (CFS/ME)** — contested whether physiological or psychosomatic
- **Fibromyalgia**
- **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)**
- **Conversion Disorder** — neurological symptoms (paralysis, tremors) from psychological trauma; DSM-5 somatic disorders

## Clinical note
Using "psychosomatic" pejoratively (meaning "all in the mind") is unhelpful. Epistemic injustice can occur when patients' lived experience is dismissed.
