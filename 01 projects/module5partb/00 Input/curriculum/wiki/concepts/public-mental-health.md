# Public Mental Health

Public mental health refers to the organised efforts and informed choices of society, organisations, communities, and individuals to contribute to population well-being by promoting mental health and preventing mental health disorders.

## Core goals

Public mental health interventions aim to:
- **Prevent mental health disorders** by addressing risk factors and promoting protective factors
- **Reduce impacts and inequalities** by minimising the negative effects of mental health conditions and addressing disparities in access to care
- **Promote well-being and resilience** by enhancing positive mental health outcomes
- **Support effective interventions** by facilitating the delivery of evidence-based treatments

## Population-level focus

Unlike individual clinical care, public mental health operates at the population level, seeking to improve mental health outcomes across entire communities through systemic, preventative, and health-promotion approaches.
