# Phineas Gage

1848 railway construction foreman. Iron rod driven through his skull (below left cheekbone → top of head). Survived. Called **"the man who began neuroscience."**

## Revised modern interpretation
- Original reports of drastic personality change likely exaggerated (he later sustained a job as a carriage driver in Chile).
- Neuroimaging of his skull: ~11 % **white-matter** loss and only ~4 % gray-matter loss in left frontal lobe.
- White matter can regenerate; gray matter cannot.
- Case illustrates the brain's resilience and capacity for renewal, not simply "frontal damage = catastrophe."
