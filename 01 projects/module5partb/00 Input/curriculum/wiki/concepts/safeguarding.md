# Safeguarding

Safeguarding refers to the measures and practices protecting individuals (especially children) from abuse, harm, and neglect, while upholding their health, well-being, and human rights. Includes protection from: neglect, physical/sexual/emotional abuse, domestic abuse, CSE, cyberbullying, FGM, trafficking, criminal exploitation, and radicalisation. Key principle: **safeguarding is everyone's business** — responsibility shared across roles (teachers, healthcare, social services). Most child protection failures result from poor inter-agency information sharing. Children from Black, Asian, and minoritised ethnic communities face additional risks from adultification (perceived as more mature/less vulnerable), unconscious bias, and overlooked protection concerns. Practitioners must use strength-based, culturally sensitive approaches.
