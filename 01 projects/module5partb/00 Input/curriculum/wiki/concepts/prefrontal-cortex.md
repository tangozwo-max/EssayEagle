# Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)

The most anterior region of the frontal lobe, responsible for higher-order cognitive functions: thinking, planning, problem-solving, decision-making, and self-control. It is the last brain region to fully mature in humans (not until mid-twenties), which explains adolescent risk-taking and novelty-seeking.

## Role in addiction

In addiction, the PFC becomes hijacked by drug-seeking behaviour. Changes in the balance between the PFC circuit and the basal ganglia/extended amygdala circuits drive compulsive substance-seeking and reduce impulse control. During the **preoccupation/anticipation** phase of the [[addiction-cycle]], the PFC is preoccupied with obtaining substances at the expense of daily tasks and long-term goals.

## Vulnerability in adolescence

Alcohol, cannabis, and other substances during adolescence impair the still-developing PFC:
- Alcohol impairs decision-making already limited by PFC immaturity
- Cannabis alters PFC activity and connectivity, impairing executive functioning
- Ongoing PFC development is a key reason why early-onset substance use significantly increases addiction risk

## In neuroimaging studies

Volkow et al. found reduced frontal brain glucose metabolism persisting 3–4 months after cocaine cessation, indicating lasting PFC dysfunction even in abstinence.
