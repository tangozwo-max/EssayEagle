# Corticotropin-Releasing Factor (CRF)

A neuropeptide (also known as corticotropin-releasing hormone, CRH) produced primarily in the hypothalamus and in the extended amygdala. It is the initiating signal of the **HPA axis stress response** and a key component of the brain's **anti-reward system** in addiction.

## Functions

- Controls hormonal, sympathetic, and behavioural responses to stressors
- Released from the hypothalamus → stimulates pituitary to release ACTH → triggers cortisol production
- In the extended amygdala: functions as a local stress mediator independent of the HPA axis

## Role in addiction

During deep addiction, CRF activity in the extended amygdala increases substantially. This is a major driver of:
- **Anxiety and stress during withdrawal**
- **Compulsive drug-taking** — driven by desire to suppress aversive CRF-mediated states

Key finding: **CRF antagonists can block excessive drug-taking during dependence**, making CRF a promising pharmacological target. [Source: Koob]

CRF is described as a key player in the **plasticity of the extended amygdala** during addiction development — its signalling changes permanently in chronic addiction, lowering the stress threshold.
