# Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome (WKS)

A severe neurological disorder caused by **thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency**, most commonly resulting from chronic heavy alcohol use. It encompasses two related but distinct stages:

## Two stages

### Wernicke Encephalopathy (acute)
- Caused by acute thiamine deficiency
- Classic triad: **ophthalmoplegia** (eye movement abnormalities), **ataxia** (gait disturbance), **confusion**
- Medical emergency; treatable with IV thiamine if caught early
- If untreated, progresses to Korsakoff syndrome

### Korsakoff Syndrome (chronic)
- Severe **anterograde amnesia** (inability to form new memories)
- **Retrograde amnesia** (loss of existing memories)
- Confabulation (unconscious fabrication of memories to fill gaps)
- Relatively preserved other cognitive functions
- Often irreversible, though some recovery possible with sustained abstinence and nutrition

## Alcohol-Related Brain Damage (ARBD)

WKS is the most well-known form of ARBD. The broader category includes:
- **Mild cognitive impairment (MCI)** from moderate alcohol use
- **Hippocampal atrophy** → memory and cognitive problems (even at moderate consumption)
- **Cerebrovascular damage** → high blood pressure, increased stroke risk
- **Malnutrition** → poor diet, low thiamine, B-vitamin deficiencies

## Why alcohol causes WKS

Chronic alcohol use leads to:
- Malabsorption of thiamine in the gut
- Poor dietary intake
- Increased thiamine requirements (alcohol metabolism)
- Thiamine is essential for glucose metabolism in neurons; deficiency causes neuronal death in vulnerable regions (mammillary bodies, thalamus)
