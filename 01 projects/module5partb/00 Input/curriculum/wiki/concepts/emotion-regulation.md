# Emotion Regulation

Any process or action by which a person influences their emotions or their emotional expression. Emotion regulation operates across multiple points: the situations a person seeks or avoids, how they think about their experiences, and how they express their feelings.

## Link to addiction and SUD
Studies consistently show that adults with substance use disorders (SUDs) display greater difficulties in emotion regulation compared to adults without SUDs. Specifically, individuals with a SUD have more difficulty:
- Controlling impulsive behaviors
- Accessing strategies to regulate emotions effectively

Deficits in emotion regulation may drive substance use as a form of self-medication — using substances to manage or suppress difficult emotional states.

## Role in internet addiction
Emotion regulation deficits are also implicated in internet addiction. Introverted adolescents and those with poor emotion regulation show increased online activity, which may enhance risk for internet [[behavioral-addiction]].

## Protective regulation strategies
Effective emotion regulation — the capacity to access and use adaptive coping strategies — acts as a buffer against substance use and compulsive behavior. Positive self-esteem, optimism, and resilience are related protective factors.
