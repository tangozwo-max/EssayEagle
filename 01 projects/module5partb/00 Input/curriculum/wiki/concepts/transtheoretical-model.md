# Transtheoretical Model of Change (TTM)

A widely used behaviour-change model developed by Prochaska, DiClemente, and Norcross that describes the process of intentional change as a series of stages. It is used extensively in addiction treatment to tailor interventions to a person's current readiness to change.

## Six stages

| Stage | Description | MDT response |
|---|---|---|
| **Precontemplation** | Person does not recognise a problem or consider change | Raise awareness, build trust, avoid confrontation |
| **Contemplation** | Aware of benefits of change but ambivalent; can last months/years | Provide information; use [[motivational-interviewing]] to explore ambivalence |
| **Preparation** | Beginning small steps; considering action | Help develop personalised care plan; link to resources |
| **Action** | Taking direct steps to change behaviour | Provide therapy, medication-assisted treatment; monitor progress |
| **Maintenance** | Sustaining change; avoiding relapse triggers | Ongoing therapy, support groups, [[relapse-prevention]] planning |
| **Relapse** | Return to old behaviour | Non-judgmental support; reassess care plan; identify triggers |

## Criticisms
- Behaviours may be too complex for a single-stage designation
- Individuals in the same stage can differ substantially
- Change may operate on a continuum rather than discretely sequential steps (Sussman et al.)
