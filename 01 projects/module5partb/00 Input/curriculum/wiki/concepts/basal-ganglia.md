# Basal Ganglia

A group of subcortical nuclei involved in voluntary motor control, habit formation, and — critically — reward processing. Key structures include the caudate nucleus, putamen, and the nucleus accumbens (part of the ventral striatum). The basal ganglia constitute a key node in the brain's reward circuit.

## Functions relevant to addiction

1. **Positive motivation** — the basal ganglia generate pleasurable responses to natural rewards (food, sex, social interaction)
2. **Habit formation** — dopamine-driven repetition embeds behaviours into automatic routines
3. **Reward circuit hub** — the primary site of dopamine release during the binge/intoxication phase

## What substances do to the basal ganglia

Addictive substances massively over-activate the basal ganglia reward circuit, producing an intense dopamine-mediated high. With repeated use:
- The brain reduces the density of dopamine receptors (tolerance)
- Natural rewards no longer produce adequate dopamine stimulation
- The person becomes unable to feel pleasure from anything other than the substance (anhedonia)

## In the addiction cycle

The **binge/intoxication** stage of the [[addiction-cycle]] is primarily mediated by the basal ganglia. The **withdrawal/negative affect** stage involves *reduced* basal ganglia activation — the drop in dopamine below baseline is a key source of dysphoria.

## In the context of gambling

Gambling disorder causes similar dopamine dysregulation in the basal ganglia as substance use disorders, affecting the same circuits for memory/learning, motivation, inhibitory control, and reward salience.
