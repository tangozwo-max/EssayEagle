# Complex Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (C-PTSD)

A diagnosis introduced in **ICD-11** (not DSM-5) for individuals exposed to prolonged, repeated trauma (captivity, childhood abuse, domestic violence) rather than a single discrete event.

## Core features
**All PTSD criteria** (re-experiencing, avoidance, hyperarousal) **plus** three additional disturbances of self-organisation (DSO):
1. **Affect dysregulation** — extreme emotional reactivity or numbing
2. **Negative self-concept** — persistent shame, guilt, worthlessness
3. **Relationship disturbances** — difficulties maintaining close relationships

## Aetiology
Typically results from:
- Childhood physical/sexual abuse or neglect
- Prolonged domestic violence
- Torture, human trafficking, slavery

## Treatment
Standard trauma therapies (EMDR, TF-CBT) may need adaptation; **Phase-based treatment** (stabilisation → trauma processing → integration) is often recommended.
