# Peter Fonagy

British-Hungarian psychoanalyst and clinical psychologist based at University College London (UCL) and the Anna Freud National Centre for Children and Families. Pioneering figure in developing mentalization-based theory (MBT) and applying attachment theory to clinical psychotherapeutic practice.

## Mentalization Theory

Fonagy's most significant contribution is the development and empirical validation of mentalization-based approaches to understanding and treating mental health difficulties. Mentalizing—the capacity to understand mental states (thoughts, feelings, intentions) underlying one's own and others' behaviour—is foundational to healthy psychological functioning and relational wellbeing.

Fonagy proposed that mentalizing capacity develops through secure attachment relationships wherein caregivers consistently reflect and name the child's mental states. Conversely, disrupted attachments, particularly those involving maltreatment or frightening behaviour by the caregiver, impair mentalizing development.

## Mentalization-Based Therapy (MBT)

Building on attachment and mentalization theory, Fonagy and colleagues developed mentalization-based therapy—a structured therapeutic approach explicitly targeting mentalizing capacity. MBT has demonstrated efficacy across a range of clinical presentations, particularly borderline personality disorder, self-harm, and aggression.

## Integration of Attachment and Psychotherapy

Fonagy's work represents a bridge between attachment theory and contemporary psychotherapy practice, demonstrating that therapeutic relationships that provide secure attachment-like functions can improve mentalizing capacity and mental health outcomes.

## Institution and Training

Based at the Anna Freud National Centre for Children and Families, Fonagy has trained numerous clinicians and researchers in mentalization-based approaches. The centre produces influential research and training in attachment-informed practice.
