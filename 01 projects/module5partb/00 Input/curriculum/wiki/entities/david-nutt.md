# David Nutt

British pharmacologist and neuropsychopharmacologist, formerly chair of the UK's Advisory Council on the Misuse of Drugs. Nutt is known internationally for research on drug harm and for advocating evidence-based drug policy.

## Key contribution to M5
Nutt et al. conducted a landmark study published in *The Lancet* in which 20 drugs were scored on 16 criteria related to harms to the individual user and to society (including mental and physical damage, addiction, crime, and economic/community costs).

**Key findings:**
- Alcohol: overall harm score of **72** (out of 100) — most harmful
- Heroin: 55
- Crack cocaine: 54
- Cannabis, tobacco, and other common drugs scored substantially lower

This study is frequently cited to argue for evidence-based rather than legally-defined harm rankings of substances.
