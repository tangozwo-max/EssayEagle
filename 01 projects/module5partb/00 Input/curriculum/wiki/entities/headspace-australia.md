# Headspace (Australia)

Headspace National Youth Mental Health Foundation was established in 2006 by the Australian Government Department of Health. It operates 100+ centres across Australia, providing integrated support for adolescents and young adults aged 12–25. Centres collaborate with schools and social services and host community events (concerts, gaming). The program nurtures young leaders who offer feedback and advocacy to reduce stigma. Evaluation studies show high user satisfaction with nearly half reporting reduced mental distress after treatment. Headspace has inspired similar models internationally (e.g., NJR in Netherlands).

## Key contributions
- Integrated community youth mental health centres (100+ nationally)
- Youth-led advocacy and community engagement model
