# Anna Freud National Centre for Children and Families

Leading UK child mental health charity and research institute based in London, closely affiliated with University College London (UCL). Combines clinical service provision, research, and professional training with a mission to improve mental health outcomes for children, young people, and families.

## Research and Innovation

The centre conducts cutting-edge research in child and family mental health, with particular emphasis on attachment theory, mentalizing approaches, and evidence-based interventions. Research conducted at the centre has substantially influenced mental health policy and practice.

## Clinical Services

The centre provides clinical services to children and families experiencing mental health difficulties, trauma, and other psychological challenges. Services incorporate evidence-based approaches rooted in attachment theory and mentalization.

## Peter Fonagy and Mentalization

Peter Fonagy, the centre's director, is globally recognised for his work developing mentalization-based approaches to understanding and treating mental health difficulties. The centre has become the leading training institution for mentalization-based interventions.

## Training and Dissemination

The centre provides professional training and consultation to practitioners across child protection, mental health, education, and other sectors. Courses, workshops, and online resources are designed to improve understanding of attachment, trauma, and effective therapeutic approaches to supporting children and families.

## Impact

The centre's work has been influential in shifting mental health practice toward attachment-informed, trauma-sensitive approaches and in emphasising the importance of early intervention in supporting healthy child development.
