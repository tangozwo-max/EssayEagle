# Nora Volkow

Director of the National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA) and one of the world's leading researchers in neuroscience and addiction. Volkow's work has been central to reframing addiction as a brain disease and medical condition rather than a moral failing.

## Key position
"Addiction is not a choice. Nobody is born with the idea that they want to choose to become addicted and lose control over their desire, intense desire to take drugs. It's something that you may have (the vulnerability) because actually you have genetic risk, family history, or because you have a very stressful environmental upbringing."

## Key contributions
- Neuroimaging research demonstrating changes in dopamine systems and brain circuits in people with addiction
- Advocacy for evidence-based, medical treatment approaches to addiction
- Research on genetic, environmental, and developmental risk factors for addiction transition
