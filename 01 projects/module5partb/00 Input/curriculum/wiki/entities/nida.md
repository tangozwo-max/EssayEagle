# NIDA — National Institute on Drug Abuse

A US federal research institute under the National Institutes of Health (NIH), dedicated to advancing science on drug use and addiction. NIDA is a primary source for substance categories, addiction genetics research, risk factor frameworks, and clinical guidance on substance use disorders.

## Key contributions relevant to M5
- Substance categories and drug charts (NIDA Commonly Used Drugs Charts)
- Historical contextualization of addiction from "moral failing" to medical condition
- Research on addiction genetics (19 cross-addiction gene variants in dopamine signaling)
- Risk factor frameworks covering genetics, environment, psychology, and comorbidities
- Clinical guidance on treating addiction as a chronic, treatable medical disease
