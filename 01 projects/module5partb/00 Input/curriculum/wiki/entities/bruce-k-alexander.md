# Bruce K. Alexander

Canadian psychologist and addiction researcher, best known for designing the [[rat-park-experiment]] in the late 1970s. Alexander challenged the dominant pharmacological model of addiction by demonstrating that environmental and social factors — not just a substance's chemical properties — are critical determinants of addictive behavior.

## Key contributions
- Designed and conducted the Rat Park experiment (late 1970s), providing evidence that isolated, impoverished environments drive substance use rather than drug chemistry alone
- Argued that social disconnection, poverty, and lack of meaningful activity are primary drivers of addiction
- Published "Addiction: The View from Rat Park" (2010) extending these findings conceptually

## Impact
The Rat Park experiment influenced the biopsychosocial understanding of addiction and the emphasis on social/environmental risk factors alongside genetic and pharmacological ones.
