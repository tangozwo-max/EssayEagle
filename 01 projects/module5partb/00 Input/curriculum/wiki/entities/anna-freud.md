# Anna Freud

Anna Freud (1895–1982) was an Austrian-British psychoanalyst and the founder of ego psychology in child analysis. She established the Hampstead Child Therapy Clinic (now Anna Freud National Centre for Children and Families). She applied psychoanalysis to education, established London Nurseries (1939–45) for children during WWII with Dorothy Burlingham, and developed theories on developmental stages and the impact of separation on children. Her work on defense mechanisms and child development significantly influenced child psychoanalysis and school-based mental health.

## Key contributions
- Ego psychology and defense mechanisms in children
- Child developmental stages and normative development
- School-based mental health; nursery care for war-affected children
