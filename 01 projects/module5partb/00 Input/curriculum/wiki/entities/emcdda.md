# EMCDDA — European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction

The EU's reference agency for information on drugs and drug addiction, providing data and clinical guidance on substance use disorders across member states. In M5 W1, EMCDDA is cited as the source of evidence-based clinical recommendations for treating comorbid substance use disorders alongside specific mental health conditions.

## Key clinical recommendations cited in M5
- **Depression + SUD:** Integrated treatment for both; SSRIs not efficacious for MH conditions where comorbid SUD is present; CBT helpful
- **Anxiety + SUD:** CBT positive but insufficient alone; combining CBT with antidepressants effective for opioid use disorder + anxiety
- **Personality disorders + SUD:** Same treatment range as others; no pharmacotherapy with strong evidence
- **Eating disorders + SUD:** Dialectical behavioral therapy (DBT) effective for both; behavioral therapy recommended
- **Psychosis + SUD:** Integrated psychological + antipsychotic treatment most effective; monitor substance use and warn about drug-medication interactions
