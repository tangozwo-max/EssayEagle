# John Bowlby (1907–1990)

British psychiatrist, psychologist, and psychoanalyst who pioneered attachment theory, fundamentally transforming understandings of child development and the foundation of lifelong mental health.

## Career and Contributions

Bowlby initially trained as a psychoanalyst but became increasingly dissatisfied with psychoanalytic theory's inattention to observable behaviour and the importance of real relational experience. Through systematic observation of children and families, alongside integration of findings from ethology (the study of animal behaviour, particularly Konrad Lorenz's work on imprinting), he developed attachment theory as a comprehensive framework explaining how infants' bonds with primary caregivers shape all subsequent development.

## Core Theoretical Contributions

**Defining Attachment**: Bowlby defined attachment as "lasting psychological connectedness between human beings," emphasizing that the formation of strong bonds with specific individuals is intrinsic to human nature and necessary for survival.

**Four Stages of Attachment**: He proposed a developmental sequence—pre-attachment, attachment-in-the-making, clear-cut attachment, and reciprocal relationships—that illuminated how the infant-caregiver bond progresses across the first years of life.

**Internal Working Models**: Perhaps his most enduring conceptual innovation, internal working models describe how early experiences with caregivers become encoded as mental representations that serve as templates for all future relationships.

**Clinical Application**: Bowlby emphasised that psychotherapy should provide clients with a secure base—replicating the function of a secure caregiver—from which clients can explore attachment-related experiences and gradually develop more secure internal working models.

## Integration of Ethology

Bowlby's revolutionary contribution was integrating ethological insights (that infants' behaviours evolved to maintain proximity to caregivers) with psychoanalytic theory. This grounded attachment theory in evolutionary principles whilst maintaining attention to psychological experience.

## Legacy

Bowlby's work spawned an enormous literature on attachment and has become foundational to child development, psychology, psychiatry, and child protection. His insistence on the importance of real relational experience over intrapsychic fantasy has shaped contemporary psychotherapy practice.
