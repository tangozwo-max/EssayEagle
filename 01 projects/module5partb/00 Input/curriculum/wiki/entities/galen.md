# Galen (~130–210 CE)

Prominent Ancient Roman physician; dissected animals (Roman law barred human autopsy). Proposed brain controls temperament and bodily function via fluid-filled ventricles. Correctly linked cerebellum with muscle control and cerebrum with senses; correctly mapped nerves to specific muscle groups. Ideas dominated for 1000+ years.
