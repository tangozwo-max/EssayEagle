# NSPCC

The National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children (NSPCC) is the UK's leading child protection charity. Beyond its statutory child protection work, the NSPCC produces evidence-based resources and training designed to help practitioners, parents, and carers support healthy child development and protect children from harm.

## Brain Development Resources

The NSPCC is renowned for translating neuroscience research into accessible, memorable metaphors and resources for non-specialist audiences. These include:

**Brain Architecture**: The house-building metaphor comparing brain development to construction, emphasising the importance of early foundations.

**Serve-and-Return**: The tennis rally analogy for reciprocal caregiver-child interaction that builds neural circuits.

**Look, Say, Sing, Play**: A campaign highlighting four simple, everyday activities that support brain development in infants and toddlers.

**Air Traffic Control**: The metaphor for executive function, comparing executive processes to airport control managing multiple planes and information flows.

## Training and Learning Platform

The NSPCC Learning platform provides online training and professional development for practitioners across child protection, education, health, and mental health. These evidence-based courses incorporate attachment theory, neuroscience, trauma-informed practice, and child safeguarding.

## Impact and Evidence

The NSPCC's resources have reached millions of parents and practitioners and have significantly influenced how early childhood development is understood and communicated within the UK and internationally. Their emphasis on making neuroscience accessible without oversimplification has made them influential in policy and practice.
