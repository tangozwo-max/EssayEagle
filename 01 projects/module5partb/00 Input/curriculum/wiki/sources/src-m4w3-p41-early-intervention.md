# M4 W3 P41 — Early intervention

Comprehensive overview of early intervention approaches across the lifespan (infants, young children, adolescents). Describes age-specific interventions including parenting support, attachment-focused work, play therapy, CBT, psychotherapy, and community-based approaches. Introduces the neuroaffirmative approach for working with neurodivergent children, emphasising environmental modification and quality of life rather than symptom reduction.

## Key concepts covered
- [[early-intervention]] — targeted interventions for identified at-risk or early-symptomatic children
- [[parenting-skills]] — programmes improving parental responsiveness and support
- [[attachment-interventions]] — promoting secure parent-child attachment through education and feedback
- [[play-therapy]] — child-centered therapeutic approach using play
- [[cognitive-behavioral-therapy|CBT]] — evidence-based psychotherapeutic approach
- [[solution-focused-brief-therapy]] — brief, goal-focused therapy approach
- [[interpersonal-psychotherapy]] — therapy addressing relationships and interpersonal functioning
- [[art-music-therapy]] — creative therapeutic modalities
- [[neuroaffirmative-approach]] — affirming neurodivergence, supporting quality of life rather than reducing traits
- [[multisystemic-therapy]] — multi-level interventions addressing family, school, and community
