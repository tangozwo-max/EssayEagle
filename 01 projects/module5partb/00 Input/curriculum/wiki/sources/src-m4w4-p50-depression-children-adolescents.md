# M4 W4 P50 — Depression in Children and Adolescents

Depression has low prevalence in childhood but rises sharply in adolescence, particularly for females, those with SEN, chronic illness, and low SES. The first depressive episode typically occurs during adolescence. Adolescent-specific symptoms include irritable mood, appetite/weight changes, insomnia/hypersomnia, and fatigue. About 50% of adolescents with depression experience recurrence. NICE recommends a stepped-care approach: mild = lifestyle + psychoeducation + watchful waiting; moderate = specialist care + CBT/IPT; severe = combined medication and psychotherapy.
