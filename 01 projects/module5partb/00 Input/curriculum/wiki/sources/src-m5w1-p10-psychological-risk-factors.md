# M5 W1 P10 — Psychological Risk Factors for Addiction

This page covers the psychological risk factors for substance use disorders and compulsive behaviors, organized into four categories: personality factors, emotion regulation and impulse control, stress and trauma, and comorbid mental health conditions. Protective personality traits include positive self-esteem, optimism, and coping skills. Adults with SUDs show significantly greater difficulty regulating emotions and controlling impulsive behavior compared to those without SUDs. Internet addiction is highlighted as an emerging concern, with European prevalence of problematic internet use estimated at 14–55%. Impulsivity is identified as a cross-cutting vulnerability.

## Key concepts covered
- [[emotion-regulation]] — difficulties in regulation associated with SUD; includes controlling impulses and accessing strategies
- [[impulse-control]] — impulsivity as a cross-cutting psychological vulnerability for SUD
- [[stress-trauma-addiction]] — trauma history, dysfunctional family, stressful life events as major risk factors; stress relief enhances substance pleasure
- [[internet-addiction]] — psychological risk: personality traits (introversion) + emotion dysregulation; European prevalence 14-55%
- [[comorbidity]] — depression, anxiety, personality disorders (BPD, ASPD) as psychological risk factors for SUD
