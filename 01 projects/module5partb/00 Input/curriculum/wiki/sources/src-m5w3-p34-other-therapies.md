# M5 W3 P34 – Other therapies

- Exercise as an adjunct to addiction treatment produces significant improvements in abstinence days and reduced cravings, particularly in inpatient and outpatient settings (Patterson et al.).
- Yoga (especially Hatha yoga) shows promising results as a complement to treatment as usual for women with substance misuse disorder, though only small research projects exist — larger studies are recommended (Brooks et al.).
- Music therapy (delivered by registered therapists) is supported by a 2022 Cochrane report finding it reduces substance craving when added to standard care; it is particularly useful for those who struggle to communicate verbally.
- Art therapy, used with SUD since the 1950s, helps decrease denial, support emotional expression, facilitate group discussions, motivate change, and move people from contemplation to action.
- Acupuncture has emerging evidence for both alcohol use disorder (reduced alcohol-related symptoms, Shin et al.) and drug addiction (modulates mesolimbic dopamine system, Lee et al.), though more rigorous research is needed in both cases.
