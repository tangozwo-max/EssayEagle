# M4 W3 P35 — A continuum of need

This page introduces the concept of a continuum of need, a framework that categorises varying levels of support and intervention required by individuals and families. It outlines four levels—universal, selective, indicated, and specialist—that are widely used in UK health, social care, and education services. The page emphasises how people may move between levels as circumstances change and introduces public mental health as the organised efforts to promote well-being and prevent mental health disorders.

## Key concepts covered
- [[public-mental-health]] — organised efforts to promote mental health and prevent disorders
- [[continuum-of-need]] — framework for categorising levels of support and intervention
- [[universal-prevention]] — services available to all without assessment of need
- [[selective-prevention]] — targeted support for those at risk of developing complex needs
- [[indicated-prevention]] — early intervention for those identified as having emerging needs
- [[specialist-services]] — specialist interventions for those with severe, complex needs
- [[social-determinants|social determinants of health]] — conditions affecting access to support and risk status
- [[spectrum-of-interventions]] — range of intervention options across the continuum
- [[protective-factors]] — factors that promote well-being and prevent escalation
- [[risk-factors]] — factors associated with developing mental health conditions
- [[evidence-based-practice]] — approach prioritised in delivering interventions
