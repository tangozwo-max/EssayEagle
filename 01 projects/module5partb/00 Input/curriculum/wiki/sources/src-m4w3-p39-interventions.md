# M4 W3 P39 — Mental health interventions

This page outlines WHO recommendations for mental health interventions with adolescents across different contexts. It describes universal interventions addressing social-emotional learning, interventions for humanitarian emergencies, pregnancy and parenting support, emotional difficulties (CBT-based), and behavioural difficulties (parenting training, multimodal approaches). The page emphasises evidence-based practice and mentions early childhood interventions, community development, and violence prevention.

## Key concepts covered
- [[psychosocial-interventions]] — psychological, social, and community approaches to mental health support
- [[cognitive-behavioral-therapy|CBT]] — evidence-based intervention addressing thoughts, feelings, and behaviours
- [[trauma-focused-cbt]] — CBT specifically adapted for trauma survivors
- [[parenting-training]] — skills-building programs for parents addressing child behaviour
- [[social-emotional-learning]] — programs building emotional regulation, problem-solving, and interpersonal skills
- [[multimodal-interventions]] — interventions combining multiple approaches across family and individual levels
- [[early-childhood-interventions]] — preventative programs starting in pregnancy and preschool
- [[violence-prevention]] — programs reducing aggressive and oppositional behaviours
- [[universal-prevention]] — school and community-based interventions for all
- [[selective-prevention]] — targeted interventions for at-risk groups
- [[indicated-prevention]] — interventions for those showing early signs of difficulties
