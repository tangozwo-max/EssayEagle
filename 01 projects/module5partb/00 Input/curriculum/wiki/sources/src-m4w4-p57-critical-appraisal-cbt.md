# M4 W4 P57 — Critical Appraisal Activity: CBT for Children and Adolescents

Worked example of CASP RCT checklist applied to a study comparing manualized CBT versus treatment as usual (TAU) for depressed adolescents. Key methodological findings: study was underpowered (88 vs. required 140 participants); both CBT and TAU showed moderate-to-large effect sizes; no significant difference between conditions; blinding of therapists not possible in psychological research; TAU comparator was heterogeneous. The study had high generalizability due to real-world clinical sample with high comorbidity and suicidal ideation.
