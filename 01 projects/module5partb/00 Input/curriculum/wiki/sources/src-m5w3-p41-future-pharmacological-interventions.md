# M5 W3 P41 – Possible future pharmacological interventions

- For gambling disorder, opioid antagonists and atypical antipsychotics show limited low-quality evidence for reducing symptom severity; long-term effectiveness is unknown; antidepressants show no clear benefit (Dowling et al.).
- New AUD pharmacotherapies under investigation include kudzu extract, metadoxine, ghrelin antagonists, and stress-response pharmacotherapies (Swift and Aston).
- For nicotine cessation, bupropion shows strong evidence for long-term quit success but may increase serious adverse events compared to placebo; it is approximately equivalent to single-form NRT but less effective than varenicline or combination NRT (Lindson et al.).
- Psychedelics (classical hallucinogens) show significant effects on cognition and emotional processing, potentially improving outcomes in substance use disorders; exact mechanisms (top-down vs bottom-up) remain unclear and require more research (Urban et al.).
- For cocaine use disorder, most pharmacotherapies are ineffective; bupropion, psychostimulants, and topiramate may improve abstinence; antipsychotics may improve treatment retention; opioid agonist therapies may reduce cocaine use (Chan et al., Bentzley et al.).
