# M5 W2 P21 — Summary: Effects of Addiction and Dependency on the Brain

End-of-section summary for the first half of W2 (P14–P20). Consolidates key learning points: differential brain region vulnerability to addiction; dopamine's role in encoding pleasurable memories; the three-phase addiction cycle; tolerance as receptor diminishment; withdrawal as brain chemistry imbalance; and the shared brain-change mechanism between gambling disorder and substance use disorders.

## Key learning points
- Some brain regions (prefrontal cortex, extended amygdala, basal ganglia, brainstem) are more vulnerable to addiction than others
- Dopamine encodes pleasure and drives habit formation and drug-seeking
- The addiction cycle has three feeding stages: binge/intoxication, withdrawal/negative affect, preoccupation/anticipation
- Tolerance = receptors diminish → reward diminishes → higher doses needed
- Withdrawal = substance cessation → brain chemistry imbalance → withdrawal symptoms
- Gambling disorder causes similar brain changes to substance use disorders
