# M5 W1 P11 — An Introduction to Substance Use Care and Services

This page introduces substance use treatment through a clinical case vignette (Audrey, liver cirrhosis from alcohol misuse), then outlines the types of services available: detoxification, residential/inpatient, behavioral therapies, group therapy (AA/NA), family therapy, harm reduction, and holistic/complementary approaches. A global context section notes that about 20% of countries lack screening and brief intervention programs. The EMCDDA recommendations for comorbid SUD with depression, anxiety, personality disorders, eating disorders, and psychosis are presented, with CBT and dialectical behavioral therapy identified as key treatment approaches.

## Key concepts covered
- [[harm-reduction]] — services such as needle exchange programs to prevent disease spread
- [[rehabilitation]] — holistic substance use treatment combining pharmacological, psychological, and social interventions
- [[cognitive-behavioral-therapy]] — first-line psychological intervention for SUD comorbidities
- [[dialectical-behavioral-therapy]] — effective for comorbid eating disorders and SUD; also referenced for anxiety-SUD
- [[emcdda]] — European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction; clinical comorbidity recommendations
- [[samhsa]] — clinical case framework; case management and individualized care planning
