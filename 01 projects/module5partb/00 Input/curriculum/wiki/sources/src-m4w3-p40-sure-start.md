# M4 W3 P40 — Case study - Sure Start

Case study of Sure Start, a UK government programme providing holistic, multidisciplinary support to young families in areas of high social deprivation. Sure Start offers health advice, parenting support, education and employment support, and mental health services. Though not directly aimed at mental health, it demonstrates how addressing social determinants of health can improve mental health outcomes. Research shows Sure Start reduced hospitalisations, improved educational outcomes, and supported earlier identification of special educational needs.

## Key concepts covered
- [[sure-start]] — UK government early intervention programme for families in deprived areas
- [[social-determinants]] — factors addressed by Sure Start to improve health
- [[holistic-support]] — comprehensive, multidisciplinary approach to family support
- [[early-intervention]] — programmes starting in early childhood to prevent problems
- [[multidisciplinary-team]] — health workers, social workers, parenting specialists working together
- [[primary-prevention]] — addressing risk factors and social determinants before problems arise
