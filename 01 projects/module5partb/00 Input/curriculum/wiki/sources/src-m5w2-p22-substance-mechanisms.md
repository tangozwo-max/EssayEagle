# M5 W2 P22 — Substance Mechanisms of Action

Focuses on alcohol as the first substance with a detailed mechanism of action. AUD is defined as a brain disorder with inability to control alcohol use despite negative consequences. Risk factors include consumption patterns, early age of first use (before 15 = higher risk), genetics (~60% contribution), and mental health (depression, PTSD, ADHD, ACE). Alcohol's mechanism: enhances GABA (inhibitory), interfering with CNS communication. Adolescent brains are especially vulnerable due to ongoing prefrontal cortex maturation. Memory effects: alcohol impairs NMDA receptor function and glutamate activity, causing fragmentary and en bloc blackouts. Alcohol overdose depresses the brainstem. Moderate use is associated with hippocampal shrinkage and Wernicke-Korsakoff risk.
