# M4 W4 P58 — Psychodynamic Therapy for Adolescents

Psychodynamic therapy explores how the unconscious mind shapes behaviour and emotions through play, exploration of early experiences, identification of defense mechanisms, and building a therapeutic alliance. Contributions of Melanie Klein and Anna Freud established play as the medium for accessing children's inner worlds. Short-term psychodynamic psychotherapy (STPP) shows a "sleeper effect" — benefits continue post-therapy. Evidence base is limited (few RCTs) but a large NIHR multicenter trial is underway comparing CBT, STPP, and specialist clinical care for adolescent depression.
