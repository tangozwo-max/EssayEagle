# M5 W2 P17 — Tolerance and Withdrawal

Detailed neurobiological account of how tolerance and withdrawal develop, focused on the cAMP pathway. Tolerance arises because opioids block cAMP production; with chronic use neurons counter-adapt via allostasis, boosting cAMP production. This means higher doses are needed to suppress cAMP (tolerance), and when the drug stops, elevated cAMP causes neuronal hyperactivity (withdrawal). Three anatomical sites of withdrawal symptom generation are identified: the locus coeruleus in the brainstem, GABAergic interneurons in the brainstem, and the gut. Allostasis also changes gene expression, leading to receptor phosphorylation and internalisation. Case study: Fahad on opioid pain relief.
