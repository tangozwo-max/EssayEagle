# M5 W3 P29 – Multidisciplinary care for addiction and dependency

- Substance misuse and behavioral disorders require holistic, coordinated care because they are multifactorial conditions; [[patient-centred-care]] (PCC) provides the evidence-based framework.
- PCC rests on four principles: a biopsychosocial approach, individualized focus on goals and preferences, shared power between person and provider, and an enhanced therapeutic alliance.
- Globally, an estimated one in six people with a substance use disorder cannot access treatment — a major equity gap.
- The [[multidisciplinary-team]] for addiction typically includes psychologists (CBT, MI, assessment), psychiatrists (medications, crisis management), mental healthcare nurses (monitoring, coordination), counselors/art-music therapists, and peer support workers.
- Peer support workers bring lived experience of recovery, build trust, offer role modeling, and assist with relapse prevention and community reintegration.
