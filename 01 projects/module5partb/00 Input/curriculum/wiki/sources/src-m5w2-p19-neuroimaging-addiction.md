# M5 W2 P19 — Neuroimaging and Addiction

Four neuroimaging studies demonstrating addiction-related brain changes in cocaine users are presented. (1) PET with [C11]Raclopride: cocaine cue videos produced reduced raclopride binding in the dorsal striatum, correlating with craving severity. (2) fMRI: reduced D2 receptor density in the striatum persisted 4 months after cocaine cessation. (3) fMRI glucose metabolism: cocaine users showed significantly reduced frontal brain metabolism that persisted after 3–4 months of abstinence. (4) sMRI + machine learning (Schinz et al.): cocaine users showed accelerated cortical thinning (BrainAGE gap) compared to healthy controls.
