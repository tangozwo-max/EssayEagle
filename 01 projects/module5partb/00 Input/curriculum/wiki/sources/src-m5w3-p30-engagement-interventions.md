# M5 W3 P30 – Engagement with interventions

- Engagement with treatment is consistently linked to better outcomes; services should use diversified settings (hospital, clinic, community, integrated) to improve access.
- Barriers and facilitators to engagement operate at three levels: individual (incorrect beliefs, fear, traits, comorbidities), social (stigma, family, peer network, therapeutic relationship), and structural (service problems, legal and policy barriers).
- The case of José illustrates how [[motivational-interviewing]] can initiate engagement, [[cognitive-behavioural-therapy]] can restructure thought patterns, and peer support can sustain recovery across prison and community settings.
- Behavioral addictions (gambling, gaming, shopping) are generally treated non-pharmacologically, though research into pharmacological options is ongoing.
- Problem-to-service matching — aligning a person's specific needs with appropriate services — and brief interventions in non-specialist settings are key engagement strategies.
