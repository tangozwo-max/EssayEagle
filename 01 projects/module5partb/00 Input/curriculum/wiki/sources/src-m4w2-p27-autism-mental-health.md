# M4 W2 P27 — Autism and mental health risk

Examines how social, psychological, and biological factors contribute to mental health risks in autistic individuals. Covers stigma, discrimination, pressure to mask, educational inequity, and sensory differences. Emphasizes importance of acceptance, accommodation, and removal of barriers rather than "fixing" autistic traits.

## Key concepts covered
- [[autism]] — neurodevelopmental difference in social communication and repetitive behaviours
- [[autistic-stigma]] — negative attitudes toward autism
- [[masking-autism]] — suppressing autistic traits to appear non-autistic
- [[stimming]] — repetitive movements/actions providing self-regulation
- [[sensory-differences]] — atypical sensory processing
- [[neurodivergent-affirming]] — acceptance-based approach
