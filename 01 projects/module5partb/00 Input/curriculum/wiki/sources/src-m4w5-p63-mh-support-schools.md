# M4 W5 P63 — Mental Health Support in Schools

Schools are the second most important environment for children after home. Teachers and school staff are well-positioned to detect early signs of distress due to their knowledge of children and families. The page provides epidemiological context: in a typical class of 30, 5 may have a mental health disorder, 7 may have self-harmed, 11 may have experienced bullying. Psychological practitioners in CAMH regularly interact with schools for assessment, formulation-sharing, and coordinated interventions. Trauma-informed and neuroaffirmative approaches are as relevant in schools as in clinical settings.
