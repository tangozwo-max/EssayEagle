# M5 W3 P42 – Summary for pharmacological interventions

- [[medically-assisted-withdrawal]] supports safe cessation of substance use; support intensity varies with the severity of dependence.
- Pharmacological interventions are required alongside (not instead of) nonpharmacological interventions for most people with SUD.
- Regulatory treatment guidelines currently exist only for opioid and alcohol use disorders.
- Pharmacological treatments for opioid use disorder typically involve opioid receptor agonists (methadone, buprenorphine) that substitute for or block illicit opioids.
- Pharmacological treatments for alcohol use disorder work via three mechanisms: blocking opioid receptors ([[naltrexone]], [[nalmefene]]), restoring neurotransmitter balance ([[acamprosate]]), or creating aversive reactions to alcohol ([[disulfiram]]).
- No pharmacological interventions are currently approved for stimulant use disorders; this remains an active research area.
