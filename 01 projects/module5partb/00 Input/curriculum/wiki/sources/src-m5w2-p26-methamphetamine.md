# M5 W2 P26 — Methamphetamine

Methamphetamine (crystal meth) is a highly addictive psychostimulant and one of the world's most abused substances. Unlike cocaine, it causes the active release of dopamine, serotonin, and norepinephrine from neurons, rather than just blocking reuptake. It also inhibits the dopamine transporter. Glutamate overstimulation causes neuronal inflammation and necrosis in the mesolimbic region — the primary mechanism of neurotoxicity. Permanent white matter damage occurs in the temporal, frontal/parietal, and occipital lobes; the cortex, basal ganglia, brainstem, and cerebellum are spared (Moeller et al.). Effects last 6–12 hours, driving binge use. Parkinson's-like symptoms emerge from midbrain neurodegeneration. Withdrawal causes severe depression, anxiety, and craving due to depleted dopamine/serotonin.
