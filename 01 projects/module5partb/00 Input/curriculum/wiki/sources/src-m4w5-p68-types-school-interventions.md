# M4 W5 P68 — Types of School Interventions

School formulation operates at two levels: organisational (whole-school needs) and individual (specific student). Indirect interventions (without child present) include consultation, facilitating reflective practice, professional meetings, staff training, observation, referral, and signposting. Direct interventions include SEL programs, individual psychological therapies, group-based approaches, and school-based assessment/screening. Consent, confidentiality, and safeguarding are particularly critical in indirect school contexts.
