# M5 W1 P03 — Categories of Substances and Diagnostic Criteria

This page defines psychoactive substances and lists the categories most commonly associated with use disorders (alcohol, cannabis, opioids, stimulants, sedatives, etc.). It examines the sociolegal context — including cultural substances like khat and mescaline — and covers the 11 DSM-5 diagnostic criteria for SUD grouped into four domains: impaired control, social impairment, risky use, and pharmacological dependence. ICD-11 criteria for harmful use and dependence syndrome are also presented, noting key differences from DSM-5 (no severity grading in ICD-11). Nutt et al.'s drug harm study is cited, with alcohol scoring highest overall.

## Key concepts covered
- [[psychoactive-substance]] — WHO definition; substances altering consciousness, mood, or cognition
- [[substance-use-disorder]] — 11 DSM-5 criteria grouped into 4 domains; severity: mild (2-3), moderate (4-5), severe (6+)
- [[icd]] — ICD-11 dependence syndrome criteria; binary diagnosis (present/absent, no severity grades)
- [[tolerance-withdrawal]] — pharmacological criteria within DSM-5 and ICD-11; excluded when medications used as prescribed
- [[alcohol-use-disorder]] — most harmful substance by Nutt et al. harm score; 400 million affected globally (WHO 2024)
- [[benzodiazepines]] — frequently prescribed to older adults; 35% develop dependence with >4 weeks' use
- [[david-nutt]] — drug harm ranking study; alcohol scored 72/100 (highest)
- [[nida]] — National Institute on Drug Abuse; provides substance categories
