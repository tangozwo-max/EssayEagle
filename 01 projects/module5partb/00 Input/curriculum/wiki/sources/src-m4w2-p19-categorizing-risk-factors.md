# M4 W2 P19 — Categorizing child and adolescent risk factors

This page presents two key frameworks for conceptualizing risk factors: the lifecycle approach (which maps risks chronologically from conception through development) and the ecological systems model. It introduces distal factors (non-specific, increasing likelihood of subsequent risks) and proximal factors (directly affecting the individual). Risk factors are examined across prenatal, perinatal, infancy, school age, adolescence, and lifelong periods.

## Key concepts covered
- [[lifecycle-approach]] — chronological risk mapping from conception onward
- [[distal-factors]] — non-specific factors raising subsequent risk likelihood
- [[proximal-factors]] — factors directly affecting the individual
- [[ecological-systems-model]] — understanding risk at multiple levels
- [[bronfenbrenner-ecological-systems|Bronfenbrenner's model]] — framework for nested systems
- [[genetic-risk-factors]] — inherited conditions affecting mental health
- [[prenatal-risk]] — maternal and fetal health factors
- [[attachment-risk]] — disruption of secure attachment relationships
- [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]] — developmental disorders requiring support
