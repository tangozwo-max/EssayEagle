# M4 W4 P47 — Acknowledging Culture

Cultural context profoundly affects how mental health is perceived, stigmatised, and treated. The Social GRACES model (Burnham) provides a framework for practitioners to reflect on their own and service users' social identities, including visible/invisible and spoken/unspoken dimensions. Most psychotherapeutic interventions were developed for Western European populations and may require adaptation. Cultural variations in parenting norms, expectations of autonomy, and behavioral standards shape CAMH presentations.
