# M5 W1 P02 — What Is Meant by Addiction and Dependency

This page clarifies the historically confused terminology around addiction and dependency, tracing the evolution of DSM language from early terms like "abuse" and "dependence" to the current unified concept of substance use disorder (SUD) introduced in DSM-5 (2013). It distinguishes physical dependence (tolerance and withdrawal) from addiction (loss of control plus compulsive use), and introduces the WHO concept of dependence syndrome. West et al.'s (2024) ontology table provides a precise definitional framework.

## Key concepts covered
- [[substance-use-disorder]] — DSM-5 unified diagnostic term (mild/moderate/severe) replacing abuse and dependence
- [[substance-misuse]] — use that deviates from medical or social norms, potentially leading to SUD
- [[dependency]] — physical tolerance and withdrawal from prolonged substance use; more common than addiction
- [[dependence-syndrome]] — WHO/ICD-10 cluster of behavioral, cognitive, and physiological phenomena
- [[addiction]] — DSM-5 does not use the term; equates to severe SUD with compulsive use despite harm
- [[apa]] — American Psychiatric Association; publishers of DSM-5
- [[asam]] — American Society of Addictive Medicine; defines addiction as a chronic medical disease
