# M5 W1 P12 — Summary: Addiction and Dependency — Risk Factors

Summary page for the risk factors section of M5 W1. Consolidates key learning points across biological, social, environmental, and psychological risk domains.

## Key learning points
- Addiction can affect anyone; marginalized groups disproportionately impacted
- Comorbid anxiety, depression common in people with SUD
- Socioeconomic risk factors: ACEs, community poverty, dysfunctional family, early drug experimentation
- [[rat-park-experiment]] — social/environmental factors central to substance misuse risk
- Genetics account for 40–60% of addiction risk (multiple genes involved)
- Gene studies (AKT1, OPRM1) open pathways for prevention and personalized treatment (e.g., [[naltrexone]])
- Psychological risk: personality, emotion regulation, coping skills, comorbid depression/anxiety
- Effective interventions: detoxification, residential treatment, behavioral therapies, counseling, [[harm-reduction]]
