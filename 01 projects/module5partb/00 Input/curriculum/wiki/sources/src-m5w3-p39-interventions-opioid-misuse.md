# M5 W3 P39 – Interventions for opioid misuse

- [[methadone]] is a full mu opioid receptor agonist and NMDA receptor antagonist; its long half-life maintains stable drug levels and reduces dosing frequency, but also risks accumulation leading to respiratory depression — careful monitoring is essential.
- [[buprenorphine]] is a partial mu opioid receptor agonist and kappa receptor partial agonist; its high receptor binding affinity and long duration provide pain relief while reducing euphoria and respiratory depression compared to full agonists.
- [[lofexidine]] is an alpha-2 adrenergic receptor agonist that reduces norepinephrine release, alleviating sympathetically mediated withdrawal symptoms (anxiety, sweating, muscle aches, agitation); it enables shorter-duration withdrawal than methadone or buprenorphine.
- First-line treatments for opioid detoxification are methadone or buprenorphine; lofexidine is an alternative under NICE guidelines for those who decline opioid agonists, choose a shorter detox timeline, or have mild/uncertain dependence.
- Jan's case study illustrates how chronic pain managed with opioids can develop into SUD; a five-pillar pain management plan (psychosocial engagement, mobility, weight management, substance use treatment, pharmacological support) is recommended.
