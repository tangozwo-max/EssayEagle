# M4 W2 P23 — Socioeconomic status as a risk factor

Evidence shows children from socioeconomically disadvantaged backgrounds are 2-3 times more likely to develop mental health disorders. Low household and parental income are strongest predictors. The page discusses implications of poverty (food insecurity, housing instability, limited access to resources) and potential benefits of cash transfer programs.

## Key concepts covered
- [[socioeconomic-status]] — SES, income, educational attainment, occupational status
- [[poverty]] — economic deprivation and related hardships
- [[household-income]] — primary predictor of mental health risk in children
- [[food-insecurity]] — lack of reliable access to adequate nutrition
- [[housing-instability]] — unstable or inadequate housing
- [[social-deprivation]] — neighbourhood disadvantage and limited resources
- [[cash-transfer-programs]] — government financial support as prevention
