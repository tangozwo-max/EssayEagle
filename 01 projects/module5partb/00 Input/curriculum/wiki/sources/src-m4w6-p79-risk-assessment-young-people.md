# M4 W6 P79 — Mental Health Risk Assessment for Young People

Risk assessment evaluates harm to self (self-harm, suicide, self-neglect), harm to others (aggression/violence), and harm from others (abuse, neglect, grooming, exploitation). All healthcare providers assessing children need competency—not just mental health specialists. Children at risk most commonly present to emergency services and primary care first. Risk assessment tools are consistently less accurate than clinical judgment—structured clinical judgment is the recommended approach. Risk cannot be entirely eliminated; positive risk management weighs benefits and harms. The risk assessment cycle: gather information → identify risk/protective factors → formulate → management plan → share information → review.
