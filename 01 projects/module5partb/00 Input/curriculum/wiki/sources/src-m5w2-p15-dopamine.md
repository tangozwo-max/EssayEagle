# M5 W2 P15 — Dopamine

Detailed coverage of dopamine's role in addiction. Three dopamine pathways are described (mesocortical, nigrostriatal, mesolimbic), with the mesolimbic pathway identified as most relevant to addiction. The D1/D2 receptor distinction is explained: D1 receptors are Gs-coupled and activate adenylate cyclase (raising cAMP), while D2 receptors are Gi-coupled and inhibit it (lowering cAMP). The four-step dopamine-addiction link is traced: reward → habit → surges (from drugs) → cravings. Drugs produce far larger dopamine surges than natural rewards, leading to receptor loss and reduced sensitivity.

## Key concepts covered
- [[mesolimbic-pathway]] — the central dopamine pathway in addiction (nucleus accumbens)
- [[d1-d2-receptors]] — opposing effects on adenylate cyclase and cAMP
- [[camp-pathway]] — secondary messenger central to receptor signalling and tolerance
- [[nucleus-accumbens]] — dopamine terminus in the reward circuit
- [[dopamine]] — excitatory/inhibitory; reward, motivation, motor control
- [[gaba]] — GABAergic neurons receive dopamine signals in the nucleus accumbens
- [[reward-system]] — activated by natural rewards and amplified by drugs
- [[cravings]] — persistent conditioning of drug-seeking behaviour
