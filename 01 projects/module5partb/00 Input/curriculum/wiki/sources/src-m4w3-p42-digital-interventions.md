# M4 W3 P42 — Digital interventions

Overview of digital mental health solutions developed particularly during COVID-19 lockdowns. Describes benefits (improved access, early intervention, increased supply) and challenges (privacy, therapeutic relationship, digital divide) of digital interventions. Covers telemedicine, online counseling, peer support platforms, mental health apps, and guided interventions. Emphasises guiding principles of collaboration, clarity, and coordination for effective digital mental health services.

## Key concepts covered
- [[digital-mental-health]] — technology-based mental health interventions and services
- [[telemedicine]] — remote healthcare delivery via video or app-based communication
- [[online-counseling]] — counseling or psychotherapy delivered via digital platforms
- [[peer-support-platforms]] — digital spaces for mutual support and shared experiences
- [[mental-health-apps]] — applications for tracking mood, guided interventions, support
- [[artificial-intelligence-mental-health]] — AI and chatbots for mental health support
- [[digital-privacy-security]] — data protection and secure communication in digital health
- [[digital-divide]] — unequal access to digital technology and services
- [[therapeutic-relationship]] — importance of trust and connection in digital contexts
- [[digital-evidence-base]] — need for quality evidence for digital interventions
