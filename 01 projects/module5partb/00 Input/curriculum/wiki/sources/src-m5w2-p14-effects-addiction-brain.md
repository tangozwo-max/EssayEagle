# M5 W2 P14 — The Effects of Addiction and Dependency on the Brain

Introductory page for Week 2, framing addiction as a disease that hijacks the brain's dopamine system. Dr. Malcolm Horn (Director of Mental Health Services, Rimrock) provides the opening quote. Four brain regions are identified as especially vulnerable to addictive substances: the prefrontal cortex, extended amygdala, basal ganglia, and brain stem. Each region contributes differently to the development and maintenance of addiction. The page also sets the learning outcomes for the week.

## Key concepts covered
- [[prefrontal-cortex]] — decision-making, self-control; last to mature; hijacked by SUD
- [[extended-amygdala]] — stress, anxiety, irritability; heightened in withdrawal
- [[basal-ganglia]] — reward circuit, habit formation; over-activated by drugs
- [[brain-stem]] — basic life functions; depressed by opioids, risking death
- [[dopamine]] — the hijacked neurotransmitter central to addiction
- [[addiction]] — framed as a disease of the survival/reward system
