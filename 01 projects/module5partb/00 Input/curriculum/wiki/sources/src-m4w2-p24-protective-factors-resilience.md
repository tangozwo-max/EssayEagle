# M4 W2 P24 — Protective factors and resilience

Resilience emerges from complex interactions between the nature of adversity, the child's qualities and experiences, and their relationships and environment. Rather than focusing on building individual resilience to manage adverse conditions, emphasis should shift to improving the child's environment to reduce the need for resilience. Notes the contentious nature of "resilience" terminology.
