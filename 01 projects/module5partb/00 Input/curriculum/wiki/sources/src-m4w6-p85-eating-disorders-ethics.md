# M4 W6 P85 — Ethical Challenges in Pediatric Eating Disorders

Pediatric eating disorders (anorexia nervosa, bulimia, BED) raise ethical challenges: involuntary treatment decisions, competency and consent, parental involvement, and confidentiality. Case study: Emma, 14, with severe anorexia — malnourishment impairs cognitive decision-making, reducing capacity to appraise severity. Medical team applies Gillick competence and, finding capacity impaired, proceeds with involuntary treatment per NICE guidance (Mental Health Act 1983/2007 or Children Act 1989) with parental consent.
