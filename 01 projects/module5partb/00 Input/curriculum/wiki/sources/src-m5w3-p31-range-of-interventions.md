# M5 W3 P31 – The range of interventions

- Five overlapping care frameworks guide addiction services: [[trauma-informed-care]], culturally competent care, responsive care, appropriate care, and [[patient-centred-care]].
- The [[transtheoretical-model]] (Prochaska, DiClemente, Norcross) describes six stages of the change journey: precontemplation, contemplation, preparation, action, maintenance, and relapse; relapses are expected and should be responded to without judgment.
- Criticism of the transtheoretical model includes that behaviors may be too multifaceted for single stages, individuals in the same stage may differ significantly, and change may be continuous rather than sequential.
- The [[matched-care-model]] (Scotland) allocates the right intensity of intervention from the outset rather than stepping up only after failure, and is grounded in the Psychological Therapies Matrix.
- When creating care plans, three elements must be distinguished: intervention (what is being delivered), practitioner (who delivers it), and situation (setting and context — inpatient, outpatient, community, peer support, third sector).
