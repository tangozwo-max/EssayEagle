# M5 W2 P25 — Cocaine

Cocaine is derived from South American coca plant leaves, available as powder or crack. Its primary mechanism is blocking the dopamine transporter (DAT) in the limbic system, preventing dopamine reuptake and causing a large dopamine build-up in the nucleus accumbens, producing euphoria. With receptor desensitisation, pleasure from normal activities diminishes. Cocaine also affects serotonin, adrenaline, and noradrenaline. The hippocampus and extended amygdala form cue-memory associations. Cocaine impairs the frontal cortex, reducing impulse control. It stimulates cortisol release via the HPA axis, causing learning/memory deficits and increasing relapse risk (Fox et al.). Structural effects: cocaine causes NAc dendrite growth, giving hippocampus/amygdala/frontal cortex more influence. Altered gene expression in the NAc (glutamate, natural opioids) accumulates with each use.
