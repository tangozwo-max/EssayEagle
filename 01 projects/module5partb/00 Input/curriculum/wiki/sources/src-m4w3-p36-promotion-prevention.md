# M4 W3 P36 — Mental health promotion and prevention

This page distinguishes between mental health promotion and prevention, two key elements of a public health approach to child and adolescent mental health. Promotion focuses on optimising positive mental health across populations, while prevention aims to reduce the occurrence of mental health conditions by addressing risk and protective factors. The page outlines methods for promoting mental health through early childhood interventions, skill-building, and community-focused programs, and describes prevention efforts at different levels (universal, selective, indicated, treatment).

## Key concepts covered
- [[mental-health-promotion]] — intervening to optimise positive mental health before conditions arise
- [[mental-health-prevention]] — minimising mental health conditions by addressing determinants
- [[positive-mental-health]] — good mental health as a state to be actively promoted, not just absence of illness
- [[youth-development-programs]] — strength-based programs building life skills and positive relationships
- [[mental-health-literacy]] — awareness and understanding of mental health and mental health conditions
- [[school-based-interventions]] — mental health promotion programs delivered in schools
- [[community-development-mental-health]] — programs supporting community links and social support
- [[protective-factors]] — factors to be encouraged and maintained in promotion efforts
- [[risk-factors]] — factors addressed in prevention efforts
- [[ptsd|post-traumatic stress disorder]] — example of condition requiring treatment when prevention fails
