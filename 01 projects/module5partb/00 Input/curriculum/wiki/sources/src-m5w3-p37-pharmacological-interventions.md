# M5 W3 P37 – Pharmacological interventions

- Pharmacological interventions currently have regulatory guidelines only for opioid use disorder and alcohol use disorder; no approved medications exist for stimulant (cocaine, methamphetamine) or cannabis use disorders.
- For behavioral addictions (e.g., gambling disorder), evidence suggests nonpharmacological interventions are more appropriate than pharmacological ones.
- Core principles: SUD is a chronic disorder requiring continuous monitoring; treatment plans must be individualized (considering substance type, severity, comorbidities, trauma history); medications support withdrawal management, craving reduction, and comorbidity treatment.
- Pharmacological interventions should always be embedded in a holistic care plan including psychosocial and community support; accessibility, acceptability, and affordability are essential for engagement.
- Benzodiazepine withdrawal is a significant challenge in multiple countries (especially the US); severe symptoms including seizures require medically supervised tapering.
