# M4 W4 P59 — Systemic Family Therapy

Systemic (family) therapy views individuals as embedded in interconnected systems and targets family dynamics. Meta-analyses show medium-to-large effect sizes (about two-thirds of cases improve). Key modalities: child-parent psychotherapy, attachment and bio-behavioral catch-up, video-feedback intervention to promote positive parenting (VIPP), and Circle of Security for disorganised attachment. Parent-child interaction therapy (PCIT) significantly reduces physical abuse. Multisystemic therapy (MST) addresses adolescent conduct disorder with tailored plans and small caseloads. TF-CBT is the best-supported treatment for sexually abused children. Family therapy shows 50–90% weight recovery rates for adolescent anorexia nervosa.
