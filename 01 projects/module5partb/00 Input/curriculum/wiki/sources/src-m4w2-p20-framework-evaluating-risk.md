# M4 W2 P20 — Framework for evaluating risk

A comprehensive framework categorizing risk factors across multiple domains: child factors (physical health, emotional wellbeing, education), family factors, peer factors, social/community factors, professional factors, organizational factors, and government factors. Demonstrates that mental health risk operates at individual, family, community, and systemic levels.

## Key concepts covered
- [[child-risk-factors]] — individual, developmental, health-related risks
- [[family-risk-factors]] — parental, relational, resource-based risks
- [[peer-risk-factors]] — social exclusion, bullying, peer-group influences
- [[social-community-risk]] — deprivation, poverty, lack of services
- [[professional-risk-factors]] — quality of care delivery
- [[organizational-risk-factors]] — systemic, institutional barriers
- [[government-risk-factors]] — policy, resource allocation, inequality
