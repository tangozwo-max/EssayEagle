# M4 W6 P80 — Policy and Legislation to Inform Risk Assessment (UK)

Gillick competence: a child under 16 can consent to medical treatment if they demonstrate sufficient maturity and understanding — assessed on age, maturity, mental capacity, understanding, and ability to communicate a reasoned decision. Fraser guidelines apply specifically to contraception/sexual health — five criteria including that the young person cannot be persuaded to involve parents, and treatment is in their best interest. Sexual activity under 13 always triggers a child protection referral. Repeated STIs or pregnancy terminations may indicate CSE. Practitioners should always encourage parental involvement but explore reluctance sensitively.
