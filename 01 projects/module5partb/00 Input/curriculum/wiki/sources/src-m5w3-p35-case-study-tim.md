# M5 W3 P35 – Case study: Tim (gambling disorder)

- Tim illustrates the four phases of [[gambling-disorder]] (Illinois Institute for Addiction Recovery model): winning (big win, cognitive distortion of skill), losing (preoccupation, solitary gambling, debt), desperation (chasing losses), and hopeless (shame, criminal risk, comorbid alcohol use).
- Screening used the Problem Gambling Severity Index (PGSI) administered collaboratively with the primary care practitioner.
- Initial intervention: brief [[motivational-interviewing]] to identify Tim's motivation for change; referral to a regional gambling support service.
- [[harm-reduction]] strategies included self-exclusion registration for in-person and online gambling, and gambling blocking software.
- [[cognitive-behavioural-therapy]] targeted automatic gambling thoughts, cognitive distortions (e.g., illusion of skill in random games), and used guided imagery to rehearse alternative responses to gambling cues.
- Mindfulness training increased Tim's awareness of bodily reactions and cognitive processes related to gambling urges; relapses occurred but were managed with rapid re-engagement with coping strategies.
