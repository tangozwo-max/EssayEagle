# M5 W1 P01 — Addiction and Dependency: Definitions and Concepts

This introductory page uses two case vignettes — Suzanne (painkiller addiction following a car accident) and Laura (antidepressant dependence following postnatal depression and relationship breakdown) — to illustrate the distinction between addiction and dependency. It also introduces Dr. Nora Volkow's view that addiction is not a moral failing but a complex interaction of genetics, environment, and exposure.

## Key concepts covered
- [[addiction]] — harmful loss of control with cravings and dysfunctional recognition of consequences
- [[dependency]] — physical adaptation to a substance producing tolerance and withdrawal
- [[nora-volkow]] — NIDA Director; addiction framed as neurobiological, not moral, failure
