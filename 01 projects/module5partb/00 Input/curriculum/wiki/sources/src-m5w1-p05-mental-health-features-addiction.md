# M5 W1 P05 — Mental Health Features of Addiction and Compulsive Behavior

This page examines the strong bidirectional link between substance use disorders and mental health conditions, noting that approximately half of people with a mental health condition will also develop a SUD and vice versa. A WHO table details the acute mental health effects of intoxication across eight substance classes. A case study explores the particular comorbidity between schizophrenia and tobacco use, connecting nicotinic acetylcholine receptor abnormalities to elevated smoking rates. The page also covers compulsive behaviors (gambling, gaming, internet use) as co-occurring with anxiety, OCD, and substance use, and discusses risk factors for developing compulsive behaviors.

## Key concepts covered
- [[comorbidity]] — SUD + mental health co-occurrence; ~50% bidirectional prevalence
- [[substance-intoxication]] — acute mental health features by substance class (WHO table)
- [[nicotinic-acetylcholine-receptors]] — lower levels in schizophrenia; linked to elevated tobacco use and addiction
- [[internet-addiction]] — on the rise; psychological risk factors: personality traits and emotion regulation
- [[compulsive-behavior]] — risk factors include genetics, reserved temperament, childhood trauma, low mood
