# M4 W4 P53 — Behavioral Disorders

Behavioral disorders under DSM-5 fall in the "disruptive, impulse-control, and conduct disorders" category. Key disorders covered: ODD (angry/irritable mood + argumentative/defiant behavior; often co-occurs with ADHD), conduct disorder (repetitive pattern violating others' rights), intermittent explosive disorder, pyromania, and kleptomania. Diagnosis is complicated by overlapping symptoms, developmental variability, and subjective assessment. Parenting interventions (8–16 sessions, NICE/WHO endorsed) have the strongest evidence for ODD; teacher-focused and CBT interventions show mixed results. Conduct disorder context matters — the diagnosis should not be applied in threatening environments where behavior is near-normative.
