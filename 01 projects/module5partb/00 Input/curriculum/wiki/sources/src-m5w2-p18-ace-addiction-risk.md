# M5 W2 P18 — Addiction Risk in People Who Have Experienced ACE

Explores the neurobiological pathways linking adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) to increased addiction susceptibility. Three key signalling molecules mediate this link: oxytocin (social bonding; early life shapes long-term oxytocin function), dopamine (ACEs alter dopamine transporter binding, baseline levels, stress-induced release), and glucocorticoids/cortisol (ACE-linked HPA axis dysregulation). Structural brain changes from ACE include hippocampal/amygdala volume reduction, locus coeruleus hyperactivity, and prefrontal cortex deficits. The Strathearn et al. diagram summarises the receptor-level changes. Intergenerational implications: parental addiction impairs caregiving, perpetuating the ACE cycle.
