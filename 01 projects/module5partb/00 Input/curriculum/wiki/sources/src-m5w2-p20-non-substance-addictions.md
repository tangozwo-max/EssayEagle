# M5 W2 P20 — Non-Substance-Related Addictive Disorders

Covers addictions to activities (gambling, gaming, shopping, sex) that produce brain changes similar to substance use disorders. DSM-5 recognises only gambling disorder as a standalone diagnosis; ICD-11 recognises both gambling and gaming. Gambling disorder risk traits include impulsivity, preference for games of chance, and co-occurring SUD — present even before disorder onset. Gambling affects dopamine reward systems across memory/learning, motivation, inhibitory control, and reward salience circuits. The DSM-5 diagnostic criteria table is reviewed (preoccupation, lying, tolerance, withdrawal, chasing losses, etc.). Neurochemical activation from gambling causes neurocognitive impairment and reduced emotional flexibility.
