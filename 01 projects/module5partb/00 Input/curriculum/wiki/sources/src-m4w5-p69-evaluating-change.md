# M4 W5 P69 — Evaluating Change

Gee et al. systematic review identified factors for successful implementation of psychological interventions in schools, arranged in 11 themes using an ecological framework (Bronfenbrenner-based). Most influential factors: intervention acceptability and practicality; organisational capacity (staff-facilitator relationships, leadership support, school environment); quality of training and supervision; provider ability to identify eligible students. Community factors include stigma, mental health literacy, and health/education system priorities. Group delivery can enhance acceptability through peer support.
