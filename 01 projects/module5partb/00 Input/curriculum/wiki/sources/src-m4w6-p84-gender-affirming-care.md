# M4 W6 P84 — Gender-Affirming Care

Gender dysphoria (incongruence between expressed and assigned gender) is not a mental health condition; gender incongruence is classified in ICD-11. Referrals to GIDS rose from 94/year (2009–10) to ~50/week. Transgender youth experience disproportionately high rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidality. Medical interventions include puberty blockers (GnRH analogues), hormone therapies, and surgery. The Cass Review examined NHS GIDS quality and safety. Debate centres on balancing mental health risks of unsupported gender incongruence against unknown long-term risks of medical intervention.

## Key concepts covered
- [[gender-dysphoria]] — distress from incongruence between expressed and assigned gender
- [[gender-affirming-care]] — spectrum from social transition to puberty blockers, hormones, surgery
- [[puberty-blockers]] — GnRH analogues suppressing sex hormone release; contention around long-term effects
- [[cass-review]] — UK national review of GIDS care quality and safety
- [[detransitioning]] — return to birth gender; debated prevalence and clinical significance
