# M4 W5 P64 — Historical Context of Mental Health in Schools

Traces the history of child mental health in education from pioneering figures (Montessori, Aichhorn, A.S. Neill, Susan Isaacs, Anna Freud) to UK policy milestones. Montessori emphasised child-centred learning; Aichhorn used psychoanalytic principles for at-risk youth (reparation over punishment); Neill founded Summerhill prioritising freedom; Isaacs blended psychoanalysis with nursery education. UK policy milestones: 1998 SEAL curriculum; 2014 Mental Health and Behaviour report; 2017 Transforming CAMH provision; 2019 Children's Commissioner estimate of 1 million children needing support.

## Key concepts covered
- [[montessori-method]] — child-centred, holistic educational approach addressing developmental needs
- [[seal-curriculum]] — Social and Emotional Aspects of Learning; UK 1998 curriculum initiative
- [[school-mental-health-policy]] — UK policy trajectory from 1955 to present
- [[reparation-approach]] — Aichhorn's principle: treat at-risk youth as individuals facing hardship, not criminals
