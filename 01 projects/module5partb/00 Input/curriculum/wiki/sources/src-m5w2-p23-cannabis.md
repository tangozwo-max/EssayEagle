# M5 W2 P23 — Cannabis

Cannabis (Cannabaceae family) contains THC and CBD as primary active compounds. About 19% of Americans used cannabis in 2021; ~3 in 10 who use it develop cannabis use disorder, with higher risk before age 18. THC primarily binds to CB1 receptors throughout the CNS; CBD to CB2 (peripheral/immune). Cannabis modulates endocannabinoids, GABA, glutamate, and serotonin — altering self-awareness networks, reducing anxiety, impairing memory, and affecting motivation. Cannabis binds to lipids, remaining detectable long after use; withdrawal is therefore slow-onset. The addiction cycle for cannabis: binge/intoxication via nucleus accumbens dopamine release; withdrawal via extended amygdala (irritability, anxiety, mood swings); preoccupation via prefrontal cortex dysfunction impairs executive functioning.
