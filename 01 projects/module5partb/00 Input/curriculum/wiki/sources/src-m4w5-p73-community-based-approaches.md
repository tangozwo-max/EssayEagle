# M4 W5 P73 — Community-Based Approaches to Mental Health

Community mental health addresses social determinants of health (unemployment, inequality, low income) through proactive, asset-focused approaches. Community psychology emerged in the USA in the 1960s from civil rights and feminist movements. Three core practices: relational (trust-building, peer support), participatory (community-based participatory research; CBPR), and strength-based (identifying community capabilities). Co-production: equal partnership between service providers and community members. Important distinction: CMHTs are statutory/clinical; community-based approaches operate outside statutory services with more flexibility.

## Key concepts covered
- [[community-psychology]] — social justice–oriented discipline; emerged 1960s USA; beyond clinical individual focus
- [[co-production]] — equal partnership between practitioners and service users in design/delivery/evaluation
- [[cbpr]] — community-based participatory research: researchers and community as equal partners
- [[strength-based-practice]] — identifying community assets rather than deficits
