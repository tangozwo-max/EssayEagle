# M5 W3 P38 – Medically assisted withdrawal

- [[medically-assisted-withdrawal]] (MAW, also called detoxification) supports a person through the physiological withdrawal that follows cessation of a substance; in severe cases (especially alcohol), withdrawal can be life-threatening (seizures, delirium tremens), while opioid withdrawal is highly distressing but not directly fatal.
- Standard MAW components: thorough medical assessment, gradual dose reduction, medication to manage withdrawal symptoms, and continuous medical supervision.
- Inpatient MAW is indicated for severe substance use disorder or significant comorbid health problems requiring close monitoring; community/outpatient MAW may be appropriate for lower-risk individuals, with medication dispensed via primary care or pharmacy.
- Specialist addiction training is associated with better MAW outcomes, but non-specialists can deliver MAW if they follow appropriate clinical guidance.
- After MAW, immediate support programs for substance-seeking behaviors and cravings are essential to prevent relapse.
