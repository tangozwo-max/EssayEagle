# M5 W1 P07 — Addiction and Dependency: Risk Factors

This page introduces the multi-factorial nature of addiction risk, covering genetics, socioeconomic background, psychological traits, neurodivergent conditions (ADHD), and comorbid mental health conditions as contributing factors. The historical view of addiction as a moral failing is contrasted with modern neurobiological understanding. The Rat Park experiment by Canadian psychologist Bruce K. Alexander is presented as a landmark study demonstrating that social and environmental factors — not just drug pharmacology — are central to addiction risk. The finding that socioeconomic deprivation, particularly low education, significantly elevates alcohol-attributable mortality is also highlighted.
