# M5 W3 P36 – Summary for multidisciplinary care for addiction and dependency

- An [[multidisciplinary-team]] offers holistic, person-centred treatment for addiction and dependency; no single intervention is sufficient alone.
- Barriers and facilitators to recovery operate at personal, structural, and social levels — acknowledging all three is essential for effective care planning.
- [[brief-interventions]] can be effective in non-specialist settings, delivered by non-mental health professionals, to reduce substance-seeking behaviors.
- The evidence base for nonpharmacological interventions varies widely; CBT, MI, mindfulness, and contingency management have the strongest evidence for SUD.
