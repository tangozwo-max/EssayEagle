# M5 W1 P04 — Definitions of Behavioral Addiction and Compulsive Behavior

This page examines the distinction between compulsion and addiction, then applies this to behavioral addictions. Only gambling disorder is recognized as a standalone behavioral addiction in DSM-5 (classified there since 2013, previously an impulse-control disorder). ICD-11 additionally recognizes gaming disorder and compulsive sexual behavior. Dr. Mark Griffiths' six criteria for behavioral addiction — building on R.I. Brown's earlier framework — are presented as the field's leading diagnostic model. Neuroimaging evidence supports reclassifying gambling as an addiction due to changes in neurotransmitter systems identical to those seen in substance addictions.

## Key concepts covered
- [[behavioral-addiction]] — ICD-11 category; gambling and gaming disorders; compulsive sexual behavior
- [[compulsive-behavior]] — behavior reducing anxiety or distress but not always providing pleasure; distinct from addiction
- [[gambling-disorder]] — only DSM-5 recognized behavioral addiction; reclassified from impulse-control disorder in 2013
- [[griffiths-brown-criteria]] — six criteria: salience, euphoria, tolerance, withdrawal, conflict, relapse/reinstatement
- [[mark-griffiths]] — leading expert in behavioral addiction; developed six-criteria model
