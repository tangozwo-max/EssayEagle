# M4 W3 P37 — Screening

This page outlines mental health screening in children and young people as a crucial step for identifying those who may need further psychological support. It explains the purpose of screening tools (standardised questionnaires to identify potential mental health issues), describes six common screening instruments, and emphasises the importance of conducting screening as part of structured conversations rather than standalone measures. The page also addresses ethical challenges in screening, particularly in developing countries, including concerns about cultural appropriateness, availability of follow-up services, and stigma.

## Key concepts covered
- [[mental-health-screening]] — using standardised questions to identify potential mental health concerns
- [[screening-tools]] — validated instruments for assessing symptoms and concerns
- [[sdq|Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire]] — brief screening tool for behavioural and emotional disorders (3–17 years)
- [[psc|Pediatric Symptom Checklist]] — identifies psychosocial issues affecting child functioning (4–16 years)
- [[cbcl|Child Behavior Checklist]] — assesses behavioural and emotional problems (6–18 years)
- [[rcads|Revised Child Anxiety and Depression Scale]] — evaluates anxiety and depression disorders (8–18 years)
- [[beck-youth-inventories]] — evaluates emotional and social impairment (7–18 years)
- [[m-and-ms|Me and My School]] — school-based mental health measure (8–19 years)
- [[clinical-cut-off]] — threshold score indicating need for further assessment
- [[self-reporting]] — child/young person self-completion of screening measures
- [[screening-ethical-challenges]] — concerns about cultural appropriateness, stigma, and availability of support
- [[cultural-competence-screening]] — need for tools appropriate to population and culturally responsive screening practices
