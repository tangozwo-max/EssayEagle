# M5 W2 P24 — Benzodiazepines

Benzodiazepines (BZD) are among the most prescribed medicines worldwide, used short-term for anxiety, insomnia, and alcohol withdrawal management. Mechanism: BZD increase GABA-A channel opening in the CNS, causing hyperpolarisation and a calming/sedative effect. Misuse risk is highest in combination with opioids — BZD prolong and intensify opioid effects, causing brainstem depression and drug-related death. Schmitz's US data identifies non-Hispanic white young adults (18–35) as highest-risk group, with strong comorbidity with psychiatric disorders and antisocial personality disorder. Tolerance and dependence develop rapidly; abrupt withdrawal can cause fatal convulsions.
