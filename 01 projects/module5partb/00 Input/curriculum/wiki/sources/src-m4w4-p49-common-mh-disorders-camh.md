# M4 W4 P49 — Common Mental Health Disorders in Children and Adolescents

Introductory page establishing that anxiety is the most common mental health disorder in children and adolescents, followed by depression and behavioral disorders. Eating disorders and psychosis also present during youth but require specialist care. The page sets context for deeper coverage of the three main categories in subsequent pages.

## Key concepts covered
- [[anxiety-in-children]] — most prevalent CAMH disorder globally (~6.5% of children)
- [[depression-in-adolescents]] — increases sharply in adolescence, especially in females
- [[behavioral-disorders-camh]] — ODD, conduct disorder, IED
