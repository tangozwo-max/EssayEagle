# M4 W4 P56 — Adapting CBT for Children and Adolescents

CBT for children requires developmental adaptation. Under-7s can engage with CBT using visual aids, play, and stories. Ages 7–11 benefit from mixed verbal/nonverbal techniques; metaphors (e.g., "anger volcano") aid understanding. Adolescents benefit from Socratic dialogue (objective empiricism) and collaborative partnership emphasising autonomy and self-efficacy, with flexible parental involvement. Reciprocal CBT formulation addresses how multiple people's thoughts/feelings/behaviors influence each other — useful in social anxiety within peer/family contexts. Initial phase of CBT always includes psychoeducation.
