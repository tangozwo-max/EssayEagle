# M4 W2 P18 — Child and adolescent risk factors

This page introduces the concept that certain groups of children and adolescents face heightened risk for poor mental health outcomes. Understanding these risk factors enables early intervention. Risk can operate directly (as with fetal alcohol spectrum disorder) or indirectly through complex pathways (such as parental trauma affecting parenting capacity). Protective factors like secure attachment and met developmental needs help create resilience.

## Key concepts covered
- [[risk-factors]] — characteristics increasing likelihood of poor mental health outcomes
- [[protective-factors]] — aspects of environment and personal qualities that buffer against risk
- [[resilience]] — ability to adapt to and recover from adversity
- [[fetal-alcohol-spectrum-disorder|FASD]] — neurodevelopmental condition from prenatal alcohol exposure
- [[secure-attachment]] — foundational protective factor in early childhood
