# M5 W1 P06 — Summary: Addiction and Dependency — Concepts

Summary page for the first section of M5 W1. Recaps the key distinctions between addiction (loss of control, compulsive use despite harm), dependence (physical tolerance and withdrawal, more common), and substance use disorder (DSM-5 umbrella term, mild to severe). Reinforces that psychoactive substances alter consciousness and mood; alcohol is most harmful overall. Behavioral addictions share characteristics with substance addiction; approximately half of people with mental health conditions will also experience a SUD.

## Key learning points
- Addiction = harmful loss of control, cravings, dysfunctional harm recognition
- Dependence = physical adaptation (tolerance + withdrawal); more common than addiction
- DSM-5 replaces "addiction" with [[substance-use-disorder]] (mild/moderate/severe)
- [[psychoactive-substance]] = alters consciousness, mood, thinking
- Alcohol is the most harmful substance to individual and society
- [[behavioral-addiction]] shares features with substance addiction (intense urges, negative consequences)
- ~50% of people with mental health conditions experience comorbid SUD
