# M4 W5 P67 — Mental Health Interventions in Schools

Whole school approach (WSA) to mental health means developing policies and practices that promote mental health for pupils, parents/caregivers, and staff. Public Health England and DfE identified eight principles: ethos and environment, curriculum/teaching/learning, student voice, staff development, identifying needs/monitoring impact, working with parents/carers, targeted support, leadership/management. WHO's Health Promoting Schools model covers formal curriculum, school ethos/environment, and community engagement. NICE recommends trauma-informed and neuro-affirming practices. Three strategies for comprehensive school health: teams in schools, identifying needs, and community partnerships.
