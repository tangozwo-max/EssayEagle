## Concepts
*Theorien, Konzepte, Modelle, Begriffe*

- [[attachment-theory]] — Bowlby's foundational theory of infant-caregiver bonds shaping lifelong relationships *(M4 W1)*
- [[attachment-styles]] — Four patterns of attachment security assessed via the Strange Situation Procedure *(M4 W1)*
- [[internal-working-model]] — Mental representations of self, others, and relationships formed through early attachment *(M4 W1)*
- [[mentalizing]] — Ability to understand mental states (thoughts, feelings, intentions) underlying own and others' behaviour *(M4 W1)*
- [[still-face-experiment]] — Tronick's 1978 paradigm demonstrating infant distress in response to caregiver unresponsiveness *(M4 W1)*
- [[infant-mental-health]] — Infant's capacity to form relationships, manage emotions, explore environment, and communicate *(M4 W1)*
- [[serve-and-return]] — Reciprocal caregiver-child interaction analogous to tennis that builds neural circuits *(M4 W1)*
- [[toxic-stress]] — Excessive or prolonged stress activation without protective relationships that disrupts brain development *(M4 W1)*
- [[executive-function]] — Mental skills for planning, prioritisation, impulse control, and sustained focus developed across infancy to mid-20s *(M4 W1)*
- [[working-memory]] — Foundational executive function: mental workspace for processing and storing information short-term *(M4 W1)*
- [[developmental-milestones]] — Age-specific skills most children demonstrate across physical, cognitive, language, social, emotional domains *(M4 W1)*
- [[developmental-domains]] — Five interconnected domains of child development: physical, cognitive, language/communication, social, emotional *(M4 W1)*
- [[object-permanence]] — Understanding that objects continue to exist when not visible; emerges around 12 months *(M4 W1)*
- [[theory-of-mind]] — Ability to understand that others have thoughts, beliefs, and perspectives different from one's own *(M4 W1)*
- [[separation-anxiety]] — Infant distress upon separation from primary caregiver; normal developmental milestone that peaks around 12 months *(M4 W1)*
- [[social-referencing]] — Infant's use of caregiver's emotional expression to determine appropriate response in ambiguous situations *(M4 W1)*
- [[joint-attention]] — Shared focus between infant and caregiver on an object or event; emerges between 4-6 months *(M4 W1)*
- [[dcd]] — Developmental Coordination Disorder: persistent difficulty sequencing and executing coordinated movements *(M4 W1)*
- [[storm-and-stress]] — G. Stanley Hall's 1904 term describing adolescence as period of emotional upheaval and identity formation *(M4 W1)*
- [[brain-architecture-nspcc]] — NSPCC metaphor comparing brain development to house construction; emphasises early foundations *(M4 W1)*
- [[look-say-sing-play]] — NSPCC campaign for brain-building activities with babies/toddlers: Look, Say, Sing, Play *(M4 W1)*
- [[concrete-operational-thinking]] — Piaget's cognitive stage (~5-11 years) characterised by logical thought and concrete reasoning *(M4 W1)*
- [[risk-factors]] — Characteristics, conditions, or experiences increasing likelihood of negative mental health outcomes *(M4 W2)*
- [[protective-factors]] — Aspects of child's environment and personal qualities that buffer against risk *(M4 W2)*
- [[resilience]] — Capacity to adapt to and recover from adversity through interaction of personal qualities, relationships, and environment *(M4 W2)*
- [[resilience-child]] — Resilience in children: emerges from complex interaction of individual, relational, and environmental factors *(M4 W2)*
- [[lifecycle-approach]] — Framework for understanding risk factors chronologically from conception through development *(M4 W2)*
- [[distal-proximal-factors]] — Non-specific (distal) vs direct (proximal) factors in risk chains *(M4 W2)*
- [[fetal-alcohol-spectrum-disorder]] — FASD: neurodevelopmental condition from prenatal alcohol exposure *(M4 W2)*
- [[perinatal-depression]] — Depression during pregnancy or after birth; major risk factor for infant development *(M4 W2)*
- [[intimate-partner-violence]] — IPV: abuse affecting children through toxic stress and attachment disruption *(M4 W2)*
- [[socioeconomic-status]] — SES: major determinant of child mental health; low SES is strongest predictor *(M4 W2)*
- [[poverty]] — Economic deprivation affecting child mental health through multiple mechanisms *(M4 W2)*
- [[toxic-stress]] — Chronic stress activation in adversity without adequate support; alters threat/memory/reward systems *(M4 W2)*
- [[child-maltreatment]] — Abuse and neglect; major ACE associated with trauma and multiple neurodevelopmental conditions *(M4 W2)*
- [[neurodevelopmental-conditions]] — NDC: group of disorders with developmental onset and known/potential causes *(M4 W2)*
- [[neurodiversity]] — Natural variation in human brain functioning encompassing all people *(M4 W2)*
- [[neurodivergence]] — Established cognitive differences without always-specified cause or onset *(M4 W2)*
- [[autism]] — Autism Spectrum Disorder: neurodevelopmental condition affecting social communication and behaviour patterns *(M4 W2)*
- [[double-jeopardy]] — Compounded mental health risk when child has both NDC and adverse childhood experience *(M4 W2)*
- [[latent-vulnerability]] — Increased future mental health risk from early trauma; adaptive changes creating future difficulties *(M4 W2)*
- [[intergenerational-trauma]] — Trauma effects passing across generations through biological, psychological, social mechanisms *(M4 W2)*
- [[teratogen]] — Substance causing birth defects during pregnancy; alcohol is teratogen causing FASD *(M4 W2)*
- [[continuum-of-need]] — Framework categorising varying levels of support (universal, selective, indicated, specialist) based on need *(M4 W3)*
- [[public-mental-health]] — Organised efforts to promote mental health and prevent disorders at population level *(M4 W3)*
- [[targeted-early-help]] — Non-diagnostic services for individuals at risk of developing complex mental health needs *(M4 W3)*
- [[specialist-services]] — Intensive interventions for severe, enduring, complex needs requiring formal diagnosis *(M4 W3)*
- [[social-determinants]] — Non-medical factors influencing health outcomes (economic stability, education, environment) *(M4 W3)*
- [[spectrum-of-interventions]] — Full range of mental health options from universal to specialist services *(M4 W3)*
- [[clinical-approach-camh]] — Four-stage CAMH clinical process: assessment, formulation, intervention, evaluation *(M4 W4)*
- [[5ps-formulation]] — CBT-based formulation: Presenting, Predisposing, Precipitating, Perpetuating, Protective factors *(M4 W4)*
- [[social-graces-model]] — Burnham's framework for identity and cultural reflection in systemic therapy *(M4 W4)*
- [[depression-in-adolescents]] — Sharp rise in adolescence; stepped-care treatment; recurrence risk ~50% *(M4 W4)*
- [[anxiety-in-children]] — Most common CAMH disorder (6.5%); includes EBSA, separation anxiety, selective mutism *(M4 W4)*
- [[ebsa]] — Emotionally based school avoidance; anxiety-driven; doubled post-COVID *(M4 W4)*
- [[nssi]] — Non-suicidal self-injury; 17.7% of adolescents; strong predictor of later suicidality *(M4 W4)*
- [[oppositional-defiant-disorder]] — ODD: angry/irritable + defiant patterns; parenting interventions first-line *(M4 W4)*
- [[conduct-disorder]] — Persistent violation of rights/norms; early onset worsens prognosis *(M4 W4)*
- [[behavioral-disorders-camh]] — DSM-5 disruptive impulse-control category: ODD, CD, IED, pyromania, kleptomania *(M4 W4)*
- [[parenting-interventions]] — Skills-based 8–16 session programs; strongest evidence base for ODD *(M4 W4)*
- [[psychodynamic-therapy-children]] — Play-based unconscious exploration; Klein and Anna Freud; STPP sleeper effect *(M4 W4)*
- [[systemic-family-therapy]] — Improving family dynamics; medium-large effect sizes; PCIT, MST, TF-CBT *(M4 W4)*
- [[dbt-adolescents]] — DBT-A: emotion regulation, mindfulness, distress tolerance, interpersonal effectiveness *(M4 W4)*
- [[school-mental-health]] — Schools as key detection and intervention setting; ecosystem-based; team structures *(M4 W5)*
- [[whole-school-approach]] — WSA: eight PHE/DfE principles for school-wide mental health promotion *(M4 W5)*
- [[sel-programs]] — Social and Emotional Learning; CASEL CDI model; sustained by leadership and co-creation *(M4 W5)*
- [[community-psychology]] — Social justice–oriented; co-production; CBPR; global examples MAC-UK, Headspace *(M4 W5)*
- [[safeguarding]] — Child protection; shared responsibility; adultification and racial bias issues *(M4 W6)*
- [[adultification]] — Bias perceiving BAME children as more mature and less vulnerable *(M4 W6)*
- [[gillick-competence]] — Under-16 consent to treatment based on demonstrated maturity *(M4 W6)*
- [[fraser-guidelines]] — Five-criteria framework for contraception/sexual health without parental consent *(M4 W6)*
- [[bpd]] — Borderline personality disorder; diagnosis in under-18s ethically contested *(M4 W6)*
- [[gender-dysphoria]] — Gender incongruence distress; transgender youth at elevated mental health risk *(M4 W6)*
- [[addiction]] — Compulsive substance/behavioral engagement despite harm; requires loss of control (DSM-5 = severe SUD) *(M5 W1)*
- [[dependency]] — Physical adaptation to substance with tolerance and withdrawal; more common than addiction *(M5 W1)*
- [[substance-use-disorder]] — DSM-5 unified diagnostic term (11 criteria; mild/moderate/severe); replaced abuse and dependence *(M5 W1)*
- [[substance-misuse]] — Use deviating from medical/social norms causing harm; can lead to SUD *(M5 W1)*
- [[dependence-syndrome]] — ICD-11/WHO binary diagnosis; 6 criteria; present or absent (no severity grades) *(M5 W1)*
- [[psychoactive-substance]] — WHO definition: alters consciousness/mood/thinking; includes alcohol, cannabis, opioids, nicotine *(M5 W1)*
- [[behavioral-addiction]] — Compulsive non-substance behavior; DSM-5: gambling only; ICD-11: gambling + gaming + compulsive sexual behavior *(M5 W1)*
- [[compulsive-behavior]] — Behavior neutralizing anxiety/distress, not necessarily rewarding; can evolve toward addiction *(M5 W1)*
- [[gambling-disorder]] — Only DSM-5 behavioral addiction; reclassified 2013 from impulse-control based on dopamine evidence *(M5 W1)*
- [[griffiths-brown-criteria]] — Six criteria for behavioral addiction: salience, euphoria, tolerance, withdrawal, conflict, relapse *(M5 W1)*
- [[tolerance-withdrawal]] — Pharmacological criteria 10-11 in DSM-5 SUD; excluded when medications used as prescribed *(M5 W1)*
- [[rat-park-experiment]] — Alexander (1970s): enriched social environment dramatically reduced drug consumption vs. isolation *(M5 W1)*
- [[genetic-risk-addiction]] — 40-60% heritability; 19 genes linked to dopamine signaling; AKT1, CHRNA5, OPRM1 key variants *(M5 W1)*
- [[naltrexone]] — FDA-approved opioid receptor blocker for OUD and AUD; OPRM1 gene predicts response *(M5 W1)*
- [[emotion-regulation]] — Impaired in SUD; controlling impulses and accessing adaptive strategies are key deficits *(M5 W1)*
- [[internet-addiction]] — Compulsive internet use; 4-55% European prevalence; risk: introversion, poor emotion regulation *(M5 W1)*
- [[harm-reduction]] — Public health approach reducing drug use harms without requiring abstinence (e.g., needle exchange) *(M5 W1)*
- [[alcohol-use-disorder]] — Most harmful substance (Nutt et al. score 72/100); 400M affected globally (WHO 2024) *(M5 W1)*
- [[benzodiazepines]] — Sedatives; 35% dependence risk with 4+ weeks use; frequently overprescribed to older adults *(M5 W1)*
- [[stress-trauma-addiction]] — ACEs, trauma, and chronic stress as major psychological/social risk factors for SUD *(M5 W1)*
- [[impulse-control]] — Impulsivity as cross-cutting psychological vulnerability for SUD; linked to gambling and substance disorders *(M5 W1)*
- [[prefrontal-cortex]] — Last brain region to mature; executive control, decision-making; hijacked in addiction preoccupation/anticipation stage *(M5 W2)*
- [[extended-amygdala]] — Stress and negative emotion processing; drives withdrawal/negative affect stage; sensitised by ACE and chronic use *(M5 W2)*
- [[basal-ganglia]] — Reward circuit hub; habit formation; over-activated in binge/intoxication; includes nucleus accumbens *(M5 W2)*
- [[mesolimbic-pathway]] — VTA → nucleus accumbens dopamine pathway; primary reward pathway in addiction neuroscience *(M5 W2)*
- [[d1-d2-receptors]] — Opposing dopamine receptors on GABAergic neurons; D1 raises cAMP (excitatory), D2 lowers cAMP (inhibitory) *(M5 W2)*
- [[camp-pathway]] — Intracellular second messenger; central to opioid tolerance and withdrawal via adenylate cyclase and PKA *(M5 W2)*
- [[allostasis]] — Feed-forward brain adaptation shifting reward set-point; underlying mechanism of addiction escalation *(M5 W2)*
- [[anti-reward-system]] — CRF, norepinephrine, dynorphin; counter-regulates reward pathway; activated during withdrawal *(M5 W2)*
- [[corticotropin-releasing-factor]] — CRF; stress neuropeptide; key anti-reward player; CRF antagonists can block compulsive drug-taking *(M5 W2)*
- [[dynorphin]] — Endogenous kappa-opioid modulator; produces dysphoria; part of anti-reward system *(M5 W2)*
- [[locus-coeruleus]] — Brainstem norepinephrine nucleus; hyperactive in opioid withdrawal → panic, insomnia, tachycardia *(M5 W2)*
- [[neuroimaging-addiction]] — PET/fMRI/sMRI evidence for addiction-driven brain change: D2 loss, frontal hypometabolism, cortical thinning *(M5 W2)*
- [[raclopride]] — [C11]Raclopride PET radioligand; competitive D2 antagonist measuring dopamine release in vivo *(M5 W2)*
- [[ace-addiction-pathway]] — Integrated neurobiological pathway (Strathearn/Kim): oxytocin, dopamine, glucocorticoids link ACE to addiction risk *(M5 W2)*
- [[gaming-disorder]] — ICD-11 recognised behavioural addiction; shares dopamine reward circuit changes with substance disorders *(M5 W2)*
- [[cannabis]] — Cannabaceae plant; THC/CBD; CB1 receptor mechanism; ~30% of users develop CUD; slow-onset withdrawal *(M5 W2)*
- [[cannabinoid-receptors]] — CB1 (CNS/reward) and CB2 (immune); THC's primary target; G-protein inhibition of cAMP *(M5 W2)*
- [[thc-cbd]] — Delta-9-THC (psychoactive, addictive) and cannabidiol (non-psychoactive); primary cannabis compounds *(M5 W2)*
- [[cocaine]] — DAT blocker; limbic dopamine surge; frontal cortex impairment; cortisol/HPA activation; neuroimaging documented changes *(M5 W2)*
- [[methamphetamine]] — Active dopamine release + DAT blockade; glutamate neurotoxicity; white matter damage; Parkinson's-like neurodegeneration *(M5 W2)*
- [[dopamine-transporter]] — DAT; reuptake protein; blocked by cocaine; reversed by methamphetamine; downregulated in chronic use *(M5 W2)*
- [[wernicke-korsakoff]] — Thiamine deficiency syndrome from chronic alcohol use; Wernicke (acute) + Korsakoff (chronic amnesia) *(M5 W2)*
- [[alcohol-blackout]] — Alcohol-induced amnesia while conscious; fragmentary or en bloc; NMDA receptor impairment mechanism *(M5 W2)*
- [[reward-system]] — VTA → nucleus accumbens dopamine circuit; encodes reward salience and habit; hijacked by all addictive substances *(M5 W2)*
