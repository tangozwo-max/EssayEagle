## M5 W2 P20 – Non-substance-related addictive disorders

"I feel like I can handle most things, but when I see money, my brain changes."

Person experiencing gambling disorder

It is possible to become addicted to or dependent on things other than alcohol or opioids. As mentioned earlier in this module, people can also develop compulsive or addictive behaviors toward activities like shopping, gambling, gaming, or sex.

Only gambling is recognized in the DSM-5 as a condition with standalone diagnostic criteria. However, the ICD-11 recognizes both gambling and gaming as addictions.

People at risk of gambling disorder demonstrate certain traits. These include:

- increased impulsivity
- preference for games of chance
- increased incidence of substance use disorder

The risk is present before gambling disorder develops, even in children.

\[Source: Pérez\]

As with other addictive conditions, gambling can cause changes to the dopamine reward systems in the brain. Multiple systems are affected, including:

- memory and learning
- motivation and drive
- inhibitory control
- reward and salience

The effect of gambling on the brain can lead to:

- sleep deprivation
- increased stress hormones
- chronic activation of neurochemical systems

The activation of neurochemical systems can cause issues such as:

- neurocognitive impairment
- increased emotional pain or distress
- decreased flexibility and adaptability, and increased rigidity

The DSM-5 gives criteria for the diagnosis of gambling disorder. Here you can see the associated behaviors and cognitions.

| DSM-5 criteria | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Preoccupation with gambling | Unable to stop thinking about gambling and how it makes them feel. |
| Lying | Covering up the extent of the habit. |
| Tolerance | Needing bigger and longer gambling fixes. |
| Withdrawal | Craving gambling, sometimes to the detriment of relationships, work, or education. |
| Focus on lost opportunities | Brain dismisses logical information and overestimates chances of winning. |
| Chasing losses | Person always thinks they can make up losses, losing is never intolerable. |
| Not knowing when to bail out | Persisting with gambling despite warnings to the contrary. |
| Inability to stop gambling | Gambling is seen as the solution to problems. |
| Gambling to escape feelings | Avoidance of unpleasant feelings. |
