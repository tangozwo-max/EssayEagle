## M5 W1 P06 – Summary for addiction and dependency - concepts and risk factors

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- **Addiction** refers to harmful behaviors such as loss of control of substance use, with active cravings and dysfunctional recognition of negative consequences.
- **Dependence** is a state resulting from prolonged use of a substance where the body adapts to its presence, leading to tolerance and withdrawal symptoms when use is reduced or stopped. Dependence is much more common than addiction.
- In the most recent iteration of the *Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders* (DSM), the term ***addiction*** has been replaced with ***substance use disorder***, which can be classified diagnostically as mild, moderate, or severe.
- **Substance misuse** occurs when someone uses certain substances in a way that harms themselves and/or people around them and canpotentially lead to the development of substance use disorder.
- Psychoactive substances are defined by the World Health Organization (WHO) as substances that, "when consumed or administered, have the ability to change consciousness, mood or thinking processes."
- Studies on alcohol demonstrate that this substance is the most harmful to the individual person who misuses it and to society generally.
- Similar to how people addicted to a substance might feel, those with a behavioral addiction experience intense urges to engage in a particular behavior despite the negative outcomes of this behavior.
- Examples of behavioral addiction include compulsive buying, sexual behavior, internet use, gambling, and gaming.
- A strong link has been established between addiction and mental health conditions, and studies provide evidence that about half of the people who experience a mental health condition will also experience a substance use disorder, and vice versa.
