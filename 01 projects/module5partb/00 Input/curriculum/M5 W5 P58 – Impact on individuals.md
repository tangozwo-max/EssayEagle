## Impact on individuals

Fred has been writing about his gambling in his diary as part of his recovery.

Select Enter to read his words.

"I was an ordinary working bloke. I drove a taxi for a living, had a wife and two kids. Liked to watch horse racing on the TV and have a drink or two down the pub with my friends. I used to place the odd bet on the big races but nothing much. Then they put these betting machines in the bookies..."

![Locked diary](https://learn.iheed.org/static/media/diary.fb1e7e33754711047e6c.png) ![Open diary](https://learn.iheed.org/static/media/diary-revealed.ded02d4ef43af1b0967c.png)

Fred is talking to one of his support workers about his use of betting machines.

Alan

Tell me how the betting machines affected you, Fred.

Fred

It took over my life; it was like I was on crack cocaine or something.

Alan

What happened to you?

Fred

Every day I'd go and work a 12-hour shift, driving a taxi, cash in hand, and then go to the betting shop and blow the whole lot on the machines. Even after they banned me, I started gambling using apps on my phone. It's too easy these days, you don't even see the money change hands like you used to.

Alan

And then what happened?

Fred

I was gambling compulsively and I became a compulsive liar, trying to hide what I was doing from my family. My wife found out and tried to persuade me to get help, but I wouldn't accept it. I couldn't see the scale of the problem. In the end, she took the children and left after we lost our home. I had to declare bankruptcy.

Alan

That was hard for you.

Fred

Yes, I became depressed and thought about hurting myself. To get away from the bad feelings, I started to drink alcohol but soon I was addicted to that as well. I didn't know how to change what was happening. I couldn't control the cravings or see an end to it short of suicide.

As you can see from Fred's story, gambling and then alcohol use affected many areas of his life. Addiction and dependency may affect:

- mental health
- physical health, including sexual health and well-being
- economic well-being
- relationships with family, friends, and their wider society
- other impacts, such as entering the criminal justice system and housing insecurity
