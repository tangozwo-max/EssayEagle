## Summary for impacts of substance use

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Substance misuse has wide-ranging impacts on the individual, their partners, children, communities, and wider institutions.
- Individual impacts include negative physical and mental effects, economic and educational impacts, and negative impacts on relationships.
- Partners of people who use substances report dilemmas, stigma, shame, lack of support, lack of security, and feelings of hopelessness.
- Children of people who use substances can be neglected and/or abused, which negatively impacts their physical and mental well-being.
- Parental substance use can lead to children experiencing direct trauma (witnessing harm or suffering harm) and/or indirect trauma, such as age-inappropriate responsibility.
