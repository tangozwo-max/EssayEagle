## What do you think?

**What do you think might be the challenges around adapting interventions to suit a range of cultures?**

An intervention will have been developed with a specific group of participants, and therefore the information collected about reliability, validity, and effectiveness may not be valid for a different group of participants from a different culture.

Making significant changes to the intervention to better account for culture will mean that new information needs collecting to create validity and reliability data. It may be ineffective, or at worst unethical, to deliver an intervention as standard if we suspect it would not be equally relevant for people from differing cultures. Adaptations would need to be made in line with the theoretical principles of the intervention and then new data would be needed to test it.

More research is required in order to develop a comprehensive framework to support the adaptation of services to work with a range of cultures.

## Service design

Service providers that are culturally responsive can extend their reach into the communities that their service users belong to. Cultural responsiveness means that service providers can learn from and relate respectfully to cultures other than their dominant one. They are able to incorporate and respect an individual's cultural beliefs.

Service providers need to create psychologically safe services and address any unaddressed cultural needs that could act as barriers to accessing health care.

Services can be adapted so they take into account a person’s culture, language, and context to give them better meaning and value to the service user. This also can support addressing social determinants of health, structural racism, and discrimination. Reasons to adapt them include:

- making them more effective
- reducing harm from the program
- making them more acceptable to the service user
- increasing attractiveness and engagement
- improving retention
- acknowledging the influence of culture on behavior
- demonstrating respect for the cultural group, cultural knowledge, and world view
