## M5 W2 P26 – Methamphetamine

Methamphetamine is part of the amphetamine family of drugs. It is sometimes called crystal meth.

Methamphetamine is one of the most abused substances in the world. It is highly addictive and acts as a psychostimulant. Long-term use of methamphetamines can cause neurodegeneration, memory impairment, and cognitive decline.

Methamphetamine addiction causes degeneration of the neurons in the midbrain, with decreases in the levels of dopamine and dopamine transporters. Methamphetamine also causes neuronal inflammation, which also leads to neuronal degeneration and makes the brain more susceptible to neuropathology such as Parkinson's-like symptoms.

Methamphetamine can make people feel:

- exhilarated
- aroused
- alert
- paranoid
- confused
- aggressive
- disinhibited

> [!-info] -info
> **Further info:** Depending on its form, methamphetamine can be swallowed, snorted, or injected.
> 
> Smoking the purer, crystalline form of methamphetamine, known as crystal meth, produces a very intense high similar to that produced by crack cocaine, but much longer lasting.

### Mechanism of action

Methamphetamine acts as a psychostimulant by causing the release of the neurotransmitters [[dopamine]], [[serotonin]], and [[noradrenaline|norepinephrine]]. As with other addictive substances, the high from methamphetamine is largely caused by dopamine release. Similar to [[cocaine]], it inhibits the reuptake of dopamine by interfering with the [[dopamine-transporter|dopamine transporters]].

These increased levels of specific neurotransmitters, plus the release of glutamate, cause neuronal cell inflammation and necrosis in the mesolimbic region of the brain. Glutamate overstimulates neurons, which is the cause of methamphetamine's neurotoxicity.

## Methamphetamine and the addiction cycle

Select each heading to learn more.
![[Pasted image 20260614155639.png]]
Like other addictive substances, methamphetamines cause an intense, long-lasting euphoria by flooding the brain with dopamine. It causes blood pressure to rise, as well as racing thoughts and users often feel as though they have to keep moving, even when they’re not accomplishing anything.

The effects of methamphetamines can last from 6–12 hours which can cause people to continue using for an ongoing high. People have stayed awake for as long as ten days, and they often ingest very little food and drink during this period.

Methamphetamine can cause the illusion of being more powerful and accomplishing more than they can in reality. Although people may think they are being more social, they can be behaving bizarrely and becoming distant from positive social relationships.
![[Pasted image 20260614155646.png]]
After a while, the physiological reaction to methamphetamine use starts to diminish. This can lead to a state that is almost psychotic, with hallucinations, paranoia, and picking, scraping, or digging at the face or skin due to breakouts or the feeling of phantom bugs crawling on the skin. This is due to increased body temperature and dehydration. The skin also becomes oily, leading to breakouts.

When the high wears off, users may sleep for days. Other effects include aches, heartburn, lethargy, nausea, and confusion. Depleted dopamine and serotonin can cause anxiety and depression. To avoid these unpleasant effects, people will seek another dose.
![[Pasted image 20260614155655.png]]
Despite the negative outcomes of methamphetamine use, people use it to temporarily reduce unpleasant physical or emotional sensations. This behavior negatively reinforces the drug-taking behavior by removing these unpleasant sensations. When a person stops using methamphetamines, the sensations return, causing them to crave the drug and seek it out to remove the sensations again.

\[Source: Hazelden Betty Ford; May et al.\]
