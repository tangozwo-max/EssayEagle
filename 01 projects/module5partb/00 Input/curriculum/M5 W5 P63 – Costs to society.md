## Costs to society

Substance use and behavioral addictions, such as gambling disorder, create substantial social costs.

For example, in the United Kingdom, alcohol useis estimated to cost society between £34 billion and £40 billion every year. Putting strategies in place to reduce alcohol use could be of economic benefit to the country.

In England:

- around half of the cost is due to crime and disorder
- about £5 billion is the cost to the NHS (National Health Service)
- about £2.9 billion is the cost to social services
- about £5 billion is the cost to the wider economy
	\[Source: Public Health England\]

In England, the costs to areas other than the health services, break down like this:
### Crime
Each year there are estimated to be over 4 million alcohol-related crimes in England.

The costs are broken down into:

- **anticipation** of crime (prevention measures, e.g., alarms)
- **consequence** of crime (e.g., value of stolen or damaged property, and emotional harm to the victim)
- **response** to crime (policing and justice costs)
### Social services
Social services are involved in both treatment for addicted adults and support for children who are put at risk because of substance use by their parents or carers.

- alcohol treatment and support services for adults and children (£185m)
- child social services (£2705m)
### wider econom
The impact on the wider economy is presented in three forms:

1. presenteeism (being at work but with reduced productivity)
2. absenteeism (not being at work due to illness)
3. unemployment

## Costs to health services

The burden of disease due to substance use causes high healthcare costs, disability, premature mortality, and reduced, health-related quality of life.

Health costs can be divided into three categories—direct, indirect, and intangible.

Select each heading to learn more about these categories.
### direct costs
Direct costs to health services fall under several different headings. These headings are not definitive and the services under each will vary among jurisdictions. There is also likely to be some overlap. Services include:

**Inpatient care:** This includes physical and mental health reasons for hospital admission, sheltered housing, psychiatric nursing homes, and inpatient rehabilitation.

**Outpatient care:** This includes primary care, outpatient psychiatry and psychology, medical specialists, day centers, medical-social care centers, mental health care centers, and home-based care.

**Social work services:** This includes general welfare, child protection, housing and employment advice and support, and telephone and online support.

**Pharmaceuticals:** Pharmaceuticals include medication for physical and mental health treatment, and medication to support recovery from addiction and dependence.

**Prevention:** This includes prevention measures that are aimed at health promotion, harm reduction, research, and coordination of these measures.
### indirect costs
Indirect costs are due to people acquiring disabilities, whether long- (>365 days), or short-term (<365 days). The costs may also be caused by productivity losses due to premature mortality among people with addiction and dependency.
### intangible costs
Reduced disability-adjusted life years (DALYs) can be due to disease, injury, or traffic accidents.

DALYs are a universal measurement that researchers and policymakers use to calculate how diseases and medical conditions affect the length and quality of life for a given population.

\[Source: Lievens et al.\]

The study by Lievans et al. reports that legal substance use, such as alcohol or tobacco, imposes greater societal costs than illegal substances. Health conditions are the primary contributors to this cost.

For illegal substances, the cost of law enforcement surpasses health care costs.

The impact of substance use on health services may not just be due to the costs of treating people who use substances. Services may also be affected by substance use among their staff. Working in health services can be stressful. In addition to societal pressures to use substances, there may be a temptation to use them to relieve stress.

An example of this was a finding that substance use increased among nurses as a response to the trauma of the COVID-19 pandemic.
