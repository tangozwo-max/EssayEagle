## Case study: Chioma's service

Sam, our trainee mental health nurse, has come to talk to Chioma, who works in a mental health service for children, to get information for an assignment.

Sam

Thanks for taking the time to talk to me, Chioma. I wanted to find out more about how substance use is affecting children's mental health services.

Chioma

It's my pleasure, Sam. As you know, we have a real employment crisis in our area, and many families are struggling for money, which places them under great stress. Adult services have reported an increasing prevalence of people using substances. Now we've noticed that an increasing number of children accessing our services either have a family affected by addiction and dependency, or have been using substances themselves.

Sam

So, what does that mean for you as a service?

Chioma

We're seeing increasing numbers of children with mental health issues as a result of parental substance use. Obviously, this puts a strain on our service because resourcing doesn't keep up with demand. Our wait times are increasing, which isn't good.

Sam

What about the children using substances themselves?

Chioma

We don't deal with them directly. They get referred to a specialist service, but we do a lot of joint work with them. The number of referrals is rising and this is a real worry because they are using substances at such an important developmental stage, and the school systems can only provide limited support around addictions too.

Chioma's service is seeing an increase in demand in several different areas. Children are presenting with anxiety, depression, and behavioral problems, while some adolescents are using substances themselves.

### Complex care needs
Children with a history of exposure to substance use may present with complex mental health needs that require specialized interventions. This can complicate treatment plans and necessitate a more integrated approach that involves multiple service providers, including mental health professionals, social workers, and educational staff.
### Complex care needs
The impact of substance use can perpetuate cycles of mental health disorders across generations. Children of parents with substance use disorders are at a higher risk of developing similar issues, which can create a continuous loop of need for mental health services.
### Improving access
Families affected by substance use may face additional barriers to accessing mental health services, such as stigma, lack of awareness, or financial constraints. This can lead to delays in seeking help, exacerbating mental health conditions.
### Holistic approaches
Effective mental health services for children affected by substance use often require a holistic approach that addresses not only the mental health needs of the child but also the family dynamics and environmental factors contributing to the issues. This includes collaboration with child welfare services and community resources.

How might Chioma's service respond to this increased demand?

Chioma's service could investigate the following:

- **integrated care** for mental health and substance use disorders
- **trauma-informed care**, as this approach can improve outcomes
- **community-based programs** to improve access
- **prevention and education** about the risks of substance use and to promote healthy coping strategies
- **multidisciplinary working** to create a more robust support network
- **crisis response** to provide rapid help and safeguarding in times of crises
