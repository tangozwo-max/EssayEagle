## Summary for recovery as an approach to psychiatric care

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Recovery is a vision of care directed by people in recovery, emphasizing the reality and hope of long-term recovery and recognizing that there are many pathways that people may undertake.
- Recovery encompasses all areas of life including, health, home, purpose, and community.
- Recovery-orientated practice is essential but may vary according to the setting and culture in which it is embedded.
- There are multiple benefits and challenges in relation to recovery-orientated practice.
- Valuing lived experience is a key aspect of recovery-orientated services—for example, peer mentor roles.
