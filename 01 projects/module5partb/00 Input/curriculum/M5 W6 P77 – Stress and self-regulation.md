## Stress and self-regulation

Chronic stress and poor emotional self-regulation are common in addiction. Techniques like mindfulness and cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) used in recovery-oriented treatments can help modulate stress responses and improve emotional self-regulation by altering brain activity in regions involved in emotion regulation and executive function.

## Stress response

Substance use can alter the brain's stress response systems, making individuals more susceptible to stress and negative emotions. Recovery involves retraining the brain to manage stress more effectively, often through therapies like cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and mindfulness practices.

There are several different pharmacological interventions that may be used to support people in managing stress during recovery. Not all are suitable for everyone and some work differently according to gender. Research is ongoing to develop effective treatments that target stress-related aspects of addiction.

## Emotional self-regulation

Emotional regulation, as the term suggests, involves managing one's emotions effectively in various situations. It encompasses the ability to handle emotions appropriately and build emotional resilience.

For many individuals recovering from substance use disorder, mastering emotional regulation can be particularly challenging, especially if they have previously relied on substances to numb their feelings. However, emotional regulation is crucial in recovery. It involves recognizing emotions, understanding how to process them, and developing constructive behaviors. These skills are essential for maintaining long-term sobriety and overall well-being.

### Benefits of emotional regulation for recovery

Recovery from substance use disorder is a continuous and evolving journey. While it is possible to stop using substances, individuals may still exhibit behaviors reminiscent of their addiction. Focusing on emotional regulation can bring numerous benefits to the recovery process.

### Improved coping skills

Emotional regulation helps individuals in recovery develop healthier ways to manage distressing emotions. Instead of turning to negative outlets, they learn to handle their feelings constructively. For instance, rather than letting stress trigger a desire to drink, emotional regulation techniques can help them find healthier ways to decompress.
### Emotional resilience

Incorporating emotional regulation into recovery builds emotional resilience. Recovery is a vulnerable time, and managing a wide range of emotions can be challenging. Emotional regulation provides the tools needed to handle these emotions without letting them escalate into bigger problems.
### Mental health benefits
Many people in recovery also experience mental health conditions. Working on emotional regulation can help reduce symptoms of common mental health conditions like depression and anxiety. By gaining control over their emotions, individuals can better manage their mental health.
