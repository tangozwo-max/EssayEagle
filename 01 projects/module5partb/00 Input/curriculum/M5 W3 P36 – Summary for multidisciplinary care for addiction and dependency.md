## Summary for multidisciplinary care for addiction and dependency

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- A multidisciplinary team can offer holistic, person-centered treatment for a person experiencing addiction and dependency.
- There are numerous barriers and facilitators to recovery operating at the personal, structural, and social levels.
- Brief interventions are often delivered in low-intensity settings by people who are not mental health professionals. They can be effective in reducing substance-seeking behaviors and consumption.
- There are a range of nonpharmaceutical interventions that can be used with people experiencing substance misuse, but the evidence base for them varies.
