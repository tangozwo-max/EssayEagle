## Summary for the neuroscience of recovery and interventions

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Understanding the neuroscience behind neuroplasticity, the reward system, stress and self-regulation, and craving and impulse control may help the teams treating people for addiction and dependency to adjust treatment for the individual.
- To be in recovery means that the brain is learning new skills and habits in order to change behaviors. Through the use of a combination of medication, therapy, and counseling, a person can be helped to adopt new cognitive modifications.
- Psychological interventions that support recovery include cognitive behavioral therapy and mindfulness-based interventions.
- Pharmacological interventions facilitate recovery by promoting positive neuroplastic changes, helping the brain to heal and adapt away from the patterns established by substance use.
- Several systems are involved in recovery. These include the reward system, stress and self-regulation, and craving and impulse control.
- Social connection is an important part of recovery.
