## Summary for pharmacological interventions

## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Medically assisted withdrawal aims to support a person in stopping substance misuse safely. The level of support will vary according to the level of substance misuse.
- Pharmacological interventions are sometimes required alongside nonpharmacological interventions.
- At present, there are only treatment guidelines for pharmacological interventions for opiate and alcohol use disorders.
- Pharmacological interventions used in opioid use disorder are usually opioid receptor agonists that block the action of the opioids.
- Pharmacological interventions for alcohol use disorder may block opioid receptors, restore neurotransmitter balance, or create an unpleasant reaction to alcohol.
- There is no current pharmacological intervention approved for stimulant use disorders.
- Many other interventions are being investigated for substance use disorders as well as behavioral disorders.
