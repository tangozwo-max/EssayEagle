## Paste this into Elicit (deep research / systematic review)

**Topic:** Evidence-based and emerging interventions for anxiety in children and young people (aged ≤18), with
particular emphasis on how interventions have evolved for the "digital generation" and which newer treatments
show promise.

**Research questions:**

1. **Current prevalence / epidemiology** of anxiety disorders in children and adolescents (global and UK),
   2020–2026, including post-COVID trends and any evidence of rising incidence. *(Need a current figure to
   replace Polanczyk et al. 2015.)*

2. **Established psychological interventions — efficacy and durability.** Recent meta-analyses of CBT
   (individual and group), family-based CBT, and parent-led / parent-only treatment (e.g. SPACE) for youth
   anxiety. Report effect sizes vs active and inactive controls, and maintenance at follow-up.

3. **Pharmacological interventions.** SSRIs alone and combined with CBT for paediatric anxiety — efficacy,
   safety/adverse effects, and place within stepped care. Recent evidence preferred.

4. **Digital and emerging interventions (priority — go deep).** Internet-delivered CBT; app / smartphone-based
   interventions; guided vs unguided self-help; digitally-augmented / blended and parent-led digital CBT;
   single-session interventions (SSIs); conversational-agent / chatbot interventions; VR exposure therapy;
   just-in-time adaptive interventions (JITAIs) / EMA-based. For each: efficacy, **engagement / adherence /
   dropout**, and effectiveness in routine / real-world care (not just efficacy trials).

5. **Preventive and early interventions.** Universal and targeted school-based programmes and transdiagnostic
   prevention for youth anxiety — recent meta-analyses; effect sizes and durability.

6. **Temporal / generalisability question.** Is there evidence that intervention effect sizes have changed
   over cohorts/time, or that pre-digital-era trial evidence does or does not generalise to today's
   digitally-immersed young people?

**Inclusion preferences:** systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and RCTs; **2018–2026** preferred for the
emerging-intervention items (Q4) and prevalence (Q1); child/adolescent samples (≤18 — exclude adult-only
studies); high-tier peer-reviewed journals. For each study, capture sample size, design, effect size,
follow-up, and stated limitations.

**Exclude:** adult-only samples, non-peer-reviewed/low-tier sources, opinion pieces.

**Output:** a synthesis organised by the six questions above, with full citations, **and an RIS export** for Zotero.
