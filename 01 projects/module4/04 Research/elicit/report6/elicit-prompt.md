## Paste this into Elicit (deep research / systematic review)

**Topic:** Why does the burden of anxiety in children and young people (aged ≤18) continue to rise despite the
existence of effective, evidence-based treatments? Adjudicate between competing explanations, and identify which
interventions or delivery strategies are best evidenced to address whichever explanation the evidence most
supports.

**Research questions:**

1. **Persistence & sequelae (why acting early matters).** Evidence that childhood/adolescent anxiety, when
   untreated or under-treated, *persists* into later life and *predicts later disorders* — homotypic and
   heterotypic continuity, secondary depression, substance use, behavioural problems, and educational/functional
   impairment. Include any developmental-timing or neuroplasticity evidence that early, well-timed intervention
   alters the long-term trajectory.

2. **The treatment-access gap.** What proportion of anxious young people actually reach evidence-based care?
   Unmet need, referral thresholds, CAMHS/specialist waiting times and waiting-list attrition, and help-seeking
   barriers. Recent figures, UK-specific where possible.

3. **The efficacy–effectiveness gap.** How well do established treatments (CBT, family/parent-led, SSRIs) perform
   in *routine, real-world care* compared with controlled efficacy trials? Remission vs response rates, residual
   symptoms, and especially **relapse / durability of gains beyond 1–2 years**.

4. **Developmental / transfer fit.** Are youth anxiety treatments largely downward extensions of adult protocols,
   and is there evidence that this limits their effectiveness for children specifically (as opposed to
   adolescents)?

5. **The changing-incidence hypothesis.** Is the rising burden driven by genuinely rising *incidence* (versus
   improved detection / diagnostic expansion / reduced stigma), and by an intensifying or novel risk environment
   (social media, social comparison, cyberbullying, loneliness, sleep loss, academic and post-COVID stress)
   operating at *population scale* — i.e. a prevention-paradox in which individual treatment cannot offset rising
   population-level incidence?

6. **Synthesis — the adjudication.** Weighing the evidence in Q2–Q5, what does the balance of evidence identify
   as the *dominant* driver(s) of the persistent and rising burden despite effective treatment being available?
   Rank or weight the explanations and flag genuine uncertainty / contested findings.

7. **Solution mapping.** For the dominant gap(s) identified in Q6, which interventions or delivery strategies are
   *best evidenced* to address that specific gap, and why? For example: scalable low-intensity / digital or
   parent-led delivery for an access gap; mechanism-targeted or preventive approaches for an incidence-driven
   rise; relapse-prevention / maintenance approaches for a durability gap. Name the 1–3 strongest candidates with
   effect sizes, follow-up, and limitations.

**Inclusion preferences:** systematic reviews, meta-analyses, RCTs, and large cohort / epidemiological /
health-services studies; **2018–2026** preferred; child/adolescent samples (≤18 — exclude adult-only studies);
high-tier peer-reviewed journals. For each study capture sample size, design, effect size, follow-up, and stated
limitations. **Where an explanation lacks supporting evidence, say so explicitly — a null or absence is itself a
useful finding.**

**Exclude:** adult-only samples; non-peer-reviewed / low-tier sources; opinion pieces without data.

**Output:** a synthesis organised by the seven questions above, with full citations, **and an RIS export** for
Zotero.
