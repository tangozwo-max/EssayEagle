# Run 6 — source selection & DOI list (for v013 narrative rebuild)

**Decision logic:** the synthesis adjudicates the "why does burden rise despite effective treatment" question to:
**(1) access gap = dominant driver; (2) rising incidence + prevention paradox = secondary overlay; (3) relapse/
durability = secondary; (4) developmental fit = partial modifier.** Crucially, the **efficacy–effectiveness gap is
NOT the driver** — routine CBT ≈ trial CBT (Wergeland 2020). This reframes the essay away from the old
"treatment content is digitally outdated" thesis toward **"effective treatment exists but most never reach it, and
incidence is rising faster than we can treat."**

Champions (Q7): **parent-led/parent-mediated CBT** + **guided low-intensity digital CBT** (both scale *access*);
**indicated prevention / sleep** as the secondary prevention lever (incidence overlay).

Selective per `CLAUDE.md` (depth over breadth). Many v012 refs carry over (CBT efficacy, SPACE/Lebowitz,
Creswell, Nordh, Dunn, prevention, Casey/Sisk, Cybulski, Bie). Below = NEW sources to add.
