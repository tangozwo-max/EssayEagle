Your "kids don't play outside / lost real friendships" idea — the honest verdict

This instinct is worth including, and I've built it in — but the evidence forces one important qualification, and that qualification is exactly what earns marks at postgraduate level.

  • Supported: in-person peer interaction has declined and loneliness has risen across the cohorts reaching adolescence in the 2010s (Twenge 2019).
  • Not supported: the idea that the harm comes from being sedentary / not moving. Boers (2019) tested precisely that — screen time showed no link to reduced exercise; the route to symptoms was social comparison, not lost physical activity.
  • Caveat: Twenge's outcome is loneliness, measured at cohort level — not an individual-level causal test of anxiety.
Recommended framing: present displacement as a proposed route to loneliness, explicitly not an established activity-mediated cause of anxiety. This honours the intuition while keeping the essay out of the undifferentiated screen-time moral panic — the distinction markers reward.
