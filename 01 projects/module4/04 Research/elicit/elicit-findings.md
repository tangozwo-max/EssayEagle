# Synthesis layer — `Elicit - Systematic Review.md`

A full narrative systematic-review write-up of the **same 18 papers** (abstract → methods → results-by-domain
→ discussion → limitations → conclusions, 20 footnotes). **No new sources**, but it supplies a graded,
critical synthesis — and three analytical refinements that matter more than any single citation:

## Three A&E-grade refinements (calibrate the essay's argument to the evidence)

1. **The two "legs" of the thesis are not equally strong — say so.** Systemic framing is **strongly**
   supported for the **family** domain (accommodation r=0.47; longitudinal transmission; genetically-informed
   *environmental* pathways) but **weaker** for the **digital** domain (cross-sectional; longitudinal anxiety
   evidence "lacking"; bidirectionality unresolved). → Reframe digital as a *plausible contextual
   amplifier/correlate*, **not an established maintaining cause**. The essay's §3.3 gestures at this; the
   synthesis lets it state it precisely. This asymmetry *is* the critical-evaluation marks.

2. **Temper "beyond the individual child" → "at least as good, more scalable, and uniquely targets the
   maintaining factor."** The intervention evidence shows family/parent-engaged treatment **beats
   no-treatment** but is **equivalent (not superior) to active/individual** treatment (Jewell 2022: not
   superior to active; Sigurvinsdóttir 2019: individual CBT not superior to family CBT). → The defensible
   claim is *not* "systemic > individual" but "systemic ≈ individual on outcomes, **superior on scalability
   + on attacking accommodation directly** (SPACE, Creswell 2024)." Pre-empting this objection is a clear A+ move.

3. **"How" digital is used > "how much."** Problematic use r=0.35 (Shannon) and Yoshizawa's meta-regression
   (+0.15 for problematic vs duration) both confirm the essay's existing "problematic ≫ general" line — now
   meta-backed and quantified.
