# Teesson et al. (2024) Effectiveness of a universal, school-based, online programme for the prevention of anxiety, depression and substance misuse among adolescents: 72-month follow-up. *The Lancet Digital Health*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Teesson, M. et al. (2024) 'Effectiveness of a universal, school-based, online programme for the prevention of anxiety, depression and substance misuse among adolescents: 72-month follow-up', *The Lancet Digital Health*, 6(5), pp. e334–e344. doi: 10.1016/S2589-7500(24)00046-3

**Evidence tier:** Cluster-randomised trial, long-term follow-up — **Tier T1 (RCT)** · **PDF:** none on file (OA — easy fetch) · **DOI:** 10.1016/S2589-7500(24)00046-3

## Key usable claims
1. Universal, school-based digital prevention slowed anxiety-symptom growth short-term but showed **no lasting difference at six-year follow-up**.
2. The strongest single piece of evidence that universal prevention underdelivers for anxiety over time.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§4 SOLUTION — the honesty about universal prevention's limits that motivates the pivot to indicated prevention.
