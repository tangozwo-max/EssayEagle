# Casey, Glatt & Lee (2015) Treating the developing versus developed brain. *Neuron*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Casey, B.J., Glatt, C.E. and Lee, F.S. (2015) 'Treating the developing versus developed brain: translating preclinical mouse and human studies', *Neuron*, 86(6), pp. 1358–1368. doi: 10.1016/j.neuron.2015.05.020

**Evidence tier:** Translational review — **Tier T3 (review)** · **PDF:** full-pdf · **DOI:** 10.1016/j.neuron.2015.05.020

## Key usable claims
1. The prefrontal cortex exerts top-down control over an earlier-maturing amygdala, and continues to mature into the mid-twenties — so the self-regulation adult treatments assume is, in the young, still being built.
2. The developing brain responds differently to intervention than the developed brain: timing matters for treatment, not just for risk.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§1 NEED — establishes the developing-brain substrate that makes early, developmentally-matched intervention both possible and important (paired with Sisk & Romeo, Ladouceur, Kim).
