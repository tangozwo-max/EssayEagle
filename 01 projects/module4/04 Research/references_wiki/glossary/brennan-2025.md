## Key usable claims
1. Family accommodation (FA) — where parents and caregivers allow avoidance, attempt distraction, or manage distress on behalf of their child — is present at least as frequently in families of anxious autistic youth as in families of non-autistic but equally anxious children, and may be even more prevalent.
2. FA in autistic youth is not limited to anxiety: high rates were found across obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) and restricted and repetitive behaviours (RRBs), indicating FA is a broad systemic pattern in this population rather than disorder-specific behaviour.
3. RRBs in ASD can be differentiated into low-level repetitive sensory-motor (RSM) behaviours and high-level insistence-on-sameness (IS) behaviours — accommodating IS behaviours (e.g., maintaining rigid routines) may reinforce avoidance mechanisms analogous to anxiety accommodation.
4. OCD comorbidity in autistic youth is substantially elevated (8.26–37%) compared to non-autistic youth (1.39–3.6%), making FA a particularly prevalent family-system dynamic in this group.
5. Some form of parental involvement was a consistent feature across the treatment studies reviewed, suggesting family-level participation is a near-universal component of effective intervention for anxious autistic youth.
6. FA rates decreased following treatment in the studies examined, demonstrating that interventions targeting accommodation are feasible and produce measurable reductions in accommodating behaviours in autistic populations.

## Numerical findings
- 17 peer-reviewed articles included in the systematic review (PRISMA guidelines)
- OCD prevalence in autistic youth: 8.26–37% (Hollocks et al., 2023; Leyfer et al., 2006; Martin et al., 2020; van Steensel et al., 2011)
- OCD prevalence in non-autistic youth: 1.39–3.6%
- Published 19 February 2025; accepted 03 February 2025

## Study design
PRISMA-guided systematic review of 17 peer-reviewed studies; inclusion criteria: (a) caregivers of autistic youth included, (b) clearly delineated autism participant group, (c) at least one quantitative outcome measure of FA; covered anxiety disorders, OCD, and RRBs.

## Main limitation
Heterogeneity across included studies in FA measurement instruments, disorder subtype populations, and outcome definitions limits cross-study comparison; the review is restricted to quantitative FA measures, excluding qualitative and mixed-methods evidence; small number of studies (k=17) limits generalisability of treatment conclusions.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
Use to support the argument that family accommodation is a systemic familial factor that maintains and potentially amplifies anxiety in autistic children and adolescents, and that parental behaviour modification is a necessary component of effective CBT-based treatment for this population. Cite specifically for the elevated OCD comorbidity rates in ASD and the finding that FA occurs across RRBs — not only diagnosable anxiety — demonstrating the pervasiveness of accommodating patterns in autistic family systems.
