# Khetawat & Steele (2023) Examining the association between digital stress components and psychological wellbeing: a meta-analysis. *Clinical Child and Family Psychology Review*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Khetawat, D. and Steele, R.G. (2023) 'Examining the association between digital stress components and psychological wellbeing: a meta-analysis', *Clinical Child and Family Psychology Review*, 26(4), pp. 957–974. doi: 10.1007/s10567-023-00440-9

**Evidence tier:** Meta-analysis — **Tier T1 (meta)** · **PDF:** full-pdf · **DOI:** 10.1007/s10567-023-00440-9
**Note:** distinct from the `khetawat-2023` card (the *parental* digital-stress paper, doi 10.1007/s10567-023-00439-y). This is the digital-stress-components/wellbeing meta the v013 essay cites.

## Key usable claims
1. Digital-stress components (availability stress, approval anxiety, FOMO, connection overload, online vigilance) show significant small-to-moderate associations with reduced wellbeing/anxiety.
2. Approval anxiety and FOMO, rather than screen time as such, carry the strongest associations.
