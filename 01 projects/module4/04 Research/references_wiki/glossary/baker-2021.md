# Baker et al. (2021) The effectiveness of psychological therapies for anxiety disorders in adolescents: a meta-analysis. *Clinical Child and Family Psychology Review*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Baker, H.J., Lawrence, P.J., Karalus, J., Creswell, C. and Waite, P. (2021) 'The effectiveness of psychological therapies for anxiety disorders in adolescents: a meta-analysis', *Clinical Child and Family Psychology Review*, 24(4), pp. 765–782. doi: 10.1007/s10567-021-00364-2

**Evidence tier:** Meta-analysis (16 studies, n=766 adolescents) — **Tier T1 (meta-analysis)**
**Validity estimate:** 80/100 (focused adolescent-only synthesis; modest pooled n)
**DOI:** 10.1007/s10567-021-00364-2
**PDF status:** full-pdf

## Key usable claims
1. Only **~36% of adolescents were in remission post-treatment**, compared with the **~50–60%** typically reported for mixed child/adolescent samples.
2. The authors conclude there is **"a clear need to develop more effective treatments for adolescents"** specifically.
3. Evidences a **developmental-fit modifier**: protocols largely validated on mixed/younger samples underperform for adolescents, whose profiles (comorbidity, social anxiety) differ.

## Numerical findings
- 16 studies, n = 766; adolescent remission ~36% vs ~50–60% in mixed samples.

## Main limitation
Modest pooled sample; heterogeneity in adolescent definitions and outcome measures; remission thresholds vary across trials.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§3 — a *secondary*, partial driver: treatments fit mid/late adolescents imperfectly (a quality-of-fit limit), but the effect is too small to explain population burden. Use as nuance, not as the main gap. Pairs with Waite & Creswell (2014).
