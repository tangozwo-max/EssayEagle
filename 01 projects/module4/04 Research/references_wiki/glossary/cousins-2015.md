## Key usable claims
1. SSRIs (notably fluoxetine) remain a key component of treatment for moderate-to-severe **depression** in adolescents.
2. Adolescence is a period of major, ongoing brain development, and the **impact of SSRIs on the developing adolescent brain remains unclear**.
3. Implication: prescribing antidepressants to young people acts on a still-maturing brain whose long-term consequences are not well understood — a reason for caution and careful, reserved use.

## Numerical findings
- Depression 1-month prevalence rises ~2% (late childhood) → ~6% (late adolescence).
- 60–77% of responders to NICE-approved treatment go on to relapse.

## Study design
Narrative review of the developing adolescent brain and the (largely unresolved) effects of antidepressants on it.

## Main limitation
**Depression-focused, not anxiety-specific.** Narrative/conceptual; reaches no firm conclusion on SSRI effects on the developing brain (states they are "unclear"); adolescent-framed, not younger children.
