# Zhou et al. (2019) Different types and acceptability of psychotherapies for acute anxiety disorders in children and adolescents: a network meta-analysis. *JAMA Psychiatry*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Zhou, X. et al. (2019) 'Different types and acceptability of psychotherapies for acute anxiety disorders in children and adolescents: a network meta-analysis', *JAMA Psychiatry*, 76(1), pp. 41–50. doi: 10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2018.3070

**Evidence tier:** Network meta-analysis — **Tier T1 (meta)** · **PDF:** full-pdf · **DOI:** 10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2018.3070

## Key usable claims
1. Most active psychotherapies outperformed waiting-list control for childhood anxiety.
2. **Group CBT was the only modality also to surpass other active therapies** — the strongest single comparative result for CBT.
