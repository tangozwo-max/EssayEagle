# Reardon et al. (2018) Barriers and facilitators to parents seeking and accessing professional support for anxiety disorders in children: qualitative interview study. *European Child & Adolescent Psychiatry*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Reardon, T., Harvey, K., Young, B., O'Brien, D. and Creswell, C. (2018) 'Barriers and facilitators to parents seeking and accessing professional support for anxiety disorders in children: qualitative interview study', *European Child & Adolescent Psychiatry*, 27(8), pp. 1023–1031. doi: 10.1007/s00787-018-1107-2

**Evidence tier:** Qualitative interview study (~15 families) — **Tier (qualitative)**
**Validity estimate:** 60/100
**DOI:** 10.1007/s00787-018-1107-2
**PDF status:** full-pdf (open access)

## Key usable claims
1. Only a minority of children with anxiety disorders access professional help, and the bottleneck sits in the **parent-led help-seeking pathway**.
2. Help-seeking is a staged process — parents first **recognising the difficulty**, then **recognising the need for professional help**, then **contacting and navigating services** — with barriers arising at each stage.
3. **Stigma** around anxiety/mental health, and uncertainty about whether a problem is "bad enough," shape whether parents disclose concerns and seek help.

## Numerical findings
- Semi-structured interviews with parents of children with elevated anxiety (~15 families, mostly mothers; confirm exact N).

## Study design
Inductive thematic analysis of semi-structured interviews with parents of community-identified anxious children (Oxford/Reading group — same programme as Radez, Waite, Creswell).

## Main limitation
Qualitative (describes, does not quantify, barriers); small UK sample, predominantly mothers and white-British; not generalisable in frequency terms.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
"Structural limitations / closing the gap" — supports the demand-side reach point that **anxious children depend on parents to recognise the problem and navigate services**, so access fails at the parent gateway and only a minority reach help. Complements [[radez-2021]] (adolescents' own barriers) and the parental "double disadvantage" alongside [[haine-schlagel-2019]].
