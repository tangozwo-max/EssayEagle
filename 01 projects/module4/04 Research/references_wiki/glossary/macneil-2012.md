# Macneil, Hasty, Conus and Berk (2012) Is diagnosis enough to guide interventions in mental health? Using case formulation in clinical practice. *BMC Medicine*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Macneil, C.A., Hasty, M.K., Conus, P. and Berk, M. (2012) 'Is diagnosis enough to guide interventions in mental health? Using case formulation in clinical practice', BMC Medicine, 10, 111. DOI: 10.1186/1741-7015-10-111

**Evidence tier:** Commentary / conceptual (open access) — not empirical
**Validity estimate:** 55/100
**DOI:** 10.1186/1741-7015-10-111
**PDF status:** full-text

## Key usable claims
1. Diagnosis alone is limited for selecting mental-health interventions; collaborative case formulation identifies the idiosyncratic factors driving a presentation and selects interventions on that basis.
2. The widely used "Five Ps" formulation organises a case into predisposing, precipitating, **perpetuating**, protective and presenting factors.
3. Perpetuating factors are those "which maintain the current difficulties" and explicitly include "avoidance or safety behaviors in anxiety disorders... or cognitive patterns such as attentional biases, memory biases, or hypervigilance" (verbatim) — i.e. the maintenance mechanisms that CBT targets.
4. Formulation-led treatment targets these individualised maintaining factors (and patient strengths), giving a more personalised approach and potentially better outcomes than diagnosis-led treatment selection.

## Numerical findings
- None (conceptual commentary).

## Study design
Commentary in BMC Medicine reviewing the limitations of diagnosis-led intervention selection and outlining case-formulation models (incl. the Five Ps) and their clinical utility.

## Main limitation
Opinion/commentary, not empirical evidence; transdiagnostic and largely adult-framed, not anxiety- or child-specific; provides NO treatment-efficacy data. Therefore it supports the *perpetuating-factors concept* only — it must NOT be cited for "CBT is first-line/best-evidenced" or any efficacy/mechanism-of-CBT claim (use Zhou 2019 / Sigurvinsdóttir 2020 / Wergeland 2021 for those).

## Cite for (essay relevance)
"Existing Treatment": grounds *why* anxiety treatment works — within a case-formulation frame, childhood anxiety is sustained by perpetuating factors (avoidance, threat-focused attention/hypervigilance) which treatment dismantles rather than the fear itself. Scope the citation to the perpetuating-factors clause only.
