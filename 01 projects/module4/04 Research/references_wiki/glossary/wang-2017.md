# Wang et al. (2017) Comparative effectiveness and safety of cognitive behavioral therapy and pharmacotherapy for childhood anxiety disorders: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *JAMA Pediatrics*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Wang, Z., Whiteside, S.P.H., Sim, L. et al. (2017) 'Comparative effectiveness and safety of cognitive behavioral therapy and pharmacotherapy for childhood anxiety disorders: a systematic review and meta-analysis', *JAMA Pediatrics*, 171(11), pp. 1049–1056. doi: 10.1001/jamapediatrics.2017.3036

**Evidence tier:** Systematic review & meta-analysis — **Tier T1 (meta)** · **PDF:** full-pdf · **DOI:** 10.1001/jamapediatrics.2017.3036

## Key usable claims
1. SSRIs increase response and remission over placebo; CBT generally matches or exceeds medication on primary symptoms.
2. The **combination outperforms either treatment alone** — the basis of stepped/combined care.
