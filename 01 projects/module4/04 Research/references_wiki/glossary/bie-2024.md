# Bie et al. (2024) Rising global burden of anxiety disorders among adolescents and young adults: trends, risk factors, and the impact of socioeconomic disparities and COVID-19 from 1990 to 2021. *Frontiers in Psychiatry*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Bie, F. et al. (2024) 'Rising global burden of anxiety disorders among adolescents and young adults: trends, risk factors, and the impact of socioeconomic disparities and COVID-19 from 1990 to 2021', *Frontiers in Psychiatry*, 15, 1489427. doi: 10.3389/fpsyt.2024.1489427

**Evidence tier:** Global burden analysis (GBD 2021) — **Tier T2** · **PDF:** full-pdf · **DOI:** 10.3389/fpsyt.2024.1489427

## Key usable claims
1. ~52% increase in global anxiety incidence (1990–2021) in adolescents/young adults, with a sharp post-2019 acceleration; 10–14 the steepest.
2. Identifies **bullying victimisation** among the leading drivers of the rising burden.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§1 post-2019 acceleration; §3 the social-media-era risk environment (bullying/cybervictimisation as a driver of rising incidence).
