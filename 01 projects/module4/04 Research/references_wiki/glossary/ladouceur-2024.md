## Key usable claims
1. CBT is well-evidenced in pediatric anxiety, but effects are **moderate at best**, motivating a developmental-neuroscience account of *when* and *why* it works.
2. Trial evidence indicates CBT **normalises fronto-parietal network activation** in unmedicated early adolescents — i.e. therapy acts on the maturing top-down control circuitry.
3. Implies the adolescent **plastic window is a treatment window**: well-timed intervention can shape circuits still under construction.

## Main limitation
A perspective/editorial, not a primary trial; the neural-normalisation evidence base is still small and heterogeneous.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§1 NEED — pairs with Casey (2015) / Sisk & Romeo (2019): the developing brain is not just *vulnerable* but *plastic*, so early, well-timed CBT may achieve more and last longer. Converts the neuroplasticity point from aetiology into a *treatment-timing* argument.
