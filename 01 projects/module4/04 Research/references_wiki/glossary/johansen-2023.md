# Johansen et al. (2023) Effects of escitalopram on synaptic density in the healthy human brain: a randomized controlled trial. *Molecular Psychiatry*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Johansen, A., Armand, S., Plavén-Sigray, P., Nasser, A., Ozenne, B., Petersen, I.N., Keller, S.H., Madsen, J., Beliveau, V., Møller, K., Vassilieva, A., Langley, C., Svarer, C., Stenbæk, D.S., Sahakian, B.J. and Knudsen, G.M. (2023) 'Effects of escitalopram on synaptic density in the healthy human brain: a randomized controlled trial', *Molecular Psychiatry*, 28(10), pp. 4272–4279. doi: 10.1038/s41380-023-02285-8

**Evidence tier:** Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT with [11C]UCB-J PET (n=32 healthy adults) — **Tier T1 (RCT), mechanistic/imaging**
**Validity estimate:** 70/100 (rigorous design and first-in-human imaging evidence, but small n, healthy volunteers, primary endpoint null, key effect from a secondary analysis)
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41380-023-02285-8
**PDF status:** full-text

## Key usable claims
1. SSRIs are widely used, but their mechanism and the reason effects take several weeks to appear are not fully understood; the neuroplasticity hypothesis is supported in preclinical work, with limited human evidence.
2. **First in vivo human evidence** consistent with the neuroplasticity hypothesis: daily escitalopram is associated with a **time-dependent rise in synaptic density (SV2A) over 3–5 weeks**, offering a biological explanation for the delayed onset of SSRI effects.

## Numerical findings
- Double-blind RCT (NCT04239339), n=32 healthy adults with no psychiatric/cognitive history: 20 mg/day escitalopram (n=17) vs placebo (n=15); [11C]UCB-J PET (SV2A density) in hippocampus and neocortex after ~29 days (range 24–38).
- **Primary outcome NULL** — no statistically significant group difference in SV2A binding at ~29 days.
- **Secondary (key) finding** — a positive, time-dependent association between SV2A binding and *duration* of escitalopram intervention, i.e. synaptic density appears to build over 3–5 weeks.

## Study design
Double-blind, placebo-controlled randomized trial in healthy volunteers using [11C]UCB-J PET to index presynaptic (SV2A) density as a proxy for synaptic plasticity.

## Main limitation
**The primary group comparison was non-significant; the "over weeks" effect comes from a secondary/exploratory analysis.** Small sample (n=32), **healthy adults (not anxious patients, not children)**, single SSRI (escitalopram), and SV2A is a proxy for plasticity. So the result is *suggestive*, not definitive — cite with "suggests / preliminary" hedging, never as a proven effect.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
"Treatments / medication" — the strongest *human* evidence that SSRI treatment is associated with rising synaptic density over 3–5 weeks, supporting the neuroplasticity explanation for the delayed onset of SSRIs. Adult/healthy-brain, general-mechanism (not child-anxiety).
