# Broeren et al. (2012) The course of childhood anxiety symptoms: developmental trajectories and child-related factors in normal children. *Journal of Abnormal Child Psychology*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Broeren, S., Muris, P., Diamantopoulou, S. and Baker, J.R. (2012) 'The course of childhood anxiety symptoms: developmental trajectories and child-related factors in normal children', *Journal of Abnormal Child Psychology*, 41(1), pp. 81–95. doi: 10.1007/s10802-012-9669-9

## Key usable claims
1. In a **normal/community** sample, childhood anxiety symptoms (specific fears, social anxiety, generalised anxiety, separation anxiety) follow **distinct developmental trajectories** — they are not uniform or uniformly transient.
2. Trajectory membership (including **persistent/elevated** courses) was associated with child-related factors implicated in the **pathogenesis of anxiety** (e.g. temperamental/vulnerability factors).
3. Supports the framing that anxiety symptoms differentiate early into those that decline and those that persist — so identifying and acting on the persistent course matters even before clinical threshold.

## Numerical findings
- n = 224 children, aged 4–11; three waves of parent-completed standardised anxiety questionnaires over ~2 years; separate trajectory analyses by anxiety type.

## Main limitation
Community (non-clinical) sample and **parent-report only**; modest n; symptom trajectories, not diagnoses; OCR copy (verify exact statistics against publisher text if a precise figure is quoted).
