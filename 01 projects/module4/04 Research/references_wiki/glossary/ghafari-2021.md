# Ghafari et al. (2021) Global prevalence of unmet need for mental health care among adolescents: A systematic review and meta-analysis. *Archives of Psychiatric Nursing*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Ghafari, M., Nadi, T., Bahadivand-Chegini, S. and Doosti-Irani, A. (2022) 'Global prevalence of unmet need for mental health care among adolescents: a systematic review and meta-analysis', *Archives of Psychiatric Nursing*, 36, pp. 1–6. doi: 10.1016/j.apnu.2021.10.008

**Evidence tier:** Systematic review & meta-analysis (15 studies, n=51,309) — **Tier T1 (meta-analysis)**
**Validity estimate:** 74/100 (pooled global estimate; wide CI, heterogeneous "unmet need" definitions)
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.apnu.2021.10.008
**PDF status:** abstract (image-only; figures verified via abstract — confirm pages on full text if used)

## Key usable claims
1. Pooled prevalence of **unmet mental-health-care need among adolescents = 54%** (95% CI 41–67%).
2. Among adolescents with **any diagnosed mental disorder**, unmet need rose to **58%**.
3. Gives the essay a single global figure for the scale of the access gap, complementing the UK-specific CAMHS data.

## Numerical findings
- 15 studies, n = 51,309; unmet need 54% (95% CI 41–67%); 58% in the diagnosed subgroup.

## Main limitation
Wide confidence interval and heterogeneous definitions of "unmet need" across the 15 studies; abstract-level access only; not anxiety-specific.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§3 GAP — the global headline number (over half of adolescents who need care don't get it), used briefly to globalise the UK CAMHS picture. Cite cautiously given the wide CI.
