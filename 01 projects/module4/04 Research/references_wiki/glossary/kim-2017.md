## Key usable claims
1. Anxiety disorders are **neurodevelopmental**, with median age of onset ~10–11 years — childhood is when the relevant fear circuitry is being shaped.
2. **Extinction-based learning** — the core mechanism of exposure CBT — is particularly *efficient* during childhood, drawing on a decade of developmental rodent work with human parallels.
3. Argues this is a **genuine neurobiological window**, not merely convenient labelling: acting in childhood is mechanistically advantaged, so delay is costly.

## Main limitation
Heavy reliance on rodent models; human translational evidence is indirect; a review rather than a trial.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§1 NEED — the mechanistic complement to Ladouceur/Casey/Sisk: not only is the young brain plastic, the specific learning process CBT relies on (extinction) works *best* early. Supports early/well-timed intervention.
