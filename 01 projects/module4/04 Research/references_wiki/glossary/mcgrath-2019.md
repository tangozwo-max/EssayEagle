# McGrath and Abbott (2019) Family-Based Psychological Treatment for OCD in Children and Adolescents. *Clinical Child and Family Psychology Review*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
McGrath, C.A. and Abbott, M.J. (2019) 'Family-Based Psychological Treatment for Obsessive Compulsive Disorder in Children and Adolescents: A Meta-analysis and Systematic Review', Clinical Child and Family Psychology Review, 22, pp. 478–501. DOI: 10.1007/s10567-019-00296-y

**Evidence tier:** Meta-analysis / SR — Meta-analysis and systematic review (k=37 studies, n=1727 participants)
**Validity estimate:** 78/100
**DOI:** 10.1007/s10567-019-00296-y
**PDF status:** abstract-only

## Key usable claims
1. Family-based psychological interventions for child and adolescent OCD produce large effect sizes for both OCD symptom reduction and reductions in Family Accommodation (FA), effects that are maintained at follow-up.
2. Family Accommodation is significantly correlated with OCD symptom severity and poorer treatment outcomes, and there is evidence it may mediate OCD symptom outcomes.
3. The number of family factors explicitly targeted within treatment significantly moderates FA outcomes — the more family factors addressed, the greater the reduction in accommodation at post-treatment.
4. Moderation of family factor breadth on treatment outcome was specific to FA measures; it did not significantly moderate Children's Yale-Brown Obsessive Compulsive Scale (C/Y-BOCS) symptom scores.
5. A significant subset of children and adolescents shows poor response to the gold-standard CBT with ERP, highlighting the need to address family variables to optimise outcomes.
6. Findings support the importance of incorporating a range of family-targeted components in child OCD treatment — not merely adding parental presence, but directly addressing family accommodation and related family processes.

## Numerical findings
- k=37 eligible studies included
- n=1727 OCD participants
- Pooled effect size for OCD symptoms at posttest: g=1.56
- Pooled effect size for Family Accommodation at posttest: g=1.00
- Pooled effect size for OCD symptoms at follow-up: g=1.69
- Pooled effect size for Family Accommodation at follow-up: g=1.98
- Moderator analysis for number of family factors on FA outcomes: z=2.21, p=0.03
- Moderator analysis for number of family factors on C/Y-BOCS outcomes: not significant

## Study design
Systematic review and meta-analysis of 37 studies (n=1727 children and adolescents with OCD) examining the effect of family-based psychological interventions on OCD symptom and family accommodation outcomes, including moderator analyses of parental involvement and number of family factors targeted.

## Main limitation
As an abstract-only printout the authors' own limitations section is not visible; however, the design is limited by the heterogeneity of included studies (varying levels of parental involvement, different treatment formats) and the absence of randomised controlled comparison conditions in many included studies, which constrains causal inference about which specific family components drive gains.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
Use this source to support the argument that family accommodation is a critical systemic factor in child and adolescent OCD — both as a correlate of symptom severity and as a treatment-relevant variable — and to demonstrate that CBT outcomes are optimised when treatment explicitly targets a range of family processes rather than simply including parents as bystanders.
