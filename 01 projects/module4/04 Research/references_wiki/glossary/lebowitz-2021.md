# Lebowitz et al. (2021) Moderators of response to child-based and parent-based anxiety treatment: machine learning analysis. *JCPP*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Lebowitz, E.R., Zilcha-Mano, S., Orbach, M., Shimshoni, Y. and Silverman, W.K. (2021) 'Moderators of response to child-based and parent-based child anxiety treatment: a machine learning-based analysis', *Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry*, 62(2), pp. 1176–1185. doi: 10.1111/jcpp.13393

**Evidence tier:** RCT secondary analysis with machine learning moderator detection (N=124)
**Validity estimate:** 75/100
**DOI:** 10.1111/jcpp.13393
**PDF status:** full-pdf

## Key usable claims
1. SPACE (Supportive Parenting for Anxious Childhood Emotions) is as efficacious as child-focused CBT overall; however, individual children respond differentially to each treatment, meaning treatment selection matters and should be informed by moderator data.
2. Parent negativity moderates outcomes for both treatment conditions: it is a key family-level variable that determines whether a child responds better to SPACE (parent-based) or CBT (child-based) — high parent negativity favours SPACE over CBT.
3. Child oxytocin levels moderated parent-reported outcomes, and parent oxytocin levels moderated child-reported outcomes — biological/affiliative variables distinguish optimal treatment assignment.
4. Baseline anxiety severity moderated both child-reported and independent-evaluator-reported outcomes, confirming that more severe presentations are a key clinical indicator for treatment selection.
5. Children assigned to their statistically optimal treatment condition (as identified by machine learning) showed significantly greater anxiety reduction than those assigned to their non-optimal condition, demonstrating the clinical value of moderator-informed prescriptive matching.
6. Machine learning identified significant interactions between moderators — suggesting that no single variable determines optimal treatment, but combinations of parent negativity, baseline severity, and biological factors together predict differential response.

## Numerical findings
- N = 124 children with primary anxiety disorders; drawn from the SPACE vs CBT noninferiority RCT (Lebowitz et al., 2020)
- Machine learning approach applied to identify moderators across three reporter types: parent, child, independent evaluator
- Parent-reported outcomes moderated by: parent negativity (p sig), child oxytocin (p sig)
- Child-reported outcomes moderated by: baseline anxiety, parent negativity, parent oxytocin
- Independent evaluator-reported outcomes moderated by: baseline anxiety
- Children in optimal treatment: significantly greater anxiety symptom reduction vs non-optimal condition
- Significant interactions emerged between identified moderators

## Study design
Secondary moderator analysis of a randomised clinical trial (SPACE vs CBT; Lebowitz et al., 2020); machine learning (random forest and linear LASSO methods) applied to identify and validate moderators of treatment response across three reporter perspectives; inclusion required a primary anxiety disorder diagnosis; potential moderators included demographic, socioemotional, parenting, and biological variables.

## Main limitation
Secondary analysis of a single RCT with relatively modest sample size (N=124); machine learning moderator identification requires replication in independent samples before clinical implementation; oxytocin measured at a single time point; moderators identified retrospectively, not prospectively validated; sample from a clinical trial context may not generalise to routine care.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
Use this source to support the argument that the choice between parent-based (SPACE) and child-based (CBT) treatment for childhood anxiety should be clinically informed by family-level variables — particularly parent negativity — rather than defaulting to a single protocol. Also useful for grounding the argument that parental factors are not only transmission mechanisms for anxiety but also key determinants of which treatment pathway will be most effective for the individual child.
