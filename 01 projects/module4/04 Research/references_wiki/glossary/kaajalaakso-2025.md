# Kaajalaakso et al. (2025) Predictors of dropout, time spent on the program and client satisfaction in an internet-based, telephone-assisted CBT anxiety program among elementary school children. *European Child & Adolescent Psychiatry*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Kaajalaakso, K. et al. (2025) 'Predictors of dropout, time spent on the program and client satisfaction in an internet-based, telephone-assisted CBT anxiety program among elementary school children', *European Child & Adolescent Psychiatry*, 34, pp. 249–258. doi: 10.1007/s00787-024-02486-8

**Evidence tier:** Cohort / predictor analysis within a digital programme — **Tier T2** · **PDF:** none on file · **DOI:** 10.1007/s00787-024-02486-8

## Key usable claims
1. Even guided digital CBT programmes lose a substantial minority to **dropout**.
2. Dropout is predicted not by demographics but by **weak therapeutic alliance** — engagement, not access, becomes the binding limit.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§4 SOLUTION — the honest limitation on the guided-digital champion: reach is gained but engagement caps it.
