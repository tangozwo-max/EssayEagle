# Ball et al. (2022) Inequalities in children's mental health prescribing and referrals for specialist mental health services. *International Journal of Population Data Science*.

## Key usable claims
1. CAMHS **referrals rose ~20%** over 2015–2021, but the proportion of referrals **rejected rose from 18% to 31%**.
2. Net effect: the number of children actually **reaching specialist care stayed roughly flat** while demand climbed — a structural demand–capacity divergence.
3. **Mental-health prescriptions rose ~40%** over the same period — pressure displaced into primary-care medication as specialist access tightened.

## Numerical findings
- ~100,000 children/year; referrals +20%; rejection 18%→31%; prescriptions +40% (2015–2021, NHS Grampian).

## Main limitation
Single Scottish health board; **conference abstract** so methods are abbreviated and not peer-reviewed in full; figures should be cited as from the abstract.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§3 GAP — concrete UK evidence that rising demand is met by rising *rejection*, not rising capacity: the access gap is structural. Pair with Abel (2025) and the Children's Commissioner waiting-time figures.
