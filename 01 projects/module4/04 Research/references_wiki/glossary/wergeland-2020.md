# Wergeland, Riise & Öst (2021) Cognitive behavior therapy for internalizing disorders in children and adolescents in routine clinical care: A systematic review and meta-analysis. *Clinical Psychology Review*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Wergeland, G.J.H., Riise, E.N. and Öst, L.-G. (2021) 'Cognitive behavior therapy for internalizing disorders in children and adolescents in routine clinical care: a systematic review and meta-analysis', *Clinical Psychology Review*, 83, 101918. doi: 10.1016/j.cpr.2020.101918

**Evidence tier:** Systematic review & meta-analysis (58 routine-care studies, n=4,618 youth) — **Tier T1 (meta-analysis)**
**Validity estimate:** 86/100 (large, routine-care focus, benchmarking design; heterogeneity across services)
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.cpr.2020.101918
**PDF status:** full-pdf (open access, CC BY)

## Key usable claims (THE HINGE of the v013 gap argument)
1. CBT delivered in **routine clinical care** produces large effects: post-treatment **g = 1.28–2.54**, follow-up **g = 1.72–3.36**.
2. Remission rates at post-treatment ranged **50.7–77.4%** — clinically meaningful in real services, not just trials.
3. A benchmarking analysis found the **similarities between routine care and efficacy trials were greater than the differences** — i.e. the **efficacy–effectiveness gap is small**.
4. Decisive implication for the essay: the treatment *works* when delivered. So the persistent burden is **not** explained by ineffective treatment — "the problem is who gets in, not what happens once they arrive." This redirects the essay's gap to **access**, not treatment content/quality.

## Numerical findings
- 58 studies, n = 4,618; post g = 1.28–2.54; follow-up g = 1.72–3.36; remission 50.7–77.4%.

## Main limitation
Routine-care studies are heterogeneous (varying protocols, fidelity, settings); few have inactive control conditions; publication/availability bias possible.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§3 GAP — the pivotal citation that *rules out* the "treatments don't work / aren't good enough" explanation and reframes the essay around the access gap. Use to pivot from §2 (status quo works) to §3 (but most never reach it).
