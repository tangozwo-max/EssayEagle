# Waite & Creswell (2014) Children and adolescents referred for treatment of anxiety disorders: differences in clinical characteristics. *Journal of Affective Disorders*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Waite, P. and Creswell, C. (2014) 'Children and adolescents referred for treatment of anxiety disorders: differences in clinical characteristics', *Journal of Affective Disorders*, 167, pp. 326–332. doi: 10.1016/j.jad.2014.06.028

**Evidence tier:** Cross-sectional clinical comparison (routine referral sample, n=200; children 6–12 vs adolescents 13–18) — **Tier T2 (cross-sectional)**
**Validity estimate:** 72/100
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.jad.2014.06.028
**PDF status:** full-pdf

## Key usable claims
1. Compared with children, anxious **adolescents** presented with significantly **higher clinician-rated anxiety, more social anxiety, more comorbid depression, and more school refusal**.
2. These profiles **map poorly onto the cognitive content of protocols** developed for younger children (or extended down from adults).
3. Provides the clinical-characteristics basis for the developmental-fit critique (why one protocol does not fit the whole 6–18 range).

## Numerical findings
- n = 200 (routine referrals); children 6–12 vs adolescents 13–18; adolescents higher on severity, social anxiety, comorbid depression, school refusal.

## Main limitation
Single UK clinic referral sample; cross-sectional (characteristics, not treatment-response outcomes); modest n per age band.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§3 — supports the developmental-fit nuance (adolescents differ clinically, so standard protocols fit imperfectly) and also feeds §1's point that adolescent anxiety increasingly co-travels with depression and school avoidance. Secondary, supporting citation.
