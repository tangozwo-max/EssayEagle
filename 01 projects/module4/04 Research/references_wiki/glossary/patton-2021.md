## Key usable claims
1. Drawing on Geoffrey Rose's prevention principles: **expanding individual treatment without addressing structural and social risk factors cannot reduce burden at population scale**.
2. The **alcohol analogy**: individual-level counselling has not moved population alcohol-harm rates; population-level risk-factor reduction has. The same logic applies to youth mental health.
3. Names **digital technology, shifts in peer relationships, and social determinants** as the key structural drivers requiring population-level (not just clinical) action.

## Main limitation
An editorial — makes a conceptual/strategic argument rather than presenting new data.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§3/§4 — the high-authority framing for why "treat our way out" fails at scale, complementing Skinner's modelling. Justifies pairing the access-scaling champions with a prevention/structural lever in the conclusion.
