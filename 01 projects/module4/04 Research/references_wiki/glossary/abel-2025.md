# Abel et al. (2025) Enhancing referrals to Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services: the EN-CAMHS mixed-methods study. *Health and Social Care Delivery Research*.

**Citation (Harvard-WMS):**
Abel, K.M., Whelan, P., Carter, L.-A., Tranter, H., Stockton-Powdrell, C., Gutridge, K., Hassan, L., Elvins, R. and Edbrooke-Childs, J. (2025) 'Enhancing referrals to Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services: the EN-CAMHS mixed-methods study', *Health and Social Care Delivery Research*, 13(21). doi: 10.3310/GYDW4507

**Evidence tier:** Mixed-methods health-services study (9 CAMHS sites; n=497,502 referrals, 2020–21; >100 stakeholders in focus groups) — **Tier T2 (large health-services study)**
**Validity estimate:** 80/100 (very large referral dataset + qualitative depth; English CAMHS)
**DOI:** 10.3310/GYDW4507
**PDF status:** full-pdf (78-pp NIHR monograph + 5-pp article both on file)

## Key usable claims
1. **Nearly one-quarter of CAMHS referrals were unsuccessful**, frequently after long waits and **without redirection** to alternative services.
2. Stakeholder focus groups identified the primary problems as **confusion about CAMHS remit, lack of referral support, and failure to communicate during the waiting period**.
3. Crucially, several of these are **fixable organisational failures**, not purely resource shortfalls — which keeps the essay's gap actionable rather than fatalistic.

## Numerical findings
- 9 CAMHS sites; n = 497,502 referrals (2020–21); ~25% unsuccessful; >100 stakeholders consulted.

## Main limitation
English CAMHS during the COVID period (2020–21) — may overstate system strain; "unsuccessful referral" definitions vary across sites.

## Cite for (essay relevance)
§3 GAP — the large-n anchor for the access gap: a quarter of referrals fail, often silently. Use alongside Ball (2022) to show the gap is at the *front door* of services, and Radez (2021) for *why* young people struggle to get through it.
