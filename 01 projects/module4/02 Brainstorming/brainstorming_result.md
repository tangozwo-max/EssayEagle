## Selected: Option C — "Beyond the Child"

**Core angle:** Anxiety develops and is maintained within relational/family systems. Treating the child in isolation is insufficient. Family accommodation sustains anxiety; parental anxiety transmits via social referencing. Essay evaluates family-based interventions as critical complement — or alternative — to individual CBT.

**Key tensions to exploit:**
- Individual therapy (CBT) vs. family systems approaches
- Parental anxiety transmission (social referencing, intergenerational patterns)
- Family accommodation as maintaining mechanism
- Evidence: systemic family therapy comparable to individual therapy; but when is family involvement counterproductive (adolescent autonomy)?
