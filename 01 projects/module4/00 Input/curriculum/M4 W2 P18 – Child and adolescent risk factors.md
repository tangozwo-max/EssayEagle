Certain groups of children and adolescents are at greatest [[risk-factors|risk]] for poor mental health outcomes. Knowing about the [[risk-factors]] enables intervention as early as possible.

![[Pasted image 20260417163601.png]]
Risks may cause poorer outcomes directly, as in the case of [[fetal-alcohol-spectrum-disorder|fetal alcohol spectrum disorder (FASD)]].

Alternatively, risks may impact in a more complex way, such as when parents experience traumatic circumstances that make parenting harder for them, leading to poor outcomes for the child. Risk can affect any of the factors that contribute to a child's mental health.

> [!-info] -info
> **Note**: In some instances, being in an at-risk group may not cause risk in itself; rather, the lack of support or extra adversity associated with it leads to poor outcomes, such as when autistic children are unsupported in a mainstream school system.

## Children's walls of [[protective-factors|protective factors]]

In the previous week, you learned about attachment and brain development during the early years. Based on this knowledge, what do children need to develop and have good mental health?

Select the arrow to learn more.
![[Pasted image 20260417163621.png]]
   

Children begin to have needs even before they are born, and they depend on their parents to provide everything they need to grow and develop.
As they grow, many of these needs remain, and they develop new ones.
When children's needs are met, it helps their development, and they begin to develop a positive sense of self.
When a child has all their needs met, their protective wall is strong, and they are more likely to be able to handle and overcome adversity and develop [[resilience]].

## Learning outcomes

On successful completion of this week, you will be able to:

- analyze the categories of risk and protective factors relevant to child and adolescent mental health
- evaluate the evidence concerning specific risk factors and analyze the concept of resilience in the context of child and adolescent mental health
- analyze the correlation between neurodevelopmental conditions and mental health risk
- describe the impact of trauma during childhood and adolescence on brain development and mental health risk
