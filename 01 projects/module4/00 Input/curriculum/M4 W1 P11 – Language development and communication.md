## Infancy and toddlerhood

Select each tab to learn about language and communication development in the first 3 years.
![[Pasted image 20260417161844.png]]
#### 0-12 months

**3-4 months:** The infant babbles.

**7 months:** The infant babbles for social interaction and personal amusement.

**6-9 months:** The infant starts to understand words.

**6-14 months:** The infant babbles with consonants and syllables and imitates speech sounds.

#### 12 months

At 12 months, toddlers often begin to say words that are important to them, which usually include names for important people in their lives (like “mama” or “dada”), names for common objects, or simple greetings like “hi” or “bye.”

Receptive language is more advanced than expressive language, which means that toddlers typically understand more complex vocabulary than they can speak.

12-15 months

The toddler understands "no" and uses gestures to communicate (pointing, lifting arms to be picked up, and nodding or shaking the head).

#### 18-24 months

The toddler's vocabulary increases from 50 to 300 words.

#### 2-3 years

**Around 2 years:** The toddler speaks simple sentences or two-word phrases.

**2-3 years:** The toddler follows two-step instructions.


## Pre-school and childhood

### **Pre-school**

Receptive language is greater than expressive language until age four. The child experiences an explosion, or rapid increase in their ability to use vocabulary and grammatical structures.

### **Childhood**

The child uses language increasingly as a communication tool. They hold conversations, talk about and describe experiences, and express ideas.
