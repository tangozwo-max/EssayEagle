Key messages in the case include the following:

- The school observed behaviors that healthcare and social services were unaware of, while healthcare and social services knew of risk factors that the school did not. This lack of coordination and communication between the different agencies ultimately proved fatal for Daniel.
- This case highlights the importance of safeguarding as a shared responsibility across various roles, including teaching staff, children's services, and healthcare providers. The message is clear: **safeguarding is everyone's business.** Many child protection failures have been due to services not sharing information and coordinating with each other.
- Keeping children safe involves hearing the perspectives of everyone involved and triangulating the facts. While the majority of families want the best for their children, professionals must always keep in mind that some may either deliberately or inadvertently cause harm.
- While this case is unusual in its severity, it highlights that safeguarding needs to be proactive and assertive rather than reactive and passive (for example, waiting for families to seek support).

In this week’s learning, you will learn about risk assessment and safeguarding, and will look at some of the common ethical dilemmas in child and adolescent mental health.

## Learning outcomes

On successful completion of this week, you will be able to:

- recognize the types of risks children are exposed to that involve ethical considerations on the part of the healthcare professional
- evaluate mental health risk assessment for children and adolescents
- outline areas commonly covered within risk assessments for children and adolescents
- critically evaluate the key ethical issues in mental health care for young people

> [!-info] -info
> **Note**: As you consider risk assessment and safeguarding topics this week, keep in mind that practitioners must maintain a low threshold for seeking supervision, recognizing the limits of their own expertise.
> 
> It is essential to seek advice from appropriate individuals, such as supervisors, other clinical team members, specialist forensic teams (in cases involving threats of serious violence), specialist self-harm teams, privacy officers, data protection officers (for complex confidentiality issues), and social workers (in potential child protection cases).
> 
> This collaborative approach ensures that all aspects of risk are addressed comprehensively, safeguarding both the practitioners and those they support.
