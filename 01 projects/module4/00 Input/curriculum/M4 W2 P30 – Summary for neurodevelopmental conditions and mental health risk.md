## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Neurodevelopmental conditions significantly impact mental health, with up to 80% of affected children experiencing mental health disorders, leading to poor quality of life and functional outcomes. These conditions are influenced by and affect the individual’s family, community, and economic situation, highlighting the complexity of their relationship with mental health risks.
- Autistic individuals often face social, psychological, and physical challenges that can impact their mental health, including stigma, discrimination, and the effort of masking, which can lead to anxiety and depression. Supportive environments in schools and workplaces, acceptance of sensory differences, and encouragement to pursue interests are essential for their mental well-being.
- Maltreated children are nearly 10 times more likely to have multiple neurodevelopmental conditions, placing them in double jeopardy for poor health outcomes due to the combined impact of adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) and neurodevelopmental conditions.
- Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD) encompasses a range of conditions caused by prenatal alcohol exposure, leading to variable symptoms depending on the timing and amount of exposure, as well as other factors like genetics and maternal health. It can result in distinct facial features and a wide array of cognitive, motor, and behavioral issues, but is often under-diagnosed due to a lack of standardized diagnostic approaches and other challenges.
