## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- In adolescents, signs of depression often include irritable mood, significant changes in weight or appetite, insomnia or hypersomnia, and loss of energy or fatigue.
- It is important to treat depression in adolescents to reduce the risks of recurrence, other mental health disorders, high-risk behaviors, and negative impacts on physical health and psychosocial functioning.
- The NICE guidelines for managing depression in adolescents recommend a stepped-care approach, including active monitoring, evidence-based psychological therapies, medication for moderate to severe cases, and collaboration with mental health specialists.
- Anxiety is the most common mental health disorder in children and adolescents. Separation anxiety disorder, selective mutism, and emotionally based school avoidance (EBSA) particularly impact young people.
- Internationally, the approach to diagnosing behavioral disorders like oppositional defiant disorder (ODD) and conduct disorder varies, with some countries diagnosing young children readily, while others reserve diagnoses for severe cases. Emphasis is often placed on understanding underlying causes rather than immediate diagnosis, and these disorders are characterized by difficulties in controlling emotions and behaviors that conflict with societal norms.
