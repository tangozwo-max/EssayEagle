## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Community psychology offers an alternative to traditional mental health services, particularly for young people in disproportionately disadvantaged communities. These communities face the greatest risk factors for poor mental health and often encounter significant barriers to accessing traditional services. Therefore, the need is increasing for more flexible, accessible services tailored to the needs of these young people.
- Social justice issues are at the heart of community approaches.
- Community-based approaches are grounded in the culture of their local communities. Co-production is an essential part of community-based approaches, which means that local communities determine what they need, rather than services.
- Community mental health teams provide structured mental health services within the community, often through clinics and outreach programs. In contrast, community-based approaches engage the community itself in the design and delivery of mental health support, leveraging local resources and participation.
