In the following weeks of this module, you will explore risk factors that affect child and adolescent mental health, and factors that affect the various domains of development. In recent years, the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted people's lives in ways that affected child development.

Can you think of some ways in which the pandemic might have affected children's development?

Select **Reveal** to view examples.

- Some children have experienced delays in speech, communication, and language development.
- Providers are making more referrals for external help than before the pandemic, and are waiting longer for this specialist help.
- Some children’s personal, social, and emotional development have been negatively affected. Many children lack confidence in group activities.
- In some cases, older children experienced new separation anxiety when their parents dropped them off at school or childcare services.

Although it is too soon to know what the longitudinal impact of the changes caused by the pandemic might be, several studies have been conducted on the effects of the pandemic on child development.

Select each heading to learn more.
## US

A 2024 study led by Johns Hopkins Children’s Center found that infants and children 5 years old and younger experienced only modest delays in developmental milestones due to the COVID-19 pandemic disruptions and restrictions. Using the Ages and Stages Questionnaire-3 (ASQ-3), researchers evaluated communication, problem-solving, personal-social skills, and motor skills among children in the study. Overall, while there were some changes, the impact was not severe. This finding provides cautious optimism about the development of young children exposed to pandemic-related restrictions.

\[Source: Johns Hopkins Medicine\]

A small study supported by the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) and conducted in the San Francisco Bay area found that during only 10 months of the COVID-19 pandemic, teen brains appeared to age an average of about 3 years. This effect mirrors what typically happens in teen brains after exposure to adversity or traumatic events.

\[Source: National Institute of Health\]

## JAP

A study in Japan analyzed data from January 2017 to May 2021 and found that rates of mental disorders (such as anxiety, depression, and stress) increased among preadolescents and adolescents during the pandemic.

\[Source: Matsumoto et al.\]

## UK
A UK study on children’s socio-emotional development during the pandemic found that changes to educational experiences (remote learning, disrupted routines) and parenting practices (including parental stress and working-from-home situations) impacted children's well-being and social and emotional development.

\[Source: GOV.UK\]
