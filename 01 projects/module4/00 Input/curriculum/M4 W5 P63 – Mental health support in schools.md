After home, children and adolescents spend most of their time at school. In addition to learning, children develop socially and emotionally at school. Their teachers and other school staff often know the children and their families well, including their family history, strengths, and risk factors. This familiarity places them well to notice early signs of distress.

Psychological practitioners working with children and adolescents often need to interact with schools for assessments, sharing formulations, or coordinated interventions, supporting the child at school and home.

This week, you will examine how mental health can be supported in schools and communities.

Here are some key points to consider:

- Schools influence children’s development and well-being.
- Mental health needs can initially be noticed in a school setting.
- Many children in a classroom may have experienced difficult life events, and the links between mental health and learning are bidirectional. Therefore, schools need trauma-informed practices and neuro-affirmative approaches similar to those in mental health care.

This week you will consider some of the interventions and supports in schools, and why they are important. Remember that young people in school often bring challenges from other aspects of their lives.  
  

## In a class of 30 students:

- one could have experienced the death of a parent

- four could be living in lone-parent families

- five could have a mental health disorder

- five could be living in poverty

- seven may have self-harmed

- eleven could have experienced bullying

## Learning outcomes

On successful completion of this week, you will be able to:

- critically evaluate the role of schools in supporting mental health
- analyze and apply the ecological systems model to the school environment, identifying key roles within the school mental health ecosystem
- analyze the unique challenges and considerations in school environments that impact mental health support
- evaluate whole school approaches that integrate health promotion, prevention, and intervention strategies
- analyze eight principles of a whole school approach for promoting mental health and well-being
- outline the components of a whole school approach to mental health
- analyze the purpose of a community psychology approach to mental health
