## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Many interventions that can be used to support children and young people's mental health are not solely mental health interventions.
- Universal interventions are generally about mental health promotion, education, and prevention.
- Sure Start is an example of a non-mental-health intervention that aims to support the health and well-being of families.
- Early intervention is put in place when children or young people are at risk of, or displaying symptoms of, poor mental health, but before a diagnosis has been made.
- Early interventions vary according to the age of the child or young person involved.
- Social prescribing is one such intervention that connects children and young people to their communities.
- Digital mental health services have the potential to provide better access and earlier interventions if properly developed.
