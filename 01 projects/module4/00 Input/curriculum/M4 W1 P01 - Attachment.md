## Attachment

"It’s impossible to overestimate how important the early years are."

Dr. Jack Shonkoff

Professor of Child Health and Development at Harvard University

Experience and relationships during infancy and early childhood form the foundation for lifelong cognitive, emotional, and social functioning. Neural connections are established that shape how a person perceives the world, learns, and interacts with others.

In this week's learning, you will explore the nature of attachment relationships and the role of attachment on infant brain development.

### What is infant mental health?

Infant mental health refers to the infant’s capacity to:

- form and maintain close relationships
- manage and express emotions
- feel comfortable about exploring their environment
- communicate in the context of their family, community, and culture

Infant mental health is dependent on secure, trusting relationships with their caregivers.

\[Source: Emerging Minds Australia\]

## Learning outcomes

On successful completion of this week, you will be able to:

- outline the basic concepts of attachment theory and its relevance to mental health
- analyze the role of attachment in providing mental health care and services
- relate attachment theory to neurodevelopment
- analyze the role of caregivers in infant brain development
- identify some basic developmental milestones that are expected for the majority of children
