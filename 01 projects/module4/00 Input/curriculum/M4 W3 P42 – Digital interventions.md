## Digital interventions

"As far as I’m concerned, there are only three challenges facing most first-world health systems. They are equity, access and digital – in that order. And the reason why they are in that order is that if digital isn’t helping you with the first two, then why are you using it?"

Lord Victor Adebowale

Chair, NHS Confederation

COVID-19, lockdown, and social distancing meant that mental healthcare providers needed to think about new ways to provide services. As a result, digital innovations became part of promotion and prevention.

The challenge now is to work out the role of digital mental health support and how to build the evidence base for its use.

What are the benefits of digital mental health technology?

### Improved access

Digital mental health solutions can overcome some of the barriers that prevent access to mental health services such as distance, mobility, and a preference for online rather than in-person services.

Examples of services that can help with this are:

- telemedicine apps
    
- online counseling
    
- clinically moderated peer support platforms
    

To ensure privacy, providers should make sure that the technology creates secure communication channels between mental health professionals and service users.

### Early intervention and prevention

Digital tools that track patterns in behavior and mood can enable early detection of mental health conditions. The data analysis provided by these tools, particularly if linked to appropriate artificial intelligence, can identify signs of distress and provide timely interventions.

However, there are also extra ethical issues for digital mental healthcare for children. These can include:

- children accessing them without parental knowledge
    
- availability of appropriate moderation and ensuring that the children are not vulnerable
    
- availability of safety procedures if it is thought that a child may be at risk of harm
    

### Improving supply

The demand for mental health services always exceeds the supply. Digital solutions can help to increase supply as they can be scaled up as required. Particularly useful are those that empower people to independently manage their mental health and that build community among people experiencing mental health conditions.

Examples of these apps might be:

- guided meditation
    
- stress management
    
- virtual support groups
    
- peer-to-peer platforms
    

There are a number of opportunities, benefits, and challenges that come with the use of digital services.

|Opportunities|Benefits|Challenges|
|---|---|---|
|Increased accessibility: can reach those in remote areas and underserved communities|Improved treatment outcomes: evidence-based interventions can lead to positive outcomes|Privacy and security: robust data protection measures required|
|Personalization: interventions can be based on personal needs and preferences|Reduced stigma: online interventions can reduce the stigma of seeking mental health support and can be accessed from private spaces|Regulatory compliance: particularly complex in the case of cross-border data sharing and telemedicine services|
|Data-driven insights: digital tools collect large quantities of data, which can provide insights into trends, outcomes, and behaviors|Scalability: larger audiences can be reached quickly|Equal access to digital technology: the digital divide|
|Cost-effectiveness: lower costs than traditional therapies|Diverse modalities: mobile apps, augmented reality, online therapy|Quality, efficacy, and managing risk: is there good evidence for the technology being used?|
|Early intervention and prevention: early detection of mental health conditions enabling timely interventions|Value for money: saves money for clients and providers through early intervention (preventing the need for more intensive services), increased reach (avoiding the need for outreach clinics or expensive travel), ongoing support (to prevent relapse)|Therapeutic relationship: a trusting relationship with a therapist is important for therapy to be effective. It is harder to create without the in-person context. Some apps facilitate accessing a therapist in person via telemedicine, other apps may rely on chat bots powered by AI without a therapist. It is usually easier to form a relationship in the first rather than the second.|

## Guiding principles

Digital mental health services can guide improved mental health among a population if three principles are taken into account.

Select each heading to learn more about these principles.

Collaboration

Collaboration can ensure that expertise is shared across mental health and digital sectors as well as across statutory, voluntary, and independent organizations. Services users and their communities should be involved as well as service providers.

Clarity

Digital mental health developments should be shared between stakeholders at all levels so that people are aware of innovations and initiatives. This will improve impact and opportunities for learning and sharing from people's experiences.

Coordination

Coordination between national policy makers and mental health service providers would ensure that the providers are better equipped to make decisions about digital mental health services. It would help providers to ensure that services align with best practice guidance and national systems. Good coordination can enable choice and access, allowing benefits for local population health systems.

[Source: NHS Confederation]
