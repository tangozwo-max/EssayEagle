## Developmental milestones

[[developmental-milestones]] are a set of age-specific tasks that most children can demonstrate at a certain age range. They represent significant steps in a child’s development and include skills such as moving, talking, playing, and interacting with others. These milestones are used to track a child’s progress and to identify if they are developing as expected.

## Principles of development

A child’s journey through various developmental phases is influenced by a distinctive combination of innate physical and psychological traits, along with external factors like socioeconomic status, exposure to substances before birth, or the presence of nurturing caregivers.

Development across different domains is interconnected but may progress unevenly. While the categories may vary depending on context, the underlying concepts remain consistent, regardless of classification.

Social

The social domain focuses on how children interact with others and navigate social environments. It includes skills such as cooperation, sharing, empathy, forming friendships, and working in groups.

Emotional

This domain involves the ability to experience, express, and manage emotions. It includes developing self-awareness, self-regulation, and the ability to form secure and healthy relationships with others.

Cognitive

Cognitive development involves learning, attention, memory, language, thinking, reasoning, and creativity. It is about how children think, explore, and figure things out. This domain also includes problem-solving skills and the acquisition of knowledge throughout life.

Language communication

This domain is about how children develop their language skills. It includes the progression from babbling in infancy to acquiring a full vocabulary and the ability to communicate effectively. Language development is necessary for expressing thoughts, and emotions, and engaging in social interactions.

Physical

The physical domain covers the growth and changes in the body and brain, including the senses, motor skills, health, and wellness. It involves both fine and gross motor skills, such as walking, crawling, or grasping, as well as changes in height, weight, and muscular strength.

## Why are developmental milestones important?

Developmental milestones provide important information for mental health care. Knowledge of these milestones enables practitioners to adjust assessment methods, consider specific information to include in assessments, and refine treatment goals appropriately.

Select each heading for some examples.

Motor skills (Physical)

Having difficulty with motor skills can affect a child and adolescent's self-esteem, mental health, and social functioning. These skills affect a person's ability to perform personal care, engage in physical activity, and perform academically.

Coordination difficulties occur when a person has difficulty sequencing and moving their body, so if a child experiences these difficulties, they might also experience difficulties with planning thinking ahead, and maintaining attention. These difficulties may be signs of [[dcd|developmental coordination disorder (DCD)]], previously known as dyspraxia.

Motor skills are also closely related to sensory modulation, including, for example, the ability to tolerate noise or certain clothing.

Language and communication

Language skills are important to mental health professionals because when individuals cannot easily express their emotions through language, they may express them behaviorally.

Children who have developmental difficulties with language may have poorer psychosocial functioning later on in life. For example, as children, they may struggle more academically and, as adults, might have difficulty with employment.

Social
Progression against the social milestones can be an important indicator of neurodevelopmental conditions, such as [[autism]], and can indicate difficulties with an underlying foundational skill, such as language or [[cognitive-development-stages|cognitive functioning]]..

## Considerations when using information from developmental milestones

These milestones may be very culturally dependent; e.g., children start school at different ages internationally. Expectations around appropriate behavior also vary by culture; e.g., in some cultures, children are expected to sit quietly and still from a very young age, whereas in others, this behavior is expected only much later.

Typical behaviors are shown and experienced differently by neurodivergent children. This is sometimes framed as failure to achieve, or missing [[developmental-milestones]]. However, from a [[neuro-affirmative]] point of view, it could be seen simply as a difference; e.g., [[autism|autistic]] adults having friendships built more on shared interests.

The ranges for these milestones can be broad, and it is not uncommon for a child to appear to miss a milestone but then show a fast burst of development in a particular area.

> [!-info] -info
> **Note**: The focus on rigid monitoring of developmental milestones is, again, culturally specific, with the goal of early intervention from health care, social, or education services. Thresholds for intervention differ by culture and country, partly due to cultural beliefs, and partly due to available resources.
