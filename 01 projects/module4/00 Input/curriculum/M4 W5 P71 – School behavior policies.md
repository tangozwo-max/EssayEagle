Schools develop behavior policies to promote positive behavior and manage misconduct. These policies clearly outline the:

- expected standards of behavior for students
- procedures for handling behavior incidents
- consequences of misbehavior

They aim to create a safe and conducive learning environment by setting rules, providing rewards for good behavior, and detailing sanctions for rule violations.

Repeated violations of behavior policies can negatively impact learning and well-being, leading to consequences that range from detention and suspension to, in the most extreme cases, exclusion.

Can you think of some possible limitations or disadvantages of behavior and conduct policies in schools?

 

School behavior policies are problematic for several reasons:

- Policies may be applied unevenly, leading to perceptions of unfairness among students.
- Many policies focus on punishment rather than positive reinforcement, which can negatively impact student behavior and the school environment.
- These policies often disproportionately affect vulnerable students, such as those who belong to minority groups and those with disabilities, exacerbating existing inequalities.
- These policies are unlikely to be effective if they do not take into account key factors such as unmet special educational needs, child poverty, and mental health challenges.

In 2024, As I Am, an autism charity in Ireland, published *What We Wish You Knew,* a policy analysis of school behavior codes throughout Ireland.

**Read this excerpt from the report.**

"... current Code of Behaviour policies are not rooted in a rights-based, neuro-affirmative approach but rather are often implicitly, explicitly and structurally ableist and discriminatory in nature. On the one hand, rewarding and promoting neurotypical norms for communication, processing, cognition and learning and on the other hand othering or even punishing young people based on their neurotype and associated support needs."

\[Source: As I Am\]
