## Why are the early years of a child's life critical for brain development?

Attachment relationships and early experiences influence and are influenced by brain development, particularly during the first few years of life.

Select the arrow to learn more.

  

The brains of infants and children are highly adaptable, which facilitates rapid learning. This period is characterized by rapid brain growth and development.
[[synaptic-pruning]] occurs rapidly between the ages of two and ten. Constant stimulation can cause synapses to grow and become permanent, while a lack of stimulation may result in fewer connections. [[synaptic-plasticity]] makes it easier for children to learn, but it can also account for childhood experiences affecting long-term development.
New stimuli and experiences in the infant's environment lead to a form of learning called [[associative-learning]]. Associative learning occurs when someone forms an association between two stimuli or between a behavior and a stimulus.

## Brain development in childhood and adolescence

### Embryonic stage
![[Pasted image 20260417161112.png|212]]
By the end of the embryonic stage, (gestational week 10), the basic structures of the neural system are established. They continue to grow throughout the fetal period and into early childhood. New synapses form between neurons during the second and third trimesters of pregnancy, and increase rapidly during infancy, peaking around age two.
### Childhood
By the age of six, 90% of the brain has developed.

Synaptic pruning reduces excess synapses and improves neural efficiency. Both genetic factors and experiences influence pruning.


### Adolescence

- Neocortical myelination continues throughout adolescence and into early adulthood.
    
- The pre-frontal cortex, which is associated with self-control and rationality, develops later than the ventral striatum, which is associated with reward. This process might explain risk-taking behavior and addiction in teenagers.
    
- Changes to the limbic system, which controls emotions, also occur during adolescence.
    
- Changes in the ventromedial prefrontal cortex, which is associated with self-evaluation, occur during puberty. These changes can improve a person's understanding of social interactions and their ability to form friendships, but they can also make teenagers more vulnerable to social anxiety.
    ![[Pasted image 20260417161126.png|321]]
- Adolescence is a key period of vulnerability for mental health disorders.
