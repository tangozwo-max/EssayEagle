### Neurodiversity

**Neurodiversity** refers to the differences in how people's brains work and encompasses everyone. Neurotypical people are included within neurodiversity.

  
### Neurodivergence

**Neurodivergence** refers to established differences in cognitive functioning, but the point of onset and cause are not always specified.

Neurodivergence is a nonmedical term that describes people whose brains develop or work differently for some reason. This means the person has different strengths and struggles from people whose brains develop or work more typically. Some neurodivergent individuals have medical conditions, while others do not have any diagnosed condition.

  
### Neurodevelopmental conditions

**Neurodevelopmental conditions** are a group of disorders with onset in the developmental period that impact an individual’s functioning. With neurodevelopmental conditions, the point of onset and cause are potentially known, as in certain genetic conditions.

Neurodiversity and neurodivergence are not definable medical conditions, while neurodevelopmental conditions are.

![[Pasted image 20260417164840.png]]
The relationship between neurodevelopmental conditions and mental health risks is complex. These conditions do not exist in isolation; they are influenced by and might affect the person’s family, community, and economic situation.

One in ten children meet diagnostic criteria for neurodevelopmental conditions.

Up to 80% of children with NDCs experience mental health disorders, which contribute to poor quality of life and functional outcomes.

Children with NDCs such as autism and ADHD represent one-quarter of all pediatric mental health presentations in emergency departments.

> More than half of the children referred to child and adolescent mental health services meet the diagnostic criteria for at least one neurodevelopmental condition (NDC).

\[Source: Boulton et al.\]
