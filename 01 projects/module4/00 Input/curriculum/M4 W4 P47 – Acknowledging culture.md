## Acknowledging culture

Throughout this course, you have been encouraged to consider how culture is related to mental health. Cultural norms and values can affect the stigma associated with mental health disorders, the types of support systems available, and the methods of treatment that are considered acceptable. Understanding and integrating cultural context into mental health care can enhance the effectiveness of interventions, promote better communication between caregivers and patients, and ensure that care is respectful and relevant to the individual’s background.

The Social GRACES model is a framework developed by John Burnham and colleagues to help understand and reflect on aspects of personal and social identity, including those related to culture. It provides a starting point to consider the diverse elements of both practitioners’ and service users’ identities and their impact on how the world is experienced. These characteristics may be visible or invisible to others, and spoken about or not spoken about to others.
