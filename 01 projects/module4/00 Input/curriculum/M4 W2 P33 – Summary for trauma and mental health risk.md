## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Experiencing trauma can significantly impact a child’s mental health by altering their behavior and social interactions. Children who have faced trauma may overreact or become overly passive, which can lead to social isolation and educational difficulties, as their behavior might be seen as inappropriate by peers and adults.
- Without supportive friends or adults, these children miss out on social interactions that are essential for building confidence and social skills. This lack of support can hinder their ability to find employment or seek help in the future.
- Complex trauma, which involves multiple, interconnected traumatic experiences often beginning in early childhood, can have long-lasting effects on mental health. It also intersects with broader societal issues, particularly impacting marginalized communities.
