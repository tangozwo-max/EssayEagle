## Risk assessment is a core part of mental health care

In a clinical approach to child and adolescent mental health, risk assessment initially occurs during the assessment phase, although it is important to reassess risk continuously.

**Risk assessment** involves evaluating the potential risks that a young person might pose to themselves or others, as well as the risks they are exposed to. This requires considering the young person within the context of their entire system, including family, school, and social environments.

Identifying and managing these risks helps ensure the safety and well-being of the child or adolescent. Therefore, risk assessment is a critical step toward a formulation, treatment plan, and successful intervention.

\[Source: Cantrell et al.\]

### **Harm to self**

Risk to self refers to the potential for a young person to engage in behaviors that could cause harm to themselves, such as self-harm, suicide, and self-neglect.

### **Harm to others**

Risk to others includes aggressive or violent behavior toward peers, family members, or others within their family, school, or social environment.

### **Harm from others**

The risk of harm from others involves abuse and neglect, which include a broad range of risks that are sometimes overlooked, such as grooming and exploitation.

## Principles of assessing mental health risk in young people

- Ensure the assessment is suitable for the young person's developmental stage, including their ability to express themselves verbally.
    
- Conduct assessments in a non-shaming and supportive manner to prevent disengagement, particularly in cases involving self-harm.
    
- Use structured, evidence-based methods that are consistent among practitioners.
    
- Ensure documentation is thorough and adheres to established guidelines.
    
- Regularly re-assess risk, as it can change rapidly, especially in situations involving emotional dysregulation or substance use.
    
- Pay special attention to risk assessment during transitions, such as between inpatient settings and community care.
    
- Foster strong communication and collaboration within a multidisciplinary team to ensure comprehensive and cohesive care.
    

[Source: Cantrell et al.]

## Risk assessment cycle

The mental health risk assessment cycle is a process designed to ensure the safety and well-being of individuals, particularly children.

Select the arrow to learn more.
![[Pasted image 20260419151632.png]]

Structured clinical judgment is at the center of the risk assessment cycle. It involves using a combination of clinical expertise, evidence-based guidelines, and structured tools to make informed decisions about risk. The aim is to use these to make the best decision that can be made at that time.

Gathering information for risk assessment should be thorough and multisource, including medical records, interviews with parents and school, and direct assessments with the young person. Effective information gathering and sharing ensure a comprehensive understanding of the risks involved.

Identifying the factors that increase or decrease the likelihood of harm involves understanding the child's environment, behaviors, and any support systems in place.

Creating a formulation regarding the risk involves developing a comprehensive understanding of the identified risks and considering how they interact and impact the child's safety and well-being. This holistic view helps in addressing the complexities of each case effectively.

Developing a management plan involves outlining strategies and actions to manage or mitigate the identified risks. This plan should be practical, evidence-based, and tailored to the child's specific needs.
Sharing information involves informing relevant services about the risks and management plan. Effective communication and coordination among healthcare, schools, and social services can prevent child protection failures.

[Source: Department of Health]

Information about risks and management plans should be reviewed regularly, with processes in place to ensure updated information is shared between services as quickly as possible.
