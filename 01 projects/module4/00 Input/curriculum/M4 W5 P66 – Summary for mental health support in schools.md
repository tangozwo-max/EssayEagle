## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Public policy in the UK reveals a historical context for addressing emotional and mental health needs in education.
- Innovators in this field include Maria Montessori, August Aichhorn, A.S. Neill, Susan Isaacs, and Anna Freud.
- In the context of mental health, school is an ecosystem in which students, staff, and the broader community are interconnected.
- Schools significantly influence children’s development and are often the first place where mental health needs are noticed.
- Many children in a classroom may have experienced difficult life events, and the links between mental health and learning are bidirectional. Therefore, schools need trauma-informed practices and neuro-affirmative approaches similar to those in mental health care.
- The team in the school, including teachers and counselors, collaborates with the team around the school (such as external mental health professionals and community services) to support students’ mental health comprehensively.
