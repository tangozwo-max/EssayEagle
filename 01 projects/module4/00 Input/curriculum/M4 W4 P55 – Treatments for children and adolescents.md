Treating young people with mental health disorders requires a multifaceted approach to ensure effective and comprehensive care. As you have already learned, early intervention can help identify and address mental health risks, prevent disorders from developing, and lead to better long-term outcomes.

Individualized treatment plans often combine psychotherapy, medication, and lifestyle changes tailored to the person's specific needs. A collaborative approach involving healthcare providers, caregivers, educators, and other professionals supporting the child ensures a well-rounded support system.

The therapies you will learn about include:

- cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
- psychodynamic therapy
- systemic family therapy
- dialectical behavioral therapy

Check your understanding of some of the therapies used with children and adolescents.

Match each description to the therapy it describes.

- Examines the dynamics and relationships within a group to address issues affecting the entire unit Answer: **Systemic family therapy**
- Explores unconscious processes and past experiences to understand current behavior Answer: **Cognitive behavior therapy**
- Focuses on identifying and changing negative thought patterns and behaviors
- Aims to enhance communication and social skills by addressing relational issues and improving social functioning Answer: **Interpersonal therapy**
- Focuses on teaching skills to manage their emotions, cope with stress, and improve relationships, using strategies like mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness Answer: **Dialectical behavioral therapy**
