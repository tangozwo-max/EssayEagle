## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Therapies commonly used to treat children and adolescents with mental health disorders include cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), psychodynamic therapy, and systemic family therapy.
- Research indicates that children as young as 5 can engage with CBT techniques if developmentally appropriate methods like visual aids, games, and stories are used. CBT for children and adolescents must be adapted to their developmental levels.
- Psychodynamic therapy explores the unconscious mind’s influence on behavior and emotions, examining early experiences and relationships. The evidence base for psychodynamic child psychotherapy is limited due to the small number of randomized controlled trials (RCTs).
- Systemic family therapy, or family systems therapy, is a psychotherapy approach that views individuals as part of interconnected systems, focusing on improving dynamics within these systems. It is effective for addressing child-focused problems like attachment issues, aspects of child abuse, disruptive behavior disorders, and adolescent eating disorders.
- There is increasing evidence for using dialectical behavioral therapy to address self-harm.
