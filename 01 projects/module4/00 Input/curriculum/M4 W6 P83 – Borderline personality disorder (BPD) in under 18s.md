Healthcare professionals aim to ensure that young people receive the earliest possible access to specialist interventions, and are provided with explanations of their experiences that are understandable, clinically valid, and informative regarding their condition, prognosis, and support needs. This approach is intended to best support their identity and quality of life.

There are often polarizing views among healthcare professionals regarding these issues, and some professionals may hold views that oppose the dominant perspective within their healthcare services. One clinically relevant example of this is BPD.
![[Pasted image 20260419151847.png]]
BPD is a psychiatric diagnosis. Symptoms range from mild to severe and relate to:

- emotional instability (affective dysregulation)
- altered patterns of thinking or perception
- impulsive behavior
- intense and often unstable interpersonal relationships

\[Source: American Psychiatric Association\]

The concept of borderline personality disorder (BPD) is often controversial due to several factors:

- the stigma associated with the diagnosis
- the high proportion of individuals with BPD who have experienced trauma and adverse events
- the insufficient consideration of social context in understanding the disorder
- historical critiques suggesting that the diagnosis reflects a misogynistic view of women's distress

These issues contribute to ongoing debates about the validity and implications of the BPD diagnosis.

\[Source: Anna Freud\]

## Diagnosing BPD in young people

BPD is noted to emerge in adolescence, remaining through adulthood. However, there is controversy regarding diagnosing children and adolescents with BPD. Cases are made on both sides.

Select each card to learn more.

### Advocates
for early diagnosisemphasize the role of early intervention in helping people to understand their difficulties and receive effective support, improving well-being and both immediate and long-term outcomes. BPD is a condition that can present with increased risk to self and suicide attempts, which are very distressing for young people and their families.

### Advocates 
against diagnosing young people with BPD highlight:

- The validity of the diagnosis is questioned.
- BPD symptoms must be pervasive, which is easier to confirm in adults than children.
- Adolescence involves significant emotional, relational, and identity development, making it important to distinguish symptoms from typical teenage challenges.
- Early diagnosis could shape self-perception and experiences, potentially leading to self-stigma and stigma from others.
