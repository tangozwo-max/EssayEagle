## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Risk assessment is a fundamental part of mental health care for children and adolescents, involving continuous evaluation of potential risks to ensure their safety and well-being.
- Mental health risk assessment requires considering the young person's entire system, including family, school, and social environments and involves collaboration among various professionals to provide comprehensive care.
- Interprofessional and multidisciplinary collaboration among services and professionals is critical in safeguarding children and young people. While each professional might have individual concerns, collaboration can provide a comprehensive understanding of the young person's experience.
- Mental health risk assessment involves assessing risk of harm to self, harm to others, and harm from others.
- Mental health risk assessment is based on structured clinical judgment and involves information gathering and sharing, identifying risk and protective factors, formulation, and managing risk.
- Child safeguarding involves measures to protect young people from abuse, harm, and neglect, ensuring their health, well-being, and human rights are upheld.
- Certain ethnic and cultural groups face particular safeguarding challenges that involve adultification, overlooking child protection concerns, and bias. Adults who work with children from these groups must be aware of these challenges and take steps to follow best practices to protect the children they work with.
- Gillick competence evaluates a child's maturity and understanding to make informed decisions about their health care.
- Fraser guidelines specifically apply to decisions regarding contraception and sexual health, ensuring that the child comprehends the implications and that the treatment is in their best interest.
