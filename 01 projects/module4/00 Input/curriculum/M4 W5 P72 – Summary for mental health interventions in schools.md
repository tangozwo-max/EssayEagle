## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- A whole-school approach to mental health means developing and implementing policies and practices that aim to promote the mental health and well-being of pupils, parents and caregivers, and staff.
- The eight principles of a whole school approach are leadership and management, student voice, staff development, identifying needs and monitoring impact, working with parents, families, and carers, ethos and environment, curriculum, teaching, and learning, and providing targeted support and appropriate referrals.
- Direct mental health interventions in schools involve providing individual or group therapy sessions to students, while indirect interventions include training and supporting school staff to create a positive and supportive environment for all students.
- The effectiveness of school interventions depends on intervention characteristics, school characteristics, provider characteristics, and community factors.
- School behavior policies aim to promote positive behavior and manage misconduct, but they often face challenges such as inconsistent application, over-reliance on punitive measures, and disproportionate impacts on vulnerable students.
