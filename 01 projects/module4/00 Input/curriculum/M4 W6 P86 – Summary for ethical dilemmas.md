## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Ethical challenges in child and adolescent mental health care include confidentiality and disclosure, informed consent, autonomy, capacity, and competency, best interests of the child, mandatory reporting, and cultural and social sensitivity.
- Diagnosing borderline personality disorder (BPD) in young people presents ethical challenges, including the risk of misdiagnosing typical adolescent behavior as pathological and the potential for stigma, which can affect self-perception and social interactions.
- Certain elements of gender-affirming care require balancing the need for early intervention to support mental health and well-being against the potential long-term risks of medical treatments. Healthcare professionals must compassionately navigate the contentious and highly emotive nature of the debate, which is influenced by societal, political, and nonmedical factors.
- Ethical challenges in treating pediatric eating disorders include ensuring that interventions respect the autonomy and developing capacity of young people while also addressing the immediate health risks posed by the disorder. Healthcare professionals must balance providing necessary medical treatment and avoiding potential harm from invasive intervention.
