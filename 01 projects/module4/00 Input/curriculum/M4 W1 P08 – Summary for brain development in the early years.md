## Key learning points

You have completed this section. Review each key learning point before you progress.

- Brain development begins during the embryonic period, with the basic neural system established by the tenth week of gestation.
- By age six, 90% of the brain’s volume is developed, highlighting the critical nature of early experiences. While significant growth occurs in childhood, some brain areas, like the prefrontal cortex, do not fully mature until around age 25, affecting behaviors related to self-control, risk-taking, and addiction.
- Secure attachments in infants and children foster a conducive environment for play, which is necessary for their learning and brain development.
- Parents can best support this development by engaging in responsive, reciprocal interactions, creating a safe space for exploration, and being attuned to the child’s individual needs.
- Executive function refers to a set of mental skills that are necessary for managing tasks, solving problems, and adapting to new situations. To foster executive function, caregivers should establish routines, help children distill information into chunks, and play games that teach self-control.
