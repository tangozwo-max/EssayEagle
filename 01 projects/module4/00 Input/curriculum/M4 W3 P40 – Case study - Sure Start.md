Sure Start is a program of support for young families in areas with high indicators of social deprivation in the UK. The aim is to provide holistic support to improve families' health and well-being in all areas—a one-stop shop for support.

The services provided by Sure Start vary from area to area but usually include:

- health advice
- parenting advice
- education support
- employment support
- mental health and behavioral support

Sure Start was not directly aimed at improving mental health; its aim was to improve social determinants of health and reduce risk factors among the populations it served. This has been shown to lead to improved mental health.

As well as children's support workers, Sure Start centers are staffed by health workers, social workers, parenting support workers, and others. They liaise with education, health, local government, and charitable services, connecting them and offering holistic support.

The Institute for Fiscal Studies conducted independent research into the outcomes of Sure Start services in England for children's health and education. Their findings were:

- Sure Start reduced the likelihood of hospitalization among children of primary school age.
- The reduction in hospitalization was mainly due to reductions in infections for younger children and reductions in injuries for older children.
- Children who had access to a Sure Start center had improved educational grades at age 16.
- Children who had special educational needs were more likely to have them recognized earlier on and were less likely to need special support as they got older.
- There was a reduction in hospitalization for mental-health-related reasons in adolescence.

\[Source: Cattan et al.; Carneiro et al.; Cattan et al.\]
